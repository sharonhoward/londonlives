City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of March in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of George Wilkinson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Nelson , Richard Wrather , Thomas Holmes John Woolley , Thomas Stephens , John Tranfield , William Hewlet William Grant , Peter Copeland , Henry Norton , John Millener and John Watkins good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said George Wilkinson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That on the Twenty fifth day of March in the Year aforesaid the said George Wilkinson was driving a certain empty Cart in the Kings high way Piccadylli in the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and going through the Turnpike Gate at Hyde Park Corner It so happened that an Iron Hook belonging to the said Gate Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune caught hold of the left shoulder of the said George Wilkinson , and forced the Head of the said George Wilkinson between the Iron Hinge of the said Gate and the near Wheel of the said Cart by reason thereof the said George Wilkinson then and there Accidentally received divers Bruises and Fractures in and upon the Head of him the said George Wilkinson of which said mortal Bruises and Fractures he the said George Wilkinson then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said George Wilkinson in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Nelson Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

James Nelson Foreman
