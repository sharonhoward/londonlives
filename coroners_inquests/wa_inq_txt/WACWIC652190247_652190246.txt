City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Knights bridge in the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of June in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third; by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Charles Hardy then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Ralph Mills , Robert Jackson , Robert Anderson , William Stradling  Ruben Jackson James Jones , Samuel Rudd , Richard Tomkins , Thomas Brasier  John Bristow William Hinshelwood and Thomas Field , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Charles Hardy came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Charles Hardy , a Boy, on the thirteenth day of June in the year aforesaid getting upon a certain Bay Mare at Knights bridge in the Parish aforesaid and not Knowing how to Ride, It so happened that the said Charles Hardy then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Mare unto the Ground, by Means whereof he the said Charles Hardy did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive divers Bruises in and upon his Head and Body, of which said Mortal Bruises he the said Charles Hardy then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Charles Hardy the Boy in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Ralph Mills Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Ralph Mills Foreman
