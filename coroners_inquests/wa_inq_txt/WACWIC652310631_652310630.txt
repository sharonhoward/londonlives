City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex . } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty first day of November in the Thirty Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Audas then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of William Mourn , Henry Leech , Samuel Casley , William Gibson John Elridge , Laurence Kyte , Richard Nowster , Thomas Mc.Neaser George Colings , John Powell , John Wilson , Cuthbert Hilton , Arthur Haires and Edward Selven good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Audas came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Eighteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid the said Thomas Andas being at Work upon a Chimney belonging to a House in King Street in said Parish of. St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and these standing upon a Scaffold on the outside of the said House and near the Top thereof, It so happened that the Scaffold gave way and that the said Thomas and as Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell of and from the said Seaffold unto and upon the Stone Pavement in the said Street and thereby did then and there receive a Fracture in and upon his left Thigh a Fracture upon the left Arms and a Rupture of a blood Vessel in the Head of which said Fractures and Rupture he the said Thomas Audas from the said Eighteenth Day of November in the Year aforesaid until the Nineteenth day of the same Month and Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Nineteenth day of November in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County of aforesaid he the said Thomas Audas of the mortal Rupture aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon then oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Andas in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death & not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Mourn  Foreman of the said Jurors in the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in the presence have to this Inquisition at their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

William Mourn Foreman
