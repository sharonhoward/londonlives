City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteen the day of May in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Matthews then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Jobbins , Joseph Wilmot , John Carter , William Flowers , Henry List , Benjamin Bailey , Nathan Smith , James Parrott , William Cook , Richard Burton , Lionel Ellenthorpe and James Jones good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Matthews came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said William Matthews on the Eighteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid, being a Person liable and subject to Fits, was gently riding on a certain Bay Horse in the King's High Way in Hyde Park in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being so riding as aforesaid, It so happened that he the said William Matthews was then and there suddenly Seised with one of his usual Fitts, and by reason of the violence thereof he the said William Matthews then and there fell from the Back of the said Horse to and against the Ground in the said Highway, and then and there instantly died, but had no Marks of Violence or Bruises appearing on his Body. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Matthews by the Violence of the Fit aforesaid and in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his Death and not otherwise; and that the said Black Gelding was in no sort unruly, or the Occasion of the Death of the said William Matthews . In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Jobbins Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Js. Jobins . Foreman
