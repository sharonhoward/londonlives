City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of November in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Price then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Horwood , William Hill , William Robertson , Joseph Atkinson , Edward Gregory Charles Newman , Henry Lewer , John Jones , John Wallis , Thomas Storer , Thomas Hodgson and Benjamin Taylor good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the king, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Price came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Price on the said Fifth day of November in the Year aforesaid was found drowned and Suffocated in the River near the Fire Engine at Pimlico in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, That the said Thomas Price has no Marks of Violence appearing on his body, but how or by what Means he became Drowned and Suffocated no Evidence there of doth appear to the Jurors.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Horwood Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prichard  Coroner Tho Horwood Foreman}
