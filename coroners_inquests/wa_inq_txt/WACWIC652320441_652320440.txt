City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex .} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square and held by Adjournment from 31st. day of July within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the First day of August 1792 in the thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq.  Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Gunning Perriaman then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of William Storey , William Blackwill  Edward Stephens , Richard Thacher , David Wernham , George Brian , John Barton , John Wonham , Thomas Wigfield , Edward Millar , Daniel Bradds , William Motlock John Forster , and John Phillips good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Gunning Perriman came to her Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Sarah Gunning Perriman not being, of sound Mind, Memory, and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, on the thirtieth day of July in the year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid, To Wit in a certain Piece of Water in Hyde commonly called the Bason, there did cast and throw hereself by means of which said casting and Throwing she the said Sarah Gunning Perriman in the Water of the said Bason was then and there suffocated and drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning, she the said Sarah Gunning, Perriman then and the died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Sarah Gunning Perriman in manner and by the Means aforesaid not being of sound Mind, Memory, and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted did down and Kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Storey  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day year, and Place above written.

Anthy. Gell Coroner Wm Storey Forman
