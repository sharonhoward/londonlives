An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Tothill Fields  Bridewell in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of September in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the ing for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Susannah Ray a Prisoner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Barrett , Thomas Hamilton , John Marsden , George Douglass , William Whitworth , Thomas Watson , William Foster William Collins , Thomas Stanley , Samuel Shudwick  Thomas Dinham Humphrey Hedges and George Young good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chose, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Susannah Ray came to h er Death, do upon their oath say , That the said Susannah Ray, being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields  Bridewell in the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Twenty Eighth day of September in the year aforesaid at Tothill Fields Bridewell aforesaid departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way. (to wit) of a Fever and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Barrett Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Barrett Foreman
