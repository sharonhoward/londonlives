City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the First day of January in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Peter Eaton then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Mourn  George Morrison  Edward Richardson  William Capper , John Chewter , John Pattison , John Chivers , John Ward  William Hancock  Robert Bigby , John Skilling , John Draper , John Hilman , Thomas Readworth & Gabriel Percy Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Peter Eaton came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Peter Eaton being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields  Bridewell in the Parish Aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the First day of January in the Year aforesaid at Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way to Wit, of a Fever, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Mourn Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above mentioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Wm. Mourn

Foreman
