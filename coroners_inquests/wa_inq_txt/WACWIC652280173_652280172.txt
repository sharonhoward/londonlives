City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fourth day of March in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Dale then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Rogers , John Beach , John Griffiths William Littleboy , John Hill , William Burl , William Jacobs , William Withers , William Jordan , John Chivers , George Lucas , and Edward Lowe good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Dale came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said John Dale on the sixth day of February in the Year aforesaid between the Hours of Eleven and Twelve at night being Intoxicated with Liquor and standing under a Gateway leading from Downing Street into the Treasury Yard near the Corner of the Dwelling House of William Cooper in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid that George Healy was then carefully driving a certain Coach through the said Gateway into the Treasury Yard And that the said John Dale endeavouring to avoid the said Carriage struck his Foot against a Wooden Spur Post fixed against the said House and fell upon the Stone Pavement in the said Yard by means whereof the said John Dale then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received a Fracture in and upon his Left Leg of which said Fracture he the said John Dale from the sixth Day of February in the Year aforesaid until the twenty second day of March in the same year at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said twenty second day of March in the year aforesaid he the said John Dale at the said Parish Liberty and County of the mortal Fracture aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Dale in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Richard Rogers Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard Coronor Richd. Rogers Foreman
