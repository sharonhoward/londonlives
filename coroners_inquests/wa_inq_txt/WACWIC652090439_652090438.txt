City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighteenth day of October in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner, of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Bradshaw then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Benjamin Watkins , Phineas Philemore , John Topham , Jonathan Davies , John Kennedy , William Walsh , George Short , Thomas Coleman , Peter Bennett , John Osborn , Joseph Hall , Thomas Cooper , Benjn. Hinton , James Barber  James Mathews , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Bradshaw came to his Death, do upon their Oath say ,That the said Thomas Bradshaw a Laborer on the Sixteenth day of October in the Year aforesaid being at Work on a certain New Building situate in Durham Yard on the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and then and there coming down a Ladder from the top of the said Building, It so happened that the said Thomas Bradshaw then and there Accidentally Casually and by misfortune fell off and from the said Ladder at the Height of two Stories, unto the Ground, by means of which said fall he the said Thomas Bradshaw did then and there receive divers Mortal Bruises in and upon the Body and Head of him the said Thomas Bradshaw of which said Mortal Bruises he the said Thomas Bradshaw Languished about the Space of one hour, and thereof then did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Bradshaw in manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally casually, and by misfortune, came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Benjamin Watkins Foreman of the said Jurors the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and place first above written.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Benjn Watkins Foreman
