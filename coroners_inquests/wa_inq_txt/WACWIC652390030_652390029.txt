City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Dwelling House of Mr. Grant the Sign of the White House in Knights bridge in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty fourth day of January 1799 in the thirty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of Body of Duncan Grant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said Duncan Grant came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that Some song Persons to these Jurors unknown not having the Fear of God before his or their Eyes but moved and seduced by the Justigation of the Devil on the twenty sixth day of December in the said thirty ninth Year of the Reign four said Sovereign Lord King. George at the Parish of Saint Giles in the Fields in the County aforesaid in the Force and Arms in and upon the said Duncan Grant in the Peace of God and our said Lord the King, then and there being feloniously wilfully and their Malice forethought did make un Assault. And that the said Person or Persons unknown in the certain Cutlapes and Bludgeous which he and they said persons or persons unknown the & there had and held in his and their right hand or hand him the said Duncan Grant in and upon the Head, Shoulders and Arms of him the said Duncan Grant then and the divers times feloniously wilfully and of his and their Malice forethought did strike cut & bead And that the said persons or Person unknown did then & there give unto him the said Dunean Grant in and upon the head Shoulders & Arms of him the said Duncan Grant with the Cut capes and Bludgeous aforesaid divers mortal Cuts, Wounds and Bruises; Of which said Cuts Wounds and Bruises be the said Duncan Grant from the said twenty sixth day of December to the twenty second day of January in the yo aforesaid at the Parish of Saint George within the liberty aforesaid to Wit in Saint George Hospital these situate did languish, and languishing did live; On which said twenty second day of January in the year aforesaid he the said Dune an Grant in the Hospital aforesaid at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty aforesaid of the Mortal Cuts Wounds & Bruises aforesaid, did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Person or Persons unknown him the said Duncan Grant in manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously wilfully and of his and their Maliee forethought did Kill and murder against the Peace of our Said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }
