City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of May in the Fourteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Roach then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Vaughan , John Peate , Henry Pitkin , James Browning , Robert Abraham , Charles Inderwich , James Middleton , John Heath , Donald Chambers , George Cummings , Robert Bigby and James Sinclair good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ann Roach came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Ann Roach on the Fourth day of May in the Year aforesaid, being alone in her Lodging Room on the First Floor in the Dwelling House of William Whitaker situate in the Great Almery in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid And being Intoxicated with Liquor, It so happened that the said Ann Roach Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell out of and through the Window into the Street, and thereby received divers Fractures and Bruises in and upon both her Legs, of which said mortal Fractures and Bruises she the said Ann Roach from the said Fourth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty second of the same Month and Year, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on Which said Twenty second day of May in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid, she the said Ann Roach of the mortal Fractures and Bruises aforesaid, did die In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Vaughan Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

James Vaughan Foreman
