City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of April in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Parsons then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Myers , Edward Hirst , John Mander , Samuel Fuller , Francis Hunter  Edward Lloyd , George Henniken , Richard Turner  Henry Fetcher  Christopher Lonsdale John Dory , Anthony Smith & Timothy Birchmore good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Parsons came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the Said James Parsons a Laborer on the First day of April in the Year aforesaid being in the Brewhouse of Thomas Taylor a Brewer situate in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and being then and there carrying a Tub filled with Grains down Stairs in the said Brewhouse It so happened that the said James Parsons then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Stairs into a certain Tan fall of beer, adjoining to the said Stairs and in the Beer in the said Sun was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said James Parsons then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oat aforesaid do say, that the said James Parsons in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentlly Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Edward Myers Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Edward Myers Foreman
