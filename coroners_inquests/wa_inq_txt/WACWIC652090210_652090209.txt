City and Liberty of Westminister in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminitst within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminister , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of May in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Patrick Caffry then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John William , Robert Blair , John Strong , Thomas Branch , John Davis , Thomas Davies , Samuel Oldacres , William Longton , Richard Lewis , Thomas Fisher , Thomas Jones , William Porter , and Joseph Baylis , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sorn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Mean the said Patrick Caffry came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Patrick Caffry , on the Twelth day of May in the year aforesaid, was found drowned and suffocated in the River Thames , to wit, at Westminister Bridge , in the Parish aforesaid with in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, that the said Patrick Caffry had no Marks of Violence appearing on his Body, but how or by what means he became drowned and suffocated no Evidence thereof doth appear to the Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Williams Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself, and the rest of his fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Seals the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Williams Foreman
