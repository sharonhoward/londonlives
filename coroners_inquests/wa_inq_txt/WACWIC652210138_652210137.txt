City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of March in the Twenty fist Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Edward Thomas then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Christopher Day , Moses Burton , William Collett , Jacob Knight , Andrew Kennedy , John Caunt , Samuel Daniel , William Hollier , Edward Jones  Nathaniel Man  Thomas Scott , Andrew Rogers , and George Burford good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Thomas came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Edward Thomas on the said Nineteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid was coming down Stairs in his dwelling House situate in King Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And being in Liquor, it so happened that the said Edward Thomas did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fall down the said Stairs unto the bottom, and thereby did then and there receive a violent Concussion in and upon the Brain of him the said Edward Thomas , of which said Wound Contusion and Concussion he the said Edward Thomas then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Edward Thomas in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Christopher Day Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Chris. Day Foreman
