City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of July in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Henry Loader then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Noble , Thomas Barber , Henry Bowman , Stephen Bowman , John Dunford , John Blake , Joseph Trollope , Angustus Elliott , Thomas Allsop , James Gordon , William Jordon , William Warner and William Young good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Henry Loader came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Henry Loader on the said fourth day of July in the Year aforesaid, being in a certain Barge or Lighter in the River Thames , at Mr. Medley's Wharf in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened, that the said Henry Loader Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, fell out of and from the said Barge or Lighter into the said River Thames , and in the Water thereof was then and there suffocated and Drowned, of which said suffocation and Drowning he the said Henry Loader , then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Henry Loader in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said John Noble Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Noble Foreman
