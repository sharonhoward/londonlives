City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of April in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Wade then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Beckett , William Dean , Richard Wardel  Rice Williams  Thomas Perrin , Bernard Briggs , Samuel Titmus, Thomas Horwood , Thomas Honour , Stephen Preston  Richard Dove and Robert Hammerton good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Wade came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Richard Wade , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but Lunatick and distracted, on the Sixth day of April in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto a Hand Rail upon the Stairs in the Fire Engine at Pimlico in the said Parish Liberty and County and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle himself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said Richard Wade then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Richard Wade not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Beckett Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

J Beckett Foreman
