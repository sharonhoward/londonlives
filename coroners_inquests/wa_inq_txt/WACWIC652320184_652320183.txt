City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex ,} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign for our Lord the King at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fifteenth day of March in the Thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of mary Franks an Infant then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of William Payne , Samuel Davies , Charles Moss , Andrew Oliver  James Bussell , John Terry , Matthew Burberow , Edward Allen , David Gordon , Thomas Warren , Peter Hewlins , and Alexander Miller good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen,who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how,and by what Means the said Mary Franks came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Mary Franks an infant of the Age of three Years be the fourteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid being alone in the Lodging room or Apartment of William Franks her Father in the dwelling house of Thomas Glasser situate and being in Craven Court in the said parish of Saint martin in the Fields within the Liberty & County aforesaid, it so happend that the Linen Clothes which the said Mary Franks the Infant then and there had on her Body accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took fire, and that the said mary Franks was then and there violently burnt in and upon the Belly breast sides neck and Arms of her the said Mary Franks of which said Burning she the said Mary Franks then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Mary Franks accidentally Casually and by Misfortune Came to her death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Payne  Foreman of the said Jurors on the Behalf of himself and therest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day Year and place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Payne Foreman
