City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of May in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Grat Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Pirckard, Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Bambridge then and there lying dead upon the Oath of  John Sutton , Peter Hay , Samuel Gold  Charles Bricre  John Shirley , George Curling John Dixon , John Walker , Thomas Clement  John Stone , Isaac Hull and William Elsbee good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Bambridge came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Bambridge a Lamplighter on the Tenth day of May in the Year aforesaid was fixing up Lamps in the Hall with in the Dwelling House of the Right Honourable Lord Villers situate in Grafton Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and then standing upon a Ladder It so happened that the said John Barmbridge then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Ladder unto and upon the Stone Pavement in the said Hall, and thereby then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon the Right side of the Head of him the said John Bambridge of which said Mortal Fracture he the said John Bambridge then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Bambridge in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Sutton Foreman of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard  Coroner

John Sutton Foreman
