City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of March in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Elliott then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Dainton , Thomas Brown , James Southgate , Henry Beale , John Lock , Thomas Kent , Gavin Rogers , Robert Caldell , Charles Stewart , David Richardson , William Rodgers , Thomas Theobald William Hitchin and Hugh Anderson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Elliott came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Elliott (suspected to have been murthered) on the Fourteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid was found Dead in a certain Stall in a Stable in the Stable Yard belonging to the White Bear Inn Situate in Piccadilly in the said Parish of Saint James that the said John Elliott had no Marks of Violence appearing on his Body and that the said John Elliott departed the Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way. (to wit) by the bursting of a blood Vessel in a Fit of Couching.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Thomas Dainton Foreman of the said Jurors, on behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thomas Damton Foreman
