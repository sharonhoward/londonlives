City and Liberty of Westminster  in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of February in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of God Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Edward Smith then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of David Bower , Moses Laney , John Adam Mangar , Joseph Kendall , William Bond , Thomas Smith , John Withers  Robert Leeming , Benjamin Flight James Elden , Thomas Speding  John Redman , John Priestman Thomas Settree and Saml. Osborne good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Smith came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Edward Smith not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted on the Sixth day of February in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, a certain Pistol charged with Gunpowder and a leaden Bullet which he the said Edward Smith then and there had and held in his Right Hand to and against the Neck of him the said Edward Smith did then and there shoot off and discharge, by Means whereof he the said Edward Smith did then and there give unto himself with the leaden Bullet aforesaid so discharged and shot out of the Pistol aforesaid by the force of the Gunpowder aforesaid in and upon the forepart of the Neck of him the said Edward Smith one mortal Wound of the Length of four Inches and of the Depth of three Inches, of which said mortal Wound he the said Edward Smith then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Edward Smith , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said David Bower Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Daniel Bower Foreman
