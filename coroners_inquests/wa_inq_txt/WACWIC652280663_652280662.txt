An Inquisition Indednted. taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirtieth day of October in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Whitby then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Josiah Day , James James , John Waller , William Payne , James Dixon , John Dutton , Robert Sabine , William Hall , Thomas Davies , William Nethersole , James Maclean , John Robinson , Robert Hawksley , Willdham Wright , John Richards , John Havers and John Owen good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Whitby came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Richard Whitby not being of sound Mind Memory and Under standing but Lunatick and distracted, on th Twenty sixth day of October in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, a great quantity of Arsenic and Landinam being deadly poison with a certain quantity of the Fincture of Cantharides did take drink and swallow down by meand whereof he the said Richard Whitby became then and there sick and distempered his Body, and of the Poison aforesaid and of the sickness and distemper thereby occasion'd he the said Richard Whitby at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid of the poison aforesaid and of the sickness and distemper occasioned thereby did die And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Richard Whitby in manner and by the means aforesaid not being of sound mind, memory and understanding but Lunatick and distracted did kill himself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Josiah Day Foreman of the said Jurors on the behald of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jos. Day Foreman
