City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of October in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain,  France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on view of the Body of Francis White then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Girdler Robert Bigly , William Atwood , John Glassbrook , Isaac Bunting , William Crocket , Charles Gazy , William Twine , John Blatch , Ignatius Jordon,  Benjamin Virtu , John Martin Francis Phillips and Charles Tutop good and lawful men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King , when, how and by what Means the said Francis White came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Franicis White not being of sound mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Fifth day of October in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid , one End of a certain piece of small cord unto an Iron Hook fastened into the beiling of a Garret in an Empty House situate in Dartmouth Street, in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other end thereof about his own Neck, did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle himself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling, he the said Francis White then and there died, and so the aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid say, that the said Francis White , being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did fill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner. as the said Thomas Girdler Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their Presence , have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Tho. Girdler Foreman
