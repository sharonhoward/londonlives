City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fifth, day of May in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of a New born Male Child then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Reeves , Robert Peck , Alexander Falkner , John Benson , Nicholas Thompson , John Dunstan , Matthew Place , George Belts , Edward Hutchinson  John Reilly , George Findlay , Andrew Taylor and Henry Lowtelow good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said new born Male Child came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That Ann Caple on the Twenty fourth day of May in the Year aforesaid being Pregnant and big with a certain Male Child, afterwards to Wit, on the same day and Year, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, with lingering Pains hard Labour and great Difficulty, occasioned by a fall she had then lately before received, did bring forth the said Male Child alive, And that the said Ann Caple then being alone and in a very weak and languid State and Condition, then and there for the Preservation of the Life of the said Male Child, according to the best of her Skill and Understanding, did as soon as she was capable, tie and bind the Navel String of the said Male Child, Whereupon It so happened that the saw Male Child for want of the Navel String being properly bound and tied, then and there by Misfortune and against the Will of the said Ann Caple, by Means of a great Quantity of Blood issuing and flowing from his Body through the said Navel String did bleed to Death. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Reeves Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jn Reeves Foreman
