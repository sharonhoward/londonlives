City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St.Martin in the fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St.Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of November in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for then the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Smith then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Sutton , Henry Hansaw , James Morrison , Richard Katharine , Robert Gayes , John Field , John Marshall , Charles Card , Joseph Bridgford , Joseph Allen , John Grace , Samuel Conno , Thomas Batrick , Joseph Fleming . George walker , John Sowarsby , and Simon Bayley , good and lawful Men and said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Smith came to his Death, do upon their oath say That the Thomas Smith , not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Thirty first day of October in the year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, in the Necessary House or Privy adjoining to the Dwelling House of Anna Sophia Lowe there situate, with a certain Penknife made of Iron and Steel, which he the said Thomas Smith then and there had and held in his Right hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said Thomas Smith did then and there Strike stab and penctrate, thereby, then and there giving unto hmself, with the Penknife aforesaid, in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Thomas Smith , one mortal wound, of the Length of three Inches and of the depth of one Inch, of which said mortal wound he the said Thomas Smith then and there died .and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Smith , not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Sutton Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and place first above written

Tho.Prickard Coroner

Thos. Sutton Foreman
