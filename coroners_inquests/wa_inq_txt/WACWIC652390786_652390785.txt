City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of William Newman the Sign of the wheat sheaf in poland Street in the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirteenth day of December 1799 in the fortieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Chales Duchemin of Normandy in the Kingdom of France then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Charles Duchemin came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Charles Duchemin a Native of the Parish of Serceil in Normandy in the Kingdom of France on the eleventh day of December in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit in Poland Street aforesaid died, by the Visitation of God in a natural Way To Wit of an Apoplexey and not otherwise IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy. Gell Coroner Jas. Hartley John Richard Henry Christer Robt. Canham John Wells
