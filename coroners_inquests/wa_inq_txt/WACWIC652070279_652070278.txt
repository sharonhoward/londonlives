City and Liberty of Westminster  in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twenty eighth Day of April in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of William Grimsditch then and there lying dead upon the Oath of John Rowntree , Robert Wright , William Harvey , Thomas Hutchinson , John Doleman , Henry Steel , James Keys , John Hepworth , John Ellis , Robert Anderson , John Lawrence ,  Alexander Smelley , and John Cooknol good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said William Grimsditch came to his Death, do upon their Oaths That the said William Grimsditch on the Twenty fifth day of April in the Year aforesaid, was found Dead in a certain Place called Rochester Row in Tothill Fields in the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, That no Marks of Violence appeared on his Body, And that the said William Grimsditch departed this Life, by the Visitation of God, in a Natural Way. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Rown tree Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Rowntree Foreman
