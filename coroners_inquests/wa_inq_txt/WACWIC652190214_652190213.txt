City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fourth day of May in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender, of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Catherine Suttle then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Anderson , Ralph Glover , Thomas Park , Joseph Horner , William Ashton , Thomas Guyde , Thomas Shields , Nicolas Tuckerman Joseph Hughes , Henry Baker , John Mackey , John Hart , James Morrison , David Ross & John Hewlett good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Catherine Suttle came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Catherine Suttle on the Ninth day of May in the year aforesaid being in her lodging room or Apartment in the Dwelling House of Joshua Friends situate in Gardener's Lane in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being then and there assleep with, a lighted Candle upon the Table before her It so happened that the Clothes which the said Catherine Suttle had on her Body Accidentally catched Fire, and that the said Catherine Suttle by the Flames and Smoke issuing from the said Fire, was then and there Burnt in and upon her Face, Throat and divers parts of her Body, of which she the said Catherine Suttle at the said Parish Liberty and County languished intil the Twenty first day of the same Month and year, and of the said Burning then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Catherine Suttle in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death, and not otherwise. In Witness Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Tho Anderson Foreman
