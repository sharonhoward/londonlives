City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St.Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of June in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Corner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Margaret Pendegrass then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Nichols  James Griffin , John Shelmerdine , Henry Sharde , James Sadler Saul Richardson , William Buckland , Thomas Sears , Richard Bower , Robert Pike , James Lee , James Addison , Thomas Stevens , James Allen Robert Brook , Thomas Wilson , Thomas Gore , James Gilbert , William Richardson , James Cliffe , Joseph Comport & William Dent , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Margaret Pendegrass came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Margaret Pendegrass late the Wife of Peter Pendegrass (suspected to have been Murdered) on the Twenty fifth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid died suddenly by the Visitation of God in a natural way, and not by any Injury from the said Peter Pendegrass. to the Knowledge of the said Jurors.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said John Nichols  Foeman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Nichols Foreman
