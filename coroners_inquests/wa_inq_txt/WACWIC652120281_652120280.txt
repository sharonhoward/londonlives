>  City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of May in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Thompson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Meyer , Barney Cook , Obadiah Oxford , George Pickering , William Edmonson , George Burt , James Taylor , John Poultney , James Wheatley , Jams Milne , Joseph Haigh and James Gardner good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Thompson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Thompson a Laborer on the Eleventh day of May in the Year aforesaid being at Work in the Brewhouse of Richard Green situate in the Kings Road in the County aforesaid, and being then and there in a certain Left at the height of about Eighteen feet, taking in at a Window certain Empty Casks drawn up with Pullies, in order to be placed in the said Loft, It so happened that the said Thomas Thompson then and there Accidentally Causally and by Misfortune fell from the said Loft out of and through the said Window into the Yard upon the Stone Pavement, and thereby received one mortal Wound and Fracture in and upon the left side of his Head, of which said mortal Wound and Fracture he the said Thomas Thompson from the said Eleventh day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Twelfth day of the same Month and Year, at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twelfth day of May in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Thomas Thompson of the mortal Wound and Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Thompson in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally, Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Henry Meyer Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Henry Meyer Foreman
