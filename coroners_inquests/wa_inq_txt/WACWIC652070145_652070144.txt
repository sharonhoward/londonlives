City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex To wit. }  In Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields , within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Seventeenth Day of March in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & liberty on View of the Body of Elizabeth Poole , suspected to have been Murdered then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Clement , George Whitfoot , Caesar Pigunit , George Hawkswell , William Wynn , Thomas Coleman , Lawrence Howard , George Hammersmith , William Wynn , Thomas Coleman , Lawrence Sullen , Joseph Sturgis , Samuel Short , John Hamon , and Thomas Wheat good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Poole came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Poole on the Fifteenth day of March in the year aforesaid, died suddenly in Convulsions, at the Workhouse in the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid, That no Marks of Violence appeared on the Body of the said Elizabeth Poole , and that the said Elizabeth Poole died by the Visitation of God, in a natural Way, and not by any hurt or injury received from any Person or Persons whatsoever, to the Knowledge of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Clements Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself, and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Wm.. Clements Foreman
