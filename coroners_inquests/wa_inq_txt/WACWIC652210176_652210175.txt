City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the Country of Middlesex , the Sixth day of April in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Price then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Job Dakin , George Bell , James Gordon , Antony Tabry , John Leech , John Hewlitt , Samuel Clay , Joseph Niblow , Thomas Brooks , James Lambert George Galley , William Pasmore , John Bicknell , Samuel Mitchell and Angus Mc.Kay good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who, being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Price came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said John Price on the Fifth day of April in the Year aforesaid being on the side of a certain Barge floating upon the River Thomas at the Wharf of John Pilkington in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and Country aforesaid, and then and there stepping out of the said Barge into a certain Lighter It so happened that the foot of the said John Price slipped, and that the said John Price thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Barge into the said River Thames , and in the Water thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said John Price then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Price in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to hie death. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Job Dakin Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Job Dakin Foreman
