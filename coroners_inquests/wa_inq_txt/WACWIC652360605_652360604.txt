City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex , } to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of John Cuttle the Sign of the hags head orange Court parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the third day of October 1796 in the thirty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Lucy Wilson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the Said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Lucy Wilson came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Lucy Wilson not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic & distracted on the first day of October in the year aforesaid at the parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To wit in a certain Copper filled in the water belonging to and within the poor House of the said parish there did cast & throw herself and in the said water was then and there suffocated & drowned: of which said Suffocation and Drowning she the said Lucy Wilson then and there instantly died And to the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Lucy Arllow not being of sound Mind Memory and Under -Standing bur lunatic & distracted in manner & by the Means aforesaid, did Bell hereof

 IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy. Gell Coroner }
