City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit} 

An Inquisition Indented, taken, for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of July , in the Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Isabella Brown then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of William Marcellus , Thomas Stockdale , Christopher King , Robert Mc Culler , Robert Haines , William Scott , William Hussey , George Saunders , Thomas Ewart , Constantine Toulings , Robert Thompson , James Bigins  Thomas Collett , William Clement , Thomas Thompson , Charles Lefley , James Mc. Dougall , Thomas Raymond , William Palmer , John Shove , and John Griffin good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, and who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said Isabella Brown , came to her Death, Do upon their Oath say, That the said Isabella Brown not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but Lunatick and Distracted, on the Eighth day of July in the Year aforesaid, in the Work house belonging to the Parish of St. Martins in the Fields aforesaid, situate within the said Parish, in the Liberty and County aforesaid, the Throat of her the said Isabella; with a certain Penknife, of no Value, did cut and penetrated giving to herself the said Isabella with the Penkin aforesaid, one Mortal Wound in the said Throat, of which said Mortal Wound she then did there instantly Died; And that the said Isabella Brown , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted as aforesaid, Herself in Manner and Form aforesaid did kill. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said William Marcellus Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovementioned

Tho Prickard Coroner .

Wm. Marcellus Foor Man
