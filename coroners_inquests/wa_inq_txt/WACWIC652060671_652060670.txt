City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  Ann Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty f the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fourth day of December the Seventh year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of Edward Little then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Roberts , John Bakewell , Stephen Gagon , Benjamin Turnbull , Richard Curtis  Arthur Collins , Jeffery Burch , John Jackson , John Crump , Lewis Edwards , George Holden , and John Evans Good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord he King, when how , and what Means the said Edward Little came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Edward Little Laboring under a grievous Disease of Body, to wit, a Fever, and by reason of the violence of the said Disease being delirious and out of his Mind, on the Eighteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty of County aforesaid which he the said Edward Little then and there had and held in his right Hand, the Throat of him the said Edward Little did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby, then and giving unto himself the said Edward Little , so being delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid, with the Scissars aforesaid, in and upon the Throat of him the said Edward Little one mortal Wound of the Length of one Inch and an half, and of the depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound and Fever he the said Edward Little from the said Eighteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty second day of the same Month and Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty second day of December in the Year aforesaid he the said Edward Little at the parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid of the mortal Wound and Fever aforesaid, did dye. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Roberts Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Jno Roberts Foreman
