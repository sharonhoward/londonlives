City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty second day of May in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Tanner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Minns , Richard Rogers , Daniel Hawkes , John Moore , George Livesay , John Manning  William Cope , John Ludham , James Adams , John Smith , Henry Smith and William Sylvanus Price , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Tanner came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Tanner on the Twentieth day of May in the Year aforesaid being upon a seafold Painting the outside of the Workshop of William Cole by the side of the River Thomas in the Parish of St. Mary Lambeth in the County of Surry, And that It so happened that the said John Tanner then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Scaffold upon a certain Boat in the said River Thomas, and thereby then and there received a violent concession of the Brain, and Bruises in his Body, of which said violent Concession and Bruises he the said John Tanner from the said Twentieth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty second day of the same Month, at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish, and thereof then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say the the said John Tanner in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Richard Minns Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Robt. Minns Foreman
