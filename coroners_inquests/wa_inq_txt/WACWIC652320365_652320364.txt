City of Liberty of Westminster in the County of middlesex  } to Wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist  Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of middlesex , the twenty first day of June 1792 in the Thirty Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq.  Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Hopkins then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John Baker , Samuel Sealy , John Dore , William Mash , John Winget , John Deeks , Henry Great , John Edy , James Beecher , Thomas Johnson Henry Hunt , James verry , William Clark , John Blackburn , Robert Hall , James Rundall , and Charles Snook good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the king, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Hopkins came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Sarah Hopkins on the nineteenth day of June, in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid, not having the fear of God before her Eyes, but moved and seduced by the Instigation of the Deirl with force and arms, in and upon herself in the peace of God and of our said Sovereign Lord the Kign, then and there being feloniously, wilfully, and of her Malice fore thought, did make an Assault. And that the said Sarah Hopkins , a certain Quantity of white Arsenic, being indeadly Poison into a certain Quantity of water feloniously, wilfully, and of her Malice forethought, did then and there put and mix, and then and there well knowing thew said white Arsenic to be a deadly poison And that the said Sarah Hopkins a great Quantity of the said Water in which the said white arsenic was so put and mixt as aforesaid, afterwards to wit, on the same Year, at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid, feloniously, wilfully, and of her Malice forethought did take, drink and swallow down, by balxus whereof she the said Sarah Hopkins then and there became sick and distempered in her Body; and of the said Poison and the said Sickness and Distemper there by occasioned she the said Sarah Hopkins did then and there die. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Sarah Hopkins in manner, and by the means aforesaid feloniously, willfully and of her Malice forethough, did kill and under herself. against by Peace of our said Lord the King, his Crown and Dignity. And that the Sarah Hopkins at the time of the committing the Felony and Murder aforesaid, was a pauper and had no Goods or Chatels, Lands or Tenements, in their the said City and Liberty or elsewhere, to the Knowledge of the said jurors In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said John Baker  Foreman of the Said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written.

Anthy. Gell Coroner

John Baker Foreman
