City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of June in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Ann Willis then and there lying dead, upon the Oath or Thomas Porter , Thomas Gale , William Sweet  George Graves , Joseph Washboard , Charles Chandler , William Huddleston , John Moody , Alexander McDaniel , Arnold Myer , Samuel Mc. Nemi and Benjamin Plantin good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Ann Wills came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Mary Ann William Infant of the Age of two Years on the Twenty fifth day of June in the Year aforesaid being in St. James's Street and walking in the Footway there, in the Parish of St. James within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Man unknown was then driving a Coach drawn by two Horses in the said Street, and that the said Mary ann Wills the Infant then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down under the said Coach and that the hinder Near wheel of the said Coach then and there passed over the left side of the stead of the said Mary Ann Willis by Means where of the Said Mary Ann Wills then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received one mortal Bruise in and upon the left side of her Head, of which said mortal Bruise she the said Mary Ann Willis then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Mary Ann Willis in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the aid Thomas Peter Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Thos Peter Foreman
