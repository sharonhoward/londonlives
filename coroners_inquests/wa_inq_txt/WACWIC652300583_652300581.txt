City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty seventh day of September in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Bull then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Oakes , Robert Watson , Thomas Brown , Matthew Shaw , Thomas Dean , John Wansell , Michael Duff Thomas Bridgen , Benjamin Evans , Edward Baldwin , Peter Fozard and William Collen good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Bull came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That on the Twenty second day of September in the year aforesaid a Man was driving a certain Stage Coach in the Kings High way at Knightsbridge in the Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster in the County aforesaid and that the said Thomas Bull was then riding behind the said Coach and that another Stage Coach drawn by Two Horses followed in the Kings Highway and was near to the former Stage Coach going towards Brentford It so happened that the said Thomas Bull till from behind the said Coach upon the Ground in the said High was and that the kindermost Coach passed over the Body of the said Thomas Bull so lying upon the Ground as aforesaid by means whereof the said Thomas Bull did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Fracture of the Right Thigh and a mortal Bruise in and upon the lower part of his Belly of which and mortal Fracture and Bruise he the said Thomas Bull from the said Twenty second day of September in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty fifth day of the same Month at St George's Hospital in the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid until the Twenty fifth day of the same Month and year did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty fifth day of September in the year aforesaid at the Hospital aforesaid, he the said Thomas Bull of the mortal Fracture and Bruise aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas, Bull in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Causally and by Misfortune came to his Death and that the said Stage Coach was moving to the Death of the said Thomas Bull in of the value of Forty Shillings and is the Property and in the Possession of Thomas Attride of Brentford in the County aforesaid Coach master.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Richard Oakes Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Richd Oakes Foreman
