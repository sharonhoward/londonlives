City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex }  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of January in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Griffiths a Labourer then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Aslat , Thomas Coward , James Nelson , Henry Akin , John Baynes , James Crosby , James Sinclear , Michael Belford , Thomas Godding , John Wheatley , Thomas Airley and Richard Prevate good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Griffiths came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Griffiths on the Fifteenth day of January in the Year aforesaid, being carefully driving a certain Cart loaden with Ice and drawn by two Horses, in Hyde Park in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said John Griffiths then and there Accidentally Casually and by misfortune slipped in his feet and fell to the Ground near unto the Near Wheel of the said Cart, by reason whereof the said Near Wheel of the said Cart did then and there go upon and pass over the Body of the said John Griffiths , by Means whereof he the said John Griffiths did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive divers mortal Bruises in the upon his Body, of which said Mortal Bruises he the said John Griffiths then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Griffiths Accidentally Causally and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Richard Aslat Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

Richd. Aslat . Foreman
