An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of January in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Corner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Squires then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Dobson , John Egleston , William Bramton , Richard Marthwaite John Slann , John Bales , John Tate , William Hutchins , William Phillips , William Pinchbeck , Joseph Otty , James Richardson , Francis Lee , Thomas Mitchell , Benjamin Goodal & George Donalien good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Squires came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Squires on the Thirty first day of January in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid departed this Life through the Inclemency of the weather, and the want of the common Necessaries of Life, and by no violent Ways or Means whatsoever, to the knowledge of the said Jurors. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Dobson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Thos. Dobson Foreman
