City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of January in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Summers then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Sampson , John Jersey , Esan Alfred, Robert Ward  Henry Leach , Edward Williams , John Atkin , Richard Carter , William Atwood Lawrence Collin , John Harris , William Cook , Francis Ridley , Richard Johnson and Thomas Dunea good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Summers came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say

That the said William Summers on the Twenty second day of December in the year aforesaid at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the Liberty aforesaid in the County aforesaid to wit in Cliffords Row being employed as a Laborer in Repairing the Roof of or House these situate and the said William Summers laying hold with his Hands of the top port of a Ladder in the Fillet Gutter resting against the Ridge of the said House (the said Ladder not being fixed) and at the same time setting his Feet on the said Ladder, it so happened that thereby the said Ladder was forced out of the Fillet Gutter, and the said William Summers accidentally casually and by Misfortune fell from the tip of the said House unto the Ground by Means whereof he the said William Summers received several Mortal Bruises and wounds as his Back Loyns and Hips and other internal Injuries of which he the said William Sumpers languish from the said Twenty second day of December untill the Eleventh day of January in the year aforesaid On the Parish of St. Margaret in the City and Liberty aforesaid to wit in the Westminster Hospital and then & there Died. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisitions set their Hands and Seals the Day year and at the Place first abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Robert Sampson  Foreman
