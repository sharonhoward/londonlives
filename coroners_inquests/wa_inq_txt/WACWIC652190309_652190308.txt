City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty seventh day of July in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third,by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Thomas Hardie then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Squire , John Roberts , Henry Fenner , Thomas Porter Abraham Hepworth , John Oliver , Samuel Collett , John Gray William Coleman  James Benge , John Nose , James Blunt , John Jestin and William Wild good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said Thomas Hardie came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Hardie an Infant on the Twenty sixth day of July in the Year aforesaid being near a certain Wooden Fruck in Cambridge Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that George Strutt was then and there carefully driving a Hackney Coach, and in passing by the said Truck, It so happened that the Shaft of the said Truck Struck the said Thomas Hardie the Infant down and that the Off hinder Wheel of the said Coach then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the Belly of the said Thomas Hardie, so lying upon his Back in the said Street as aforesaid, by Means whereof the said Thomas Hardie then and there received Mortal BRuises in and upon his Body, of which said mortal Bruises he the said Thomas Hardie then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Hardie in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casuallly and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Squire Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

Wm Squire Foreman
