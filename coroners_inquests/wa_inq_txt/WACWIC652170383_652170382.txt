City and Liberty of Westminster in the Country of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King,at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the Country of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of August in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Simms then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Josiah Gibson , Peter Taylor , Nicholas Watterers , Giles Lawrence . John Reade , Mark Chobham , George Davies , Thomas Knibbs , William Reade , Robert Hambridge , Hugh Thorp , James Watkins , David Wornham , and Thomas Hall good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who, being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said Thomas Simms came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Simms on the Twenty fourth day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and Country aforesaid to wit in Whitehorse Yard in New Bond Street in a Stable there being liftting up Dung with a Shovell, the Feel in of him the said Thomas Simms slipped by means whereof he the said Thomas Simms fell to the Ground, and a Mare being then in the said Stable near to him the said Thomas Simms it so happened that accidentally casually and by Misfortune the said Mare gave to him the said Thomas Simms with one of her Legs One Violent and Mortal Hick on the back of part of the Head of him the said Thomas Simms which Fractured the Skull of which said violent and mortal Kick he the said Thomas Simms in Saint George's Hospital near Hyde Park Corner in the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and Country aforesaid from the said Twenty fourth day of July in the year aforesaid untill the Twelfth day of August in the said Year did languish and languishing did Live on which said Twelfth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Thomas Simms of the said Violent and Mortal Kick did Dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say That the said Thomas Simms in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally casually and by Misfortune come to his Death and not otherwise, And that the said Mare was Moving to the Death of the said Thomas Simms and is the Property and in the Posession of the Reverend and Doctor Backworth of New Street in the Liberty and Country aforesaid, or of Spalding in Lincolnshire, and of the Value of Forty Shillings. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the next of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Joseph Gibson Foreman
