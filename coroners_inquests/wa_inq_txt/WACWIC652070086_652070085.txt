City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex . the Sixth day of February i the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of John Lumley then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Freeman , George Newton , Richard James , Joseph Baylis , William Proctor , Benjamin Johnson , James Mason , John Bathhorne , James Wild , John Finleyson, Alexander Gordon , Edward Champ  John Grainger and Thomas Hitchen good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being the and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire or our said Lord the King when, how and by what Moans the said John Lumley came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Lumley on the Fifth day of February in the Year aforesaid, was found dead in the Church Yard belonging to the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid That no Marks of Violence appeared on the Body of the said John Lumley , and that the said John Lumbley died by the Visitation of God, in a natural Way, and not by any hurt ex injury received from any Person or Persons whatsoever, to the Knowledge of the said Jurors In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Freeman Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thomas Freeman Foreman
