City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of April in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Bastin then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Alderson , Samuel Mills , Henry Renshaw , Simon Bailey , John Norfolk , Robert Law , Abraham Sangenetti , Thomas Batridge, Philip Barrow , James Bennet Samuel Foxen and Anthony Andrews good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Bastin came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Bastin an Infant of the Age of five Years on the Sixth day of April in the Year aforesaid being at play with other Children in the Street commonly called Drury Lane in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that James Cole being then carefully driving a certain Empty Cart drawn by three Horses in the said Lane, It so happened that the said James Bastin then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down near unto the Off Wheel of the said Cart, and that the Off Wheel of the said Cart passed over the Right side of the Head of the said James Bastin ; by Means whereof the said James Bastin did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Facture in and upon the Right side of his Head, of which said Mortal Fracture he the said James Bastin then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Bastin in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said John Alderson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Alderson Foreman
