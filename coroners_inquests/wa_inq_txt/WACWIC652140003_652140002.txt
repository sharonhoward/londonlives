City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of January in the Fourteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Wilcox then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Curtis , John Skeats , Matthew Birredge , Anthony Ronelle , Matthew Gregory John Phillips , James Chambers , John Nicholls , John Salt , Thomas Ball Thomas Greenwood and Hugh Riddle good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Wilcox came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Joseph Wilcox on the Thirty first day of December in the Year aforesaid, in the Evening, being Walking in a certain Footway called the Willow Walk in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being Old, and Intoxicated with Liquor, It so happened that the said Joseph Wilcox Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell into the Ditch on the South side of the said Willow Walk , and the said Joseph Wilcox then and there sticking fast in the Mird and Water in the said Ditch, he the said Joseph Wilcox then and there departed this Life by the Inclemency of the Weather and not otherwise. In Witness whereof fas well the said Coroner, as the said William Curtis Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Curtis Foreman
