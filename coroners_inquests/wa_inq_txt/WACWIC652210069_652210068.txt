City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirteenth day of February in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Susanna Thompson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Mourn  William Cotterell , Ralph Jorden , Samuel Ray , John Hodges , Thomas Gyde , Peacock Vincey , Thomas Scott , Hugh Kennedy , Stephen Haines , Joseph Tucker and William Price good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Susanna Thompson came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Susanna Thompson on the said thirteenth day of February in the Year aforesaid being in her Lodging Room or Apartment in the dwelling House of William Wright Situate in James Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the Stock of Chimneys were blown down upon the said House, and that the back part of the said House then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell in, by Means whereof the said, Susanna Thompson was their and there under the Ruins and Materials thereof smothered suffocated and killed, of which said Smothering Suffocation and killing she the said Susanna Thompson then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Susanna Thompson in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Mourn Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner Wm. Mourn Foreman
