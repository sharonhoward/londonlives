City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Intended, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of June in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Carter an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Hance , John Perry , John Scott , Richard Otley , Edward Owen  David Richards , Peter Bathurst , James Lee , Anthony Smith , Bernard Rose , William Smith and Anthony Midford , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Carter came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Joseph Carter the Infant on the Twenty seventh day of May in the Year aforesaid being with other Children, in the Lodging Room or Apartment of William Carter Father of the said Joseph Carter , on the First Floor in the Dwelling House of William Jones situate in Swallow Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said Joseph Carter the Infant then and there Accidentally casually and by Misfortune fell out of and through the Window upon the Stone- Pavement in the Yard behind and adjoining to the said House, and thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the Right side of his Head, of which said Mortal Fracture he the said Joseph Carter then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Joseph Carter in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Hance Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Hance Foreman
