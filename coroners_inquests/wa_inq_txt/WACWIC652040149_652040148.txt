City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret in the City of Westminster , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Second Day of May in the Fourth Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard  Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Peter Hammond then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Grainge , Thomas Dyer , Peter Gowe Huil Honour , William Lockie , John Stead , George King , John Patton , John Lowry , John Burchin , James Arnold and John Thrift good and lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said Peter Hammond came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Peter Hammond , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Second day of May in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid one End of a Certain piece of small Cord unto the Wooden Banisters upon the Staircase in the dwelling House of Elizabeth Waller situate in the Great Almery, in the said Parish City Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck, did fix, tye, and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang, suffocate, and strangle himself, of which said hanging suffocation and strangling he the said Peter Hammond then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Peter Hammond , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did Kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Samuel Grainge Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Saml Grainge Foreman
