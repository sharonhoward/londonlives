An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twentieth day of July in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on on View of the Body of Henry Collin then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Gosheron , William Jordan , Mathew Hill David Allen  Ebenezer Johnson , William Dawns , John Poole Thomas Reeve , Robert Archer , James Miller , Henry Mc. Gregor and Leddyman Lya good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the king, when, how, and by what Means the said Henry Collin came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Henry Collin not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted on the Eighteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid a certain Gun or Musket charged with Gun Powder and Stone Pebbles, which he the said Henry Collin then and there had and held in his Hand, to and against the Body of him the said Henry Collin did then and there Shoot off and discharge, by means where of he the said Henry Collin did then and there given unto himself with the Stone Pebbles aforesaid, so discharged and shot out of the Gun or Musket aforesaid, by the Force of the Gun powder aforesaid, one mortal Wound in and thought the Body of him the said Henry Collin, of which said mortal Wound he the said Henry Collin then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Henry Collin, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did hill himself

In Witness where of as well the said Coroner , as the said Thomas Gosheron Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place abovementioned.

Tho Prickard  Coroner Thos. Goshen Foreman
