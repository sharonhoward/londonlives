City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex ,} to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the House of Mr. Camp Apothecary in new Bond Sheet in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourth day of April 1796 in the thirty Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Kirk then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Kirk came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Mary Kirk not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanting but lunatic and distracted on the third day of April in the Year aforesaid at the Parish & in the Liberty aforesaid in the Dwelling House of Miss Neale Millener there situate one end of a certain Ribband unto the Certain Rod at the foot of the Bed in the Apartment of her the said Mary Kick and the other End theseof about her own Keck did fix tye and fasten & therewith did then & there hang suffocate & shangle hereself of which said hanging Suffocation & Strangling she the said Mary Kirk then and there instantly died and to the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid dosay taht the said Mary Kirk not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted in manner and by the Means aforesaid did Kill herself IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy. Gell Coroner }

Abrm. Skegg Heny Thompson Roent Banks Wm Elvir A Daun Melins
