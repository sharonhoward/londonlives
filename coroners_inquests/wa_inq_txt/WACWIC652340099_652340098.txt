City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex .} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Arthur Hill Sign of the Nags head  Oxford Street parish of in St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of February 1794 in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Johnson an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the Several Jurors a bore Names are hereunder Written and Seals affixed good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Johnson came to h er Death,do upon their Oath say that the said Mary Johnson on the Eighth day of February in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid being alone in her Mother's Apartments To Wit in queen Street Oxford Road It so happened that the Clothes which she the said Mary Johnson bad on her Body accidentally casually and by Misfortune took Tue by reason whereof and from the Smoke and flame from the said she the said Mary Johnson was then and there Suffocated and Crowd Of which said Suffocation & Burning she the said Mary Johnson on the following day died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said, Mary Johnson in Manner and by the Mean aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors leave to this Inquisition set Seals the day Year & Place above Written

Anthy Gell Coroner }  Charles Foxwell Foreman Robt Worrall Mark Wade
