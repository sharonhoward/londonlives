City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Stephen Tilbury  Sign of the White Horse & Plough in St. Martins Lane  parish of St Martins in ye Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of Octr 1793 in the thirty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Lockey then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the Several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written and Seals affixed good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Lockey came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said James Lockey on the Eighteenth day of October in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid being in a certain Croft a Barge lying on the River Thames opposite to the Hungerford Stairs in the Strand It so happened that accidentally casually and by Misfortune he the said James Lockey out of the said Croft a Barge into the said River Thames did fall and in the Waters thereof was then & there suffocated and drowned Of which said Suffocation & Drowning he the said James Lockey then and there instantly died. And so the Jams aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said James Lockey in manner & by the Means aforesaid Accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jams have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day Year and Place first above Written

Anthy. Gell Coroner }

A Salomon Foreman Geo: Cock Jas: Barrington Duncan

Andw. Oliver Saml Price James Sheriff Mitchell George Smith John Bess Henry Mick
