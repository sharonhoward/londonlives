City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Dwelling House of Francis Hopper the Sign of the Coach & Horses in little Compton Street  Parish of St. Ann Soho within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourteenth day of May 1798 in the thirty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Edward Bussell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Bussell came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Edward Bussell not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic & distracted on the twelfth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish & in the Liberty aforesaid one end of a certain piece of Wood in the Yard near the foot of the Stairs leading up to the Dwelling House of him the said Edward Bussell & the other end thereof about his own Neck did fix tye & fasten & therewith did then & there hang suffocate & strangle himself, of which hanging Suffocation and Strangling to the said Edward Bunce then & there instantly died: And So the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say that the said Edward Bunce not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic & distracted in manner & by the Means aforesaid did kill himself

 IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }

Jd. Gaugain P Brown Jas Jenkins Edw Barres Anthony Warwick
