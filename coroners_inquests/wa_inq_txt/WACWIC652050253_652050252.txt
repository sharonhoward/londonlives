City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields . within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the First day of May in the Fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard  Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Lydia Palmer then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Matthew Wells , Henry Chelton , William Smith , Edmund Summerton , John Rechter , John Leach , John Spilsbury , Alexander Mc. Donald , Edward Baythorne , Richard Christmas , Edward Evans , William Knight , George Cleere , Thomas Battersby , and John Morris good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Lydia Palmer came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Lydia Palmer, Laboring under a grievous Disease of Body, to wit, a Fever, and by reason of the Violence of the said Disease, being Delirious and out of her Mind, on the First day of May in the Year aforesaid in the Dwelling House of Michael Martin situate and being in Drury Lane in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, with a certain Knife made of Iron and Steel which she the said Lydia Palmer then and there had and held in for Right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of her the said Lydia Palmer did then and there strike, penetrate, thereby, then and there giving unto herself the said Lydia Palmer, so being delirious and out of her Mind as aforesaid, with the Knife aforesaid, in and upon the Threat or Gullet of her the said Lydia Palmer, one mortal Wound, of the Length of four Inches, and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound she the said Lydia Palmer then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Lydia Palmer, by reason of the Disease aforesaid, being delirious and out of her Mind as aforesaid, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Matthew Wells Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Mattw: Wells Foreman
