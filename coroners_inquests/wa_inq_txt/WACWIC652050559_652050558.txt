City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex , to Wit}  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth Day of November in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth; before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on view of the Body of Alexander Robb (suspected to have Murdered) there and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Elisha Dobree John Garrard , William Jones , Robert Smith , Edward Griffin , John Arkill , Richard Dickson , Henry Faust , Joshua Chassercan , Thomas Holroyd , Alexander Hurlbatt , Thomas Mackie , William Harland and John Sturdy good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Alexander Robb Came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Alexander Robb on the Fourteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, to Wit, at the Lodging Room or Apartment of him the said Alexander Robb in the Dwelling House of James Lamb situate in a certain Street called the Hay Market was found Dead, and lying across a Bed in the said Room, with his Face down wards in and upon the Bed-Cloths, That the said Alexander Robb being Intoxicated with Liquor, and the Stock being Buckled very tight around the Neck of the said Alexander Robb, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the said Alexander Robb was thereby then and there Choakd Suffocated and Strangled, of which said Choaking, Suffocation, and Strangling, he the said Alexander Robb then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Alexander Robb in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Elisha Dobree Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

Elisha Dobree Foreman
