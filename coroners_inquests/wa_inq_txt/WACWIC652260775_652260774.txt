City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of December in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain,France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman,Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Lawrence Finn then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Hickinbotton , Leonard Spurling  Robert Seabrook , John Latham , John Perey , John Bowler , Richard Fitchiner , Robert Hutchins , John Fardell , William Storer Peter Fozard , Thomas Agar , John Debenham , and John Stalker Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Lawrence Fenn came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Lawrence Finn on the said Eleventh day of December n the Year aforesaid, being in his Lodging Room in the Dwelling House of Martin Healy  Victualler Known by the Sign of the House Shoe in Jemryn Street in the Parish of St. James within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the said Lawrence Finn fell out of and through one of the Windows in the said Lodging Room, being at the Knight of two Stories upon the Tops of the Iron Rails in the front of the said House in Jermyn Street aforesaid, by Manns whereof the said Lawrence Finn did there and there receive driver Mortal Wounds in and upon the Right side of him the said Lawrence Finn of which said Mortal Wound he the said Lawrence Finn at the said Parish of St. James and also at St. Georges Hospital in the said Parish of St George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, for the space of one hour did languish and live on which said Eleventh day of December n the year aforesaid, at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square he the said Lawrence Finn of the Mortal Wound aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Lawrence Finn in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death.

In Witness whereof, aswell the said Coroner as the said John Hickinbottom  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Persons have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Jno Hickinbottom Foreman
