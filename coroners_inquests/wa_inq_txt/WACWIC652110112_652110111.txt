City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of March in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Harding then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Hudson , Thomas Fidgeon , William Wilson , Thomas Grieve , John Lawrence , James Doble , Michael Castledon , Henry Pickup , Thomas George , Richard Herring , Henry Boxley and Richard James good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Harding came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Harding a Lighterman on the Seventh day of March in the Year aforesaid being in a certain Barge Loaden with Coals upon the River Thomas near the Horse Ferry in the Parish of St. John the Evangelist within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, and the said John Harding then and there stepping into another Barge lying near to that in which the said John Harding was Rowing, It so happened that the Right Leg of the said John Harding Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune slipped down with great force between the Barge into which he stepped, and a piece of Wood fixed at the Head thereof called the Bitt, by means whereof the said John Harding then and there received divers Wounds and Bruises in and upon his said right Leg, of which said mortal Wounds and Bruises he the said John Harding from the said Seventh day of March in the Year aforesaid until the Nineteenth day of the same Month and Year, at the said Parish of St. Margaret with in the City Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Nineteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Margaret, he the said John Harding of the Wounds and Bruises aforesaid, did dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, That the said John Harding Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the Said Thomas Hudson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner . Thos Hudson Foreman
