An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at Tothill Fields  Bridewell in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty sixth day of March in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great- Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary How then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Scott , John Curtis , James Falkner Joseph Phillips , Richard Mason , John Bastin , William Moulding , John Weems , Samuel Richardson , George Coleman , Owen Davis and Frederick Habel good and lawful Men of the said LIberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Hour came to h er death, do upon their Oath say That the said Mary How being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields  Bridewell situate in the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid on the Twenty fifth day of March in the Year aforesaid to Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way to wit of a Fever and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Scott Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of this Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition seth their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos. Scott Foreman
