City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty eighth day of April in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grave of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Edward Wood then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Cowper  Edward Jones  Joseph Ball , John Llewellyn , Philip Wyatt  Richard Stanford  Frank Rochford  John Corass , David Styles  James Corneck , Nicholas Philipson  Thomas Harrison  John Watson John Shirley  John Burton & John Triston good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Wood came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Edward Wood on the Twenty fifth day of April in the Year aforesaid being Riding upon a certain Black Horse upon Epsom Downs nigh unto Sutton in the County of Surry, and being then and there very much Intoxicated with Liquor and unable to stop the said Horse It so happened that the said Edward Wood then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Black Horse unto the Ground and thereby then and there did receive several Contusions in and upon his Head, and a Concussion in the Brain, of which said mortal Contusions and Concussion he the said Edward Wood from the said Twenty fifth day of April in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty sixth day of the same Month in the same Year at Sutton aforesaid and also at the said Parish of St. James within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty sixth day of April aforesaid he the said Edward Wood at the said Parish of St. James of the mortal Contusions and Concussion aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Edward Wood in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Cowper Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho Prickard Coroner

John Cowper Foreman
