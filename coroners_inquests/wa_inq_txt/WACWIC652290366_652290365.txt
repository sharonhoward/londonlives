City of Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty fourth day of June in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Michael Tims then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Boot , Henry Hill , Henry Oxford , Charles Chillett , Edward Blissett , George Pearce , Dennis Carco , Isaac Clements , John Headen , John Reeves , Thomas Maycock , Joseph Hall , William Dennison , William Strutton , Thomas Smith , William Webb and Thomas Hall good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Michael Tims came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Michael Tims (suspected to have been minthered) on the Twenty first day of June in the Year aforesaid, at the said Parish of Saint James within the Liberty and County aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way (to wit) of a Putied fever and rest otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Thomas Boot Foreman of the Said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thomas Boot Foreman
