City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in The County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of July in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Blackshaw then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Ralph Mills Thomas Jones , William Knight , Edward Hill , Nicholas Preston , Robert Hindmarsh , Richard Clarke John Shirley , Richard Secome , Richard Burton , John China , and Joseph But good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sorn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Mean the said William Blackshaw came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said William Blackshaw on the Twelfth day of July in Year aforesaid in the Parish and in the Liberty an County aforesaid to wit at a Wharf near to the Fire Engines at being Working a Windless for lowering of Timber into a Barge, and being intoxicated with Liquor it so happened that accidently casually and by Misfortune he the said William Blackshaw last and quitted the hold of the Bar of the said Windless, and the Said Bar of the said Windless was thereby put into swift Motion and by means whereof he the said William Blackshaw was by the Bar of the said Windless violently and forcibly struck against the Breast or Stomach of him the said William Blackshaw which caused a Mortal on the Breast or Stomach of him the said William Blackshaw of which said Mortal Concession and pressure he the said William Blackshaw then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Blackshaw in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise, and that the Bar of the said Windless was moving to the Death of the said William Blackshaw and the said Bar is of the Value of One Shilling, and the Property and in the Possesion of Wade of Eaton Street in the Parish aforesaid Wharf or of his Assigns. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Ralph Mills Foreman
