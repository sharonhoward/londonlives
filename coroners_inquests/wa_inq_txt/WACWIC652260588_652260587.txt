City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of August in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Day then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Barnett , John Austin , John Ritcherson , Edward Thomas , John Molloy , Thomas Slater , George Watts , John Williams , Richard Huntly , William Rhodes , John Rook , Alexander. Reed , and Alexander Miles , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Day came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said Thomas Day a Bricklayer on the fifteenth day of August in the Year aforesaid, being at Work at the House of Captain Berkley situate in Dover Street in the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And then and there standing upon a Board on the outside and on the back part of the said House, It so happened That the said Thomas Day Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said Board at the Height of two Stones unto and upon the Stone Pavement on the Back Flat and thereby then and there received a Mortal Wound and Fracture on the Back part of the Head of him the said Thomas Day of which said Mortal Wound and Fracture he the said Thomas Day then and There instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say. That the said Thomas Day Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, came to his Death.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Richard Barnett  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above mentioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner Rd. Barnett Foreman
