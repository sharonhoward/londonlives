City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of May in the twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of George Griggs then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Storey , Daniel Carnley , James Martin  Robert Walker John Leman , John Barnard , Joseph Exer , William Pritchard , Joseph Storer , Edward Hunt , Richard Westmacott , and John Upful l good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said George Griggs came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said George Griggs on the Eighth day of May in the Year aforesaid being driving a certain Chariot drawn by two Horses in Albemarle Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being then and there in Liquor, It so happened that the said George Griggs then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the Box of the said Chariot unto and upon the Stone Pavement in the said Street, and thereby then and there received a violent Contusion in and upon the Breast, and a Concussion in and upon the Brain of him the said George Griggs , of which said Contusion and Concussion he the said George Griggs , from the said Eighth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Ninth day of the same Month and Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Ninth day of May at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said George Griggs of the Contusion and Concussion aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said George Griggs in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Storey  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Storey Foreman
