City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of April in the Twenty Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Grace Hobbins then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Weaver , James Clements , Thomas Masters , John Poole , William Sills , Thomas Burbidge , William Booth , Thomas Knibbs , George Walker , John Brewster , Robert Barker , Peter Delasson and Jeremiah Dove good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Grace Hobbins came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Grace Hobbins not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Fifth day of April in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, to Wit, into the Serpentine River in Hyde Park there did cast and throw herself, and by Means of which said Casting and Throwing she the said Grace Hobbins in the Waters of the said River was then and there suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning She the said Grace Hobbins then and there Instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Grace Hobbins not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding not being of and distracted, in Manner and form aforesaid did drown and kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Weaver Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands presence, have Day Year and Place abovewritten

Thos. Prickard Coroner

Tho Weaver Foreman
