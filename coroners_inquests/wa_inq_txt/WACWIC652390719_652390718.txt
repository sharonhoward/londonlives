City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Dwelling House of James Morris the Sign of the Craven head Drury Lane in the parish of St. Clement Danes within the Liberty of Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the eleventh day of November 1799 in the thirty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on, View of the Body of John Day then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Day came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said John Day on the tenth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the liberty aforesaid To wit in the said Dwelling House of the said James Morris in Davis Lane aforesaid died, by the Visitation of God in Natural Way, to wit of an Apoplectic Fit , and not otherwise IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner } Tho. Champion Foreman } Griff. Griffiths Tho. Clark Chas
