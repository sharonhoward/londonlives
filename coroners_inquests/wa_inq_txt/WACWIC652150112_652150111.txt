City and Liberty of Westminster in the county of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of April in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Hennings then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Aldridge , John Berkley , John Stanford , Peter Paul , John Saffin , Peter Dillen , William Hall , James Wise , John Parrot , William Moule , George Williams , John Dethick . Charles Ednott , Robert Young , Samuel Asbourn and John Wolfe good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Hennings came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Hennings, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Ninth day of April in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid a great Quantity of White Arsenic, being a deadly Poison into a certain Quantity of Water, did then and there put and mix, and the said White Arsenic, so put an mixt as aforesaid, he the said John Hennings did then and there take drink and swallow down; by Means whereof he the said John Hennings became then and there sick and diste ppered in his Body, and of the Poison aforesaid, and of the Sickness and Distemper thereby occasioned, he the said John Hennings then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Hennings not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaidm, did Poison and kill himself. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said William Aldridge Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Wm Aldridge Foreman
