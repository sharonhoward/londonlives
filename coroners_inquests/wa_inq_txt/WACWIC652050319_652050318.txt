City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Thirty first Day of May in the Fifth Year of the Regin of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of William Smalley then and there being Dead, upon the Oath of Richard William William James , John Evans , John Bray , Thomas Hitch , Francis Steward , John Cockram James Maund  Thomas Lommy  William Sheppard , Robert Lexham  Harry Flint , Charles Dick John Green and Robert Swain good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King, when how, and by Means the said William Smalley came to his Death, do upon their Oath says, That on the said Thirty first day of May in the Year aforesaid the said William Smalley was crossing a certain Street called the Hay Market in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Man to the Jurors aforesaid Unknown was then and there carefully driving a certain Cart loaden with Coals and drawn by three Horses, up the said Street, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the said William Smalley did fall down did then and there pass over the Body of the said William Smalley, by Means whereof the said William Smalley did then and there Accidentally, Casually and by Misfortune receive divers mortal Bruises in and upon his Body and right side, of which said mortal Bruises he the said William Smalley then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said William Smalley in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise And that the said Off Wheel of the said Cart moved to the Death of the said William Smalley and is of the Value often shillings and the Property of a person unknown In Witness whereof as well the said COroner, as the said Richard Williams Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho: Prickard  Coroner

Richd Williams Foreman
