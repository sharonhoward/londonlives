City & Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Third day of May in the Fifth year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Charles Holloway then and there lying Dead upon the Oath of Anthony Vere , John Shove , Mansel Alcock , Richard Perle , William Baldwin , Richard Bride  William Flight , Godfried Borneman , John Carter , William Fulforth , James Sedgwick , William Mould and William Gray good and lawfull Man of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by what Means the said Charles Holloway came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That the said Charles Holloway not having the Fear of God before his Eyes, but being moved and reduced by the Instigation of the Devil, on the Second day of May in the Year aforesaid, with force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, in and upon himself in the Peace of God, and of our said Sovereign Lord the King then and there being feloniously, wilfully, and of his Malice fore thought did make an Assault; And that the said Charles Holloway with a certain Penknife made of Iron and Steel of the of six pence which he the said Charles Holloway , then and there had and held in his right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said Charles Holloway did then and there strike and cut, thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Penknife aforesaid, in and upon the said Throat or Gullet of him the said Charles Holloway one mortal wound of the Length of three Inches and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound he the said Charles Holloway there died the same Day and Year last mentioned. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Charles Holloway in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, feloniously, wilfully, and of his Malice fore thought, did Kill and Murder himself against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. And the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Charles Holloway at the time of the doing and committing of the Felony and Murder aforesaid, or at any time since had not any Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements in the said Liberty of Westminster or elsewhere, to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Anthony Vere Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Anthony Vere Foreman
