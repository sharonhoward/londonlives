City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter, of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of July in the twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Roberts then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Barber , Benjamin Wall , Joseph Turner , Andrew Oliver  John Edwards , Thomas Forrester  James Barclay , Richard Holloway  John Trindall , Richard Lyfford , John Maiden , Robert Ollyett , Thomas Weaver John Waller , William Morrison , James Smither and James Milton good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Roberts came to his Death, do upon their oath say, That the said Thomas Roberts not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Nineteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto an Iron Bar in the Cellar of George Swinley Victualler situate and being in May's Brickings in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle himself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said Thomas Roberts then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Roberts not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Barber Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seal the Day Year and Place above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

John Barber Foreman
