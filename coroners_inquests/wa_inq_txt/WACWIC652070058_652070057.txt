City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex To wit}  An Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Gatehouse Prison situate in the Parish of St. Margaret within the City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex the Fifteenth Day of January in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING. Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & Liberty on View of the Body of John Bowren then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Parker  Robert Bray William Arnold , Joseph Smith , John Goldsmith , William Turner , Samuel Clerk , John Tomlinson , Paul Wilson , George Palmer , John Marson , and Robert Anderson , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord KING, when, how, and what Means the said John Bowren came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Bowren being a Prisoner in the Gate house Prison aforesaid, in the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, on the thirteenth day of January in the Year aforesaid, at the Gatehouse Prison aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God, in a natural Way, to Wit of a Fever, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward Parker Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Ed Parker Foreman
