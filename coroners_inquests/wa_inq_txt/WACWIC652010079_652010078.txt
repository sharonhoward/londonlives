Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex .} to wit An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of Saint Peter at Westminster the Nineteenth day December in the Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand Seven hundred and Sixty one. Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid, Upon a View of the Body of George Badderley then and there lying Dead b the Oaths of Samuel Adams , William Lewis , Thomas Cloes , Thomas Scates , Edward Jay , Samuel Sambro , Thomas Bevan , John Stephens , John Hainslow , Richard Clapham , Robert Eastham , James Beckett , and Alexander Grant Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said George Badderley came to his Death say upon their Oaths, That on Thursday the Seventeenth day of December Instant in the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid the said George Badderley did by the Visitation of God dye a Natural Death And so the said Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said George Badderley the Day and Year aforesaid in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid did by the Visitation of God as aforesaid Dye a Natural Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Jno. Feary Coroner .

Saml. Adams Foreman
