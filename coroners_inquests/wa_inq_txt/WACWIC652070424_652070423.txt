City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Ninth Day of June in the Seventh Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of William English then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Ashmore , William Pepper , John Stone , Samuel Latham , William Spinney John Robinson , Robert Horwood , William Bend , Samuel Tyler , Charles Miller , Alexander Gordon , John Stowers and John Doleman , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King when how, and by what Means the said William English came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William English a Carpenter, on the Twenty second day of June in the Year aforesaid being at Work in a certain House called by the name of and used for the Salt Office, situate in Buckingham Street in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there standing upon a Ladder, two Stories high on the outside of the said House, It so happened that the said William English Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did then and there fall off and from the said Ladder upon certain Iron-Rails before the said House, and thereby did then and there receive two mortal Wounds in and upon his left Thigh, and Bruises in his Body, of which said mortal Wounds and Bruises he the said William English from the said Twenty second day of June in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty seventh day of the same Month and Year at the Westminster Hospital situate and being in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty seventh day of June in the Year aforesaid he the said William English at the Hospital aforesaid, of the mortal Wounds and Bruises aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William English Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Joseph Ashmore Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Jos: Ashmore Foreman
