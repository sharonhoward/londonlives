City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Mary Tilling , Sign of the Spread Eagle  Mill Bank  parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the nineteenth day of September 1796 in the thirty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of patience Banfield aged five years then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Patience Banfield came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say that the said patience Banfield on the sixteenth day of September in the year aforesaid at the parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit in the Garden belong to My Ann Hall then & there being It so happened that acciden tally casually and by Misfortune she the said Patience Banfield unto a certain Dipping place situate in the said Garden did fall and in the water thereof was then and there suffocate & drowned: of which said suffocation and drowning she the said patience Banfield then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say that the said Patience Banfield in manner and by Means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came where death and not otherwise

 IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy, Gell Coroner }
