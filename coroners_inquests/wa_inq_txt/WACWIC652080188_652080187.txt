City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of May in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Hansell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Spencer Draper , William Cross , George Cowdell , John Wood , Robert Walker , John Stevens , James Walters , John Chew , Alexander Grant , John Gee , Charles Scott , Ezra Eads , Joseph Eyre and William Budge good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Hansell came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Sarah Hansell on the Twelfth day of May in the Year aforesaid being a Lodger on the Second Floor in the Dwelling House of John Turner situate and being in David Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there hanging out Wet Linnen to dry upon a certain Line fastened on the outside of the Window of the said Sarah Hansell, on the back part of the said House, It so happened that the said Line broke, And that the said Sarah Hansell did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fall out of and through the said Window upon certain Wooden Rails near unto the said House and thereby did then and there receive one mortal Wound in and upon her Stomach, of the Dept of three Inches, of which said mortal Wound she the said Sarah Hansell then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Sarah Hansell Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to her Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Spencer Draper Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Spencer Draper Foreman
