City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex } to wit an Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of July in the Twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and So forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our Said Lord the King for the Said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Charles Curchill then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Sheppard , John White , Charles William , James Wilson Samuel Weston , James Greason , William Brown , Charles Smith , Richard Hatch , George Coleman , Henry Burt , Thomas Hilder and George Audsley good and lawful Men of the Said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to enquire for our Said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the Said Charles Churchill came to h is Death, do upon their Oath Say, That the Said Charles Churchill a Carter on the Twenty Eighth day of June in the Year aforesaid being with his Cart at Charing Cross in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields in the County aforesaid, waiting to be hired, And one of the Horses in the said Cart being unruly. It so happened that the Said Charles Churchill in endeavouring to present any Mischief being done by the said unruly Horse was Accidentally casually and by Misfortune forced against another Cart then and there being and pressed between the said Carts. Whereby the said Charles Churchill then and there received a Fracture on the left side of the Ribs, and Mortal Bruises in his Body, of which Said Mortal Fracture and Bruises he the said Charles Churchill from the said twenty Eighth day June in the Year aforesaid, untill the said tenth day of July in the same year at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live on which said Tenth day of July in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish last mentioned, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Charles Churchill of the Mortal Fracture and Bruises aforesaid did die.And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Charles Churchill in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death

In Witness whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said William Sheppard  foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Shepperd Foreman
