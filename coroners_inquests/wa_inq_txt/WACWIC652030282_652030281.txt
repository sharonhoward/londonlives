City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of September in the third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Phebe Fellows then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Lee , Michael Eaton Bennett Marshall , David Simpson , John Briant , Joseph Gibbons , John Eadus , Duncan Watson , Peter Goostrey , Peter Collins , Jabis Penry , Joseph Morcing , James Eagars , John Miller , Edward Ducell , John Saviney and John Burnett good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what Means the said Phebe Fellows came to her Death, Do upon their Oath say, That the said Phebe Fellows not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted, on the twenty ninth day of September in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain piece of small Cord unto a Pulley fastened into the Cieling, in a Kitchin belonging to the dwelling House of Edith Munday Tallow Chandler, situate in Pall Mall , in the said Parish Liberty and County and the other End thereof about her own Neck, did fix, tye, and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang, suffocate, and strangle herself, of which said Hanging, Suffocation and Strangling, she the said Phebe Fellows then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Phebe Fellows, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did hill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Lee Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Please first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner . Thos. Lee Foreman
