- City and Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex  } An Inquisition Indented taken of our Sovereign Lord the King of the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of June in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Daniel Aldridge then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Blatch , James Mathews , Thomas Spencer , John Taylor , John Card , George Eyre , William Mitchell , William Lockey , William Moffat , John Price , Thomas Wheatley , William Gilroy , Thomas Langton , John Storr , Robert Watson , and John Lumsden , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to Inquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by what Means the said Daniel Aldridge came to his death, do upon of their Oath say, That the said Daniel Aldridge a Carter on the said Fifteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid, being driving a certain Cart loaded with Dust and Ashes and drawn by two Horses, through a Gateway under part of the Gatehouse Prison, in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Man unknown was then and there carefully riding upon a certain Bay Horse, and leading another Horse in his Hand, And the said Gateway being Narrow, It so happened that the said Daniel Aldridge the and there Accidentally as aforesaid, did then and there go upon and pass over the lower part of the Belly of the said Daniel Aldridge , by reason whereof the said Daniel Aldridge did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Bruise in and upon the lower part of his Belly, of which said mortal Bruise he the said Daniel Aldridge then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Daniel Aldridge , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Blatch Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day and Place first above Written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Blatch Foreman
