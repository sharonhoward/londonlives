City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westminster , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of June in the Fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Walker then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Cox , Robert Watson , Emanuel Lloyd , Robert Patten , Andrew Staines , Henry Burnett , William Holmes , Robert Smith , John Nash , John Barnard , Richard Rowe , John Spiller , John Smith , Thomas Wharton Hugh Douglass and Alexander Smeele , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said Richard Walker came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty sixth day of June in the Year aforesaid, the said Richard Walker being with divers other Persons in a Boat or Wherry upon the River Thames , in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that about ten of the Clock in the Evening of the said Twenty sixth day of June, It so happened that the said Boat or Wherry, with the said Richard Walker therein, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune struck against and went under a Lighter loaden with Chalk, and lying at Anchor in and upon the said River Thames , by Means whereof the said Boat or Wherry sunk, and the said Richard Walker in the Waters of the said River was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Richard Walker then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Richard Walker in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Cox Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovewritten,

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Cox Foreman
