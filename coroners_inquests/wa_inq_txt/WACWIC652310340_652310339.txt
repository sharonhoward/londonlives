City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of July in the thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Dunn an Infant then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Joseph Jarvis , John Banks , Thomas Osbourn , John Bogne Joseph Clout , John Notes , William Curnock , John Darley Robert Perkins , Richard Bulter , John Baker , Thomas Hill and Robert Birchmore Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty. duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Robert Dunn came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty Second day of July in the Year aforesaid a Man was carefully driving a certain Empty Cart drawn by one Horse in Carnaby Street in the said Parish of St. James within the Liberty and County aforesaid and that the said Robert Dunn the Infant aged about Five years was playing in the said Street, It so happened that the said Robert Dunn the Infant Fell down and that the off wheel of the said Cart accidentally Casually and by Misfortune Passed over the Head of the said Robert Dunn so lying upon the Ground as aforesaid by Means whereof the said Robert Dunn their and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon the Back part of his Head of which said mortal Fracture he the said Robert Dunn then and these instantly died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Robert Dunn in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Said Joseph Jarvis Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself. and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Jas Jarvis  Foreman
