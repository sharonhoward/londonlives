City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of September in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas White then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Foster John Sturgis , Evan Skegg , John Riley , Thomas Ward , Robert Wood , Thomas Wilkinson , John Boville , Thomas Clark , Francis Belwine , Benjamin Combes and Nicholas George , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas White came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas White , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Tenth day of September in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain piece of Cord unto the Iron Rails, fastened into the Stone Steps leading out of the Area into the Garden of His Excellency Count Welderen, situate and being in Hanover Square in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did thin and there hang suffocate and strangle himself: of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling, he the said Thomas White , then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas White , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Foster Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, in the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Foster Foreman
