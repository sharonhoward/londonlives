City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Knightsbridge in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirteenth day of November in the twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Mason then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Sacrey Gilpin , William Inchelwood , Mark Kempley , William Gee , William Keable , Avery Tyrrell , Willaim Sharp , John Maxwell , John Colley , Samuel Rudd , Henry List , and Reuben Jackson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duty sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Mason came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Mason on the Twelfth day of November in the Year aforesaid being at Work in a certain Gravel Pitt in Hyde Park in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there digging Gravel out of the said Pitt, It so happened that a great Quantity of Earth then and there fell into and covered the said William Mason in the said Gravel Pitt, whereby the said William Mason Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune was then and there Suffocated and smothered of which said Suffocation and Smothering he the said William Mason then and there instantly died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Mason in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Sacrcy Gilpin Foreman of the said Jurors on the on behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Thos. Prickard Coroner .

S Gilpin Foreman
