City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St, Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of June in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Lawrence Kenndy then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Oakes , William Warren  John Imeson , John Palen , Alexander Henderson , Isaac Barnett , William Jeffreys , Thomas Oakes , Thomas Shankster , Charles Lewis , John Dizney , and John Miliner good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Lawrence Kennedy came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Lawrence Kennedy not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted on the fourteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, with a certain Razor made of Iron and Street which he the said Lawrence Kennedy then and there had and held in his Right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said Lawrence Kennedy did then and there Strike stat and penetrate there by then and there giving unto himself with the Razor aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Lawrence Kennedy , one mortal Wound of the Length of three Inches, and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said Mortal Wound he the said Lawrence Kennedy from the said fourteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid, until the seventeenth day of the same Month in the same Year, at the said Parish of Saint George Hannover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said seventeenth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Lawrence Kennedy of the Mortal Wound aforesaid, did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Lawrence Kennedy not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding. but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid,did kill homeself.

In Witness where of aswell the said Coroner as the said Richard Oakes Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Handsand Seals the day Year and Place above mentioned.

Tho, Prickard  Coroner

Richd Oakes Foreman }
