Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Fifth day of May in the First year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so Forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one. Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid, Upon a Veiw of the Body of Jacob Hooper then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of James Faith , William Jones , Jeremiah Bridges , Humphrey Hyall , James Warrant Joseph Stevens , John Jennings , Henry Hurd , William Jones , Edward Thompson , Henry Allibon , John Vincent Thomas Hedges , James Sedwick , and Thomas Read Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty, who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for an Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Jacob Hooper came to his Death say upon their Oaths That on Monday the fourth day of this Instant May the said Jacob Hooper being in a House now building in May Fair in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Liberty and County aforesaid it so happened that the Foundation of the said House giving way the front and other parts of the said House fell in and upon the said Jacob Hooper so being in the said House as aforesaid by means whereof the said Jacob Hooper was Smothered and suffocated of which said Smothering and Suffocating the said Jacob Hooper then and there instantly Died And so the said Jurors upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Jacob Hooper the day and year aforesaid in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid in manner and by the means aforesaid unfortunately and accidentally came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition put their hands and Seals the day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Jno. Feary  Coroner .

James Faith Foreman
