City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty ninth day of July in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Wright then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Parker  John Lambert , George Bailey , George Cullen Henry Frisby , William Rogers , Joseph Emmott , John Bealby , Follet Clark Joseph Robson , William Thomas , William Anderson , John Godfrey , Thomas Kent and Alexander Saunders good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Wright came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said James Wright not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted, on the said Twenty ninth day of July in the Year aforesaid, at the said Parish of Saint James within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain piece of Cord unto an Iron Staple fastened in the Wainscot in the Workshop of the said James Wright situate in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other and thereof about his own neck did fix and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle himself, of which said hanging suffocation and strangling he the said James Wright then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Wright ; not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted, in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill himself.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said William Parker Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner William Parker Foreman
