City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the king, at the Parish of Saint Annel within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Fourth day of July in the fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Alexander Steward then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Ellerback Thomas Belgrave , John Glazier , David Watson , Samuel, Griffith  William youal , John Patison Charles Green , William Daugh foild , Edward Smith , Joseph Mashers , John Howlet , William Hambetton  Thomas Misbet good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty, duty chosen, who being then and there duty Sworn ad charged top enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, an by what Means the said Alexander Steward came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say

That the said Alexander Steward not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic, and Distracted ibn the Twenty third day of July in the year aforesaid in the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Dwelling House of Robert Henderson in little Ryders Court there situate in the Apratment or Lodging Room of the said Alexander Steward in the said House with a certain Razor made of Jum and Steel which he the said Alexander Steward then and, there had in his Right Hand the Throat or Gullet of his the said Alexander did and there and pentrate thereby then and there giving unto himself the said Alexander Steward with the Razon aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Alexander Steward and Mortal Wound of there inches and of the depth of our inch of which said Mortal wound he the said Alexander Steward then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Alexander not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Corner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Joseph Ellenback Foreman
