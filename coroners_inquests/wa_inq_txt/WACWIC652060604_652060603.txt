City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of November in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Rivers then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Carter , Thomas Wallington , Christopher Gundrey , James Whalley , Benjamin Hall , William Yates , John Jessey , Richard Scrase , John Monk , John Beardmore , Thomas Riddle , James Sinclair and Bright Hemmings , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what Means the said William Rivers came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Rivers a Labourer on the Ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid being in a New Built House situate near Berkley Square in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there going up the Stairs in the said House, the Banister not being put up, It so happened that the said William Rivers then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down, off and from the said Stairs unto the Ground, by Means whereof the said William Rivers did then and there receive divers mortal Wounds and Bruises in and upon his Head Body and Legs, of which said mortal wounds and Bruises he the said William Rivers languished about four Hours, and thereof then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Rivers , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Carter Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their Presence, have to this Inquisition, set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner . Thos: Carter Foreman
