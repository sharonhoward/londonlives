City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of July in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Hannah Hayward then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Loton Thomas Dickeson , James Don , Thomas Bywater , Robert Parker , John Harkness , William Aldmon , William Briggs , Peirce Tent , Joseph Perkins , Edward Thomas , and James Chisholme good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Hannah Hayward came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Hannah Hayward on the Twenty ninth day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid by the Visitation of God did then and there depart this Life in a Natural way and not otherwise. In Witness whereof at will the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Juras have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner James Loton Foreman
