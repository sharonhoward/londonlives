City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Mary le Strand within the Liberty of the Deanand Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Seventeenth day of January in the Fifth Year of our Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of John Huddlestone then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of William Taylor , John Frewin , Richard Histed , Richard Farlow , John Lacy , Richard Phasay , James Coales , Michael Charles Robert Gardner , William Lindon , William Saul , John Bagshaw , John Oldroyd , William Guy , Luke Lyon  Edward Bailey , and James Hensboom good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King when how and by what Means the said John Haddlestone came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Huddlestone , not being of sound Mind, Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted on the Sixteenth day of January in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforeaid, at his Lodging Room or Apartment in the dwelling House of Richard Farlow , situate and being in Swan Yard, in the said Parish Liberty and County, with a certain Razor made of Iron and Steel, which he the said John Huddlestone then and there had and held in his right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said John Huddlestone did then and there strike, stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself the said John Huddlestone , with the Razor aforesaid, in and upon his Throat or Gullet aforesaid, one Mortal Wound of the Length of four Inches, and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said Mortal Wound he the said John Huddlestone then and there instantly died. And to the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said John Huddlestone , not being of sound Mind, Memory, and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Taylor Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard } Coroner

Wm. Taylor} Foreman
