City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of November in the thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Jameson then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John West , Evan Evans , Thomas Fogarts , John Withers Thomas Pate , Matthew Butler , Gabriel Cox , Edward Winstanley William Pearce , Henry Nash , Matthew Hodson and Henry Hodgkins good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Jameson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Jameson on the Eleventh day of November in the Year aforesaid, died suddenly in the Publick Tap Room of John Belworth  Victualler at the Sign of the Feathers situate in Hart Street in the said Parish of St. Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty and County aforesaid that the said James Jameson departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way and not by any Injury or Violence from any Person whatsoever to the Knowledge of the said Jurors

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Jameson  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jno: West Foreman
