City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of December in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great- Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Henry Finlayson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Gray , Thomas Morgan , John Ostler Humphrey Joseph , Nathaniel Bone , William Eastgate Robert Taylor , Peter Dawson , William Oddy , John Waller , William Jones , John Martin , Robert Knipe and Thomas Kennett good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Henry Finldyson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Henry Finlayson not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and distracted on the said nineteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Dwelling House of Mary Grant there situate with a certain Knife made of Iron and Steel which he the said Henry Finlayson then and there had and held in his right Hand the Throat or Gullet of him the said Henry Finlayson did then and there strike stab and pernitrate thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Knife aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Henry Finlayson one Mortal Wound of the Length of Four Inches and of the Depth of one Inch of which said Mortal Wound he the said Henry Finlayson then and there instantly died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Henry Finlayson not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill himself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Gray  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Thos Gray  Foreman
