City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indedented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty first day of October in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Hodges then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Roger Tremells , Thomas Horne , Henry Frame Charles Creag , Josiah Day , Joseph Stutely , Rice Jones Joseph Wood , John Jackson , George Fowler , William Clarke and Leonard Phillips good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Hodges came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Hodges on the Twentieth day of October in the Year aforesaid, was loading a certain Carriage with Timber, at the Whart of Richard Hodges situate in Scotland Yard in the said Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the said William Hodges being then and there standing upon a round Piece of Timber on the said Carriage, It so happend, that the said William Hodges Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Carriage, into the Dock, and that a piece of the said Timber followed, and fell upon the Back of the said William Hodges , by means whereof he the said William Hodges did then and there receive mortal Bruises in the upon his Body of which said mortal Bruises, he the said William Hodges then and there instantly died, And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Hodges in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwsie

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Roger Tremells Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Roger Tremells
