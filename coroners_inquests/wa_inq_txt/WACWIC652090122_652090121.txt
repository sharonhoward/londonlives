City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of March in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Owen then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Williams , Daniel James , Richard Flynn , Richard Jones , Thomas Adcock , Thomas Goddard , John Keir , George Pulley , Richard Haighton , Henry Simpson , Peter Nicholson , Andrew Betty , Alexander Gregg , Edmund Curtis , John Dunston , John Benson Stephen Wilkins and William Lowen , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who bing then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Owen came to h is- Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Owen not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Eighteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto an Iron Hook fastened into the Brick Wall in the Cellar in the Dwelling House of Thomas Goddard , situate and being in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck, did fix tye and faster, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle himself, of which said Hanging, Suffocation and Strangling, he the said Thomas Owen then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Owen , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Williams Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Williams Foreman
