City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit, 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty seventh day of July in the Fifth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of. God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so, Forth, before Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Charles Ravenshaw then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Lamb , Robert Gildon , John Austin , John Wells , Samuel Morrison William Franklin , John Wallis , William Allis , John Stewart , Thomas Hatchard John Smith and John Finlayson , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the Kingwh how, and by what Means the said Charles Ravenshaw came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Eighteenth day of July in the Yar aforesaid Charles Ravenshaw a Carpenter being at Work on a new built House for Edward Green Esquire, situate in the Parish of Chelsea in the said County of Middlesex , It so happened that a small piece of Timber used in the Seaffold on the inside of the said House, (commonly called a Putlock) did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fall down out of and from the said Seaffold on the Story, upon the Head of him the said Charles Ravenshaw, then being on the Ground Floor in the said new built House, by Reason whereof he the said Charles Ravenshaw then and there received a mortal Fracture in and upon the Rightside of the Head of him the said Charles Ravenshaw, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Charles Ravenshaw from the said eighteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid until the Twentysixth day of the same Month and Year, at certain Hospital situate in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, called the Westminster Hospital , did languish, & languishing did live, on which said Twenty sixth day of July in the Year aforesaid the said Charles Ravenshaw at the said Hospital, of the mortal Fracture aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Charles Ravenshaw in Manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Lamb Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Thos: Lamb Fourman
