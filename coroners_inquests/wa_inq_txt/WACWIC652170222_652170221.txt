City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden Within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of May in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Patrick Fagan then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Hassell Hutchins  Edward Elliott , John Gilphin , Thomas Weston , Isaac Froome , Thomas Haynes , Joseph Selth , Henry Vint , Robert Timewell , Joseph Weathers , Moses Lancy , John Parrott , John Shenton George Hutchinson and Thomas Francis good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged, to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by Means the said Patrick Fagan came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That Daniel Eccles on the fourteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid being in the House of David Low in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and having in his Hand a certain Pocket Pistol which was out of Repair, And that the said Daniel Eccles was then and there giving the said Pistol (not knowing that the same was loaded) unto Samuel Bunny a Gunsmith to be Repaired It so Happened that the said Pistol went off and was discharged and that the said Patrick Fagan thereby did then and there Casually by Misfortune and against the Will of him the said Daniel Eccles receive Wound in the Month of him the said Patrick Fagan on the Right side of which said Mortal Wound he the said Patrick Fagan from the said Fourteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Nineteenth day of the same Month and Year at the Parish aforesaid. within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did liver on which said Nineteenth day of May aforesaid he the said Patrick Fagan at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid of the mortal Wound aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Partick Fagan in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of the said Daniel Eccles came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Hassell Hutchins Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above Written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Hassell Hutchins Foreman
