City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of July in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Elizabeth the Wife of Thomas Sadler , then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Stephen Joyce John Battersby , John Tennant , John Bennett , John James Bonhote , William Potier , John Befts , John Southall , William Hooper , William Budd , Robert Spencer , George Hewitson , George Bartholomew , Pelling Rose , John Heys , John Smith , William Stevenson & William Broadstock , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Sadler came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Sadler , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Fifth day of July in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, a great Quantity of White Arsenic, being a deadly Poison, into a certain Quantity of small Beer did then and there put and mix, and the said White Arsenic, so put and mixt as aforesaid she the said Elizabeth Sadler , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted as aforesaid, did their and there take, drink and swallow down; by Means whereof she the said Elizabeth Sadler became then and there sick and distempered in her Body, and of the Poison aforesaid, and of the Sickness and Distemper thereby occasioned, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish about two hours, and then and there she the said Elizabeth Sadler of the Poison aforesaid, and of the Sickness and Distemper thereby occasioned did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Elizabeth Sadler , not being of sound Mind, Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted as aforesaid, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did Poison and Kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Stephen Joyce Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Sten: Joyce Foreman
