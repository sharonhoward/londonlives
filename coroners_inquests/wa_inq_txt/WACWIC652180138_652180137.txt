City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of May- in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John White then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Francis Cann , Joseph Casters , James Pringle . John Hembrow . John Stone , William Storer John Pettis , Richard Oaks  William Hemming , John White , William Harding and Thomas Rising good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John White came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John White an Infant, of the Age of Seven Years on the Second day of May in the Year aforesaid being with John White his Father, a Mason, who was at Work on the New built House of William Hemming situate at Knights bridge in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the said John White the Infant being in the Garret, the Floor not being laid, It so happened that the said John White the Infant then and there Accidentally casually and by Misf fell through and between the Joists into the Kitchen, and thereby then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon the back part of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said John White then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John White in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Francis Cann Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Francis Cann Foreman
