City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirty first day of July in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Rae then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Milner , Richard Taylor , Gabriel Kingson , John Westbrook , William Vernon , Benjamin Brown , Robert Fauldar, John Andree , Matthew Clark , Francis Carbuary , Dennis Delaney , Henry Portar , John Cheneley , John Midlane , John Morgan , John Taylor , and John Cole good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and changed to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what by Means the said William Rae came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Rae on the Twenty sixth day of July, in the Year aforesaid, being carefully Riding upon a certain Bay Horse in Oxford Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, That a little Boy was flying his Kite in the said Street, the said Horse took Fright and Galloped away. And that the said William Rae then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Horse upon the Stone pavement in the said Street and thereby then and there received a mortal Fracture of his Skull, of which said Mortal Fracture he the said William Rae from the said Twenty sixth day of July in the Year aforesaid, until the Twenty seventh day of the same Month, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty seventh day of July in the Year aforesaid, at the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square he the said William Rae , of the Mortal Fracture aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say. That the said William Rae , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid. Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said James Milore Foreman of the said Jurors on, the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above mentioned

Tho Prickard Coroner

James Milner Foreman
