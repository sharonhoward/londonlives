City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex } to wit An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of April in the Thirtieth, Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and So forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our Said Lord the King for the Said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Mc. neal then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Nicholas Monrey , John Evans , John Buchannon, Thomas Marycock , John Jones , Thomas Silvester , Thomas Hall James Dalton , Charles Bell , charles Penny , Richard Bates and James marks good and lawful Men of the Said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our Said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the Said Ann Mc. Neal came to her Death, do upon their Oath Say That the Said Ann Mc. Neal suspected to have been murthered on he fourteenth day of April in the year aforesaid was foud dead at her Lodgings in the Dwelling House of William Stretton Situate in Little Poultney Street in the Said Parish of Saint James within the Liberty and County, aforesaid, that no marks of Violence appeared on the Body and that the Said Ann Mc. Neal, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a Natural Way.

In Witness whereof as well the Said Coroner as the said Thomas Money Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho.Prickard Coroner N J. Monrey Foreman
