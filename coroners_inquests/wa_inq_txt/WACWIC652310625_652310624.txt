City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex . } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of November in the thirty Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Lydia Lawrence then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John Hardy , Benson Clifford , Charles Clifford , Charles Ady Chisafer Gildershaw , John Collins , John Newans , John Thomas William Bennett , Henry Townsend , Abraham Bear and Joseph Ibbett good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Lydia Lawrence came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Eighteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid the said Lydia Laurence was found drowned and suffocated in the Water in a certain but belonging to the Water Works at Pimlico in the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid That no Marks of Violence appeared on the Body, but how or by what means the said lydia Laurence became drowned and suffocated no Evidence thereof doth appear to the Jurors

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Hardy  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jabes Suedy Foreman
