City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westmisnter, in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of June in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Peter Deckery then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Parker , John Emblin , Lewis Hamilton , Henry North  Richard Harford  Adam Bosanworth , Joseph Monepeny  William Booth , James Laycock , Nicolas Gribble Edward Williams and James Byfield good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Peter Deckery came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Peter Deckery on the Ninth day of June in the Year aforesaid being Climbing up a large Free growing in St. James's Park in the Parish and County aforesaid, It so happened that the feet of the said Peter Deckery then and there slipped, and that the said Peter Deckery Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell from the said Free unto and upon the Ground, by Means whereof he the said Peter Dickery did then and there receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Peter Deckery on the same day and Year did die at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Peter Deckery Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Robert Parkts
