City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } To Wit.  An Inquisition indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Workhouse belonging to and within the Parish of Saint Clement Danes within the liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of Saint Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty seventh day of November in the thirty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esqr. Coroner of our said Lord the King, for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Hannah Hubbard then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by what Means the said Hannah Hubbard came to her Dearth, do upon their Oath say, that Watt Diddle alias Walter Diddle late of the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty aforesaid, on or about the twenty seventh day of October in the year aforesaid, with force and Arms, at the Parish of St. Margaret aforesaid in the Liberty aforesaid, in and upon the said Hannah Hubbard (then being with a certain female Child) in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King, then and there being feloniously did make an Assault And that the said Watt Diddle alias Walter Diddle did then and there with both his hands, her the said Hannah Hubbard and upon the face and head of her the said Hannah Hubbard feloniously strike and beat: And that the said Watt Diddle alias Walter Diddle did then and there with the feet of being the said Watt Diddle alias Walter Diddle in and upon the Belly and Thighs of her the said Hannah Hubbard (so being pregnant of the female Child as aforesaid feloniously kick and strike, thereby then and there giving unto her the said Hannah Hubbard divers mortal Bruises in and upon the Belly and Knights of her the said Hannah Hubbard : Of which said Mortal Bruises she the said Hannah Hubbard at the said Parish of Saint Margaret and also at the said Parish of Saint Clements Danes from the said twenty seventh day of October in the year aforesaid untill the twenty fourth day of November in the same Year did languish and languishing did like On which said twenty fourth day of November in the same year, in the Workhouse of the said Parish of Saint Clements Danes , in the Liberty aforesaid, She the said Hannah Hubbard of the Mortal Bruises aforesaid, did die: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Watt Diddle alias Walter Diddle her the said Hannah Hubbard , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, feloniously did kill and slay against the peace of our said Lord the King, his brown and Dignity. And that, at the time of the doing and committing there of or at any time since he had no Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements within the said Liberty of elsewhere to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day, Year and Place first above-mentioned

Anthy Gell  Coroner J: Elsworth Jas Wilson Richd Eden Saml. Foxon John Smart

Wm Savage John Bellamy Richard Allaway North Jolley John Bibbs
