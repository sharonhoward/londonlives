City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquitition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Fifth day of April in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Elson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Elliston , Allen Bowman , James Hull , Alexander Lindsey , John Short , John Binks  John Wilson , Alexander Mackey , John Brogden William Clarke , Thomas Ravenscroft , James Pinnell , James Gouley , Lewis Jenkins & James Mc.Farnall good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Elson came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say ,That the said Mary Elson , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted on the Twenty fourth day of April in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid out of and through a certain Window at the height of three Stories in the Workhouse of and belonging to the said Parish, there situate, did cast and throw herself, whereby the said Mary Elson then and there received divers Bruises in her Body, of which said Mortal Bruises she the said Mary Elson then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Mary Elson , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Elliston Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten.

Tho. Prickard Coroner Thos. Elliston Foreman
