City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminser within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of middlesex , the Fifteenth day of April in the Eight Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Hugh Monro and Instant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Blatch , Daniel Rainbow , John Ellis , James Franklin , John Robertson , Thos. Hytt  Jeremiah Church , Alexander Gordon , Thomas Latham , William Powell John Terrell and William Grandell good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Hugh Monro came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Hugh Monro an Instant (to Wit, of the Age of Five Years, on the Fourteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid, being alone in the Lodging room or Apartment of Alexander Monro his Father, in the dwelling House of Satcliff situate in White Hart Court in the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said Hugh Monro the Infant did ten and there accidentally Causally and by Misfortune fall out of and through the Window of the said Lodging room unto and upon the Stone Pavement of the said Court, whereby the said Hugh Monro did then and there receive one Mortal Fracture in and upon the right said of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Hugh Monro then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforeaid do say, that the said Hugh Monro Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Batch Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Blatch Foreman
