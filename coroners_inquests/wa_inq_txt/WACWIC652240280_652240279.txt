City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of May in the Twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of god of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner. of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body, of Nicholas Casson then and there lying dead upon the Oath of Hassell Hutchins , Peter Burnell , Andrew Allsop , James Fisher , William Fleming William Ward , Martin Lowe , Richard Hewetson , John Andrews , Isaac Froome , James Elden and Edward Bowers , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Nicholas Casson came to his Death to upon their Oath say that on the Tenth day of May in the Year aforesaid the said Nicholas Casson a Constable being at the Hustings in Covent Garden in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid at the Election of Members to serve in Parliament for the said City and Liberty of Westminster And that a Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid as  yet unknown not having the fear of God before his or their Eyes but moved and seduced by the Instigation of the Devil on the said Tenth day of May in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said Nicholas Casson in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously wilfully and of his or their Malice forethought did make an Assault And that the said Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown with a certain Wooden Bludgeon or Bludgeons which the said Person or Persons unknown then and there had and held in his or their Hands him the said Nicholas Casson in and upon the Head and left side of the Body of him the said Nicholas Casson then and there feloniously wilfully and of his or their Malice fore thought did strike and beat, And that the said Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown did then and there give unto him the said Nicholas Casson by such striking and beating with the Bludgeon or Bludgeon aforesaid one Mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of the Head of him the said Nicholas Casson and a mortal Fracture in and upon the Ribs on the left side of the Body of him the said Nicholas Casson , of which said Mortal Fractures he the said Nicholas Casson at the said Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Tenth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Eleventh day of the same Month in the same Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Eleventh day of May in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Nicholas Casson of the mortal Fractures aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown him the said Nicholas Casson in Manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously willfully and of his or their Malice fore thought did kill and Murder against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Hassell Hutchins Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Hassell Hutchins Foreman
