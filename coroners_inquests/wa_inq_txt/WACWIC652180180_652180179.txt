City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of May in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grave of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Goddard then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Sutton  Abraham Hatchett , Jonathan Andrews , Robert Martin  Edward Pagitt , James Fry , John Ward  William Carr , Nathan Mead  Thomas West , John Hayward and William Elmore good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Goddard came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Richard Goddard on the Twelfth day of May in the Year aforesaid being riding behind and upon a certain Coach drawn by two Horses in the Publick Road at Knightsbridge in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being in Liquor It so happened that the said Richard Goddard fell off the said Coach unto the Ground, and that the Off Wheel of the said Coach then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune instantly passed over the Body of the said Richard Goddard , by Means whereof several of the Ribs of the said Richard Goddard on the left side were Fractured, and the said Richard Goddard then and there likewise received mortal Bruises in his Body, of which said mortal Bruises and Fractures he the said Richard Goddard then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Richard Goddard in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Sutton Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Sutton Foreman
