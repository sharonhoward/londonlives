City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of March in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Linvell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Reynell , John Mc. Donald , James Markes John Moore  Thomas Allen . Henry Hankey , John Bristow , Thomas Gardner , Henry Beale , William Price , John Evans , William Thompson , Joseph Mickelwhite , Iliff Linney & Henry Jones good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Linvell came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said William Linvell in the Evening of the sixth day of March in the Year aforesaid was driving a certain Hackney Coach in the Publick Street or Highway in or near Golden Square at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty & County aforesaid It so happened that the said William Linvell then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the Coach Box of the said Coach upon the Stone Pavement in the said Publick Street or Highway and thereby did then and there receive a mortal Fracture in and upon the Right side of the Head of him the said William Linvell of which said Mortal Fracture he the said William Linvell then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said William Linvell in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually & by Misfortune came to his Oath In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Henry Reynell  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands & Seals the Day Year & Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner Henry Reynell  Foreman
