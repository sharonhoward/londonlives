City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex  } to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of February in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Cox then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Flower , John Thacker  John Newbold , Matthew Angle , James Newbold , Edward Lamborn , Henry Curry , William Rogers , Francis Sadler , George Watson , John Wheelright , John Thresher , James Hutchins and William Jennings good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Cox came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Cox on the Twentieth day of January in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish of Kensington in the County aforesaid, a certain Gun or Fowling Piece leaded with Gunpowder and leaden Shots, which he the said John Cox then and there had and held in both his hands, at and to some Sparrows did let off and discharge, whereby the Stock of the said Fowling piece broke in several pieces, and the Breech of the Barrel recoiling back with great force to and against the Forehead of the said John Cox , by Means whereof the said John Cox then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received one mortal Wound and Fracture in and upon his Forehead, of which said mortal Wound and Fracture he the said John cox from the said Twentieth day of January in the Year aforesaid until the Thirtieth day of the same Month and Year at the said Parish of Kensington , and also at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Thirtieth day of January aforesaid at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square , within the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said John Cox of the mortal Wound and Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Cox in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Flower Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard  Coroner

William Flower Foreman
