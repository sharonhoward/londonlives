City & Liberty of of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to wit} 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St.Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty third day of October in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God , of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and liberty, on View of the Body of job Bilk then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Harris , James Bean , Henry Savory , William Healy , Charles Carey , Miles Wells , James Similar , Samual Mitchell , Henry Wilson , Jacob Hunt , Henry Steere and John Hilton good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Job Bilk came to his Death, do upon their oath say ,Tthat the said Job Bilk on the Thirteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid, being driving a certain Cart drawn by one Horse, over Westminster Bridge at the Parish and in the Liberty and Country aforesaid, and being then and there Sitting upon a certain Empty Cash in the said Cart, It so happened that the said Job Bilk, by a sudden jolt of the said Cart, was Accidentally and violently thrown out of the said Cart to and against the Ground, and by Means thereof did then and there Accidentally and by misfortune receive one mortal Wound in and upon the leftside of his Head, of which said mortal wound he the said Job Bilk from the said Thirteenth day of May in the year aforesaid until the Twenty first day of October in the same Year, at the Westminster Hospital situate in the Parish Liberty and Country aforesaid, did languish and launguishing did live, on which said Twenty first day of October aforesaid, at the Hospital aforesaid , he the said Job Bilk of the mortal Wound aforesaid, did die. and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say, that the said Job Bilk, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Edward Harris Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Edwd. Harris Foreman
