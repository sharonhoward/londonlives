Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented and taken aer within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of Saint Peter at Westminster the Twenty first day of February in the First Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and Sixty one . Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid. Upon a View of the Body of a Man where Name to the Jurors in unknown then and there lying Dead, by the Oaths of William Maddox  Robert Clarke , Robert Lockley , Matthew Nesham , John Rodwell , Robert Ridge , Thomas Vaugh Thomas Gunten , Benjamin Knight , Francis Fleet , Peter Burn , William Posey , Marshall Fleet , and John Spiller Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Inquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Man unknown came to his Death say upon their Oaths That on Thursday the nineteenth day of February Instant the said Man unknown being found in a certain River called the River of Thomas near the Horseferry in the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City and County aforesaid and no marks of Violence appearing on the Body of the said Man unknown to occasion his Death, nor any Evidence to be found to prove how he come into the said River. We the said Jurors upon our Oaths do say and find that by the appearence of the Body of the said Man unknown he in the said River aforesaid was Drowned but whether by his own Will or by any Accident or Misfortune the Jurors know not. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition put their hands and Seals the day Year and at the Place abovesaid

Jno. Feary  Coroner

Wm. Madox Foreman
