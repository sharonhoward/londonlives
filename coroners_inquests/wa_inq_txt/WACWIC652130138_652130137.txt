City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of March in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Ironmonger then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Boyton , William Beresford , Angus Tuthic , John Reckter , John Evans , John Hooper , John Fisher , Benjamin Roberts , John Lawrence , John Sarbitt , William Dodd and Nehemiah Spicer good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Ironmonger came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Sarah Ironmonger an Infant of the Age of four Years, on the Tenth day of March in the Year aforesaid, being alone in the Lodging Room or Apartment of Thomas Ironmonger her Father in the Dwelling House of Mary Ingwood situate in Whitehart Yard in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the Linnen Gown which the said Sarah Ironmonger then and there had on her Body Accidentally Causally and by Misfortune took Fire, by Reason whereof, and by the Smoke and Flame arising from the said Fire, the said Sarah Ironmonger was then and there Burnt in and upon her Belly Beast Sides Neck and Face of which said Burning and Suffocation she the said Sarah Ironmonger languished about three Hours, and thereof then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Sarah Ironmonger in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Causally and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said John Boyton Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Boyton Foreman
