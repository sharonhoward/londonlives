City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourth day of November in the twenty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Keates an Infant than and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Gladhom  Richard Ward  William Newman Thomas White  Thomas Chapman  William Chriton , William Ashbey  James White  George Webb , Alexander Swindley , David Pinder and Alexander Grant good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said William Keates the Infant on the Twenty ninth day of October in the Year aforesaid being at Play in Oxford Buildings in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the Cellar Door belonging to the Dwelling House of Jane Baynes in the said Buildings being open, It so happended that the said William Keates then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down into the said Cellar, and thereby received a Bruise on the right side of the Head and a Fracture in and upon the Collar Bone of him the said William Keates , of which said Bruise and Fracture he the said William Keates from the said Twenty ninth day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Thirty first day of the same Month at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and live, on which said Thirty first day of October, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said William Keates of the Bruises and Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Keates in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Gladham  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner Wm Foreman
