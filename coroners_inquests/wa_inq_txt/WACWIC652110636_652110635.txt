City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit, An Inquisition Indented, taker for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Seventh day of December in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Urin then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Holland , Thomas Harwood , James Cook , Richard Broadbridge , Edward Foster , William Powell , Henry Haszell , Richard Williams , William Liddall , Henry Clements , John Cann , Richard Greenwood , Thomas Coward and John Johnson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Urin came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That said William Urin, having for a long time labored under a grievous Disease of Body, to Wit, the Venerial Disease; on the Twenty sixth day of December in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, departed this Life by the Violence of the said Disease, and through want of the Necessaries of Life. and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Holland Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Tho Holland Foreman
