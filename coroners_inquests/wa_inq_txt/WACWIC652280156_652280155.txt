City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fifteenth day of March in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Wallis . then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph White , William Helsea , Sabarton Juby Matthew Miller , John Ward , William Patterson , Richard Hensly ; Robert Patton , Richard Taylor , George Rimick William Wickin , Edward Carver , John Shinton and Thomas Black good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Wallis came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Elizabeth Wallis an Infant of the Age of two Years and upwards on the Eleventh day of March in the Year aforesaid being left by her Mother with two other Young Children in the Lodging Room of John Wallis her Father in the Dwelling House of David Mossect situate in Crown Court in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid and the said Elizabeth Wallis the Infant going near to the Fire in the said Room It so happened that the Linen Pinning Cloth which the said Infant then and there had on her Body Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took fire and that the said Elizabeth Wallis by the said Fire and the Flames issuing therefrom was then and these very much burnt in and upon her left side Arm and Fall, of which said burning she the said Elizabeth Wallis at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Eleventh day of March in the Year aforesaid untill the Thirteenth day of the same Month and year did languish and live on which said Thirteenth day of March at the said Parish Liberty and County she the said Elizabeth Wallis by reason of the burning aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say the said Elizabeth Wallis Accidentally casually and by misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph white Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day year and place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jerh White Foreman
