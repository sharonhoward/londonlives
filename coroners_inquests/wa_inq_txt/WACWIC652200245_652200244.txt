City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Ann within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Second day of June in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Chapman then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Francis Younge , William Branwell , John Wright , William Hope , William Waters , Wells Hilles , George Bowes , Robert Hill Edward Osborn , John Valli , Richard Rowney , and Thomas Hughes good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Chapman came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Chapman on the Twenty first day of June in the Year aforesaid being alone in his Apartment on the Second Floor, in the Dwelling House of George Garkar situate in Greek Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there standing at one of the Windows, in the said Room, (the Sash being up) and being very weak and infirm, It so happened that the said John Chapman Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell out of and through the said Window unto and upon the the Stone Pavement in the said Street, and thereby then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon his Right Thigh, and Mortal Bruises in his Body, of which said mortal Fracture and Bruises he the said John Champman then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Chapman Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in, Manner and by the means aforesaid came to his death. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Francis Younge a Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the read of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Frans Younge Foreman
