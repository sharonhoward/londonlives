City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the tenth day of March in the thirty second year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France,and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith,and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Elliot then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Samuel Strong , William Towse . Samuel Price John Chiseley, Joseph Maylor, Richard Miles , William Rowe John Bodden, John smith , Thomas Dawson , Benjamin Howlett and Brilard Bradley good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Elliot came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Ninth day of Mant in the Year aforesaid the said James Elliot was dresing a certain Cart loaded with Ashes and drawn by two Horses in the Public Highway near Tyburn Turnpike in the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid It so happened that the said James Elliot slipped in his Feet & fell down upon the Ground in the said public Highway and that the near wheel of the said Cart then and then Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed near the Breast of the said James Elliot by Means whereof the said James Elliot received a mortal Bruise and Fracture in and upon his Breast of which said mortal Bruise and Fracture he the said James Elliot then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said James Elliot in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Samuel Strong  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

J Strong Foreman
