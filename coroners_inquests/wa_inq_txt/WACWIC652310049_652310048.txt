City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Knightsbridge in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of February in the Thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Widdooson then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John Glover , Thomas Heath , James Elwell , Samuel Rudd James Brown , Daniel Smith , William Wesby , Benjamin Perrin Thomas Sharland , John Marsden , Benjamin Thompson and Jonathan Pointer good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Widdooson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty fourth day of February in the Year aforesaid the said Joseph Widdooson being in the Stable of George Grimwood Stable Keeper at Knightsbridge in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and going into the Hall there, wherein a certain Horse or Gilding was standing It so happened that the said Horse kicked with his hinder Foot, and that the said Joseph Widdooson then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received a Bruise in and upon the lower part of his Belly, of which said Bruise he the said Joseph Widdooson from the said Twenty fouth day of February in the Year aforesaid, until the Twenty fifth day of the same Month at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live; on which said Twenty fifth day of February in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Joseph Widdooson of the mortal Bruise aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Joseph Widdooson in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Glover  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Glover Foreman
