An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Bye sign of the Olivier Mount in Mount Row  Mount St . Parish of St. George Hanr . within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the  twelfth day of October 1793 in the thirty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Lane then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the Several Jurors where Names one hereunder written and Seals affixed good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty,duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Lane came to his Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Thomas Lane on the seventh day of certain Stable belonging to Mr. Hall in Down Street piccadilly there being It so happened that accidentally Casually and by Misfortune a certain light colouned bay me did put unto him the said Thomas Lane a violent Hick on his Belly by Means whereof he the said Thomas Lane did then and science ( in consequence of the said Hick ) receive a Mortal on his Belly of which said Mortal wound be the said Thomas lane did languish, the Consulling did live untill the tenth day of October in the same Year on which said tenth day of October he the said Thomas Lane of the Mortal Wound aforesaid so given as as aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Lane in manner & by the Means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise that the said Bay more was the cause of the Death and is of the value of one Shilling In Witnesses whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors been to this Inquisition set their hands & Seals the Day Year and Place first above written
