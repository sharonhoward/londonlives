Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St. James in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Nineteenth day of October in the Frist Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third by the Grace of God Great Britain, France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so Forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid Upon a View of the Body of William Wood then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of John Clough , George Holmes , Timothy Mills , John Davis , Thomas Faulkner , Roger Shakespear , William Sinawley , Richard Mangald , William Murphey Jeremiah Green , Charles Fleece , George Lunden , William Ball , Charles Long , Germain Wright , William Windsor and James Ross Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said William Wood came to his Death say upon their Oaths. That The said William Wood not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but Lunatick and Distracted on the Fourteenth day of October in the Year aforesaid at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid one end of a certain peice of Cord unto a Latticed Partition in a Cellar or back place adjoining to the Kitchen of the Work house of the said Parish situate and being in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other and thereof about his own Neck did fix tye fasten, and therewith did then and there hang Suffocate and Strangle himself, of which Lord Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said William Wood then there instantly Died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said William Wood not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did Kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Jno. Feary Coroner .

Jno. Clough Foreman
