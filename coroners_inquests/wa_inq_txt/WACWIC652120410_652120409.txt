City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of June in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Sharp then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Smith , James Beyer , Edward Field , Moses Philips , David Bynon , John Potter , Joseph Clarkson , John Glasbey , James Watson , Joseph Hardey , David Keill , Basset Lee , Thomas Trobey , Roger Mead , John Birchall , David Murray , George Seaton Samuel Cooke , and Henry Hyens good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Sharp , came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Sharp , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Fifteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, with a certain Knife made of Iron and Steel which she the said Elizabeth Sharp then and there had and held in her Right Hand the Throat or Gullett of her the said Elizabeth Sharp did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto herself with the Knife aforesaid, in and upon the Throat of her the said Elizabeth Sharp , one mortal Wound of the Length of two Inches and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound she the said Elizabeth Sharp then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Elizabeth Sharp , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Smith Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Smith Foreman
