City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of middlesex , the Forth day of April in the Eight Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Hilton then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Hudson , William Williams , Peter Skym , Charles Power , Thomas Bailey , Peter Horam , William Buck , Daniel Bolton , David Fryer , Edward Shea , Thomas Holmes , Thomas Moy , john Rason , Samuel Tottey , George Strutton , and Edward Grest good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the king, when, how, and by what Means the said James Hilton came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Hilton , not being of sound Mind, Memory, and Under Standing, but lunatick and distracted on the Second day of April in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and county aforesaid, one End of a Certain Piece of small Cord unto a large Nail or Spike fastened into a piece of Timber called the Lintle, over a Window in the Lodging Room or Apartment of him the said James Hilton in the dwelling house of John Carpue situate in Chare Court in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck, did fix, tye, and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang, suffocate, and strangle himself, of which said Hanging, Suffocation and strangling he the said James Hilton then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Hilton not being of sand Mind, Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted in Maner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Hudson foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the day Year and Place first above written.

Thos. Prickard Coroner .

Thos Hudson Foreman
