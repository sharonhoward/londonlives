City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty fourth day of December in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain,France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman,Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Berry then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Rose , Jervais Wells , William Brown , Thomas Weeks , Joseph Emmott , Robert Chipehase , Charles Stuart Thomas Giffiths , William Anderson , Henry Hart , George White , Archibald Simpson , Thomas Evans , William Dennison Robert Stuart and James Walker good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Berry came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Mary Berry not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted on the said twenty fourth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one and of a certain Price of blue Ribbon unto an Iron Curtain rod, at the side of the Bed in the Lodging Room or Apartment, of her the said Mary Berry in the Dwelling House of John Brazier , Situate and being in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other end thereof about her own neck disfix the and faston, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and Strangle herself, of which said hanging suffocation and strangling, she the said Mary, Berry then and there instanlty died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid did say, that the said Mary Berry not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted, in manner and by the means aforesaid did hill hereself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Ross Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Thos Prickard  Coroner

Robert Ross Foreman
