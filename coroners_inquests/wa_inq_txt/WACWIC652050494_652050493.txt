City and Liberty of Westminster 's in the Country of Middlesex } to wit

An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Paul Covent Garden , within the Liberty of the Dean Chapter. of the Collegiate Church of St.Peter Westminster in the Country of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of September, in the Fifth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the  by the Grace of god of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Saith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On view of the Body of a new born Male Child then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Setree , John Craven , Edward Palmer , Thomas Nicholls  Robert Ellis , Samuel Gardiner , Peter Howe , Robert Symmonds , Robert Whitworth , Joseph Harris , Richard Oldfield , John Cooke , Thomas Young , William Wells , and Samuel Griffiths , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, he and by what Means the said new born Male Child-came to his death, do upon their Oath say That on the Fourteenth day of September in the year aforesaid a certain Woman Unknown did bring forth the said male Child alive, and that on the same Day and Year a certain Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown with Force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty aforesaid in the Country aforesaid, in and upon the said Male infant Child, in the Peace of God and our said Lord the King then and there being Feloniously wilfully and of his her or their Malice forethough did make an Assault, And that the said Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid unknown did then and there Feloniously Wilfully and of his her or their Malice forethought Choak and Strangle the said Male Child, of which said Choaking and Stranging the said Male Child then and there instantly died. And that the said Person or- Persons unknown of his her or their Malice forethought, the Male Child aforesaid. in manner and form aforesaid did then and there Hill and Murder, Against the Peace of our said Lord the King his brown and Dignity. In Witness whereof as well as the said Coroner as the said Thomas Setree foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Richard  Coroner Tho. Setree  Foreman
