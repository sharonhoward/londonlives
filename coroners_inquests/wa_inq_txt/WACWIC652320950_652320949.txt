An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Dwelling House of Nathen Silcock in Bird Street Brook St at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square known by the Sign of the three Compasses within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirty first day of December 1792 in the thirty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq.  Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Katherine Ryley then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John Faster , Peter Stian  Richard Pullinger , Clement Watts , Samuel Fry , James Wright , William Varnell Alexander Robinson , John Wright , Adam Richie Thomas Rowe , John Dorset , John Stone and Thomas Wilford good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Katherine Ryley came to h er Death, do upon their oath say that the said Katherine Ryley on the twenty eighth day of December, in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit at the Dwelling House of Elizabeth Martin in Bird Street aforesaid being greatly intoxicated with Liquor, it so happened that accidentally casually and by Misfortune she the said Katherine Ryle fell into the Five by means whereof she the said Katheirne Ryle was then and there suffocated Of which said Suffocation she the said Katherine Ryley then and there instantly died: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Katharine Ryley in manner and by the Means aforesaid, accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their hands & Seals the Day Year & Place above-written

Anthy Gell  Coroner  Foreman

John Wright Dent Richard Dwellingan Samull Robinson William Varnell John Hone
