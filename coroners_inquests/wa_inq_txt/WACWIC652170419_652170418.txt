City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at Tothill Fields Bridewell in the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty seventh day of August in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of George Hancock a Prisoner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Wood , Godfrey Gent , Thomas Mitchell , John Grove , Henry Smith , James Abbott , John Sedgrove , James Pratt , Oswald Edwards , Thomas Pearce James Caffrey and Job Burch good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said George Hancock came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said George Hancock being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields Bridewell aforesaid in the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the said Twenty seventh day of August in the Year aforesaid at Tothill Fields Bridewell aforesaid, departed this life by the Visitation of God, in a natural Way, to Wit, of a Fever, and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Wood Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Wm Wood Foreman
