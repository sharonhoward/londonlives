City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex , to wit} 

An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. John Evangelist in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twenty fourth day of September in the Fifth year, of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so Forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty,.on view of the Body of Elizabeth Bryley the Wife of Benjamin Bryley ,then and there lying Dead,upon the Oath of Morris Richards , John Taylor , Silas Payne , Samuel Hyley , John Knowles , John Austin William Ellis , Peter Dunn , George King , Samuel Morrison , Thomas Hook and Francis Norris good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what means the said Elizabeth Bryley came to her Death. do upon their Oath say That the said Elizabeth Bryley on the Twenty third day of September in the Year aforesaid being in her Lodging Room or Apartment in the Dwelling House and then and there going to sit down on the side of a Wooden Bedstew in the said Room, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the said Bedstead parted asunder, (being unscrew'd) and that the said Elizabeth Bryley then and there fell to and against the Floor upon he Backside with greatforce, wherebysome of the Blood Vessels within the said Elizabeth Bryley were Ruptured and broken, By Reason whereof, and by the great less and effusion of Blood thereby occasioned, she the said Elizabeth Bryley then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Elizabeth Bryley in Manner and by the Means aforesaid .Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Morris Richards Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the next of his said Hellows, in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prichard Coroner. Morris Richard Foreman
