City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .}  to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Westminster Hospital in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty fifth day of August in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Tottan then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Sutherland , Thomas Wilkinson , John Griffiths Richard Bradley , John Hayter , Richard Carwardine , Samuel Casley Thomas Weatherhall , John Machin , Henry Davarall , Humphrey Chadwick and Francis Tighe good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said John Tottan came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the twenty third day of August in the year aforesaid, the said John Tottan a Bricklayer was pointing the Front of a certain House situate in the strand in the Liberty of the Savoy in the County aforesaid and standing upon a Scaffold at the Height of four stories It so happened that the said John Tottan accidentally casually and by misfortune fell off and from the said Scaffold unto the Ground by means whereof he the said John Tottan did then and there receive violent Fractures in and upon the ribs on his left side and upon his left Arm and mortal bruises in and upon his body of which said mortal Fractures and Bruises he the said John Tottan on the said twenty third day of August at the said Parish of saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid for the space of about four Hours did Languish and live and on the same day at the Parish last mentioned he the said John Tottan of the mortal Fractures and Bruises aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Tottan in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said John Sutherland , Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day apear and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jno. Sutherland Foreman
