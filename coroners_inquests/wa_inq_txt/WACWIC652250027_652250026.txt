City of Liberty of Westminster in the County of middlesex } to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty first day of February in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Morris an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Potier , Thomas Knight , Samuel Scott , John Doldson , Thomas Glossop , John Sell , Robert Lowe , William Cook , Jacob Welsh , James Delagrange , Thomas Crown  Abraham Reynolds , Peter Granor and William Farmer good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Morris came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Mary Morris an Infant of the Age of four Years on the nineteenth day of February in the Year aforesaid being alone in the Lodging -room or Apartment of Mary Morris her Mother in the Dwelling House of Elizabeth Barrat in Greek Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid It so happened that the Linen Cloths which the said Mary Morris then and there had on her Body Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took Fire, whereby the said Mary Morris the Infant was Burned in and upon her Arms Neck and Sides, of which she languished until the Twentieth day of February in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there of the Burning aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Mary Morris in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Potier Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho Prickard Coroner

Wm. Potier Foreman
