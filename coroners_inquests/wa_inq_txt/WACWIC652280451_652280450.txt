City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the second day of August in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Grant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Walker , Thomas Mose , Alexande Henderson John Worrall , Michael Dussen , John Matthewman , Robert Haynes , William Taylor , William Earn , Thomas Grubbham George Linnell and Richard Barton good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Grant came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said James Grant on the thirtieth day of June in the Year aforesaid, went to see a certain Porcupine Bear and other Beasts shewn by a Man whose name is unknown, in a certain Room situate in Nags Head Yard in the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the said James Grant being in Liquor, went near unto the place where the said Porcupine Boar was chained, whereupon the said Porcupine Board did then and there bite the left leg of him the said James Grant , by means whereof the said James Grant then and there Received a very large and Mortal Wound in and upon the said left leg, of which said Mortal Wound he the said James Grant from the said thirteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid, until the first day of August in the same Year, at the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said first day of August in the Year aforesaid, at the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said James Grant of the mortal Wound aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said James Grant in manner and by the means aforesaid, came to his Death and not otherwise. And that the said Porcupine Boar by the bite aforesaid caused the Death of the said James Grant , and is the Property and in the Possession of a Person or Person to the Jurors aforesaid unknown.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said John Walker Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their Presence have to this Inquisition, set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prichard Coroner

Jno. Walker Foreman
