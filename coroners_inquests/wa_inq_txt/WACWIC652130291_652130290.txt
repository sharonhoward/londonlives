City of Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty First day of June in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James West then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Hales Richard Robinson , John Valler , Samuel Hazelton , John Patison , Abraham Wilkinson , Thomas Bunce , Job Carey , William Olvill , Robert Bowden , John Hadden , John Scott , and James Mathews good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James West came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James West an Infant on the Twelfth day of June in the Year aforesaid being at play in a Room on the First Floor in a new built House situate in the Parish of Bromley in the County of Kent , and part of the Floor in the said Room not having been laid, it so happened that the said James West then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell through and between the Joists in the said Room, unto and upon the Floor of a certain Room on the Ground Floor, and thereby did then and there receive one Mortal Fracture in and upon the Right side of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said James West at the said Parish of Bromley , and also at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the said County aforesaid, for the said Twelfth day of June in the Year aforesaid until the Twentieth day of the same Month, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twentieth day of June aforesaid, he the said James West at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, of the Mortal Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James West in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Hales Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard  Coroner

John Hales Foreman
