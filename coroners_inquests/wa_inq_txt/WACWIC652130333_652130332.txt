An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of July in the Thirteent Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Parsons then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Smeaton , John Simmonds , Hugh Thomas , John Hunt , William Ask , John Langstaff John Shaw , John Crece , Jacob Saywell , Richard Clayton , James Anderson  Christopher Smyth and Thomas Sanger good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the king, when, how, and by what Means the said William Parsons came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Parsons , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the First day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto the top of the Bedpost at the foot of the Bed in the Lodging room of him the said William Parsons , in his Dwelling House, situate and being in Queen Street in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten,and there with did then and there Hang Suffocate and strangle himself,of which said hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said William Parsons then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said william Parsons not being of Sound Mind Memory and understanding, but limatick and distracted, in manner and by the Means aforesaid did hill himself, In Witness where of as well the said Coroner,as the said James Smeaton Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and rest of his Fellows, in the their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

Jas. Smeaton Foreman
