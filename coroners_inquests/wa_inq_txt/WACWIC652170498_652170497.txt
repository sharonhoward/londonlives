City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of October in the Seventeenth Year of the Regin of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Murphy then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Garth , John List , John Cramme , William Long , John Dent , Benjamin Pool , Samuel Lovell , John Hawkins , William Clarke , Giles Mathews Thomas Yardington , Samuel Baker , Samuel Gibbard , Stephen Stephens and Thomas Hawkins good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Murphy came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said Thomas Murphy a Laborer on the fifteenth day of October in the Year aforesaid being at Work in the House of William Clarke situate in Portugal- -Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and then and there Standing upon a Ladder in the Necessary House or Privy belonging to the said House, It so happened that the said Thomas Murphy then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fellow and from the said Ladder into the Soil in the said Necessary House, and therein was then and there Suffocated and Strangled, of which said Suffocation and Strangling he the said Thomas Murphy then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Murphy in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise: In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Garth Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jas. Garth Foreman
