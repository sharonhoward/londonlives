City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit.  An Inquisition indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. James in the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster, in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fifth day of March in the Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard His Majesty's Coroner for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Mason then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of George Asten , Joseph Hodgson , Edward Dawson , Thomas Jones  John Freeman , Charles Partridge , Robert Martindale , Leonard Wilmot  John Drew , Thomas Brown , James Delaparel , David Mc. Cullock , Robert Timbrell , Martin Sourbeer , Joseph Holmes , John Climpson Thomas Cago & John Bern good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, and who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by what means the said Sarah Mason came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Sarah Mason not being of sound mind memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Twenty fourth day of March in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid and in the Liberty aforesaid, one End of her Garters unto a Gimlet fasten'd into the Wainscot in a Corner of the Room or Bedchamber of the said Sarah Mason , in her usual dwelling House, situate in the Parish and Liberty aforesaid, and the other End thereof about her own Neck did fix, tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle herself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling she the said Sarah Mason then and there died; And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Sarah Mason not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said George Austen Foreman, on the behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

George Austin Foreman
