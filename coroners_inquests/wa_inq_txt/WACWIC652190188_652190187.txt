City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Kensington Gore in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of May in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Sears then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Benjamin Baily , Charles Ward , Thomas Grainger , John Reed , Joseph Cox , James Lake , William Mc. Cleish , John Doney , William Vincent , John Dobinson Richard Dobinson and Thomas Reder good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Sears came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Sears on the Sixth day of May in the Year aforesaid, being in the Stable of John McPherson Esquire situate at Kensington Gore in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and the said Thomas Sears then and there standing behind a certain Bay Horse, and with a Stick striking the said Horse It so happened that the said Horse with one of his hinder feet did then and there kick the said Thomas Sears in and upon his Belly, whereby the said Thomas Sears did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Bruise in and upon his Belly and of which said mortal Bruise from the said Sixth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Seventh day of the same Month and Year, at the and languishing did live, on which said Seventh day of May at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Thomas Sears of the mortal Bruise aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Sears in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Benjamin Baily Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Benr. Baily Foreman
