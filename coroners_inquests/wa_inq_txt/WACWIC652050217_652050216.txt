City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chaple of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Sixteenth day of April in the Fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and So forth before Thomas Prichard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Elizabeth Beech then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Cartwright , Charles Taylor , John Derippy , Matthias Daws , Isaac Joseph , Thomas Hacknall , William Arnold , Edward Francis , Richard Morrell , William Hiscocks , John Roff , John Burgiss , John Nutting , Henry Norris and William Spinney , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King, when how and by what Means the said Elizabeth Beech came to her Death, doupon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Beech on the Eighteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid being in Westminster Hall in the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid It so happened that the said Elizabeth Beech then and there Accidentally, Casually, and by Misfortune did fall down unto the Ground, over the Timber then used for Execting a Seafold in the said Hall, whereby the said Elizabeth Beech did then and receive a mortal Fracture in and upon her Left Leg, of which said mortal she the said Elizabeth Beech from the said Eighteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid, until the thirteenth day of April in the same Year, at the Westminster Hospital , within the Parish City Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish, and languishing did live, on which said Thirteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid, in the Hospital aforesaid, she the said Elizabeth Beech of the said Mortal Fracture did Die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Elizabeth Beech in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally, Casually, and by misfortune came to her Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Cartwright Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Jn Cartwright Forman
