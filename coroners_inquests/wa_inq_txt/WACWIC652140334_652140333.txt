City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  In Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of July in the Fourteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Andrews then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Wall , John Merithorp , Jonathan Jackson , Joseph Elebeck , John Covill Adam Dunford , Walter Bannister , John Nalder , John Lees , Joseph Fisher Nicholas Bull , Matthew Wall , George Beard , John Hudson , Paul Carr Thomas Osborn and Frederick Westerline good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Andrews came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Andrews a Laborer on the said Twenty sixth day of July in the Year aforesaid being at Work upon the House of Sir George Saville  Baronet situate in Leicester Square in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being then and there standing, with another Laborer, upon a Scaffold on the outside of the said House, at the Height of three Stories, It so happened that the said Thomas Andrews , in throwing down a large piece of Old Lead, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said Scaffold upon the Iron Rails in the Front of the said House, by Means whereof the said Thomas Andrews then and there rec ed two Wounds in and through his Body, of which said mortal Wounds the said Thomas Andrews then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Andrews in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Wall Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner John Wall Foreman
