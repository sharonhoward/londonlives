An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of May in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Peter Philip Le Jeune then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Paul Pinard , Robert Desbrow , Daniel Payan , Lewis Alexander Armaud, John Young , Robert Dennison , James Wever , William Heming Stephen Artend , John Jacobs , Augustus Jacobs and John Jacobs The Younger, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said Peter Philips Le Jeune came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Peter Philip Le Jeune laboring under a grievers Disease of Body, to Wit, a Fever, and by reason of the violence of the said disease, being Delirious and out of his Mind, on the Tenth day of May in the year aforesaid with a certain PenKnife which he the said Peter Philip Le Jeune had and held in his right Hand at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, the Throat or Gullet of him the said Peter Philip Le Jeune did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Penknife aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Peter Philip Le Jeune on e mortal Wound of the Lenth of three Inches and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound he the said Peter Philip Le Jeune then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon the Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Peter Philip Le Jeune, by reason of the violence of the Disease aforesaid being Delirious and out of his Mind, in Manner and by the means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well said Coroner as the said Paul Pinard Foreman of the said Jurors, on the Behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Pickard Coroner

Paul Pinard Foreman
