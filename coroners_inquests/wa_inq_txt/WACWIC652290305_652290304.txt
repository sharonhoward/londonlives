City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty second day of May in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Ann Stokes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Redden , James Elwell , Benjamin Thompson Griffith Harris , William Cade , John Townley , Stephen Timmonds John Coomes , Richard Richards , Joseph Mathews , George Pusser and Thomas Lomaon good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Ann Stokes came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty first day of May in the Year aforesaid the said Mary Ann Stokes an Infant of the Age of Seventeen Months being crossing the Publick Road at Knightsbridge in the said parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid and that John Cookey a Carter was then driving a certain Cart drawn by three Horses and loaded with Dung in the said Publick Road It so happened that the said Mary Ann Stokes the Infant fell down and that the off Wheel of the said Cart then and there passed over the Head of the said Mary Ann Stokes by Means whereof the said Mary Ann Stokes then and there Accidentally Casually and by misfortune did receive a mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of her Head of which said Mortal Fracture she the said Mary Ann Stokes then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Mary Ann Stokes in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Redden Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellow in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

James Redden Foreman
