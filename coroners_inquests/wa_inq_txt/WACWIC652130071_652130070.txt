City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of February in the Thirteenth Year of the Regin of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Walsh then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Baker  Thomas Harwood , James Mc. Cartney , John Plant  Cuthbert Smith  Henry Robert  James Chamberlain  Jacob Nelsthorp  John Street , William Winterton William Hughes and George Liddall good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Walsh came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Walsh a Laborer, on the Eighth day of February in the Year aforesaid being at Work at the Water Works by the side of the River Thomas in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there going upon and across the Floodgates, It so happened that the said John Walsh Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Floodgates into the River there, and in the Waters thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said John Walsh then and there died. and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Walsh in Manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death an not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Baker Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above writen

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Baker Foreman
