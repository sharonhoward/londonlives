City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex }to wit.  

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of May in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mark Aldersey then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Gideon Dare , John Showrd , John Reed , George Drew , Edward Birch , James Bright , Richard Driver , William Roathwell , William Millet , Adam Brown Robert Thatcher , Thomas Straight , Charles Rogers , Cossley Keene and Edward Nelson Good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, when, being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mark Aldersey came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Mark Aldersey not being of sound Mind Memory and Unerstanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Tenth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid a great Quantity of Sublimate being a deadly Poison, into a certain Quantity of hot Water, did then and there put and mix, and the said Sublimate so put and mixt as aforesaid, he the said Mark Aldersey not being of sound mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted as aforesaid, did then and there take drink and swallow down, by Means whereof he the said Mark Aldersey becme then and there sick and distempered in his Body, and of the Poison aforesaid, and of the Sickness and Distemper thereby occasioned, from the said Tenth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the said fifteenth day of the Same month at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said Fifteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish an in the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Mark Aldersey of the Poison aforesaid, and of the Sickness and Distemper occasioned thereby, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Mark Aldersey, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did Poison and kill himself. In Witness where of as Well the said Coroner as the said Gidcon Dare Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Gideon Dare Foreman
