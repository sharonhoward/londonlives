City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of July in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Baldwin then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Hill , Richard Coomes , James Young , Francis Henderson , Henry Vincent , John Angle , John Philpot , Isaac Wright , William Mallet , Robert Smith , George Topman , David Evans , John Rickarby , Thomas Westley William Lanton and John Hunter good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Baldwin came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Seventh day of July in the Year aforesaid about the Hour of Eleven in the night, William Battle was driving a certain Hackney Coach in Swallow Street in the said Parish of Saint James within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being come off his Box and loading the Horses into the Stable Yard called Sadlers Yard in the said Street, It so happened that the Pole of the said Coach then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, struck against the Stomach of the said James Baldwin by means whereof the said James Baldwin then and there received a mortal Bruise and Contusion in and upon his Stomach, of which said mortal Bruise and Contusion he the said James Baldwin then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Baldwin in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Hill Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

James Hill Foreman
