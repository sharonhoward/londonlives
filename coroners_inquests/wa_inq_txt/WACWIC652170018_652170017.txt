City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of January in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Francis Haines then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Ashmore , Richard Porter , Joseph Greening  Joseph Parks , John Gosling Duncan Mc. Intosh , John Heckman , Richard Ralph , John DeJersey , Hugh Kennedy , John Andrews & James Abbott good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said Francis Haines came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said Francis Haines on the First day of January in the Year aforesaid being Sliding upon the Conal in St. James's Park in the Parish and County aforesaid, It so happened that the Ice broke, and that the said Francis Haines then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell into the said Conal and in the Waters thereof was Suffocated & Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Francis Haines said the same day of the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon the aforesaid do say, that the said, Francis Haines in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Ashmore Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner Jos: Ashmore Foreman
