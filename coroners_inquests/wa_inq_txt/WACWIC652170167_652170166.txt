City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of April in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Newnham then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Christopher Lewis  Robert Thompson , William Ovens , John Legrix , William Thorp , John Lemater , Isaac Lewis , James Horsenop , Thomas Perkins , James Baker , John Courteer , Isaac Moor , James Drummond and John Gummont good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Newnham came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Newnham, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Third day of April in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Dwelling House of Michael Bedell there situate, with a certain Penknife made of Iron and Steel which he the said James Newnham then and there had and held in his Right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said James Newnham did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself the said James Newnham with the Penknife aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said James Newnham one mortal Wound of the Length of three Inches and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound he the said James Newnham their and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Newnham, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Christopher Lewis Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Christr. Lewis Foreman
