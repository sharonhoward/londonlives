City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Pairsh of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the thirteenth day of June, in the Fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Barlow then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Shyrm , Thomas Swardill , Thomas Hill , Francis Jones , James Boyle , William Grimbalstone , David Morris , Henry Paine , John Freeman Thomas Buck , William Adcock , Edward Collins , Alexander Donaldson , John Morgan and Charles Clarke good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the the King, when, how and by what Means the said Robert Barlow came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Robert Barlow , not being of sound mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted , on the Eleventh day of June in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain piece of small Cord unto the Iron Banisters upon the Staircase in the Dwelling House of Sir Charles Hardy Knight, situate and being in Argyle Buildings in the said Parish of Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck, did fix, tye, and fasten, and there with did then and there hang, suffocate and strangle himself, of which said Hanging, Suffocation, and Strangling, he the said Robert Barlow then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Robert Barlow , not being of sound Mind, Memory and Understanding, but Lunatick and Distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Skyrm , Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner .

John Skyrm Foreman
