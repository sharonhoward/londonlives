City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex To wit. }  In Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Gatehouse Prison, in the Parish of St. Margaret in the City and Liberty of Westmr in the County of Middlesex the Twenty fourth Day of March in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & liberty on View of the Body of James Smith a Prisoner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Jackson , George Newton , William Clissold , Leonard Clough , James Don , John Moorley , Nathaniel Clayton , Samuel Vaughan , Samuel Kemp , William Clay , George Davies , Thomas Walker and James Howarth good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said James Smith came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Smith being a Prisoner in the Gatehouse Prison aforesaid, in the Parish aforesaid, within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Twenty third day of March in the year aforesaid, at the Gate house Prison aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God, in a natural Way, to Wit, of a Fever, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Samuel Jackson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Samuel Jackson foreman
