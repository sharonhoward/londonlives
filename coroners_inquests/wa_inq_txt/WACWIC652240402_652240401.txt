City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourteenth day of July in the twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Bricknell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Daniel Bunning , William Sibley , John Baker , John Sherwod , Francis Langford , John Watson , Thomas Clouch , William Wall , Richard Noyes , James Andrews , Charles Harris , William Smith , William Carter  Edward Leeth , William Gould and Alexander Heath good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Bricknell came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That the said William Bricknell on the Seventh day of July in the Year aforesaid being upon a Load of Straw in Curzon Street at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there removing the Trusses at the top of the said Load, It so happened that the said William Bricknell Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said Load of Straw unto the Ground, and thereby did then and there receive one mortal Bruise and Fracture in and upon the Rightside of his Head, of which said mortal Bruise and Fracture he the said William Bricknell at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Seventh day of July in the Year aforesaid until the Twelfth day of the same Month did languish and live, on which said Twelfth day of July aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said William Bricknell of the mortal Bruise and Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Bricknell in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Daniel Bunning  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho Prickard Coroner

Danl: Bunning Foreman
