City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of December in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Rogers then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Graham , David Radley , William Cregg , John Evans , George Hill , John Verwick , William Pasley , Thomas Berridge , Robert Thompson , John Polman , William Douglas , James Russell , William Sheppard , Joseph Collins , John Shutter and James Powell good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Rogers came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Rogers on the Twentieth day of December in the Year aforesaid was found dead in a certain Empty Hayloft in Dawson's Alley in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, That no Marks of Violence appeared on the Body, and that the said Thomas Rogers died in a natural Way, and not by any Hurt or Injury from any Person whatever, to the Knowledge of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William G ham Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner.. Wilm Graham Foreman
