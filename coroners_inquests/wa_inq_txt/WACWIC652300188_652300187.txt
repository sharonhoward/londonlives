City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fifth day of March in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Shemill then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Benjamin Boyden , Robert Hardy , John James , Samuel Kershaw Thomas Phillips , Evan Bingham , Michael Saint Paul  Thomas Sharp William Haswell  Alexander Wellington , Samuel Suthenwood , Philip Burkinpung , William Smart  William Ford , George Thomson , George Barelays Francis Drake and Samuel Knowles good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Shemill came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty fourth day of March in the Year aforesaid a Man whose Name is unknown was carefully driving a certain loaded Waggon in a certain Street called Princes Street in the said Parish of Saint James within the Liberty and County aforesaid and that the said Elizabeth Shemill was then walking along the Footway in the said Street and being Intoxicated with Liquor It so happened that the said Elizabeth Shemill fell down from the said footway, upon the Stone Pavement in the Coach way in the said Street before the near hinder Wheel of the said Waggon and that the said hinder Wheel of the said Elizabeth Shemill so lying upon the Ground as aforesaid by means whereof the said Elizabeth Shemill did ten and there Accidentally Casually and by the misfortune receive one Mortal Fracture in and Upon the left side and back part of the Head of her the said Elizabeth Shemill of which the said Mortal Fracture she the said Elizabeth Shemill then and there Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Elizabeth Shemil in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Benjamin Boydon  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Benjamin Boydon Foreman
