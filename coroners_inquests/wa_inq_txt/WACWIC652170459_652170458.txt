City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighteenth day of September in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Isaac Layton then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Mourn , Samuel Ray , John Stone , William Ellis , John Andrews Thomas Watson , David Hatchman , Robert Hooker , John Burn , William Porter  Charles Jenkins and Evan Alford good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Isaac Layton came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That a certain Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown, not having the fear of God before his or their Eyes but moved and, seduced by the Instigation of the Devil on the Sixteenth day of September in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the Parish of Kingston upon Thames , in the County of Surry in and upon the said Isaac Layton in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously wilfully and of his or their Malice forethought did Make an Assault, And that the said Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid unknown with a certain sharp Instrument made of Iron and Steel, which the said Person or Persons unknown then and there had and held in his or their Right hand him the said Isaac Layton in and upon the left Hand and also in and upon the Head of him the said Isaac Layton did then and there feloniously wilfully and of his or their Malice forethought strike and beat, And that the said Person or Persons unknow with the sharp Instrument aforesaid by the Striking and beating aforesaid did then and there give unto him the said Isaac Layton in and upon the left Hand of him the said Isaac Layton divers mortal Wounds, and two Mortal Wounds and Fractures in and upon the top of the Head of him the said Isaac Layton of which said mortal Wounds and Fractures he the said Isaac Layton in the said County of Surry and also at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid did Languish and Languishing did live, on which said Sixteenth day of September in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of Saint MArgaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Isaac Layton of the Mortal Wounds and Fractures aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Person or Persons unknown him the said Isaac Layton in Manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously wilfully and of his or their Malice forethought did Kill and Murder against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Mourn Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence pave to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard Coroner

Wm Mourn Foreman
