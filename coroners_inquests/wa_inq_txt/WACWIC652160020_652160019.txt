City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at Tothill Fields  Bridewell in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of January in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Daniel Bradley a Prisoner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Clayton Wilkinson , Thomas Hill , John Bussey , Thomas Shields  George Williamson , James Lotton, John Hinton , William York , John Cotterell , Thomas Neal , John Leyon , John Rose , William Crockett and Leopold Otto , Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty. duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Daniel Bradley came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Daniel Bradley being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Twenty second day of January in the Year aforesaid at Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid. Departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way, to wit, of a Fever, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Clayton Wilkinson Foreman of the said James on the behalf of himself at the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner Claytor William Foreman
