City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at Tothill Fields Bridewell in the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of June in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Rachel Mede a Prisoner them and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Michael Castleden , John Watkins , James Beam , James Dobel , William Williams , Thomas Axtall , William Almond , Joseph Smith , John Garleck , Leonard Clow , George Gunry , William Edgar and William Arnold , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what Means the said Rachel Mede came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Rachel Mede being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields Bridewell aforesaid in the Parish City Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Eighteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid at Tothill Fields Bridewell aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in the natural way, to Wit, of a Fever, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Michael Castleden Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of this Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho Prickard  Coroner .

Michl. Castleden Foreman
