City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirty first day of October in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner, of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Janet Michie then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Prat , Robert Taylor , William Dellingham , George Jones , Francis Gouan , Richard Harlstone  George Snowball , Alexander Knap , Benjamin Hodgson , Joseph Harboure , Mark Shotton , Joseph Farnhough , Charles Mc. Donald , Samuel Forster  William Wynn , Richard Sangwyn and William Mountain , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Janet Michie came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Janet Michie a Washerwoman on the Thirtieth day of October in the Year aforesaid, about the hour of One in the Morning, being in the dwelling House of George Forbes and Jonathan Michie Wine Merchants, situate in Duke Street in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there without any light coming down the Stairs in the said House, It so happened that the said Janet Michie Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down over the Banisters on the third pair of Stairs unto the bottom thereof, the same being a well Stair Case, by means of which said fall she the said Janet Michie did then and there receive one mortal Wound upon her Forehead, and one mortal Fracture upon the back part of her Head, of which said mortal Wound and Fracture she the said Janet Michie then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Janet Michie in manner and by the Means aforesaid; Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Prat , Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Willm. Prat Foreman
