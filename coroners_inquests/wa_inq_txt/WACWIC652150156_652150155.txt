City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Intended, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of May in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Melcher Googe then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Davis , Morgan Bowen , Thomas Hill , Joseph Niblow , John Sudgrove , George White , Hugh Kenedy , George Tyler , William Davis , William Plasket James Brown and William Ellis good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Melcher Googe came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Melcher Googe a Laborer on the Eleventh day of May in the Year aforesaid being driving a certain loaded Cart drawn by three Horses in the Adelphi Buildings in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid, That the said Melcher Googe then and there fell down, and that the Near Wheel of the said Cart Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over both the Legs of the said Melcher Googe, by Means where of the said Melcher Googe did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive divers Bruises and Fractures in and upon his Legs, of which said Bruises and Fractures he the said Melcher Googe from the said Eleventh day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Eighteenth day of the same Month and Year at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Eighteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Melcher Googe of the mortal Bruises aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Melcher Googe in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Davis Foreman of the said Jurors in their presence, have to this Inquisition set, their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Davis Foreman
