City and Liberty of the Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of September in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Baxter , an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Leonard Clow , John Draper , John Wright , Francis Wright , Thomas Whitefoot , James Mason , Stephen Gouge, Ellis Evans , Thomas Burden , John Short , William Edgar , and Allan Bourman , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Baxter came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , that Jarvis Roughton  Labourer on the Thirtieth day of August in the Year aforesaid being driving a certain Cart drawn by three Horses and loaden with Spirituous Liquors, in New Tothill Street in the Parish City Liberty and County aforesaid, and that the said James Baxter being then and there lying down on the Ground, and picking up a piece of Leather out of the Area of a Kitchen Window in the said Street, with his Legs towards the said Cart, It so happened that the Off Wheel of the said Cart then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the left Leg of the said James Baxter , whereby the said James Baxter did then and there receive a mortal Bruise and Fracture in and upon his left Leg, of which said mortal Bruise and Fracture he the said James Baxter from the said Thirtieth day of August in the Year aforesaid until the Second day of September in the same Year at the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Second day of September in the Year aforesaid he the said James Baxter at the said Parish City Liberty and County, of the mortal Bruise and Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Baxter in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Causally and by the Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. And that the Off Wheel of the said Cart was moving to the Death of the said James Baxter , and is of the Value of five Shillings, and the Property and in the Possession of Thomas Langdale and John Moothan of the Parish of St. Andrew Holborn London Distillers, or their Assigns. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Leonard Clow Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Leonard Clow Foreman
