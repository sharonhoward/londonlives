City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of October in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Robertson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Carpenter , Francis Harand , William Ward , Henry Carpenter , William Shipley , John Arnold , Henry Wayte , Robert Peck , John Willan , Charles Price , James Bick , Edward Mathews John Evans and Joseph Thackeray , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Robertson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said William Robertson , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Eighth day of October in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain piece of thick Cord about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, the other End thereof being before fastened unto a Wooden Beam in the Workshop of Sebastian Crespel situate and being in the said Parish Liberty and County, and therewith did then and there Hang Suffocate and Strangle himself of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said William Robertson then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Robertson being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner the said Richard Carpenter Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Thos. Prickard Coroner

Richard Carpenter Foreman
