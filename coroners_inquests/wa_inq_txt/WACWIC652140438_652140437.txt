City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex . } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of October in the Fourteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Reeves then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Harrison John Fountain , James Saunders , Charles Keither , George Mills , William Oare , John Taylor , Harry Lowder , Alexander Smelle , James Beamount , John Heath , and Edward Wiggins  good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Reeves came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Sarah Reeves on the Twelfth day of October in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the LIberty and County aforesaid, and being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but Lunatic and Distracted, one end of a certain piece of Ribband about the Tester Rail of her Bed in her Apartment or Dwelling in a House situate in Wood Street , and the other and thereof about her own Neck did fix tye and fasten and therewith did then and there Hang Suffocate and Strangle herself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling she the said Sarah Reeves then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Sarah Reeves not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did Kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Corner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner Henry Harrison foreman
