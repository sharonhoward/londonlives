City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twenty fourth day of April in the sixteenth Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of Roger Martin then and there lying dead by the Oath of John Lassell  John Morris  William Green , James James , William Branston , John Barber  John Groves  John Terry  Benjamin Moor , Peter Farquharson  Richard Jones Benjamin Baxter and Charles Wright good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, who how, and by what Means the said Roger Martin came to his Death do upon their Oath saith That the said Roger Martin on the Eighteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid being on Horse back in Oxford street near the Turnpike in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Man unknown was likewise then and there on Horseback near unto the said Roger Martin, That the said Man unknown a certain Pistol charged with Gunpowder and a leaden Bullet did then and there shoot off and discharge, And that the said Man unknown with the leaden Bullet aforesaid then and there discharged and shot out of the said Pistol by the force of the Gunpowder aforesaid him the said Roger Martin in and upon the Thigh near the left Knee of him the said Roger Martin, Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of him the said Man unknown did then and there Strike and penetrate, giving unto him the said Roger Martin with the Bullet aforesaid out of the Pistol aforesaid, so as aforesaid shot off and discharged by the force of the Gunpowder aforesaid in and upon the said left Tight of him the said Roger Martin one Mortal Wound, of which said mortal wound he the said Roger Martin from the said Eighteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty three day of April in the same Year at the said Parish of St George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty third day of April at the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square he the said Roger Martin of the Mortal Wound aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Man unknown him the said Roger Martin in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Casually and by Misfortune and against the will of him the said Man unknown did kill and Slay, but what Goods or Chattles the said Man unknown had at the time of the killing and Slaying by Misfortune as aforesaid the said Jurors know not. In Witness whereof well the said Coroner as the said John Lassell Foreman of the said Jurors have to this Inquisitions set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner . John Lassell Foreman
