City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex .} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Dwelling House of Thomas Noon , the Sign of the Bag of Nails  pimlico  parish of St. George Hanover within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty seventh day of Jany 1795 in the thirty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq.  Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Cordilia Stepney an Infant then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written and Seals affixed good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Cordelia Stepney came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Cordelia Stepney on the twentheth day of January in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit at an Apartment of the Dwelling House of her Mother in Kings Court there being with her Infant Brother (aged but two years) It so happened, that accidentally, casually and Misfortune the Cloaths which she the said lord Stepney then and there had on her Body, took fire by reason where of and from the Smoke and Flame arising from the said Time she the said Cordelia Stepney was then and there suffocated and burnt: Of which said Suffocation and Burning she the said Cordelia Stepney on the following day died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon that Oath aforesaid do say that the said Cordelia Stepney in manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to he Oath, and not otherwise In Witness where of as the said Coroner as the said Jurors have to this Inquisition at their hands and Seals the day year and place fint above written

Anthey Gell Cordwainer } Richard Warell Foreman John Jones Richd Rand Wm. Young
