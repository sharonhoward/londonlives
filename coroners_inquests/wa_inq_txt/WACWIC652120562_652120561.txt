City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisicion Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of August in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Taylor then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Powell Jobias Williams , Thomas Spiares , James Barrat , Charles Simmons , William Carpandar Charles Wilson , Francis Bramley , John Hand , Thomas Sandarson , William Hayslay , Edward Bradshaw , Thomas Youngman , Richard Parker , John Hume , George Clow , and William Tripp good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Taylor came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Taylor on the first day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid being in the Gutter of the Garretts belonging to the dwelling House of James Carter situate in Hedge Lane in the said Parish Liberty and County Repairing the Filing of the said House it so happended that accidentally casually and by Misfortune he the said Thomas Taylor then and there fell from the said Gutter into the Street by Means whereof he the said Thomas Taylor Received a Violent and Mortal Wound Fracture and Concussion on the head of him the said Thomas Taylor of which said Violent and Mortal Wound Fracture and Concussion he the said Thomas Taylor then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Taylor in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

William Powell Foreman
