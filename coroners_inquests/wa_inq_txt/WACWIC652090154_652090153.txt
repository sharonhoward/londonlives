City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of April in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Dame Elizabeth Anderson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Ashton John Kiteley , Henry Sheate , John Page , Edward Brooke , Thomas West , Thomas Moon Swinburn Keep , Tall Scott , John Haynes , Edward Langbridge , Edward Breton , Samuel Room , Francis Cam , James Hastings , William Pixley , George Martin , William Payne , Robert Giblett , William Nicholson and Thomas Hawkswell good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Mean the said Dame Elizabeth Anderson came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Dame Elizabeth Anderson , on the Ninth day of April in the year aforesaid, laboring under a grievous disease of Body to Wit, a Fever, and by reason of the Violence of the said Disease being delirious and out of her Mind, one End of a certain Piece of Red Gartering unto the side Rail of the Bed in the dwelling House of her the said Dame Elizabeth Anderson situate and being in Park Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the other End thereof about her own Neck, did fix tye and fasten and there with did then and there hang suffocate and strangle herself, of which said Hanging, Suffocation, and Strangling, she the said Dame Elizabeth Anderson then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid to say, that the said Davis Elizabeth Anderson by Reason of the said Disease, being delirious and out of her Mind as aforesaid, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did hill herself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Ashton Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Wm Ashton Foreman
