City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Intended, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of October in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Henry Smith then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Cordy , James Pickernell , Edward Griffin , Robert Hopwood , Stephen Goddard , William Bowles , John Mewret , William Brooks , John Barrett , David McCullough , Thomas Richards , George Richards , Edward Cannon , Edward Wilmot , David Morris , James Shugar , Bartholomew Fowler and William Francis , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Henry Smith came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said William Henry Smith , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Twenty first day of October in the Year aforesaid, in his Lodging Room or Apartment in the dwelling House of Elizabeth Stamford situate and being in Portland Street , at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, with a certain Razor made of Iron and Steel, which he the said William Henry Smyth then and there had and held in his Right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said William Henry Smyth, did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself the said William Henry Smyth , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, with the Razor aforesaid, in and upon the Throator Gullet of him the said William Henry Smyth one mortal Wound of the length of three Inches and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound to the said William Henry Smyth from the said Twenty first day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty fifth day of the same Month and Year, at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty fifth day of October in the Year aforesaid he the said William Henry Smyth at the said Parish Liberty and County, of the mortal Wound aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said William Henry Smyth, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in manner and by the means aforesaid, did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Cordy Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Cordy Foreman.
