City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of December in the Seventh year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Cox , then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Jennings , Thomas Girdler , Charles Tutop  Richard Herring , Samuel Pryer , Evan Davies , John Curtis , George Allen , William Holwell , Elkanah Hoyle , James Cook , George King , William Spiney , John Stone , John Timbery , William Brooks , Francis White , John Mackinder , Jacob Knight , James Brannyan , and Thomas Hawkins , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what Means the said William Cox came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That the said William Cox a Bricklayer on the Twenty eighth day of November in the Year aforesaid being at work on a certain House or Building belonging to the most Noble George Duke of Montague , situate on Black Heath in the County of Kent , and then and there standing upon a Scafold erected against the said Building, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the Timber supporting the said Scafold gave way, and that the said William Cox did then and there fall down to the Ground, by Means whereof the said William Cox did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Wound in and upon the right side of his Head, and mortal Bruises in and upon his Body, of which said mortal wound and Bruises he the said William Cox from the said Twenty Eighth day of November in the Year aforesaid, until the Eighth day of December in the same Year at the Westminster Hospital situate and being in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Eighth day of December in the Year aforesaid, he the said William Cox at the said Hospital, in the Parish City Liberty and County aforesaid, of the mortal Wound and Bruises aforesaid did dye And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Cox Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Jennings Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner . Thos. Jennings  Fore Man
