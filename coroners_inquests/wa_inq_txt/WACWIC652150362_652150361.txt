City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King. at the Parish of Saint. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of November in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Scott then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Moore , John Warner , Benjamin Cayley , Charles King , Edward Owen , Richard Otley , William Croucher , Thomas Stuart , Thomas Ward , Amos Chinne Edward Ellis and Edward Baker good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire of our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Scott came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Scott not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted on the Ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, in the dwelling House of John Hance there situate, with a certain Razor made of Iron and Steel, which he the said John Scott then and there had and held in his Right Hand, in and upon the left Arm of him the said John Scott did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself, with the Razor aforesaid, in and upon the said left Arm one mortal Wound of the length of two Inches and of the depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound, and the great loss and effusion of Blood, he the said John Scott then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Scott , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Moore Foreman of the said Jurors, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Willm. Moore Foreman
