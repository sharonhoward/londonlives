City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the parish of Saint Maritn in the Fields withiin the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of December in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Whitlam then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Norrie , John Dixon , Robert Shepherd , James Swinton John Manly , Christopher, Weissenborn , Christopher Bayley , Mathews Burberow , John Burton , Thomas Taylor , Thomas Latham , Francis Delahunt , Jacob Beltz , George Davis , John Chapman , Henry Meets and Thomas Hincks good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Whitlam came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That Thomas Williams late of the Parish of St. May le bone in the said County of Middlesex Labourer on the Sixth day of December in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid in the County aforesaid in and upon the said Sarah Whitlam then and there being upon the Top or Roof of a certain Coach drawn by four Horses in the Kings Highway there called Oxford Street , and in the Peace of God, and of our said Lord the KIng, then and these being Feloniously did make an Assault And that the said Thomas Williams did then and there felonioulsy and violently drive the said Four Horses drawing the same Coach along the Kings Highway aforesaid by means whereof he the said Thomas Williams did then and there overturn the said Coach and her the said Sarah Whitlam from and off the Top a Roof of the said Coach to the Ground did then and there force and throw and that the said Sarah Whitlam thereby did then and there receive a Wound on the Right side of the Head and mortal Bruises in and upon the Body of her the said Sarah Whitlam of which said mortal Wound and Bruises she the said Sarah Whitlam from the said Sixth day of December in the Year aforesaid until the Eighth day of the same Month in the same Year at the said Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said Eighth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid she the said Sarah Whitlam of the mortal Wound and Bruises aforesaid did die. And That the said Thomas Williams her the said Sarah Whitlam in manner and form aforesaid felonioulsy did kill and slay against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. And that the said Thomas Williams at the time of committing the Felony and Man slaughter aforesaid or at any time since had no Goods on Chattells Lands on Tenements within the said Liberty of Westminster or elsewhere to the Knowledge or Notice of she said Jurors.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Norrie Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Robert Norrie Foreman
