City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Knightbridge in the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of June in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Trigge then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Smith  Thomas Gibson  Edward Hally , Robert Williams  Stephen Preston Thomas Barber , John Mallard , Edward Brown , James Green , Henry List , Avery Tyrrell and William Cook good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Trigge came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say ; That the said John Trigge on the Sixth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, going into the Serpentine River in Hyde Park , there to Bath himself, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune he the said John Trigge was in the Waters of the said River then and there suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said John Trigge then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Trigge in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not othewise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Smith Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Jno: Smith Foreman
