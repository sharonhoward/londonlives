City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  In Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at Tothill Fields  Bridewell in the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirty first day of July in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Edward Buxton a Prisoner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Patrick Walsh , John Newman , John Cooper , Bedford Baker , Samuel Studwick , William Shaw , John Coltman , George Greatenly , Duncan Watson , William Green , William Moulding , Robert Pettilow , Charles Yeates , Caleb Ludford and Charles Evans good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Buxton came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Edward Buxton being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid, in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the said Thirty first day of July in the Year aforesaid, at Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way, (to wit) of a Fever and Ague, and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Patrick Walsh Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Pak. Walsh Foreman
