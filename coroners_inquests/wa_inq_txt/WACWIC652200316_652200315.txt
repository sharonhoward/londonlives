City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex . } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of August in the Twentith Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Chillis then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Christopher Pinchbeck , William Lord , Stephen Bayley , Thomas Durham , Thomas Harris , Cambell Oliphant , William Hebb , Hugh Riddell , John Brown , James Findelson , John Whiteman , Aaron Barling , Francis Cottie , George Findley , Alexander Williamson and William Rose good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chose, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Chillis came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Chillis on the Fifth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid to wit in his Chamber or Apartment situate in the Red Lyon Inn Yard in Cockspurr Street, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a Natural way (to wit) by the sudden bursting of a Blood Arssel of the Brain of him the said John Chillis and not from any hurt injury or Violence from any Person or Persons whatsover In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Christr Pinckeck Foreman
