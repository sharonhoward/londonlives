City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Abraham Barker Sign of the blue posts King Street  Golden Square parish of St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty ninth day of Augst. 1796 in the thirty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Henry Wilkinson aged eighteen Months then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Henry Wilkinson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Henry Wilkinson on the twenty seventh day of August in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To wit in a certain Apartment in the Dwelling House of Mary Cooper in Looker's Court, King Street, Covent Garden then and there being with his Brother James (aged but four years) It so happened that accidentally casually and by Misfortune the Cloaths which he the said Henry Wilkinson then and there had on his Body took fis by means whereof and from the smoke & Flame arising thereof he the said Henry Wilkinson was then and there suffocated and burnt; of which said Suffocation and Burning he the said Henry Wilkinson then and there instantly died And so ye Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say, that the said Henry Wilkinson in manner and by the Means aforesd. accidentally casually & by Misfortune came to his death & not otherwise

 IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner Michell R Westm G Wigmore Henry Christe William Jenkins
