City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .}  to wit  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty third day of May in the thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esc  Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Baker then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Robert Hardie  Eusebius Say , William Davis  John Bond Thomas Smith , James Fidler , John Youngman  Joseph Rayner Charles Sharpe , Philip Packham , John Hart , Thomas Price and Henry Beale good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Baker came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said James Baker on the twenty second day of May in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid being in a weak and infirm state of Health It so happened, that he the said James Baker was then and there suddenly sized with a Fiet and by reason of the violence thereof he the said James Baker then and there fell backwards to and against the Floor in the Dwelling House of John Dernay at the Parish aforesaid where he the said James Baker lodged, and then and there instantly died but had no Macks of Violence or Bruises appearing on his Body And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Baker by the Violence of the Fit aforesaid and in manner and by the means aforesaid came to his death and no t otherwise In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Hardie the Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day, Year and Place above mentioned

Anthony Gell Coroner Robert Herne Foreman
