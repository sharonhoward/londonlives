City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of November in the twenty fifth Year of the Regin of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Mure  Esqr . then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Applebee , Benjamin Rawlins , William Adney , John Gale , John Shirley , John Eades , Thomas Ray , Thomas Simpson , William Barrell , Job Dakin , George Egerton , William King and Robert Gilston good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Mure came to his Death, do their Oath day , That the said William Mure, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Said Sixteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, a certain Pistol charged with and a leaden Bullet which he the said William Mure then and there had and held in his Right Hand, to and against the Head of him the said William Mure did then and there shoot off and discharge, By Means whereof he the said William Mure did then and there give unto himself with the leaden Bullet aforesaid, so discharged and shot out of the Pistol aforesaid by the force of the Gunpower aforesaid, in and upon the Right side of the Head above the right Ear of him the said William Mure one mortal Wound, of which said mortal wound he the said William mure then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said William Mure not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Applebee  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho Prickard Coroner . Thos Applebee Foreman
