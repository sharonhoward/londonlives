City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of January in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Price then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Daniel Luckhurst , Francis Englart , Daniel Tryer , James Frith , William Stanbank , John Williams , Edward Taylor , Patrick Mathews , Henry Holland , John Fossett Lyster Vaughan and John Aitkens good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Price came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Price on the Ninth day of January in the Year aforesaid being Skaiting upon the Ice on the Serpentire River in Hyde Park in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the Ice broke, and that the said Thomas Price then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell into the said Serpentire River, and in the Waters thereof was then and there suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Thomas Price then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Price in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Daniel Luckhurst Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Danl. Luckhurst Foreman
