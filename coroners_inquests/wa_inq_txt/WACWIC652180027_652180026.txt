City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of January in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Wright then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Chilver , John Sutton , Thomas Price , Christopher Allat , William Lloyd , James Verral , William Huddleston , William Banister , Thomas Theobald , Thomas Osburn , James Young , John Charlton , Thomas Newman , Thomas Dixon & William Jenings good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Wright came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Wright , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted, on the Eleventh day of January in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Leather Strap unto an Iron Staple fastened into a Wooden Beam in the Room or Apartment of the said Thomas Wright in the dwelling House of Charles Wolfran Cornwall Esquire situate in the said Parish Liberty and County and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and there with did then and there hang suffocate and Strangle himself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said Thomas Wright then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Wright , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said Samuel Chilve Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Saml. Chilver Foreman
