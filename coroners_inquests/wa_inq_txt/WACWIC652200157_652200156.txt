City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquitition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of April in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Dawson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Allen , George Jefferys , William Mc. Hurdy , John Dejersey William Stromach , Henry Pensers , William Sumare , Robert Cotton , Thomas Raison , John Walter  Samuel Titmus and Edward Jones good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Robert Dawson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say ,That the said Robert Dawson on the Second day of April in the Year aforesaid being in the House of Thomas Bridge Victualler situate at Pimlico in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and then and there going through the Back yard to the Road, and being dark, it so happened that the said Robert Dawson Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down out of the said back Yard unto the bottom of certain Stone Steps there, By means whereof the said Robert Dawson then and there did receive one mortal Wound and Fracture in and upon the back- part of his Head, of which said mortal Wound and Fracture he the said Robert Dawson their and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Robert Dawson in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Allen Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner Thos. Allen Foreman
