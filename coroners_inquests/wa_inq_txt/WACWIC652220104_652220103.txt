An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Second day of April in the Twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Butcher then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Scott , Joseph Earns , Michael Griffin , George Bennett , John Ward , Arthur Harris , Job Birch  Thomas Guillam  William Hogg , John Cartwright , Michael Ward , Colin Campbell and Samuel Ray good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Butcher came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Butcher a Brick- -layer on the Nineteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid, being at Work at the top of an Empty House at Charingcross in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields in the County aforesaid, and then standing upon some Laths on the outside of the said House, It so happened that the Laths broke, and that the said, James Butcher Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the top of the said House unto and upon he Ground, and thereby then and there did receive a wound on the right side of the Head and a Concussion in and upon the Brain, of him the said James Butcher , of which said Mortal Wound and Concussion he the said James Butcher at the said Parish of Saint Margaret with in the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Nineteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid until the Twentieth day of the same Month and Yard did languish,and live, and on the said Twentieth day of April at the Parish last mentioned he the said James Butcher of the mortal Wound an Concussion aforesaid do say that the said James Butcher in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Scott  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner Thos. Scott. Foreman
