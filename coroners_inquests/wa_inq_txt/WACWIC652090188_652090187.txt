City & Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Information Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter, of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of May in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Cattle then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of David Stables, Richard Franks , Robert Bell , Alexander Grant , James Johnston , William Hunter , William Kerby , Robert Willerton , William Burt , John Ashley , John Parry and Charles Davies , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Robert Cattle came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said Robert Cattle laboring under agrievous Disease of Body, to wit, a Fever, and by Reason of the violence of the said Disease, being delirious and out of his Mind, on the Third day of May in the Year aforesaid, from and out of a certain three pair of Stairs Window in the Dwelling House of Matthew Knapp situate in George Street in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, to the Ground, did violently cast and throw himself to and against the Stone Pavement in the Area behind the said House, by Means whereof he the said Robert Cattle , so delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid, did then and there receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the Right side of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Robert Cattle then and there died And the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Robert Cattle in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, being delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid, by reason of the violence of the Disease aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said David Stables Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner . David Stables Foremen
