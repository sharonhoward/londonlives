City and Liberty of Westminster in the County Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the Count of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of December in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Raban then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Haines , Edward Field , Thomas Ellick  Isaac Froom , Patrick Neele , John Stokes , William Bond  Joseph Fox  Charles Colly , Robert Lemming James Elden , William Gardener , Samuel Russell , William Weale Thomas Jones and Robert Lilly good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Raban came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Raban not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted on the Eighteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, a certain Pistol charged with Gunpowder and a leaden Bullet which he the said John Raban then and there had and held in his Right Hand, to and against the Head of him the said John Raban did then and there shoot off and discharge, by Means whereof he the said John Raban did then and there give unto himself with the leaden Bullet aforesaid so discharged and shot out of the Pistol aforesaid by the force of the Gun powder aforesaid one Mortal Wound in and through the Head of him the said John Raban , of which said mortal Wound he the said John Raban then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Raban , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did hill himself, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Haines Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos Haines Foreman
