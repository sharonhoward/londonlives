City & Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex the Thirtieth day of June in the Fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard  Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Lockley Widow then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Cox , Robert Watson , Emanuel Lloyd , Robert Patten , Andrew Staines , Henry Burnett , William Holmes , Robert Smith , John Nash , John Barnard , Richard Rowe John Spiller , John Smith , Thomas Wharton  Hugh Douglass and Alexander Smeele good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Lockley came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty sixth day of June in the Year aforesaid, the said Elizabeth Lockley being with divers other Persons in a Boat or wherry, upon the River Thames in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid And that about ten of the Clock in the Evening of the said Twenty sixth day of June, It so happened that the said Boat or Wherry, with the said Elizabeth Lockley therein, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune went against and under a Lighter loaden with Chalk, and lying at Anchor in and upon the said River Thamas, by Means whereof the said Boat or Wherry sunk, and the said Elizabeth Lockley in the Waters of the said River Thames was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning she the said Elizabeth Lockley then and there Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Elizabeth Lockley in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death, and not otherwise. In witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Cox Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Cox Foreman
