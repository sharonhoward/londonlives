City and Liberty of Westmister in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquitition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at Knightbridge in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of April in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Martha Stevens then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Andrews  Thomas Painter  John Dobinson , John Smith , Thomas Barbar , John Mallard , Henry List , Stephen Preston , Thomas Green , John Smith , William Henchlewood and James Jones good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Martha Stevens came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said Martha Stevens , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted, on the First day of April in the Year aforesaid at Knightsbridge in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto the top of a Bed Post in the Lodging Room or Apartment of her the said Martha Stevens in the Dwelling House of Thomas Williams situate and being in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about her own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle herself, of which said hanging Suffocation and Strangling She the said Martha Stevens then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Martha Stevens not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Andrews Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said, Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner . Thos. Andrews Foreman}
