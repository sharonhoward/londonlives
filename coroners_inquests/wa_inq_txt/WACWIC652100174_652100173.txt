City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of April in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Martin then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Short , Richard Sills , George Dobson , George Grant , Edward Clarke Robert Thatcher , Edward Webb , Thomas Pegg , William Harrison , Richard Holloway , Charles Harrison  Thomas Hopton , Thomas Symons , Thomas Gibson , William Williams , Solomon Moxey and John Sutter , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Martin came to his-Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Martin on the Fourth day of April in the year aforesaid (between the Hours of Ten and Eleven in the Evening) being Riding a certain Bay Horse in Cockspur Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said John Martin then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the back of the said Horse upon the Stone Pavement in the said Street, by Means whereof the said John Martin then and there received one mortal Contusion on the right side of his Head near the right Temple, of which said mortal Contusion he the said John Martin then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Martin in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Robert Short Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Robt: Short Foreman
