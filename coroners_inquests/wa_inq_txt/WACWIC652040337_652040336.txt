City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of September, in the Fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Eaton then & there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Jonathan Wright , John Bunce , William Clay , John Nutton , John Granger , John Grammar , James Smart , George Standorth , James Wild , Thomas Hopson , Stephen Green  Anthony Satchill , William Lukely , John Martin , William Clark , John Barbrey , James Young , and John Dodd , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to Inquire for Our said Lord the King when, how, and by what Means, the said Ann Eaton came to her Death, Do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty sixth day of June in the Year aforesaid, the said Ann Eaton being picking up Coals at a certain place called Lambeth's Wharf, situate and being in Scotland Yard in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Precincts of His Majesty's Verge in the said County of Middlesex , And that Benjamin Coomes a Carman , and other Persons being then and there drawing up a long piece of Timber, with a Crane used for that purpose, out of the Dock into and upon the said Whorf, It so happened that one End of the said piece of Timber Strock against the right Leg of the said Ann Eaton , whereby the said Ann Eaton , Accidentally, Casually, and by Misfortune, (and against the will of the said Benjamin Coomes , and the other Persons, so drawing up the said piece of Timber as aforesaid) did then and there receive one Mortal Fracture in and upon her right Leg, of which said Mortal Fracture she the said Ann Eaton from the said Twenty sixth day of June in the Year aforesaid until the Fifth day of September in the same Year, there and at a certain Hospital called the Westminster Hospital , situate in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty & County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said fifth day of September in the Year aforesaid she the said Ann Eaton at the Hospital aforesaid, of the said Mortal Fracture, did Dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Ann Eaton in manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death, and not otherwise: And that the said piece of Timber was the Occasion of the Death of the said Ann Eaton , and is of the Value of five Shillings, and the Property and in the Possession of Charles Stubbs of Warwich Street in the Parish of St. James within the Liberty of Westminster Timber Merchant, or of his assign In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jonathan Wright Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Johnathan wright Foreman
