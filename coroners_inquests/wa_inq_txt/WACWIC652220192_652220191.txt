An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter, of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of June in the twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Stockwell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Aaron Loton , William Ashton , Anthony Fabry , John Hawkins , John Littlewood , John Parsons , Walter Cross , Hugh Beach , Richard Lenn  Hugh Nash , William Baldwin , Charles Cormonel , James Pratt and John Stone good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Stockwell came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the Said William Stock well a Carpenter on the Seventh day of June in the Year aforesaid being at Work in the New bult House of Philip Crispigny Situate in Old Palace Yard in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforsaid and then and there being in the Second Floor of the said House It so happened that the said William Stockwell Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell of and from the said Second Floor into the Kitch in and thereby then and there received a mortal Concussion in and upon the Brain of him the said William Stockwell , of which said Mortal Concussion he the said William Stockwell from the said Seventh day of June in the Year aforesaid until the Eighth day of the same Month at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and Languishing did lide, on which said Eighth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Margaret he the said William Stockwell of the Mortal Concussion aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said William Stockwell in Manner and by the MeAns aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise, In Withness where of as well the said Coroner as the said Aaron Loton  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Aaron Loton Foreman
