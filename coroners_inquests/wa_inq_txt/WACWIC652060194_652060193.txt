City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for our sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westmr with in the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the First day of April in the Sixth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so Forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Arthur then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Wilkins , Thomas Loving , George King , Peter Ball , James Ford , Samuel Morrison , John Charles , Nathaniel Skeat , Thomas Hook , James Surridge William Underwood , Amos Charles and William McCrakin , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being than and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by what Means the said John Arthur came to his Dea do upon their Oath say. That John Stephens late of the Parish aforesaid in the City Liberty and County afor Laborer Keeper of the Prison of Bridewell situate in Tothill Fileds, in the Parish aforesaid in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, not having the fear of God be fore his Eyes, but moved and seduced by the Instigation of the on the Seventeenth day of March in the Year aforesaid, with Force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid in the City Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said John Arthur , then and there a Prisoner in the said Prison the care and custody of the said John Stephen , and in the Peace of God and our said Sovereign Lord the king then there being wilfully and of malise forethought on the said Seventeenth day of March, and at divers other days and times in the Year aforesaid, in the Prison aforesaid at the Parish and in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, with certain knotted Cords commonly called a Cat of nine and also with a Bull's Pizzle, and large Bunch of heavy Iron Keys which he the said John Stephens then and there severaly and at different times had and held in his right Hand, him the said John Arthur in and upon the Back Sides and Belly of him the said John Arthur did then and there hit, strike and Beat, And that the said John Stephens by Duress of Imprisonment feloniously wilfully and of his Malice fore thought him the said John Arthur into a certain dark Dungeon then and there being in the said Prison, and commonly called the Black Hole, did force and put, and with certain Iron Instruments the right Leg of him the said John Arthur did then and there Chain fasten and iron down to the Floor of the said Dungeon by means of which said hitting striking and beating aforesaid, and of the duress of Imprisonment aforesaid, he the said John Arthur became sick and distempered in Body, and the Legs and Thighs of him the said John Arthur became Swelled and Mortified, of which said Mortification Sickness and Distemper occasioned as aforesaid he the said John Arthur , from the said Seventeenth day of March in the Year aforesaid, in the Prison aforesaid at the Parish and in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, until the Thirty first day of the same Month in the same Year, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Thirty first day of March in the year aforesaid, in the Prison aforesaid at the Parish and in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said John Arthur of the Mortification Sickness and Distemper aforesaid accasioned as aforesaid did Die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Stephens him the said John Arthur in manner and by the means aforesaid feloniously wilfully and of his Malice fore thought did kill and murder against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. And that he the said John Stephen 's at the time of the doing and committing of the Felony and Murder aforesaid, or at any time sine, had no Good or Chattels Lands or Tenements within the said County of Middlesex , or else where to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Wilkins Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Thos. Prickard  Coroner .

Thos. Wilkins Foreman
