City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of November in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Close then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of David Mac Cullogh , William Simmons , Charles Young , Andrew Patterson Henry Freeman  George Staten  Baynard Charlton  James Donaldson Thomas Dawe , Joseph Street , John Whittle , John Watson , Robert Monford and John Mander Good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ann Close came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the Said Ann Close on the Eighteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid being at the End of Berwick Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a certain Coach drawn by two Horses, without a Coachman, then crossed Oxford Street into Berwick Street aforesaid, And it so happened the said Ann Close was then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune forced down to the Ground by one of the said Horses, and that the off Wheels of the said Coach then and there passed over the Head of the said Ann Close so lying on the Ground as aforesaid by Means whereof the said Ann Close did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive a mortal Bruise and Fracture in and upon her Head, of which said mortal Bruise and Fracture she the said Ann Close then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upo their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Ann Close in Manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said David Mac Cullogh Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

David Mac Cullogh Forman
