City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty ninth day of July in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Marshall then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Cock , James Randall , George Taylor , Adam Amstrong , John Robinson , Anthony Fry , Lawrence Kite , Robert Minns , Robert Sampson , Francis Day , Alexander Bower , and William Ashdon good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what by Means the said Ann Marshall came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Ann Marshall on the Eighteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid, being intoxicated with Liquor, and in the Kings Highway at the Parish of aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Man was then Driving a Cart drawn by two Horses in the said Kings Highway, It so happened that one of the Wheels of the said Cart Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the Right Leg of the said Ann Marshall whereof the said Ann Marshall then and there received on Mortal Fracture in and upon her right Leg of which said Mortal Fracture she the said Ann Marshall from the said Eighteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid, until the Twenty seventh day of the same Month and Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty seventh day of July in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty, and County aforesaid, she the said Ann Marshall of the Mortal Fracture aforsd. did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, That the said Ann Marshall Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, came to her Death.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Cock  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard Coroner

Thos. Cock Foreman}
