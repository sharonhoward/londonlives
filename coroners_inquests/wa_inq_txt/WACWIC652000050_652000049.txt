Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of Saint Martin in the fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of Saint Peter at Westminster the Eleventh day of September in the Thirty fourth Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Second by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth and in the Year of Our Lord One thousand seven hundred and Sixty Before John Feary Coroner for Our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid Upon a View of the Body of Frederick Neunberg then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of Nicholas Scott , William Ewbank , John Smith Coachmaker, John Smith Currier, William Taylor , Joseph Loyd Thomas Corner , James Campbell , Edward Poole , George Beeston , William Dowe , John Albin , Colin Frazer William Spence , James Knight , Moses Lafont , Christian Garling and Charles Orchard Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for Our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Frederick Neunberg came to his Death say upon this Oaths That on Thursday the fourth day of September Instant the said Frederick Neunberg being in a certain Street or Lane called Saint Martins Lane in the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields in the Liberty aforesaid and not being of sound mind memory and understanding but Lunatick and Distracted as certain Pistoll Eaden with Gun Powder and a London Ball into his hand did take and placing the same under his Chin, the said Frederick Neunberg then and there did Discharge and Shoot off & thereby by force of the said Gun powder the said Leader Bullet being Discharged did give unto himself One Mortal Wound in the head of which said Mortal Wound be the said Frederick Neunberg in the house of Mr. John Jobstvender Neunberg scituate in St. Martins Lane aforesaid in the City Liberty and County aforesaid from the said third day of September Instant did Languish and Languishing did live untill the Ninth day of the same Month and then Died. And so the said Jurors upon their Oaths aforesaid do say that the said Frederick Neunberg the said Ninth day of September Instant in the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid by the cause and means aforesaid by his Death came and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition put their Hands and Seals the day & Year and at the place aforesaid

Jno. Feary  Coroner

Nicholas Scott Foreman
