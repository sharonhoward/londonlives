City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty second day of December in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Margaret Byrne then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Nathaniel Hall , Job Deacon , Edward Newcomb , Isaac Swan , John Noble , Elias Glair , Richard Piddock , Richard Rogers , James Croker , William Bennet , William Ashton James Asser, and John Taylor good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Margaret Byrne came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Margaret Byrne on the Thirtieth day of November in the Year aforesaid being in her Lodging Room in the Dwelling House of John Randolph situate in Charle's Street in the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and there lying upon two Chairs before the Fire. It so happened that the Cloaths which the said Margaret Byrne then and there had on her Body Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took five and that the said Margaret Byons was then and there by the Flames thereof Burnt in and upon her Breast. Neck, Arms and Sides of which said Burning she the said Margaret Byone from the said Thirtieth day of November in the Year aforesaid, until the Twenty first day of December in the same Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid. did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty first day of December She the said Margaret Byone at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, of the Burning aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, That the said Margaret Byrne in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Nathaniel Hall Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellow's in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Handsand Seals, the Day Year and Place above mentioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner
