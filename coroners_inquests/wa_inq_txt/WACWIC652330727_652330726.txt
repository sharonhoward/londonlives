City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our sovereign Lord the King, at House of James Adams  Sign of the Hole in the wall in Panton Street  Parish of St Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of st. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the eighteeenth day of November 1793 in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain,France and Irelad, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Bellamy then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written and Seals affixed

 Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty. duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enqure for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Bellamy came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Thomas Bellamy on the Seventeenth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid to wit at him dwelling House in Panton Sheet aforesaid not being of sound Mind, Meanory & understanding but lunatic and distracted, One End of a Pocket hankerchief unto an Iron Staple fastened into the Wainscott in the Loding Room or Apartment of him the said Thomas Bellamy there Satuate and the other End there of about his ever Neck, did fix, tye and fusten and therewith did then hang suffocate and strangle himself of which said hanging, suffocation and Strangling, the the siad Thomas Bellaney then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that, the said thomas Bellamy, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did Kill himself

In Witness whereof as well the said coroner the said dJurors, have, to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day Yeat and Place first above written

Anthy Gell Coroner John Jones Foreman Geo Burne Bart Thorpe Wm., Skelton

James Gladston Thomas hipt St Thos Sickels Wm. Allen James Conelly Thos. McCleland
