City & Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex , to Wit} 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Chapter House of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the said Dean and Chapter, in the County of Middlesex , the First day of May in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Hutton , otherwise Thompson , then lying dead within the Precinct of the said Collegiate Church, in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, upon the Oath of John Merest , William Cope , Henry Brooker , Benjamin Fidoe , William Seymore , Henry Eade , John Page , John Frith , Stephen Geary , Anthony Gell , John Jones and David Evans good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to Inquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by what Means the said Elizabeth Hutton otherwise Thompson came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Hutton otherwise Thompson on the Twenty eighth day of April in the Year aforesaid die suddenly near the Door of the Prebendal House of the Reverend Dr. John Taylor one of the Prebendaries of the said Collegiate Church, situate and being in Deans's Yard within the Precinct of the said Collegiate Church, in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, That no Marks of Violence appeared on the Body of the said Elizabeth Hutton otherwise Thompson, And that the said Elizabeth Hutton otherwise Thompson departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a Natural Way; and not by any hurt or injury from any Person whatsoever In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Merest Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Merest Foreman
