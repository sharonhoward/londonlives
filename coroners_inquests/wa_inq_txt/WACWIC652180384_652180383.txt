City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of September in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Dean then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Peter Peele , John Johnson , Richard Emmery , John Debenham , Thomas Fitch John Lovitt , Thomas Pitt , Richard Parslow , John Howell , Richard Clark Crisp Isaacs and John Hayward good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Dean came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Dean a Labourer on the Seventh day of September in the Year aforesaid being at Work on the outside of a Garret Window in the dwelling House of John Howard situate in the Parish of Kensington in the County aforesaid, It so happened that the said John Dean then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down from the said Garret Window unto the Ground, and thereby then and there received divers Fractures in and upon the left Thigh and left Arm of the said John Dean , of which said mortal Fractures he the said John Dean From the said Seventh day of September in the Year aforesaid until the fourteenth day of the same Month and Year at the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said fourteenth day of September aforesaid he the said John Dean at the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square , of the mortal Fractures aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Dean in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Peter Peele For m of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition get their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Peter Peele Foreman
