City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of July in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Martha Newell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Watson , Thomas Bennett , George Cummings  Thomas Hichcock John Beaumont , John Scott , Thomas Barlow , John White , Joseph Caron Abraham Appleton , Thomas Horn & Edward Nairn good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Martha Newell came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Martha Newell, laboring under a grievous disease of Body, to Wit a Fever, and by reason of the Violence of the said Disease, being Delicious and out of her Mind, on the Sixth day of July in the Year aforesaid, into a Certain Water Tub in the Yard belonging to the dwelling House of Maria Knight situate and being in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid there did cast and throw herself, by Means of which said lasting and throwing she the said Martha Newell in the Water in the said Tub was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning she the said Martha Newell then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Martha Newell being delicious and out of her Mind as aforesaid by Reason of the Fever aforesaid, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Robert Watson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Thos. Prickard  Coroner

Robt. Watson Foreman
