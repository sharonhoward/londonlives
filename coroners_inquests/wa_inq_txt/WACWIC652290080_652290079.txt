City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex ,} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate, Church of St, Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the sixteenth day of January in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great- Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Ann Corkin then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Devereux Combe , Joseph Waldie , Aaron Lonton John Gilliott , Thomas Ridley , Charles Mc. Cockling , John Robinson Thomas Lambert , Richard Cater , Adam Slack , Samuel Casley and John Watts good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King,when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Ann Corkin  Came to h er Death,do upon their Oath say That the said Mary Ann Corkin an Instant of the Age of four Years on the Fifteenth day of January in the Year aforesaid being left with another infant in the Room of Ann Chatfiled situate in Tothill Fields in the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist within the Liberty, and County aforesaid, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the Tinen Cloaths which the said Mary Ann Cortin the Instant then and there had on her Body took fere by means where of the Thighs Belly Sides one Arms of her the said Mary Ann Cork in the Infant were very much burnt of which said burning she the said Mary Ann Cortin at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid Languished about four Hours and then died in the Parish last mentioned And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Mary Ann Corkin in Manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Richard Devereux Combe Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner R.D. Combe Foreman
