City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirty first day of August in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Thompson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Hubbard , Charles Finlow , Thomas Goddard , George Galley , Henry Jay , John Jefferys , John Blunt , Isaac Mould , George Manley , William Barnesley , William Cornmeal , John Poole , John Milbourn , Moses Philips , William Kimble , John Roberts , Thomas Meredith and Archibald Mc. Duffe , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Thompson came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Mary Thompson Labring under a grievous Disease of Body, to wit, a Fever, and by Reason of the Violence of the said Disease, being delicious and out of her Mind, on the Twenty Fifth day of August in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid with a certain Case knife made of Iron and Street which she the said Mary Thompson then and there had and held in her Right hand, the Stomach of her the said Mary Thompson , near the Breast Bone, did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto herself the said Mary Thompson , so being delicious and out of her Mind as aforesaid, with the Case Knife aforesaid, in the stomach of her the said mary Thompson , one mortal Wound of the breadth of three Inches and of the Depth of Seven Inches, of which said mortal wound she the said Mary Thompson at the said Parish of Saint James from the said Twenty fifth day of August in the year aforesaid until the Twenty ninth day of the same Month and year, did longuish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty ninth day of August in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid she the said Mary Thompson of the mortal wound aforesaid did die. And to the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid aforesaid do say, that the said Mary Thompson , by reason of the said Disease delirious and out of her Mind in maynor and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Hubbard Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hand and Seals the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Joseph Hubb rd Foreman
