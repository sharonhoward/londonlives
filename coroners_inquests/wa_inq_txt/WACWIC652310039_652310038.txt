City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the First day of February in the Thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Daniel Hill then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of William Mourn , Samuel Ray , Thomas Gordon , John Bates , Joseph Dixon , William Bromley , William Foster Christian Fouke , Edward Taylor , John Smith  Robert Bowen and John Tripp good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Daniel Hill came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty ninth day of January in the Year aforesaid the said Daniel Hill being Ignorant and unskilled and desirous to compose himself to sleep at the Parish of St. Giles's in the Fields in the County aforesaid a Quantity of Landanum mixed with Beer did take and drink, by means whereof the said Daniel Hill became Sick and distempered in his Body and from the Twenty ninth day of and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said Thirtieth day of January in the year aforesaid he the said Daniel Hill in manner and by the means aforesaid of the Sickness and Distemper aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Moun Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Willm Mourn Foreman
