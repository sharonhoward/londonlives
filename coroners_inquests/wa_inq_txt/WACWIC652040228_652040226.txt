City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret in the City of Westminster , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Eighth day of June in the Fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Goodjohn then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Robert Anderson , Richard Herring , John Curtis  Robert Wire , Benjamin Watson , John Lindsay , Robert Parker , George King , James Burdon , Henry Peake , John Rountree , John Barberry and John Steward good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Goodjohn came to his Death, Do upon their Oath say, That on the Fourth day of June in the Year aforesaid the said William Goodjohn in a certain Street called Chapel Street at the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and Country aforesaid, a certain Gun called a Fowling piece charged with Gun powder which he the said William Goodjohn had and held in both his hands, did then and there shoot off and discharge, And that the said Fowling piece in being so dicharged as aforesaid, did then and there, by Force of the Gun Powder aforesaid, burst and break into several pieces, whereby the said William Goodjohn did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive divers mortal Bruises in and upon the left Hand and Arm of him the said William Goodjohn, of which said mortal Bruises he the said William Goodjohn from the said fourth day of June in the Year aforesaid, until the Twenty seventh day of the same Month and Year, at the Westminster Hospital , within the said Parish City Liberty and County, did languish and languishing did live, On which said Twenty seventh day of June in the Year aforesaid, the said William Goodjohn, at the said Hospital of the said Mortal Bruises, did Dye And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said William Goodjohn in manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Anderson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Robert Anderson Foreman
