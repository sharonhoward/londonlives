City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Dwelling House of John Mills known by the Sign of the Prince of Wales at Kensington Gore in the Parish of Saint Margaret within and Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of Saint Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the seventeenth day of April in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Anthony Gell Esqr. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Elizabeth the Wife of William Bradley then and there lying dead upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written and seals affixed good and lawful Man of the said City and Liberty, duly chosen who being then and there duly sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when how and by what Means the said Elizabeth Bradley came to her Death upon their Oath say, that some person or Persons to these Jurors unknowns not having the fear of god before the Eyes, but moved and seduced by the Instigation of the Devil in the Sixteenth day of Apirl in the Year aforesaid with force and Arms in Hyde Park in the Parish aforesaid in and upon the said Elizabeth Bradley , in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously willfully of their Male aforethought did make an Assault And that the said Person or Person unknown with a certain Instrument to these Jurors unknown (bady which the said Jurors believe to have been a Bayonet) which They the said Person or Persons unknown then and there had and held in their right hand,her the said Elizabeth, Bradley in and through the life of the left year through the Scull into the head of her the said Elizabeth Bradley the and there did Strike that and apenetrale and that the said Person or person unknown did then and there give unto her the said Elizabeth Bradley in and through the lip of her left Year through the smell into her head with the Instrumen ta one mortal wound of the Depth of her Inches and an half Of which said Mortal Wound she the said Elizabeth Bradley  then and there instantly died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon this Oath aforesaid do say that the said Person or Person unknown her the said Elizabeth Bradley in manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously wilfully and of their Malice fore thought did kill and Murder, against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. And that the said Person or Persons unknown after he or they had committed the Felony and Murder aforesaid, in manner and by the Means aforesaid withdrew and fled for the same And at the time of the Committing thereof at any time since he or they the said person or Person unknown had not any gods or Chattils Lands or Foreman within the said county or drew here to the Know ledgs of the Jurors In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors lave to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day Year and Place first above written

Anthy Gell Coroner Willm White Foreman James Silvester Edwd King Wm. Hinshelwood John Edwards Richd Huntley George Vevers Wm Mony John Mills James Brown John Woltan William Gooder Thomas Eaton John Roberts
