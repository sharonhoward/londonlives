City and Liberty of Westminster in the County Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the Count of Middlesex , the Seventh day of December in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Stone then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Storey  John Lewis  Thomas Betteley , William Hiet , John McDonald Thomas Watts  Christopher Walton , Joseph Flicker  Luke Hogard , Richard Shaw , John Brook and Richard Exley good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Stone came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Stone not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted on the Fifth day of December in the Year aforesaid into the Bason in Hyde Park in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid did cast and throw herself, by Means of which said Casting and Throwing she the said Elizabeth Stone in the Water in the said Bason was then and there suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning she the said Elizabeth Stone then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Elizabeth Stone in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did drown and hill herself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Storey Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm. Storey Foreman
