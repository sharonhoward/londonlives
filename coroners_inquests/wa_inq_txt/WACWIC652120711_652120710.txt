City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex }to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Second day of September in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Colledge then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Burchill , John Brooks , John Dunford , Thomas Hitchins , Williams Ellis , Patrick Mathews , Thomas Ray , William Keatley , Thomas Gladwin , William Denny , John Wilson and Rice Evors Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Colledge came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Colledge on the Sixteenth day of September in the Year aforesaid being Rowing a certain Bargelsaden with Timber in and upon the River Thames in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesd, and then and there going out of the said Barge into a small Boat or wherry fastened to the Stern of the said Barge, It so happened that another Empty Barge then floating upon the said River Thames , near to the said loaded Barge, was then and there, by the force of the Waves, drove to and against the said John Colledge and that the said John Colledge, by being Squeezed between the said Barges, then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received one Mortal Fracture in and upon his Right Hip Bone, and divers Bruises in his Body, of which said mortal Fracture and Bruises he the said John College from the said Sixteenth day of September in the Year aforesaid until the said Twenty second day of the same Month and Year, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty second day of September in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said John Colledge of the mortal Fracture and Bruises aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Colledge in Manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Burchill Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place First abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner John Burchill Foreman
