City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth Day of August in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Timothy Stevens then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Jeffery , William Monk , William Lowdy , Charles Keene , Robert Burn , William Stores , Abraham Wilkinson , John Curtis , John Mackender , William Smith , George Oham , John Turpin William Blundell , Francis Russell and John Dodd , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Timothy Stevens came to his Death do upon their Oath say That on the Twenty third day of July in the Year aforesaid, a Carter (whose name is unknown) being carefully driving a certain Cart drawn by three Horses, through the Gateway leading from Scotland Yard to Charing Cross , in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Precincts of his Majestys Verge in the County of Middlesex , And the said Timothy Stevens being on foot and walking by the said Gateway, did go against the fore Horse, so drawing the said Cart as aforesaid, whereby the said Timothy Stevens was then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune forced to the Ground, And that the said fore Horse in the said Cart with one of his forefeet, did then and there Tread upon the right leg of the said Timothy Stevens , by Means whereof he the said Timothy Stevens , Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did then and there receive one mortal Fracture in and upon his Right Leg, near the Knee, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Timothy Stevens from the said Twenty third day of July in the Year aforesaid, until the Second day of August in the same Year, at the Westminster Hospital , situate and being in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Second day of August in the Year aforesaid, he the said Timothy Stevens , at the Hospital aforesaid, of the mortal Fracture aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Timothy Stevens , in manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Samuel Jeffery Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Saml Jeffery Foreman
