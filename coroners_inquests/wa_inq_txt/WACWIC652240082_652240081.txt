City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty second day of January in the twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Doyl then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Mourn , Walter Cross , Michael Griffin David Mc.Ewen , John Spong , Thomas Levinton , David Jones , Robert Sampson , Thomas Thompson Richard Hughes , Evan Oxford and John Denney , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the king, when, how, and by what Means the said James Doyl came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Doyl on the Twentieth day of January in the Year aforesaid, was found dead in the Road or Publick Highway at Pimlico in the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid and that there is no Evidence to make it appear to the Jurors how or in what Manner the said James Doyl came to his Death.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Mourn  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Budd Foreman
