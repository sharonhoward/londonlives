City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of December in the Ninth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by Grace of god of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of Basti (whose Christian Name is unknown) then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Frith , Emanuel Lloyd , Alexander Marr , Thomas Hutchinson , Thomas Lambert , William Harvey , Samuel Fullmer  William Green John Jacoby . Alexander Smelley , John Nash , Thomas Brook , Amos Charles , Samuel Mattam  James Burden Joseph Love , James Damaid and Richard Littlewort good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for own said Lord the King when, how and by what Means the said Basti, whose Christian name is not known came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Basti, not being of sound Mind, Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Nineteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid, at a Place called Mill Bank situate and being in the Parish aforesaid with in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, a certain Pistol charged with Gunpowder and a leaden Bullet, which he the said Basti then and there had and held in his Right Hand, into the Mouth of him the said Basti, did then and there shoot off and discharge, by Means whereof he the said Basti did then and there give unto himself, with the leaden Bullet aforesaid, so discharged and shot out of the Pistol aforesaid, by the Force of the Gunpowder aforesaid one mortal Wound in and through the Head of him the said Basti, of which said mortal Wound he the said Basti then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Basti, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Frith Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above writtten

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Frith Foreman
