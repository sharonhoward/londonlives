City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of October in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Wardale then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Askwith , Ebenezer Taylor , John Owen , William Hart . Richard Butler . George Jeremy , Thomas Cox  John Margram , Collet Birney , John Clarke , Thomas Moore , Henry Wood , and Joseph Hayes , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ann Wardale came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Ann Wardale not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the fifteenth day of October in the Year aforesaid, into a certain Water Tub in the Kitchen of John Andrews situate in Bridges Street in the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did Court and throw herself, by reason of which said Ca ting Throwing She the said Ann Wardale in the Water in the said Watertub was then and there Suffocated and Drowned of which said Suffocation and Drowning, she the said Wardale then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Ann Wardale no being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid, came to her Death.

In Witness whereof, as well the said Coroner , as the said Samuel Askwith  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above mentioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Saml Askwith Foreman
