City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of October in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of John Baker then and there lying dead, upon the oath of John Hall , John Beardmore , John Twigg , David Lecount , John Wynn , Richard Tomkins , John Mew , John Stephens , John Weatherby , Richard Shute , William Storer and Thomas Carter good and lawful Men the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Baker came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Baker a Laborer on the Eighth day of October in the Year aforesaid being driving a loaded Waggen drawn by four Horses in the Road at the End of Eaten Street Pimlico in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Man was then driving a certain Empty Cart drawn by three Horses in the said Road near unto the said Waggon It so happened that the said John Baker then and there Accidentally casually and by Misfortune was thrown down to the Ground and that the off Wheel of said Cart so drawn as aforesaid passed over the Belly and Breast on the Ride side of him the said John Baker , by means whereof the said John Baker did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive divers Bruises, and Fracture of the Ribs, in and upon the Right side of him the said John Baker , of which said mortal Bruises and Fracture he the said John Baker languished about two Hours, and then and there of the mortal Bruises and Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Baker in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death: and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Hall Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Hall Foreman}
