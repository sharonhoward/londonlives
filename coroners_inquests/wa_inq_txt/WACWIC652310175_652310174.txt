City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of May in the thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Ortan then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Thomas Lyne , Thomas Bland , John Burnell John Fitch , Alexander Cunning , Thomas Percival William Hobb , Francis Waters , William Jackson , John Tibbs John Curtis , John Cundell and Philip Sutton good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Orton came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said James Orton not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted on the said thirteenth day of May in the year aforesaid at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid with a certain Razor made of Iron and Steel the Throat or Gullet of him the said James Orton did then and there strike stab and trate thereby then and there giving into himself with the Razor aforesaid in or upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said James Orton one Mortal Wound of the length of Four Inches and of the Depth of one Inch of which said mortal Wound he the said James Orton then and there instantly died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Orton not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding But Lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself,

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Lyne  Foreman of the said Jurors  on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos Lyne Foreman
