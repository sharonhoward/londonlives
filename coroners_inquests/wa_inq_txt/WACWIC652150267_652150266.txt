City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of August in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great- Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Rachael Watkins then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Mimms William Williams , John Beck , Richard Sherman , Robert Burn , Henry Neale , Thomas Moon , Duncan Macintosh , James Fox , John Martin , William Plashet , and James Julian . good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Rachael Watkins came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Rachael Watkins on the First day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid to wit at the Dwelling House of John Watkins her Husband in Cortorel Street there situate accidentally Casually and by Misfortune full from the Top of the Stairs leading the Cellar of the said House to the bottom of the said Stairs on the Stone Paving, by Means whereof she the said Rachel Watkins then and there Received a violent and Mortal Bruise and Concussion on the left side of the Head. of her the said Rachael Watkins , of which violent and Mortal Bruise and Concussion she the said Rachael Watkins then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Rachael Watkins in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Robert Mourn Foreman
