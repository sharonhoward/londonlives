City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of October in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of George Clay an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Sheperd , Richard Sewet , Joseph Massingham , George Lamb , Benjamin Frears , Philip Burkinyoung , Richard Eaton , William Francis , George Waye , William London , Abraham Skegg , John Quarterman , James Duncomb , James Keys , Moses Philips , George Johnston , William Sheffield and Thomas Bryan , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said, George Clay came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said George Clay the Infant on the Eighth day of October in the year aforesaid, being in the Room or appartment of Henry Lediard on the First Floor in the Dwelling House of Charles Payne , situate in Wardour Street in the Parish of St. Ann within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the lower Sash in one of the Windows in the said Room being up, It so happened that the said George Clay then and there accidentally casually and by misfortune fellows of and through the said Window into the said street, and thereby did then and there receive me Mortal Bruise and feature in and upon the forepart of the Head of him the said George Clay , of which said mortal Bruise and Fracture he the said George Clay did languish about one Hour, and thereof then died, And the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said George Clay in manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally casually and by in fortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Sheperd Foreman of the said Jurors on the Behalf of himself and the rest of his said Follows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the day year and I abovementioned.

Tho Prickard Coroner

John Foreman
