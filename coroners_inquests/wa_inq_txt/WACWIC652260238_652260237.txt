City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of April in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Allen then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Monteath , Simon Wilson , John Evans , John Mc.Intosh , Thomas Hawkes , William Bindon , John Bride , John Green , Miles Rowlindson , John Goodson , George Ridley , Peter Pike , James Brenmer , Edward Shiliker , Timothy Essex . and Stephen Hart good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Allen came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Allen on the Sixth day of April in the Year aforesaid, about the Hour of Twelve in the Night being asleep near the five side in the Publick Tap Room of John Amphlett  Victualler situate and being in the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County afore And that Richard Tidey a Laborer being Likewise with divers other Persons in the said Taproom, And that the said Richard Tidey in Friendship endeavoured to awake the said Thomas Allen to got into Bed, That the said Thomas Allen awoke in a Passion and did then and there with his left Hand strike the said Richard Tidey whereupon the said Richard Tidey with his Right Hand did Strike the said Thomas upon his left Eye. and that the said Thomas Allen thereby Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of the said Richard Tidey received a Mortal Fracture over the lift Eye of him the said Thomas Allen of which said Mortal Fracture he the said Thomas Allen from the said sixth day of April in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, and at divers other Parishes, until the Ninth day of the same Months in the same Year did languish and languishing did live, On which said Ninth day of April in the Year aforesaid, he the said Thomas Allen at the said Parish of St.James within the Liberty and County aforesaid, of the Mortal Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, That the said Thomas Allen in manner and by the Means aforesaid. Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of him the Richard Tidey came to his Death.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Henry Monteath Foremans of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above mentioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Henry Monteath Foreman
