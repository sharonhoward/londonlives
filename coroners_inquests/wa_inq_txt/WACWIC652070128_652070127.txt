City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of March in the Seventh year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Thomas Price then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of George Peisley , Thomas Crofts , Thomas Gould , Edmond Lucas , William Maxfield , George Baggott , Richard Bullock , George Smith , George Kershaw , John Collins , Michael Pieret , James Williamson , Edward Webb , David Lewis , Nicolas Aresti , and John Drenkath good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Price came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Price , on the Thirteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid in a certain Stable Yard situate in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, being Clipping the Hair from the Heels of a certain Bay Gelding, with a pair of Scissars, It so happened that the said Bay Gelding, with his Near hinder Foot, did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune kick and strike the said Thomas Price , in and upon the left side of his Head, by means whereof the said Thomas Price did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one Mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of the Head of him the said Thomas Price , of which said mortal Fracture he the said Thomas Price then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Thomas Price Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, came to his Death, and not otherwise, And that the said Bay Horse moved to the Death of the said Thomas Price ; and is of the Value of Six Pounds, and the Property and in the Possession of Sir Edward Dering of New Burlington Street in the said Parish and County Baronet, or of his Assigns. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said George Peisley foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

George Peisley Foreman
