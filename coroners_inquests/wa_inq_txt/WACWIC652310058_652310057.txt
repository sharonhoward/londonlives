City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the First day of March in the Thirt first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Hull then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Benjamin Clarkson , Alexander Henderson , Thomas Brickdon Richard Harvey , Thomas Johnston , James Fryer , William Thompson Robert Mitchell , Henry Slack , Robert Clarks , Benjamin Quest and Abraham Musgrave good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Hull came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty sixth day of February in the Year aforesaid the said Sarah Hull being alone in her Lodging Room in the Dwelling House of Mr. Poulton situate in Chapel Court in the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid and there sitting near the Fire It so happened that the Clothes which the said Sarah Hull then and there had on her Body Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune last five by means whereof she the said Sarah Hull was then and there violently burnt in and upon her neck Face Arms and the upper part of her Body, of which said burning she the said Sarah Hull at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, from the said Twenty sixth day of February in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty seventh day of the same Month languished and lived on which said Twenty seventh day of February in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid she the said Sarah Hull of the mortal burning aforesaid did die: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Sarah Hull in manner and by means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise;

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Benjamin Clarkson  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

B: Clarkson Foreman
