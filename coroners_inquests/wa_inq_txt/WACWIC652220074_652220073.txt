City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eight day of April in the twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Edward Chamberlayne Esqr. then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Alexander Lowe , Stephen Clarke , Nathaniel Mann , William King , Thomas Langton , James Bean  Joseph Greaves  John Noble , John Pool , John Eades  Mark Taylor  John Burgess , John Count Thomas Appleby  William Chishold , William Hollier  William Brookes and Thomas Whitehead good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the king, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Chamber layne came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Edward Chamberlayne not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted on the fourth day, of April in the Year aforesaid from and out of a certain one pair of Stairs window in the Treasury Whitehall in the parish and County aforesaid unto and upon the Gravel Walk in the Garden of the Right Honourable Frederick Lord North id violently cast and the said Edward Chamberlayne did then and there receive a mortal Injury in the Spinal Marrow about the Middle of the Neck of him the said Edward Chamberlayne, of which said mortal Injury he the said Edward Chamberlayne at the said from the fourth day of April in the Year aforesadi until the Sixth day of the same Month and Year did languish and languishing did live on which said Sixth day of April he the said Edward Chamberlayne at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid of the mortal Injury aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Edward Chamberlayne not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said Alexander Lower foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Thos Prickard Coroner

Alexr: Lowe Foreman
