City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .}to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of May in the thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Miss Elizabeth Stewart then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Robert Hawkeslay , William Perkins , William Vinson Thomas Stokes , John Warne , William Payne , John Baster John Calmore , John Robinson , James Gibbs , Thomas Cowll Robert Shephard , Thomas Warren , James Waldie , William Watson Francis Cluley and Joseph Sharp good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Stewart came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Seventh day of May in the Year aforesaid Elizabeth Stewart being alone in her Bed Chamber in the Dwelling House of James Duff situate in Cecil Street in the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid then and there standing before the Fire It so happened that the Linen Cloaths which she then had on her Body Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took fire whereby the said Elizabeth Stewart was then and there Violently burnt in and upon her Bach Loins Sides Shoulders and Neck of which said burning she the said Elizabeth Stewart at the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Seventh day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Ninth day of the same Month in the same Year languished and languishing did live On which said Ninth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid she the said Elizabeth Stewart of the Mortal burning aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors on their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Elizabeth Stewart in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Hawksley  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their present have to this in Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above mentioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Robt. Hawksley Foreman
