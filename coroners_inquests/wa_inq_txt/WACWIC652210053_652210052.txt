City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of February in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Jacob Davies then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Minnitt , Edward Arnold , Samuel Sprang , William Escott , William Taylor Alexander Grant  Robert Mc.Erson , James Mitchell , Richard Bear , James Baker , Adam Fuller and George Fry good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Jacob Davies came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Jacob Davies on the Eleventh day of February in the year aforesaid being in a certain Boat Sailing upon the River Thames at Mill Bank in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid It so happened that the said Boat Overset, and that the said Jacob Davies Accidentlly Casually and by Misfortune in the Waters of the said River was then and there suffocated and Drowned of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Jacob Davies then and there died, and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Jacob Davies in Manner and by the Means aofresaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Minnitt Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands in their Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner . John Minnitt Foreman
