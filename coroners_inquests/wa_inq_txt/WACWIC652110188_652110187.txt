City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  

 An Inquitition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of May in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Forbes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Noble , John Ellis , Joseph Skinner , James Chisholm , Thomas Watson , Thomas Fidgon , William Pursell , Thomas George , John Emerson , William Iles Richard Strange and Silvester Freeman good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Forbes came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Forbes on the Seventh day of May (between the Hours of Nine and Ten in the Evening) in the Year aforesaid, being in a certain New Street called Adam Street in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there being Intoxicated with Liquor and walking by the Dwelling House of John Jones a Pastry cook in the said Street, the Iron Rails before the said House not having been put up, It so happened that the said Thomas Forbes Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did then and there fall out of and from the said Street upon certain Iron Spikes in the Area of the said John Jones , whereby the said Thomas Forbes did then and there receive one mortal Wound in and upon his Right side, of which said mortal Wound he the said Thomas Forbes at the said Parish of St Martin in the Fields and also at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish for the space of the Hours, and then at the said Parish of St. Margaret Westminster of the mortal Wound aforesaid, he the said Thomas Forbes did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Forbes in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise, In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said John Noble Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition, set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

John Noble Foreman
