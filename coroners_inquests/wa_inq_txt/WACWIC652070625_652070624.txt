City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Intended, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirtieth day of October in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Richards then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James King , John Wilson , Thomas Sims , James Horner , Thomas Gould , Peter Gally , James Simmons , Adams Bowes , William Gilks , James Howell , Peter Fowler , Samuel Smith , Thomas Hill , John Fenton , John Stockman , John Price and John Maclane , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Richards came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said Thomas Richard a Labourer, on the Twenty eighth day of October in the Year aforesaid being unloading a certain Cart, loaden with Lime, in Milford Lane in the Parish of St. Clement Danes in the said County of Middlesex , It so happened that the said Thomas Richards Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, by lifting and carrying certain Bags filled with Lime, out of the said Cart, violently Strained himself, and broke one of the Vessels in the Lungs of him the said Thomas Richards , from which a great Quantity of Blood then and there issued, And that the said Thomas Richards of the Strain and Rupture of the Vessel aforesaid, from the said Twenty eighth day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty ninth day of the same Month and Year, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty ninth day of October in the Year aforesaid he the said Thomas Richards of the great Loss and Fusion of Blood occasioned by the Rupture of the Vessel aforesaid at the said Parish of St. James within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Richard Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James King Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho, Prickard Coroner .

Jas: King Foreman
