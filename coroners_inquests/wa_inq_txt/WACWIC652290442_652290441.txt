An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty eighth day of July in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Daniel Blundell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Rogers , William Macdonald , Thomas White John Wolfe , Thomas Kettle , William Weston , William Warner , Charles Greenfield , John White , Thomas Moore , James Bell , John Blackburn , Benjamin Richardson and Charles Vyse good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Daniel Blundell came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Daniel Blundell on the twenty seventh day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist (to wit) at Milbank going into the River Thames there to Bathe himself It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune he the said Daniel Blundell was in the Water of the said River then and there suffocated and Drowned of which said suffocation and Drowing he the said Daniel Blundell then and there instantly died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say tha the said Daniel Blundell in Manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Rogers Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Rogers Foreman
