City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of April in the twenty eighth Year of the Regin of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Letitia Drink water then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Jordan , William Atwood , James Cosby , Philip Townsend , John Watson , David Allen , William Steel , John Marriott , John Lane , George Miller , John Giles , and Joseph Percival good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Letitia Drink water came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Letitia Drink water in the eighth day of March in the Year aforesaid between the Hours of Nine and ten at Night was walking in Princes Street at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid and that a Man whose name is not known was there driving a certain cart drawn by Two or Three Horses out of Queen Street into Princes Street aforesaid. It so happened that the said Letitia Drink water then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down and that the near wheel of the said cart passed upon the left arm of the said Letitia Drink water then lying on the Ground as aforesaid by means where of the said Letitia Drinkwater then and there Received one Mortal wound in and upon the said left Arm and a violent Bruise on the left side of her Body of which said mortal wound and Bruise she the said Letitia Drink water at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Eighth day of March in the Year aforesaid until the fourth day of april in the same year did languish and live on which said fourth day of april at the said Parish Liberty and County she the said Letitia Drinkwater of the mortal wound and Bruise aforesaid did die.

And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Letitia Drinkwater in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Jordan Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard  Coroner

William Jordan Foreman
