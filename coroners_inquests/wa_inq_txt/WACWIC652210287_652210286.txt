City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of June in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Mr Thomas Drake Deputy to Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Samuel Calver then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Lockie , William Rankin , George White , Aaron Loton , John Young , James Meason Edward Jones , Henry Banton , James Gordon , John Keelly , Robert Bradfield Hugh Kenneday , Richard Shelton , Esau Alford , James Abbott , and James Massey good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Samuel Calver came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Samuel Calver on the fourteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid being on a certain place called George and Rough Yard there situate and being Intoxicated with Liquor it so happened that the said Samuel Calver then and there fell to the Ground and then and there by the Fall aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune broke his Leg and thereby received a Compound Fracture in his Leg of which said Compound Fracture and also of a Fever occasioned thereby he the said Samuel Calver the said Fourteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did Languish and languishing did Live until the Twelfth day of June in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid on which said Twelfth day of June at the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Samuel Calver of the said Compound Fracture on his Leg and Fever did Dye. And to the Jurors aforesaid on their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Samuel Calver in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the Deputy to the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Thomas Drake  Deputy to Tho. Prickard  Coroner Willm Loc Foreman
