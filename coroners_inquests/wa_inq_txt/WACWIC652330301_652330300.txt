City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Dwelling House of William Jacques in petty France  Parish of St Margarets  Sign of Adam & Eve within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty seventh day of May 1793 in the thirty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Harwood then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names act hereunder written and Seals affixed

 good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Harwood came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Thomas Harwood on the twenty third day of May in the Year aforesaid being behind a Chaise near Kensington in the County aforesaid, It so happened, that accidentally casually and by Misfortune, he the said Thomas Harwood fell from between the said le haire, & that the hind wheel of the said lehaise went over the Body of him the said Thomas Harwood , by Means whereof two of the Ribs on the left Side of him the said Thomas Harwood were fractured and the Lung wounded, of which said Fracture and wound as aforesaid her the said Thomas Harwood , in St. Georges's Hospital on the following day, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Harwood in Manner and by the Means aforesaid acci dentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. An Witness whereof as well the said coroner as the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day Year and place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }

Wm. Mourn Foreman} John Bewnell
