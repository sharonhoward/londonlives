City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty Eighth day of June in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Osman then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of William Turner , James Bridie , richad Pickard John Percy . William Hewett , Thomas Jackson , Thomas More  William Fenn , Henry List , Robert Hayden , John Pather and Thomas Stephen Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Robert Osman came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Robert Osman not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatick and distracted, on the Twenty seventh day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields in the County aforesaid, out of and through a certain Window in the Lodging Room of the said Robert Osman in the Dwelling House of Mrs. Drummond situate in New Street upon the stone Pavement in the said Street did cast and throw himself whereby the said Robert Osman did then and there receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the back part of his Head of which said mortal Fracture he the said Robert Osman on the said Twenty seventh day of June in the Year aforesaid, at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say, that the said Robert Osman in Manner and by the means aforesaid not being of sound mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted did kill himself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Turner  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and place abovementioned.

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Willam Turner  Foreman
