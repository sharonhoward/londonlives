City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of October in the twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third , by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner, of our said, Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of George Stubins then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Matthew Hade  James Elphinston , David Duncan , Joseph Harper , Henry Chapman , William Stevens  Robert Jones , Joseph Taylor , Henry Izard , Thomas Fisher  William Morrison  Joseph Gotterson William Russell  Benjamin Wall , William Lowe  William Woodward Richard Hamly and John Peach good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said George Stubins came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said George Stubins on the said Nineteenth day of October in the Year aforesaid being in his Lodging Room in the dwelling House of Thomas Norton situate in Bedfordbury at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid That the said George Stubins then and there fell down and thereby Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received a mortal Concussion in and upon the Brain of him the said George Stubins of which said mortal Concussion he the said George Stubins then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon aforesaid do say, that the said George Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Matthew Slade  foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Matw Slade Foreman
