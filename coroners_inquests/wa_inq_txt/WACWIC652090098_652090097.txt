City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of March in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Welsh then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Wilson , Robert Parker , James Oliver , Nathaniel Clayton , John Clark , Thomas Turtle , James Brynnam , William Lockie , James Garden , Thomas White , John Carrick , John Wilkinson , Lewis Sulsh and James Young good and lawful Men of the Said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Robert Welsh came to h is Death, do upon their Oath Say, the said Robert Welsh a Glazier on the Seventh day of March in the Year aforesaid being at Work in cleaning a Window in the Back Front of the Queen's House situate in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the County aforesaid, and then and there standing upon the Sole, on the outside, of the said Window, It so happened that the said Robert Welsh Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down from the said Window unto the Ground, and thereby did then and there receive one mortal Fracture upon his Forehead over the left Eye, and also a violent Concussion in and upon his Brain, of which said Mortal Fracture and Concussion he the said Robert Welsh at the Westminster Hospital situate in the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid did languish about four Hours, and then and thereof the mortal Fracture upon Concussion aforesaid, he the said Robert Welsh did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Robert Welsh Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Henry Wilson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Henry Wilson Foreman
