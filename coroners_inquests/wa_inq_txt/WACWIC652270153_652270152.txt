City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of March in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Walter Warrell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Archibald Warren , Ricd Guy , Richard Hopkins , Samuel Hyth, John Amoss , Thomas Saunders , Peter Price , James Knox Christian Thiele , Henry Pitkin , Nathaniel Davies , John Lomack John Baker , Thomas Williams  Christopher James , Edward Ashton Jas. Ansel , Richard Griffith & Isaac Boyce good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Walter Warrell came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , that the said Walter Warrell not being of sound Mind Memory or Understanding but Lunatic and distracted on the seventh day of March in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty & County aforesaid a certain Pistol charged with Gun powder & a Leaden Bullet which he the said Walter Warrell then & there had & held in his Hand to & against the left side of him the said Walter Warrell did then & there Sheet off and Discharge by Means whereof he the said Walter Warrell did then & there give unto himself with the Leaden Bullett aforesaid so discharged & shot out of the Pistol aforesaid by the Force of the Gunpowder aforesaid in & upon the Left side of him the said Walter Warrell one Mortal Wound of the breadth of one Inch & of the Depth of four Inches of which said Mortal Wound he the said Walter Warrell at the Parish & in the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Seventh Day of March until the Eighth day of the same Month in the Year aforesaid did Languish and Live on which said Eighth day of March in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish Liberty & County he the said Walter Warrell of the Mortal Wound aforesaid did die and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Walter Warrell not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and Distracted in Manner & by the Means aforesaid did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as th said Archebald Warren Foreman of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year & Place above Mentioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner Archibald Warren Foreman
