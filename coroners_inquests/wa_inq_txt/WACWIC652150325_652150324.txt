City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighteenth day of September in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Francis Allaway then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Melvill , John Tucker , Robert Boyle , John Harris , George Tarr , Alexander Moore , John Corbitt  Charles Dobson , William Arpin , Henry Emmit , Thomas Hilsell  Archibald Willock , John Wilson , James Oliver and John Hadley good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Francis Allaway came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Francis Allaway, a Lad of the Age of thirteen Years, on the Sixteenth day of September in the year aforesd. being sitting upon a certain Wooden Dresser in the Warehouse of Francis Takes situate in Horton Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there wantonly and for diversion playing with a certain piece of small Cord, which was fastened to a Nail drive into the Wainscot in the said Warehouse, and putting the same about his Neck, It so happened that the said Francis Allaway slipped down from the said Dresser unto the Floor, and thereby Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, with the said small Cord, so being about his Neck as aforesaid, was then and there hanged suffocated and strangled, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling, he the said Francis Allaway then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Francis Allaway in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Melvill Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Robt. Melwill Foreman
