City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fourth day of May in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Daniel Burn then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Jones , William Wray , William Watson , John Haines , George Tupper , Samuel Floyd James Norris , Samuel Smallman , John Coates , Richard Serace , Robert Holland , Robert Potter , Francis Toplady and Joseph Whalley good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Daniel Burn came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That John Carryl late of the Parish of St. Mary le bone in the said County of Middlesex Laborer on the Eleventh day of May in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the said Parish of St. Mary le bone in the County aforesaid in and upon the said Daniel Burn in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King their and there being feloniously did make an Assault, And that the said John Carryl with both his Hands him the said Daniel Burn then and there feloniously did cast and throw to the Ground, and him the said Daniel Burn , then and there lying upon the Ground, with the Feet of him the said John Carryl , in and upon the lower part of the Belly of him the said Daniel Burn , then and there did strike and kick, thereby then and there giving unto him the said Daniel Burn one mortal Bruise in and upon the lower part of the Belly of him the said Daniel Burn , of which said mortal Bruise he the said Daniel Burn at the Parish of St. Giles in the Fields in the said County of Middlesex , and also at St. George's Hospital Situate in the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, from the said Eleventh day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty first day of the same Month and Year, did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty first day o May in the Year aforesaid at the Hospital aforesaid, he the said Daniel Burn of the mortal Bruise aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Carryl him the said Daniel Burn in Manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously did hill and slay, against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity And that the said John Carryl at the time of Committing the Felony and Manslaughter aforesaid, or at any time Since, had no Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements within the said Liberty or else where to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Jones Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Jones Foreman
