Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit

An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of Saint Peter at Westminster the Twenty Seventh day of March in the first year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid, Upon a Veiw of the Body of William Rixon then and there Lying Dead by the Oaths of Abraham Chalburn , William Meares , Benjamin Hitchcock , Thomas Dicks , James Fryer , Francis Mollison , Edward Plaistedd , Samuel Norgrave , John Jones , Mark Potter , Christopher Nettleton , Thomas Harvey , and Edward Woodcock Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for an Sovereign Lord the King how when and where and in what manner the said William Rixon came to his Death say upon their Oaths, That on Thursday the twenty sixth day of March instant the said William Rixon being in the Burying ground belonging to the Parish of St. George hanover Square in the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being digging of a Grave in the said Burying Ground, it so happened that the ground on the side of the Grave fell in and upon the said William Rixon in such Quantities as entirely to cover the said William Rixon by means whereof the said William Rixon was Smothered and Stifted and then and there instantly Died, and so the said Jurors upon their Oaths aforesaid do say that the said William Rixon the day and year aforesaid in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid in manner aforesaid unfortunately and accidentally came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition put their hands and Seals the day and year and at the Place abovesaid

Jno: Feary  Coroner .

Abrm Chatbush Foreman
