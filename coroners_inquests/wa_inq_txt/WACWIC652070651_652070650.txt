City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of November in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Hyde an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Lampard . Lewis Lubrey , William Clay , Samuel Mitchell , William Suckly , William Chapman , Alexander Smith , John Burgess , James Jones , John Watkins , James Bean , and Charles Miller good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Hyde came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said William Hyde an Infant of the Age of Three Years, on the Twenty first day of November in the Year aforesaid, being alone, near the Fire side, in a certain Room or Apartment in the Dwelling House of John Draper situate in Dartmouth  Row, in the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the Clothes which the said William Hyde  the Infant then and there had on his Body, Accidently Causually and by Misfortune took Fire, by Reason whereof, and by the Flame arising from the said Fire, the Belly, Stomach, Arms, Neck and Face of him the said William Hyde were then and there greatly scorched and burnt, of which said Burning he the said William Hyde from the said Twenty first day of November in the Year aforesaid, until the Twenty second day of the same Month and Year, at the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty second day of November in the Year aforesaid, he the said William Hyde at the Parish and in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Hyde Accidently Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Lampard Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Lampard Foreman
