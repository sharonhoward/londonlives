City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of July in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of  Heller Thoume then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Hales James Gordon , John Brook , William Rooks , John Goodall , William Busey , John Gibson , John Austin , Nicholas Pattison Wheeler , William Longton , John Graton , John Davis , and William Godbold good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Mean the said Heller Thoume came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Heller Thoume on the thirteenth day of July in the Year afoersaid being with Ralph Hudson , who was driving a certain Empty Cart drawn by three Horses in the Publick Road leading from Pancrass to London in the County aforesaid, And the said Heller Thoume being in Liquor and endeavouring to stop the said Horses, then frightened and running away with the said Cart in the said Road, fell down unto the Ground near the Wheels of the said Cart, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and Misfortune the Near Wheel of the said Cart did then and there go upon and pass over the Body of the said Heller Thoume, by Means whereof the said Heller Thoume then and there received a mortal Bruise across his Leins, of which said mortal Bruise he the said Heller Thoume languished about the space of four Hours, and then at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid of the mortal Bruise aforesaid die die. And to the Jurors aforesaid lying Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Heller Thoume in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. And that the said Near Wheel of the said Cart, so drawn as aforesaid, was moving to the death of the said Heller Thoume, and is of the Value of Five Shillings, and teh Property and in the Possession of Thomas Cranage of Hungerford Market in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields in the said County of Middlesex Coal Merchant or his Assigns, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Hales Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner John Hales Foreman.
