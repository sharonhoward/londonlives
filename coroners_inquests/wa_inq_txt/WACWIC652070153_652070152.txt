City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex To wit. }  In Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Parish of St. Ann within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twenty third Day of March in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & liberty on View of the Body of Magnus Tyro then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Thomas , Joseph Fisher , John Wall , Joseph Rebanks , John Barnett , William Paul , Joseph Harden , William Groves , Matthew Green , Henry Finch , John Ray , John Swetman , Thomas Scott , John Allison William Oversmith , Samuel Jackman , henry Edgeome , Arthur Hewetson , Robert Mendhurst & Robert Swarbrook , good and lawful Men of the said liberty, duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said Magnus Tyro came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Magnus Tyro on the Twenty first day of March in the year aforesaid, died suddenly in a certain Street called Prices Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, that no Marks of Violence appeared on the Body of the said Magnus Tyro, and that the said Magnus Tyro departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way, and not by any Hurt or Injury received from any Person or Persons whatsoever. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Thomas Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself, and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Jas. Thomas foreman
