City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of May in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Kelly then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Baythorn , Richard Townsend , Abraham Mathew , William Weaver , John Nichols , Thomas Kent , William Patterson , John Foreman , Robert Harris , Joseph Pearson  James Walden , Henry Williams and John Scott good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James kelly came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Kelly on the First day of May in the Year aforesaid being driving a certain Cart loaded with Coals and drawn by three Horses under the Adelphi towards George Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there Riding upon the Copse with the leader in his Hand, It so happened that by a sudden jolt the said James kelly fell off and from the said Copse unto the Ground near unto the Near Wheel of the said Cart, and that the said near Wheel then and there accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the left Thigh and the leftside of the Body of him the said James Kelly , by reason where of the said James Kelly then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon the said left Thigh, and Bruises in his Body, of which said mortal Fracture and Bruises he the said James Kelly then and there died: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Kelly in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward Baythorn Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner Edwd. Buythorn Foreman
