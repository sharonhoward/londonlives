An Inquitition Indented, taken for our Sovererign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Field within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster , in the Counrty of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of March in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Michael Clarkson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Charles Rymir , Hugh Donwell , Joseph Treble , Daniel Payan , Richard Hambly , William Dood , Samuel Tipping Richard Hilsley , Edward Turner , George Downing  Thomas Dubbon , Charles Presbury , Robert More , John Goll , John Lovell , William Brown , John Anderson , William Tumbell , and John Price , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by that what Means the said Michael Clarkson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Michael Clarkson not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted on the said Thirteentn day of March in the Year aforesaid into a certain Water Tub or Butt standing in the Yard adjoining to the dwelling House of the said Michael Clarkson Situate and being in New street in the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, there did Cast and there himself by means of Which said Casting and throwing he the said Michael Clarkson in the Water in the said Tub was then and there suffocated and Drowned of Which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Michael Clarkson then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their oath aforesaid, do say that the said Michael Clarkson not being of Sound Mind Memory and understanding But Lunatick and distracted, in memory and by the Means aforesaid, did drowned and hill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Charles Byner Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the Rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above mentioned

Tho Prickard Coroner.

Chas. Rymer Foreman
