An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the House of John Morris Sign of the Bulls head  Little Windmill Street Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the seventh day of August 1794 in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Moses Dipple then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written and Seals affixed good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Moses Dipple came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Moses Dipple on the Sixth day of August in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To wit at the Dwelling House of him the said Moses Dipple in Windmill Street aforesaid, he the said Moses Dipple died by the visitation of God in a natural way and not otherwise. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said Jurors have to theis Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day year and place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }

W Caborn Foreman Wm Walker B. Davis Thomas Wright K
