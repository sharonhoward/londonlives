City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of June in the Seventh year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of Daniel Stripling then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Featherston Francis Lindsay , John Keith , Isaac Meals , John Cuttell , Philip Lecreman , William Faithorn , Thomas Baxter , Isaac Brown , Edmund Ball , John Nicholson , John Roberts , John Price , James Wilson , Benjamin Farelam , Jacob Shieft , Henry Stihoff , Christopher Lewis & Thomas Hall , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what Means the said Daniel Stripling came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Daniel Stripling on the Seventh day of June in the Year aforesaid being on Board a certain Barge leaden with Dung, and lying in and upon the River Thames near Black Lion Stairs in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being then and there in the Cabin of the said Barge and the Door street, It so happened that the said Barge then and there Sunk in the said River, whereby the Cabin, wherein the said Daniel Stripling then was, became full of Water, And that the said Daniel Stripling Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in the Water in the said Cabin, was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Daniel Stripling then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Daniel Stripling Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his Death, and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Samuel Featherston Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition Set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Saml. Featherston Foreman
