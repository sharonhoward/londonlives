City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of February, in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Edward Crabb then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Skelton , William Sills , Alexander Smart James Morris , William Read , Richard Owen , John Sibthorp , George Grimet , John Donalson , James Wright and Thomas Grant good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Crabb came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Edward Crabb on the twenty seventh day of January in the Year aforesaid, being in his Lodging room in apartment in a certain Stable Yard situate in Park Street, in the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there going down a Ladder in the Dark and being in Liquor, It so happened that the said Edward Crabb Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down from the Top of the said Ladder upon the Stone pavement in the said Stable Yard, and thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received a mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Edward Crabb at the Parish and in the LIberty and County aforesaid from the said twenty seventh day of January in the Year aforesaid, until the first day of February in the same Year, did languish and languishing did live, On which said first day of February in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Edward Crabb of the mortal Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Edward Crabb in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Skelton Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner . J Skelton Foreman
