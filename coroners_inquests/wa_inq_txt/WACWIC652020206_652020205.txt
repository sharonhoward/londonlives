City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the Kingn at the Parish of St Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St Peter in Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fourth day of June , in the Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Coroner of Our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Ryley then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Fellows  John Lawrence  Thomas Bayne , James Richard Christopher , Wilfrid Sadler  Charles Scott , Mattias Fleming , Angus Duthy , John Morton  John Styles  Richard Thomas , Isaah Smith  John Lewis  James Robinson , Peter Hendly , John Bentley  William Lawrence  William Cobb  Samuel Hill and Robert Edwards good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, and who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King, when how and by what means the said John Ryley came to his Death, Do upon their Oath say,That the said John Ryley not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatich and Distracted on the twenty third day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid in the County aforesaid one End of a certain Leather Strap unto a certain upright Post standing in a Stable Yard situate at the East End of Bennetts Court in  the Parish and County aforesaid, and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix and fasten, and thirteenth the said John Ryley himself did then and there hang suffocate and strangle Of which Hanging Suffocation and Stranging he the said John Ryley then and there instantly Died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their saith aforesaid do say that the said John Ryley not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted,in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did hill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Fellows Foreman on the behalf of himself and the rest of the Jurors, in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Thos. Prickard  Coroner

Thos. Fellows Foreman
