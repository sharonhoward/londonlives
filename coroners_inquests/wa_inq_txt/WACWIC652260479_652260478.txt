City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty ninth day of July in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Clydean then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Cock , James Randall , George Taylor Adam Armstrong  John Robinson , Anthony Fry , Lawrence Kite , Robert Minns , Robert Sampson , Francis Day , Alexander Bower. and William Ashdon good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how,and by what Means the said James Clydean came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Clydean on the Twenty seventh day of July in the Year aforesaid, being riding upon the Shaft of a certain Waggon in the Kings Highway at Pimlico , in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the Horses drawing the said Waggon turning about, It so happened, that the left Foot of the said James Clydean, was pressed between the fore Wheel and the Body of the said Waggon, and thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received Wounds and Bruises in and upon the said left Foot, of which said Mortal Wounds and Bruises in and upon the said left Clydean from the said Twenty seventh day of July in the Year, aforesaid, until the twenty eight day of the same Month and Year, at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty Eighth day of July in the Year aforesaid, he the said James Clydean at the said Parish of St. Margaret of the Wounds, and Bruises aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said James Clydean, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally, Casually, and by Misfortune came to his Death

In Witness whereof, as well the said Coroner , as the said Thomas Cock  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place above mentioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos Cock Foreman
