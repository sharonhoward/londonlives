City & Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Soverign Lord the king at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty fifth day of August in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Armstrong then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Payne , David Gordon , Joseph Smith , Edward Layton , Joseph Fox , John Waller , John Gibson , Robert Jones Thomas Dixon , William Simpson , John Burton , Thomas Hambleton Josiah Davis , Thomas Marriot , George Maddock , William Grant and Thomas Down good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by that what Means the said John Armstrong came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That on the Twenty fourth day of August in the Year aforesaid the said John Armstrong an Infant of the Age of Five Years being at Play with other Children at York Buildings Stairs, in the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid It so happened that the said John Armstrong Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Stairs into the River Thames , and in the Waters thereof was then and there suffocated and Drowned, of which said suffocation and Drowning he the said John Armstrong the Infant then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Armstrong in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said William Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition, set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Wm. Payne Foreman
