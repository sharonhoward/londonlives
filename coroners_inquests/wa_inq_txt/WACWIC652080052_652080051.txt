City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Ninth day of February in the Eighth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defenders of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of a new born Female Child then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Melvill , Henry Emmit , Christopher West , William Watts , David Watts , Thomas Clean , William Appleby , William Wells , Clement Cook , John Willoughby , Robert Boyle , John Archer , Thomas Fordham , William Pittman , John Hammack and James Ivory good and law full Mon of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when he want by what Means the said new born Female Child came to her Death doupon their Oath say, That Mary Robinson late of the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Seventh day of February in the Year aforesaid, being big with the said Female Child, afterwards, to wit, on the same day in the same Year, in the Privy or Necessary house, within the dwelling House of Elizabeth Cooper situate and being in Horton Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid the said Female Child, alone and secretly from her Body, did bring forth alive; which said Female Child by the Laws and Customs of this Realm was a Bastard. and that the said Mary Robinson not having the Fear of God before her Eyes, but moved and seduced by the Instigation of the Devil, afterwards, to wit, on the same Day and Year, with Force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, in and upon the said Female Bastard Child so alive and in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously wilfully and of his Malice forethought did make an Assault And that she the said Mary Robinson with both her hands about the Neck of her the said Female Bastard Child then and there fixed, she the said Female Bastard Child, did then and there feloniously wilfully and of her Malice forethought, choak, strangle, and suffocate, of which said choaking strangling and suffocation she the said Female Bastard Child then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Mary Robinson her the said Female Bastard Child, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, feloniously wilfully and her Malice fore thought, did hill and murder, against the Peace of our said Lord the King, his Crown and Dignity. And that the said Mary Robinson at the time of committing the Felony and Murder aforesaid, or at any Fine since, had no Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements within the said Liberty of Westminster of elsewhere, to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said Robert Melvill Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of him self and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Robt Melvill Foreman
