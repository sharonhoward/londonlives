City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Knights bridge in the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventeenh day of November in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Chilton then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Chandler  Henry Higham  George Fowler  Thomas Grainger  James Jones Reuben Jackson  John Stevens  William Gee  Henry Phillips  William Cobb , Thomas Barber and Ralph Mills good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged, to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Chilton came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Chilton on the the Sixteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid was standing at the End of Old Bond Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and that a woman unknown was then crossing the said Street before the said John Chilton , That one John Baskell was then carefully driving a Coach drawn by two Horses out of Piccadilly towards the Door of Mr. Bignell situate in Old Bond Street aforesaid and that the said Woman unknown to avoid the Horses, turned back, and then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune threw the said John Chilton (who was very Old and inform) unto and against the Ground near unto the off Fore Wheel of the said Coach, and that the said Off Fore Wheel passed over the Body of the said John Chilton , lying upon the Ground as aforesaid, by Means whereof the said John Chilton then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received a mortal Fructure in and upon three of his Ribs on the left side, of which said mortal Fracture he the said John Chilton then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Chilton in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Edward Chandler Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands & Seals the Day Year an Place abovementioned

Thos. Prickard Coroner

Edwd Chandler Foreman
