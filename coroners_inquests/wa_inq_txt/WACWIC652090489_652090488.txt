City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit 

An & Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Seventh Day of December in the Tenth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Bigby then and there lying dead upon the Oath of Thomas Girdler , Charles Tutop , John Snow , William Bond , John Blatch , John Cartwright , William Roberts , Thomas Turtle , Jacob Knight Evan Davies  Thomas Wheeler , William Atwood , John Richards , John Medhurst , Abraham Terry and James Mason good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said John Bigby came to his Death do upon their Oath say that Matthew Kennedy late of the said Parish of St. Margaret in the City Liberty and County aforesaid Laborer, Patrick Kennedy late of the same Laborer, Michael Mc. Mahon late of the same Laborer and John Evans late of the same Laborer not having the Fear of God before their Eyes but being moved and seduced by the Instigation of the Devils, on the Twenty fourth day of December in the Year aforesaid, with Force and Arms, at the said Parish of St. Margaret in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, in and upon the said John Bigby in the Peace of God and of our said Sovereign Lord the King then and there being feloniously wilfully and of their Malice aforethought did make an Assault, And that the said Matthew Kennedy with a certain Iron Poker, of the Value of two Shillings, which he the said Mathews Kennedy then and there had and held in his Right Hand him the said John Bigby in and upon the hinder part of the Head of him the said John Bigby did then and there feloniously wilfully and of his Malice aforethought strike and beat, and that the said Matthew Kennedy with the Iron poker aforesaid by the Striking, and Beating aforesaid did then and there give unto him the said John Bigby in and upon the hinder part of the Head of him the said John Bigby one mortal wound and Fracture of which said mortal wound and Fracture he the said John Bigby on the said Twenty fourth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid in the City Liberty and County aforesaid for the space of three Hours did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty fourth day of December in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the City Liberty and County aforesaid he the said John Bigby of the mortal would and Fracture aforesaid did die And the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Patrick Kennedy Michael Mc Mahon and John Evans at the time of the committing of the Felony and Murder aforesaid feloniously wilfully and of their Malice aforethought were present abetting, aiding, assisting, comforting and maintaining the said Matthew Kennedy to hill and Murder the said John Bigby in Manner aforesaid; And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Matthew Kennedy  Patrick Kennedy Michael Mc. Mahon and John Evans him the said John Bigby in manner and by the means aforesaid feloniously wilfully and of their Malice afore thought did Kill and Murder, against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity; And that the said Matthew Kennedy  Patrick Kennedy Michael Mc Mahon and John Evans , nor either of them at the time of the doing and committing of the Felony and Murder aforesaid, or at any time since, had any Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements within the said Liberty, or elsewhere, to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Girdler Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Thos. Girdler Foreman
