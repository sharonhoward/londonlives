City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inqusition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourteenth day of April in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Morris then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Mortlock  Thomas Brewser  Ebenezer Bucklnand  William Davison  George Walker  Francis Mainy John Woodward  Thomas Williment  William Wheatley  Charles Lawton  George Hutchinson & William Allinder good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being, then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Morris came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Joseph Morris (suspected to have been murdered) on the thirteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid was found dead in the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid and that the said Joseph Morris suddenly departed this Life, by t he Visitation of God, in a natural Way, and not by any violence or Injury from any Person whatsoever to the Knowledge of the said Jurors In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Mortlock Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands & Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Wm Portlock  Foreman
