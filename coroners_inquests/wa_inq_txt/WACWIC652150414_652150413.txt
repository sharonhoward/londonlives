City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of December in the Sixteenth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so Forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Archibald Hannam then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Orme  Evan Morgan  Francis Taylor  John Jennings  William Oliver Thomas Kynaston  James Bathoc  Lewis Evans  John Partridge  Simon Earle , John Jefferys and William Pearson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Archibald Hanover came to his Death, do upon their Oath Say, That the said Archibald Hannam a Laborer on the Twenty second day of December in the Year aforesaid being at Work upon a New Building at Pimlico in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and being then and there carrying a Hod of Mortar upon a Scaffold on the outside of the said Building at the height of three Stories It so happened that the said Archibald Hannam then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said Seaffold unto the Ground and thereby then and there Accidentally casually and by Misfortune did receive divers Bruises in his Body of which said mortal Bruises he the said Archebald Hannam then and there died and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid on say that the said Archibald Hannam in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Edward Orme Foreman of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Ed: Orme Foreman
