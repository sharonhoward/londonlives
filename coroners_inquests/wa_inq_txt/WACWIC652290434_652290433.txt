City of Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex . } to wit.

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty seventh day of July in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Nathaniel Bargin then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Young , Thomas Cookes , John Moore , John Moore , John Gardner , William Cole , Joseph Tomkins Robert Tomson , Dignum Forbes , George Murphy Lewis Evans , William Payne , James Milton Charles Bower , John Manly , James Bussell , Hambleton Thomas Taylor and John Bess good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the king, when, how, and by what Means the said Nathaniel Bargin came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Nathaniel Bargin a Boy of the Age of Nine Years in the Twenty sixth day of July in the year aforesaid was playing with other Boys in and upon certain Coal Barges Floating upon the River Thames at Northumberland Whart in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid It so happened that in crossing the said Barges the said Nathaniel Bargin Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell into the River Thames and in the Water thereof was then and there suffocated and Downed of which said suffocation and drowning he the said Nathaniel Bargin then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Nathaniel Bargin in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Young Foreman of the said Jurors on he behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abvoementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Robt. Young Foreman
