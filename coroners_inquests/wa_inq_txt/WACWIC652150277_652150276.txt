City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margarets within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of August in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great- Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Dederick Lantware then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Wilson Andrew Robert , George Gally , James Wilson , Robert Bigby , Henry Wilson , John Webb . Thomas Langton Charles Thomas , Philip Walker , Thomas Moses , Thomas Hunt , Thomas Whiting , William Almand and Michael Castledon good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means and said William Dedrick Lantware came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said William Dederick Lantware in the Thirtieth day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields in the Liberty and County aforesaid being in a small Boat Commonly called a Funny and the said Boat being in Motion in a certain River called the River Thames in that part of the said River opposite or near to the Adelptic Buildings in the aforesaid Parish of St. Martin in the fields situate, it so happened that accidentally Casually and by Misfortune he the said William Dederick Lantware fell from out the said Boat into the said River and in the Waters thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowthy he the said William Dederick Lantware then and there Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Dederick Lantware in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death not otherwise; and that the said Boat was Moving to the Death of the said William Dederick Lantware, and is of the Value of Forty Shillings and the Property and in the Possession of Godfrey of Stangate in the Parish of St. Mary Lambeth in the County of Surry Boat Builder on of his Assigns. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Forenoon of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Henry Wilson Foreman
