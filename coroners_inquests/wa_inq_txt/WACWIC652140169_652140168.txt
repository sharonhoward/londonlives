City of Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty ninth day of March in the Fourteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Beck then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of George Findlay , Henry Kelly , David Lindsay , John Benson , William Williams , John Hill , Thomas Burke , William Rothwell , Thomas Miller , Richard Burton , Lewis Maryard and Thomas White good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Beck came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Joseph Beck , Aged Eighty two Years, on the Twenty Fifth day of March in the Year aforesaid being in St. James's Park , near unto the Queen's House, in the Parish and County aforesaid, And that several of his Majesty's Light Horse Men upon Duty were then and there Guarding the Coach of Our Gracious Queen Charlotte wherein her Majesty then rode, and that the said Joseph Beck by going too near the Horses on which the said Light Horse Men were riding, was then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune forced down to the Ground, and that one of the said Horses did then and there tread upon the Forehead of the said Joseph Beck , by Means whereof the said Joseph Beck did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Wound in and upon his Forehead, of which said mortal Wound he the said Joseph Beck from the said Twenty fifth day of March in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty eighth day of the same Month at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty eighth day of March, at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Joseph Beck of the mortal Wound aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid to say, that the said Joseph Beck in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Corner, as the said George Findlay Foreman of the Said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

George Findlay Foreman
