City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of May in the twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Thomas Simes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Gilbert , John Seaton , Frederick Strecher , Thomas Fisher , James Pearce , Joseph Hull , Robert Vining , Joseph Grace , William Edwards , Joel Richards , Archibald Coyle and William Allender good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Thomas Simes came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say ,That on the sixth day of May in the Year aforesaid the said William Thomas Simes an Infant of the Age of three Years being upon a small Wooden Bridge over the Mote at the White House in Hyde Park in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said William Thomas Simes the Infant then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Bridge into the said Mote, and in the Water thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said William Thomas Simes then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Thomas Simes in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise, In Witness whereof as well he said Coroner as the said John Gilbert  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself, and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above mentioned.

Tho Prickard Coroner

John Gilbert Foreman
