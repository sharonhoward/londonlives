Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Tenth day of September in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the second by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty. Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid. Upon a View of the Body of Hannah Cockshaw then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of William Blacketer . Thomas Richardson William Smallbone , John Crewcher , George Bott , John Fisher , William Hammond,  James Wilkinson , James Cooper , James Coghlan  John Rennie , George Summers , John Keene , John Ramsey and Thomas Chambers Good and Lawful Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Hannah Cockshaw came to her Death, Say upon their Oaths, That on Tuesday the Ninth day of this Instant September the said Hannah Cockshaw being alone in the Kitchen of the Dwelling House of Mr. Benjamin Clark situate in Duke Street in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Liberty and County aforesaid and not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted a certain Cord into her hands did take and one part thereof round her Neck did put and place and the other part thereof round a certain Iron Hook fastned in the ceiling of the said Kitchen did put round, lye and fasten, by, means of which placing, putting and fastning as aforesaid, the said Hannah Cockshaw herself did hang and strangle, by means of which hanging and strangling as aforesaid the said Hannah Cockshaw then and there instantly Died. And so the said Jurors aforesaid upon their Oaths aforesaid do say that the said Hannah Cockshaw on the day aforesaid in the Year aforesaid, in the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid in manner and form aforesaid by her Death came, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisitions set their hands and Seals the day Year and at the place abovesaid

Jno. Feary  Coroner

Wm: Blackiter Forman
