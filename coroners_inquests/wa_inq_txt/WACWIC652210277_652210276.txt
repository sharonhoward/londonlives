City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Honover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of June in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Frances Coghlan a Child then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Jasper Smith , John Edwards , Robert Lambert , Michael Pieret , Walter Poncutt , Thomas Skegs , Robert Steadman , John Vartey , Christopher Wharton , John Cameron Benjamin Humpston , and Thomas Slack . good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Frances Coghlan came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Frances Coghlan of the Age of Five Years on the Eighth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid being at a certain window on the Landing Place three pair of Stairs of a House No. 15 in Oxford Buildings there situate, it So happened that accidentally casually and by Misfortune she the said Frances Coghlan fell from the said Window upon the Brick Pavement in the Yard of the said House, and by means thereof did then and there receive by the Fall aforesaid one Mortal Bruise and Contusion on her Head of which said Mortal Bruise and Contusion she the said Frances Coghlan at the said House and also at Saint George's Hospital near Hyde Park corner in the said Parish Liberty and County did languish and languishing did Live untill the Ninth day of June in the Year aforesaid on which said Ninth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid at Saint George's Hospital aforesaid of the said Mortal Bruise and Contusion did Dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid on their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Frances Coghlan in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors Lane to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho Prickard  Coroner . Jasper Smith Foreman
