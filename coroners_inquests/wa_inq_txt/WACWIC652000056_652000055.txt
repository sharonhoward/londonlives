Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Gardon within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Tenth day of October in the thirty fourth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Second by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth and in the year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixt y. Before John Feary , Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid. Upon a View of the Body of William Norman than and there lying Dead by the Oaths of Joseph Cox , Thomas Lowe , William Griffith , Thomas Gosheron , Peter Smith , William Carter , William Smeaden , William Padgett Robert Derry , William Wells , Joseph Solth , George Snowball , John Reid , Francis Goring , Barnaby Darley , and Nicholas Lesow Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said William Norman come to his Death, Say upon their Oaths That on Thursday the Ninth Day of October Instant the said William Norman being alone in his Lodging Room in the House of Mr. William Gardiner , situate in Maiden Lane in the Parish of St. Paul Covent Garden in the Liberty and County aforesaid, and not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but Lunatick and Distracted a certain Jewell or Linnen Cloth into his hands did take, and one part thereof round his Neck did put and place and the other part thereof round a Nail fastned in the Walk of his said Lodging Room, did put round tye and fasten, by means of which putting placing tying, and fastning as aforesaid the said William Norman himself did hang and Strangle, by means of which hanging, and strangling as aforesaid the said William Norman then and there instantly Died. And so the said Jurors upon their Oaths aforesaid do say that the said William Norman , the Day and year aforesaid in the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid, in manner and form aforesaid by his Death came and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition put their hands and Seals the Day, Year and at the place aforesaid

Jno. Feary  Coroner

Jos. Cox Foreman
