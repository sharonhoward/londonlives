City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and, Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of April in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prichard , Gentleman, Coroner of our Said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Samuel Weston then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Hilary Wild , Christopher Wildman , John Scot , John Mc. Phail , Richard Wainwright  Thomas Pugh , William Allen , Thomas Striate , John Hammond , Samuel Towell  Edward Whitton , Francis Baron  Thomas Amery & John Elder good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Samuel Weston came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Samuel Weston a Lad of the Age of Nine Years on the Eleventh day of April in the Year aforesaid being at Play upon a certain Wharf, called Lilly's Wharf in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said Samuel Weston then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Wharf unto and upon the Ground, and that a large Stone did likewise then and there fall off the said Wharf upon the said Samuel Weston , by Means whereof the said Samuel Weston then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received divers Mortal Wounds Bruises and Fractures in and upon the Groin on the Rightside and on the Right Arm of him the said Samuel Weston , of which said Mortal Wounds Bruises and Fractures as he the said Samiel Weston then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Samuel Weston in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Hilary Wild Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prichard Coroner

Hilary Wild Foreman
