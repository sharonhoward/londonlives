City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex .} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Mr. Brigan in upper Mount Mount Street , Parish of St. George Hanover Square  Sign of the Sword within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty fifth day of April 1793 in the thirty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Lanham then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written and Seals affixed good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire of our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Lanham came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Richard Lanham not being of sound Mind, Memory & Understanding but lunatic & distracted, some time past, but when to these Jurors unknown but in the Month of April & Year aforesaid at the Parish & in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit in the Serpentine River in Hyde park , there did cast & throw himself by means of which said Casking & throwing, in the Waters of the said River, he the said Richard Lanham was then & there suffocated & drowned; Of which said Suffocation and Drowning be the said Richard Lanham then and there instantly died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Richard Lanham not being of same Mind Memory & Understanding but lunatic and distracted in the Manner & by the Means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner and the said Jurors leave to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day Year & Place first above written.

Anthy. Gell Coroner }

Tho Core Fore Man}
