City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourteenth day of December in the twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Dutton then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Walter Watkins , William Green , Alexander Nairn , Adam Dunkerley , John Maberley , John Barnet , Alexander Finley , Christopher Lewis William Jones , John Mc.Intosh , Thomas Skilton , James Simpson John Hawkes and john Rand , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Dutton came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Dutton a Coachman on the said fourteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid being in the Stable of John Hatchett in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there going up a Ladder into the Hayloft over the said Stable It so happened that the said Thomas Dutton Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Ladder unto and upon the Stone Pavement in the said Stable and thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did receive one mortal Fracture and Contusion in and upon the left side of his Head of which said Fracture and Contusion he the said Thomas Dutton then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Thomas Dutton Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his death. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Walter Wilkins Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten.

Thos. Prickard Coroner .

Walter Watkins Foreman
