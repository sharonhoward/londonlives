City & Liberty of Westminster . in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter, of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty eighth day of April in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of David Higgins a Chimneys Sweeper then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Mitchell  Cary Emes , George Hutchinson  Joseph Witness  Thomas Marshall , William Hall , Francis Juder , John Shillwell , Thomas Wade , William Hoys , John Jones , Robert Partridge , Charles Moran , Thomas Low , Alexander Michel , John Shove , Robert Scofield , Thomas Gamage , John Platt , Henry Davies , Francis Buckingham James Sedgwick and Samuel Bates good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said David Higgins came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said David Higgins a Chimney sweeper on the said Twenty eighth day of April in the year aforesaid, being Sweeping a Chimney in the workshop of Mr. Jennings a Taylor Situate in Henrietta Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said David Higgins Accidentally Casually and by misfortune was then in the said Chimney suffocated and smothered, of which said suffocation and Smothering he the said David Higgins then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said David Higgins in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, accidentally casually and by Misfortune come to his Death, an not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said said Coroner, as the said William Mitchell Foreman of their said, Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Will Mitchell Foreman
