City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex , To wit.}  In Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at Bridewell in the Parish of St. Margaret in the City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex the Seventeenth Day of April in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, Etc, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City and liberty on View of the Body of George Lambert then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Wade , James Bower , George King , Thomas Wheeler , John Edwards , Robert Kew , Joseph Kimbury , Isaac Gristock , Richard Lane , Samuel Graves , John Wells , and Thomas Kelly good and lawful Men of the said liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said George Lambert came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said George Lambert being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields Bridewell aforesaid, in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Sixteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid, at Bridewell aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way, to wit, of a Fever, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Wade Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Follows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Thos: Wade Foreman
