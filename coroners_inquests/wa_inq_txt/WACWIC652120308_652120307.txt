City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of May in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of the Right Honorable William Manners Esquire commonly called Lord William Manners then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Wingfield , Michael Thackworth , William Cook , John Buckley , James Ketley  Henry Edwards , Robert Wornum , Richard Hunt , James Nicholson , William Griffis , Andrew Paterson , Edward Hays , Abraham Jennens , Richard Warren , John Fenton , John Lyell , Richard Atwood , George Wilson and Nicholas Philipson , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Manners came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Manners on the said Twenty third day of May in the Year aforesaid, in Old Burlington Street in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, did Mount and get upon the Back of a certain Bay Horse, and part of the Coaths of the said William Manners being under him upon the Saddle, It so happened that the said William Manners , in a place called Vigo Lane in the said Parish Liberty and County, in endeavouring to remove and pull his Cloaths from under him, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said Horse unto the Ground, and thereby did then and there receive one mortal Wound in and upon the left side of his Head, and Bruises in his Body, of which said mortal Wound and Bruises he the said William Manners , at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the same Day and Year did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Manners in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Wingfield Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Jams. Wingfield Foreman
