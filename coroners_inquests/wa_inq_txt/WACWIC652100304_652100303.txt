City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of June in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Holden then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Brittain  Thomas Bedford , Michael Luttrell , Thomas Lambert , William Gladle , Arthur Watkins , Richard Weale , Charles Lane , John Vaughan , William Hawkins , Samuel Hurd , Thomas King , Francis Dellous and William Nicolls good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Holden came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said William Holden not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Ninth day of June in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, to Wit, into the Serpentine River in Hyde Park there did cast and throw himself, by Means of which said Casting and Throwing he the said William Holden , in the Waters of the said River, was then and there suffocated and drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said William Holden then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Holden in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, did drown and Kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Brittain Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos Brittain Foreman
