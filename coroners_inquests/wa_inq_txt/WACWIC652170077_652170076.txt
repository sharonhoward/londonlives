City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of st. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of February in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Stephen Gibbs then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Hall , John Hords , Charles Weelding , Thomas Nevett , John Gilbert , Joseph Seger , George Pickering , Edward Waller , Wiliam Stables , Thomas Waters , Edward Marr and Owen Scott good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Stephen Gibbs came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Stephen Gibbs a Bricklayer on the Nineteenth day of February in the Year aforesaid being at Work at the top of the House of Mr. Selwyn situate in Chesterfield Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And then and there standing upon a Ladder on the outside of the said House It so happened that the said Ladder Slipped, off the Coping, and that the said Stephen Gibbs then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell from the top of the said House upon the Stone Pavement in Chesterfield Street aforesaid and thereby did then and there receive a Mortal Bruises in his Body and a Fracture in and upon his Right Bruise and Fracture he the said Stephen Gibbs on the Nineteenth day of February aforesaid at St. George's Hospital within the Liberty and County aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Stephen Gibbs in Manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Hall Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos. Hall Foreman
