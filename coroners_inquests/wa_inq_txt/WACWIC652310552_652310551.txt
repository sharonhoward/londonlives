City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty seventh day of October in the Thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Brown an Infant then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Richard Kelly , Henry Parry , John Hall , Richard Groves John Quinn , William Russell , John Dunlop , David Allen James Ventis , George Badford , John Withall , and William Brundy good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Brown came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty fourth day of October in the Year aforesaid the said Elizabeth Brown an Infant of the Age of Two Years being alone in the Lodging Room or Apartment of Jane Brown her Mother situate and being in Old Pye Street in the Parish of St. John the Evangelist within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the Linen Cloths which she the said Elizabeth Brown then and there had on her Body Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took fire and that the said Elizabeth Brown the Infant was violently burnt in and upon the Belly Breast neck and sides of her the said Elizabeth Brown of which said burning she the said Elizabeth Brown at the Westminster Hospital in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the Twenty fourth day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty sixth day of the same Month did languish and languishing did live On which said Twenty saith day of October in the Year aforesaid at the Hospital aforesaid she the said Elizabeth Brown of the violent and Mortal burning aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Elizabeth Brown in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Richard Kelly Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner Richd Kelby Foreman
