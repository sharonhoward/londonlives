City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to wit}  An Inquisition Indented for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Third day of July in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on view of the Body of James Jeffreys then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of William Marcellus , Richard Jones , Richard Wale , Thomas Paget , James Ling , Thomas Eaton , John Vasey , Morgan Evans , Sylvester Harding , Francis Addy , Thomas Probey , William Brookes , William Smith , John Pearce , William Richard , John Bassi , John McAdam and Edward Walters good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what means the said James Jeffreys Came to his Death do their Oath say. That the James Jeffreys on the First day of July in the Year aforesaid being gathering Coals at a Place called Ivery Stairs in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and walking across certain Empty Barges which were there and there floating upon the River Thames , within the said Parish Liberty and County, It so happened that the said James Jeffreys Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did then and there fall down between two of the said Barges into the said River Thames and in the Waters thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said James Jeffreys then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Jeffreys in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Marcellus Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Wm Marcellus Foreman
