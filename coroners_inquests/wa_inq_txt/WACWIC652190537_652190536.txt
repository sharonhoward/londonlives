City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of November in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of a Woman unknow then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Jonathan Farlam  John Barr  Benjamin Capper  William Collier , Francis Matthews , John Cotterell , William Brain  Thomas Barley , John Mc. Neven , John Steere , Thomas Blewling  Richard Lyford  Daniel Richhorne and William Harrison good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Woman unknown came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Woman Unknown on the thirteenth day of November n the Year aforesaid being in the Strand and crossing the End of Brewer's Yard in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and that a Man was then driving a certain Empty Cart drawn by three Horses out of the Strand into Brewers Yard aforesaid and being very Dark, it so happened that the said Woman unknown was then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune forced down by the fore Horse in the said Cart and that the Wheel of the said Cart passed over the left side of the Head of the said Woman unknown, by Means whereof the said Woman unknown then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received one mortal Fracture in and upon her Fore head over the left Eye, of which said Mortal Fracture she the said Woman unknown then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Woman unknown in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jonathan Farlam Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Thos Prickard Coroner .

Jonn. Farlam Foreman
