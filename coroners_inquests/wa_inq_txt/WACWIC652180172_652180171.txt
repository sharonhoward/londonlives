City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of May in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Wallace then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Sutton , Abraham Hatchett , Jonathan Andrews , Robert Martin , Edward Pagitt , James Fry John Ward , William Carr  Nathan Mead , Thomas West , William Elmore and John Hayward good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Wallace came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said James Wallace on the Tenth day of May in the Year aforesaid being upon the Roof of a Stage- Coach in the Publick Road at Acton in the County aforesaid, and that it so happened that the said James, Wallace then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the Roof of the said Coach unto the Ground, and thereby then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon his Right Leg, of which said mortal Fracture he the said James Wallace at the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Tenth day of May in the Year aforesaid until. the Twelfth day of the same Month and Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twelfth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square he the said James Wallace of the mortal Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Wallace in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise I Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Sutton Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho Prickard Coroner.

John Sutton Foreman
