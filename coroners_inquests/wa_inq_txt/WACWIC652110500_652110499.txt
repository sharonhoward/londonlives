City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of September in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Treadwell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Wilson , William Clissold , William Sutting , James Don , Thomas Bywater , Robert Chisholm , Samuel Hardman , William Almond , Walter Tyrrell , Joseph Skinner , Philip Jones and John Noble , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Treadwell came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said James Treadwell on the Twenty second day of September in the Year aforesaid being on the outside, and at the Top, of the dwelling House of John Chetwynd situate in Downing Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that the said James Treadwell in coming down from the Ridge of the said House, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell from thence, being three Stories high, unto and upon the Ground, and thereby did then and there receive divers mortal Bruises in and upon the Right side at his Head and on his Body, of which said mortal Bruises, he the said James Treadwell languished about three Hours, and thereof then did die at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Treadwell in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Henry Wilson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Henry Wilson Foreman
