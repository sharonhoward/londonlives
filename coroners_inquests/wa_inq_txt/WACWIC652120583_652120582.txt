City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of August in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Marsh then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Milbourn Giles Savage , Joseph Sewell , William Longe , John Dent , Benjamin Poole , Stephen Stephens , William Clarke , John Hawkins , Benjamin Newman , Henry Fellins , Humphrey Loyd , Robert Vensent , Giles Mathews , Richard Haward , and Henry Lind le buny good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Jacob Marsh came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say

That the said Jacob Marsh on the Seventh day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid being Confined as a Prisoner in the Dwelling Houp of Mr. James Armstrong an Officer of the Sheriff of Middlesex situate in Carey Street he the said Jacob Marsh by the Vicitation of God departed this Life in a Natural way (to wit) of on Epileptic Fit and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the Foreman of the Jurors, aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Ed Milbourn Foreman
