City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty third day of September in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Steel then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Lennox , John Lee , John Tetlow , Joseph Impey , Joseph Ball , William Brailey , Thomas Bishop , James Findley , Thomas Newberry , William Loveless , Samuel Sabine , Isaac Wright , Samuel Chapman  Miles Williams , and Thomas Stagg good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Robert Steel came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Robert Steel a Laborer on the Twenty second day of September in the year aforesaid, being on the Tiles on the outside of the Dwelling House of John Garnsay situate in Piccadilly in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County It so happened that the said Robert Steel shipped, and then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said House (being three Stories high) unto and upon the Stone- -Pavement in the said Street, and thereby then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture his the said Robert Steel and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Robert Steel in manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward Lennox Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Edward Lennox Foreman
