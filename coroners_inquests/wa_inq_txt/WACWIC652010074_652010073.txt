Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St. Martin in the feilds in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate of Saint Peter at Westminster the Third day of November in the Third Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and Sixty one . Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid Upon a View of the Body of Elizabeth Yarrow then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of Thomas Forshall , Thomas Froll , Andrew Keill John West , John Legrix , John Stephens , William Oaker , Richard Lewis , John Wickstreet , James Renton , William Robinson , John Mitchell , Timothy Conner , Francis Pitsala , and William Taylor Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Elizabeth Yarrow came to her Death say their Oaths. That on Sunday the first day of November Instant in the Year aforesaid at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid the said Elizabeth Yarrow a Poor Person in the Workhouse of the said Parish being Intoxicated with Liquor and being walking near the Stairs leading down to the Wash house of the said Workhouse it so happened that her feet slipt from under her and she then and thereby accidentally casually and by Misfortune fell down the said Stairs by means whereof her Neck was dislocated and her Skull Fractured of which she then and there instantly Died And so the said Jurors upon their Oaths aforesaid do say that the said Elizabeth Yarrow the Day and Year aforesaid in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid in manner aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day year and at the Place abovementioned.

Jno. Feary Coroner .

Thos Forshall Forman
