City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Seventh day of June in the Thirty first Year of the Reign on our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the Said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Hughes then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of William Jordan , John Poole , Jonathan Waite Alexander Markey , James Miller , John Allcock , William Cattliff , Richard Pittway , William Brooks , Daniel Buchanan , Timothy Scott , Robert House , James Giles , Thomas Reeves John Ely and Jonathan Griffiths good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Hughes came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty fifth day of June in the Year aforesaid the said John Hughes and one James Scott were Struggling together in Tothill Fields in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid and that it so happened that the said John Hughes fell down upon the Ground and thereby then and there Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of the said James Scott received a violent concussion in the Brain of him the said John Hughes , of which said violent and mortal Concussion he the said John Hughes on the said Twenty fifth day of June at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Hughes in manner and by the means aforesaid Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of the said James Scott did die

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Jordan  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Wm Jordan Foreman
