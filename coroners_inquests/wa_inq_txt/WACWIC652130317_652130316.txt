City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty eighth day of June in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Lodge then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of George Musson , Robert March , Anthony Haynes , Thomas Brown , George Elkins , William Weeland , Thomas Watts , William Horsey , Joseph Clark , Richard Burton , Thomas Coward and Mark Potter good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Lodge came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Lodge on the Twenty sixth day of June in the Year aforesaid, being in a certain Cart drawn by one Horse in the Kings Highway at Knights bridge , in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there lying down upon a Truss of Hay in the said Cart, It so happened that the said William Lodge by a sudden Jolt was thrown out of the said Cart unto the Ground in the said Highway, and that the Near wheel of the said Cart then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the Body on the Right side of him the said William Lodge by Means whereof the said William Lodge then and there received mortal Bruises in and upon the Right side of his Body, of which said mortal Bruises he the said William Lodge then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. Musson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place First above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Geo Musson Foreman
