City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty First day of June in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Offagan an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Hales Richard Robinson , John Valler , Samuel Hazelton , John Patison , Abraham Wilkinson Thomas Bunce , Job Carey , Willam Olvill , Robert Bowden , John Hadden John Scott and James Matthews good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Offagan came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That a Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown on the Nineteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said Mary Offagan the Infant, in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being, feloniously did make an Assault And that the said Person or Persons, to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown, with a certain blunt Weapon her the said Mary Offagan, in and upon the Head of her the said Mary Offagan did then and there feloniously strike and beat, thereby then and there giving unto her the said Mary Offagan divers mortal Bruises in and upon the Head of her the said Mary Offagan of which said Mortal Bruises she the said Mary Offagan at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Nineteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid unto the Twentieth day of the same Month and Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twentieth day of June aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, she the Said Mary Offagan of the mortal Bruises aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown, her the said Mary Offagan in manner and Form aforesaid Feloniously did Kill and slay, against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Hales Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition, set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Hales Foreman
