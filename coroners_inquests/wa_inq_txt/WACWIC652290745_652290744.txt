City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Knightsbridge in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventeenth day of December in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickar, Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Davison an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Anderson , James Ellwell , George Store , William Pinnar , Abel Winter , Thomas Nichol , Thomas Heath , William Jones , William Day , Joseph Mathews , James Pagan and Benjamin Thompson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Francis Davison came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Francis Davison the Infant of the Age of three Years on the sixteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid, being left with her Brother another Infant Aged Five Years in the Lodging Room of Hannah Maria Davison their Mother in the Dwelling House of John Marsden situate at Knigthsbridge in the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the Linen Clothes which the said Francis Davison the Infant, then and there had on her Body took fire, and that the said Francis Davison by the Flames and Fire was greatly burnt upon her Belly Stomach Arms Neck and Face of which said burning she the said Francis Davison , from the said Sixteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid until the Seventeenth day of the same Month and year at Knightsbridge aforesaid did languish and live On which said Seventeenth day of December in the year aforesaid, at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, she the said Francis Davison of the mortal burning aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Frances Davison in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Anderson Fireman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition at their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Robert Anderson Foreman
