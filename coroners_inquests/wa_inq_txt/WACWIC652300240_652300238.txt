An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Tothill Fields  Bridewell in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of April in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Daniel Dixon then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Stanly , John Machintosh , Jonathan Griffiths Joseph Hopkins , William Foster , Christopher Fork , William Harris Samuel Stedwick , Edward Mears , John Newman , Thomas Pope , George Ward , Aaron Loton , Peter Seamwell , Jesse Upjohn and William Young good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Daniel Dixon came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the said seventh day of April in the Year aforesaid the said Daniel Dixon being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields  Bridewell in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the said seventh day of April at Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid departed this Fife by the Visitation of God in a natural Way (to wit) of a Jaundice and not otherwise,

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Stanly Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos. Stanly Foreman
