City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of November in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Daniel Williams then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Morgan , Abraham Hackett , John Watkins , John Pether , John Lovett , John Conn Robert Bennett , Richard Thompson , Hammond Payne , Edward Whiteall , William Hardyman and John Hayward ,, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Daniel Williams came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Daniel Williams on the Twenty Fifth day of October in the Year aforesaid in the County aforesaid to wit at a Place called Brentford being Riding on a Horse it so happened the the said Horse Stumbled and touched the Wheel of a Carriage then and there passing by by means where of he the said Daniel Williams was accidentally casually and by Misfortune thrown from off the said and Fell to the Ground end then and there by Force of the said Fall received a Violent and Mortal Contusion and Fracture on the Head of him the said Daniel Williams of which said Violent and Mortal Contusion and Fracture he the said Daniel Williams from the said Twenty Fifth day of October in the year aforesaid at Brentford aforesaid in the County aforesaid and also at Saint George's Hospital near Hyde Park corner in the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid untill the Tenth day of November in the year aforesaid did Languish and Languishing did Live on which said Tenth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid to wit in Saint George's Hospital aforesaid he the said Daniel Williams of the said Violent and Mortal Contusion and Fracture did Dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say That the said Daniel Williams in manner and by the means aforesaid, accidently casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise .In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the said Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place above mentioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos. Morgan Foreman
