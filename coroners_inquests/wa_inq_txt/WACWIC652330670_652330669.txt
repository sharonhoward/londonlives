An Inquisition, Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of George Bye  Sign of the Olivier's Mount in Mount Row  Mount street  Parish of St. George Hanover Square within die Liberty of the Dean & Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter in Westminter in the County of Middlesex the twelfth day of October 1793 in the thirty third year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Anthony Gell , Esqr . Coroner of an said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Margaret Emmett then and there lying dead upon the Oath of the several Jurors where Names are hereunder written and Seals affixed good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being them and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King when how and by wheat Means the said Margaret Emmett came to her Death do, upon their Oath, say that the said Margaret Emmett , on the said twelfth day of October in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit. at her Dwelling House No. 11 in George Sheet  Grovernor Square in Duke Street died by the Visitation of God in a natural Way To Wit, suddenly of a fit, and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors leave, to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day, Year and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }

James Edwd. Bring Job Dowsing Hitch

The Mark of John Eden }

Jno. Shaw Jno Ewen Thos. Deighton George Clark Thomas Broker
