City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of June in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Corner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Paul Hiffernan then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Raines , James Hartley , Charles Lockington , William Surgey , Peter Parry Dennis Mc. Guire , John Wagman , John Groves , William Wills , James Conely James Cummings & William Bransford good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Paul Hiffernan came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Paul Hiffernan (suspected to have been Poisoned) on the Twelfth day of June in the Year aforesaid, in the Dwelling House of Richard Fitzgerald situate in Monmouth Court Hedge Lane in the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid departed this Life in a natural Way, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Raines Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jno. Raines Foreman
