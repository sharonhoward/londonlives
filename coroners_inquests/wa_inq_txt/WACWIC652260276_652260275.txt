City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster , in the Country of Middlesex , the Third day of May in the Twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Lockwood then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Philip Mitchell , Thomas Masters , Thomas Fisher Charles Chowles , James Payne , Joseph Thrupp , John Wilkinson , Thomas Burbidge , Thomas Hendrson , Thomas Hudson , Henry Ellison , Henry Shepherand and James Perryman , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who, being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said Joseph Lockwood  came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Joseph Lockwood a Bricklayer on the First day of May in the Year aforesaid, being at work upon a certain House situate in Queens Street in the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and standing upon a Ladder in the Front of the said House, at the height of two Stories. It so happened that the said Joseph Lockwood Accidentally casually and by Misfortune fell off from the said ladder upon the Stone Pavement in the said Street and thereby did then and there receive a mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of the Head of him the said Joseph Lockwood of which said mortal Fracture he the said Joseph Lockwood then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Joseph Lockwood , in manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Philip Mitchell  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

P. Mitchell Foreman
