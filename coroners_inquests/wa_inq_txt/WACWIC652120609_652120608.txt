City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty second day of August in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Greenwood then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Jonathan Hutchkins , James Stokes , Dudley Bagley , George Goodall , Francis Musgrove , Christopher Walton , John Watson , William Kere , John Beardmore , William Allen , James Walley and Richard Parratt good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Greenwood came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say,

That the said Elizabeth Greenwood on the Eighteenth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid being in a certain Gallory in the Stable Yard belonging to Mr, John Wood situate in Park Lane in the said Parish, Liberty, and County, and being endeavouring to throw over the said Gallery into the said Stable Yard some Dirty Water in a Pot which she the said Elizabeth Greenwood then and there had and held in her hand, in reaching over too far it so happened her Feet shipped and she the said Elizabeth Greenwood Accidentally casually and by Misfortune foll over the said Gallery into the said Stable Yard by means of which said Fall she the said Elizabeth Greenwood then and thee received a Violent and Mortal Wound Bruise and Fracture on the head of her the said Elizabeth Greenwood of which said Violent and Mortal Wound Bruise and Fracture she the Elizabeth Greenwood from the said Eighteenth day of August in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty first day of the same Month in the same Year at the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did Live, on which said Twenty first day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid (to wit) in Saint Georges's Hospital there, she the said Elizabeth Greenwood of the violent and Mortal Wound Bruise and Fracture aforesaid did Die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Elizabeth Greenwood in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise. In witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands, and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jonathan Hutchins Formand
