City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint of Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of June in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Caesar Latham a Labourer then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Harriot , William Spratley , John Sprackling  Humphrey Wheelwright , James Young , James Brown  John Chewter , Richard Canadine , Thomas Rumford  John Smith  William Ellis and Joseph Meller good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Cosar Latham came to h is Death, do upon their Oath Say, That the said Casar Latham on the Twenty ninth day of May in the Year aforesaid being in Buckingham Court in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields in the County aforesaid, and there carrying a Sach filled with Coals, It so happened that the said Cesar Latham Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down into a Cellar belonging to a House in the said Court, the Door of which Cellar being open, by Means whereof the said Caesar Latham did then and there receive one Mortal Wound and Confusion in the Forehead and on the Right side of his Head of which said Mortal wound and Confusion he the said Caser Latham from the said Twenty ninth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty fourth day of June in the same Year at the Parish of St Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty fourth day of June he the said Caser Latham at the said Parish of St Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid of the mortal wound and Confusion aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Caser Latham accidentally Casually and by misfortune in manner and by the means aforesaid came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said John Harriet Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner .

John Harriet Foreman
