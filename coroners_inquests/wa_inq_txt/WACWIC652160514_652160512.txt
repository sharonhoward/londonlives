City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of December in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Barkley then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Middleton , George Staten , William Ruff , William Cross , Robert Farrier , William Smith Dean, Christopher Allat , Charles Brunsden , Christopher James , John Henry , Thomas Lewis , John Duff , Thomas Joycelyn , Cornellins Neep , Alexander Barkley and Richard Dorrington good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Barkley came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Barkley , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Ninth day of December in the Year aforesaid one End of a certain piece of small Cord unto an Iron Staple fastened into the Coiling of the Apartment of the said James Barkley in the Dwelling House of Mr. Bucklin situate in little Windmill Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and the other End thereof about his own Nock did fix tye and fasten and therewith did then and there Hang suffocate and Strangle himself, of which said hanging suffocation and Strangling he the said James Barkley then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Barkley , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and disracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Middleton Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Middleton Foreman
