Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit

An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St. Martin in the fields in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Twelfth day of June in the First Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith, and so forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid, Upon a Veiw of the Body of Hugh Bride then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of John Lowe  Alexander Johnson , Barney Dweling , Charles Lowder , Henry Flowers , Edmonds Jurors, Philip Webster , Charles Roberts , George Mortimore , William Coleby , John Loure , Bryan Linch , John Powers , Samuel Hudson , Joseph Nussey , Samuel Longville , Richard Proudley , and John Jones Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Inquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Hugh Bride came to his Death say upon their Oaths That on Wednesday the Tenth day of June Instant the said Hugh Bride being in his Lodging Room in the House of Barny Dowland scituate in Drury Lane in the Parish of St. Martin in the feilds in the Liberty and County aforesaid and not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted, a certain peice of small Cord unto an Iron hook fastned in the Wainscott in the Lodging Room of him the said Hugh Bride as aforesaid, and the other end thereof about his oun Neck did Fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle himself of which said hanging Suffocation and strangling he the said Hugh Bride then and there Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Hugh Bride not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did Kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day Year and at the place abovementioned

Jno. Feary  Coroner

Jno. Lowe Foreman
