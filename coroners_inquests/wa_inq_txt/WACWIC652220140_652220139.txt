City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of May in the twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of Our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Lattimore then and there lying dead, upon the oath of John Cliffe , Peter Cockburn , William Simpson , James Watson , Robert Saunders , John Nash , John Maitland , John Cooper , Archibald Paterson , Thomas Thompson , Joseph Fetherby , Thomas Adams and Joseph Copas good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, who being the and there duly sworn and charged to enquires for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ann Lattimore came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Ann Lattimore, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the said Ninth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, a certain Garter which the said Ann Lattimore had and held in both her hands about the Neck of her the said Ann Lattimore did then and there fix tye and faster and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle herself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling she the said Ann Lattimore then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, tha the said Ann Lattimore, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Cliffe Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals, the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Cliffe Foreman
