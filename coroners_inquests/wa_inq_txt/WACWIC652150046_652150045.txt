City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Seventh day of January in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our Said Lord the King for the Said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Barnes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Francis Snell , Peter Henderson , John Ward , Richard Rogers , John Wilson , Thomas Smith , Alexander Swinley , William Johnstone , Joseph Ray , Henry Bagnell , Mark Potter , John Penneall and Robert Hogg , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our Said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Barnes came to h is Death, do upon their Oath Say, That the said Thomas Barnes , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, on the Twenty fourth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid with in the Liberty and County aforesaid, to Wit, into a certain Bason or Reservoir in Hyde Park , there did cast and throw himself, by Means of which said lasting and throwing, he the said Thomas Barnes in the Waters of the said Bason was then and there suffocate and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Thomas Barnes then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Thomas Barnes in manner and by the Means aforesaid, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted did drown and kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Francis Snell Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Fras. Snell Foreman
