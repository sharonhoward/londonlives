City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of September in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great- Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of  John Crowe then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Curtis James Spartley , John Mackender , John Smith , Richard Hollis , Abraham Wilkinson , Henry Callis , Robert Lane , William Povey , James Froot , Joseph Love Joseph Forrester and Samuel Highley good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Crowe came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Crowe on the Seventh day of September in the Year aforesaid being in Tothill Fields in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and that the said John Crowe then and there Jumped upon a certain empty Brewer's Dray drawn by one Horse whereby the said Horse was frightened, and Gallop'd away with the said Dray, and that the said John Crowe in endeavouring to stop the said Horse fell down off and from the said Dray unto the Ground, and that the Near Wheel of the said Dray then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the Breast and left side of the Neck and Head of him the said John Crowe by Means whereof the said John Crowe then and there received divers Bruises in and upon the Breast Neck and Head of him the said John Crowe of which said Mortal Bruises he the said John Crowe then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Crowe Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Curtis Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Curtis Foreman
