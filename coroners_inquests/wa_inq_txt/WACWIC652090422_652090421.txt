An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of October in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of  then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Daniel Bower  Joseph Dencocks , Henry Harsel , Joseph Jones , William Makin , John Miller , Joseph Stephenson , Rowland Davies , Robert Pettitt , William Dane , Andrew Mackintosh , & Gilbert Robert good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Fielding came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said James Fielding on the Faith day of October in the Year aforesaid died suddenly in Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid that no Marks of Violence appeared on his Body, and that the said James Fielding departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a  natural Way and not by any by any hurt or Injury receing from any Person whatsoever to the Knowledge of the said Jurors.
