City & Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Ann within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twenty third Day of May in the Fifth year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said the King for the said City and Liberty. On View of the Body of a John Moser then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Fisher , William Robertson , William Deering, Matthew Winter , William Fisher , Thomas Meredith , Sampson Hackett , John Foden , Joseph Burgall , John Forbes , John Le graix Henry Oatley and John Biss good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Moser came to his Death do upon their Oath say That on the Twenty second day of May in the Year aforesaid, the said John Moser died suddenly in the Dwelling House of Charles Drew  Vintner situate in Grafton Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, by a Fit of an Apoplexy, or other sudden Visitation of God, and not by any violent Means or Manner whatsoever, No Marks of Violence appearing on the Body of the said John Moser . In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Joseph Fisher Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Joseph Fisher Foreman
