City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex  } to wit. An Inquisicion Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fifth day of July in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth; before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Humphreys then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Charles Alton Thomas Cross , John Bishop , James Jones , Joseph Follat , John Divershouse , John Shorland , John Sharp , Francis Musgrove , William Dorses , John Barlow , Richard Lane , and Richard Baker good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Humphreys came to his Death, do upon their Oath say

 That the said Thomas Humphrys on the Twenty fourth day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid being Walking backwards on a Scoffold of two Storys high belonging to a House new Building situate in Grosvenors Place there, where he was at Work, it to happened that in so walking backward he stepped one foot beyond and off the said Scoffold by means whereof he accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell from the said Leaffold into the Area of the said House and by means thereof did then and there receive by the Fall aforesaid one Mortal wound and Confusion on the Crown of the Head of him the said Thomas Humphrys of which said Mortal Wound and Contution he the said Thomas Humphreys then and there instantly Died.

And to the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Thomas Humphreys in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally, casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Char. Alton Foreman
