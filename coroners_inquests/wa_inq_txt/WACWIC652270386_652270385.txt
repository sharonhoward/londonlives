City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the tenth day of September in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Wilkins then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Taylor , Robert Mackay , Roderick Mc. Kinnon , Thomas Stunt , William West , Edward Greatley , John Francis , William Jaffries , William Douglass , Edward Cresy , William Rose & James Ansell good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Wilkins came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Joseph Wilkins a Gardener on the Fourth day of September in the Year aforesaid in the Evening, being directed to Watch and defend the Garden of William Bayley  Esqr . situate in the Parish of Barnes in the County of Surry from being Robbed, and then being in the said Garden it so happened that the said Joseph Wilkinstrod upon the Wire of a certain Trap gun, charged by him the said Joseph Wilkins , with Gun Powder shot Hugs and leaden Bullets by Means whereof the said Trap Gun then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune went off and was discharged, and that the said Joseph Wilkins thereby then and there received one Mortal Wound and Fracture in and upon his Right Leg, of which said Wound and Fracture he the said Joseph Wilkins at the said Parish of Saint George Hanover square within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said fourth day of September in the Year aforesaid until the Eighth day of the same Month & Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Eighth day of September in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the LIberty and County last mentioned, he the said Joseph Wilkins of the Wound and Fracture aforesaid, and of an Inflamation in part of his Bowels, did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Joseph Wilkins Accidentally, Casually and by Misfortune in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his Death, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Taylor Foreman of the said jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition their Hands & Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm. Taylor Foreman
