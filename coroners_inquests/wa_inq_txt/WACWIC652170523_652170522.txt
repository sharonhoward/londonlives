City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of November in the Eighteenth Year of the Regin of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Gray then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Nehemiah Spicer , Christopher Gill , John Thornton , Richard Hammond , William Love , Andrew Matthison Thomas Quainton , James Barker , Henry Robins , Thomas Beasley , William Willshere , Thomas Cribbin , John Young , Robert Bagnall , and Robert Hindes good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ann Gray came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Ann Gray on the Ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and Distracted one end of a certain peice of Cord tied round a Nail Fixed in the Wainscot of her Lodging Room in the House of Mr. Williams situate in Whitchart Yard and the other end there of about her own Neck did fix lye and fasten and therewith did then and there Hang Suffocate and Strangle herself of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling she the said An Gray then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say That the said Ann Gray not being of Sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatic and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did Kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and at the Place first above mentioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Nehemiah Spicer Foreman
