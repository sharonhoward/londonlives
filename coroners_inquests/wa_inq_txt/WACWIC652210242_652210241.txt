City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirty first day of May in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Reding then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Elder , William Brain , Richard Barry , Charles Wright , Nathaniel Chandley William Holmes , Vincent Armitage , John Colin , John Mc.Newin , Daniel Umbrella , James Fechem , Andrew Fruce and Samuel Dawes good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Reding came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Reding on the Thirtienth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid being in a certain Barge in a certain River called the River of Thames then and there being, it so happened that the said Thomas Reding accidentally casually and by Misfortune fell from the said Barge into the said River, and in the Waters thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Thomas Reding then and there Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do says, That the said Thomas Riding in manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner . Thos. Elder  Forman
