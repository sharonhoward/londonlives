City and Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex to Wit }  An Inquisition indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Prison of Tothill Fields , Bridewell , situate in the Parish of St. Margaret, within the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the First day of September in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on view of the Body of Eleanor Feilders then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Woolley , Samuel Tylor , Alexander Gordon , James White Alexander Donold , Joseph Mutlow , Thomas Hitchins , John Bank , Nathaniel Hunt , John Sweet , John Griffiths , John Lane , and William Gally , good and lawfull men of the said Liberty, duly chosen who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what means the said in Tothill Fields , Bridewell , aforesaid, in the parish city Liberty and County Feilders being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields , Bridewell , aforesaid, in the Parish City Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Thirty first day of August in the year aforesaid, at Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way, to wit, of a Fever, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Samuel Wooley Foreman of the said Jurors, on the Behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the Day year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Saml Woolley  Foreman
