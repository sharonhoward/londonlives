City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty sixth day of March in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Samuel Parrot then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Walter Buckmaster , Richard Pratt , Robert Matthew Smith , John Roberts , John Orger , Lewance Value , Anthony Derpman , Michael Bryan , William Bay , Owen Gill , Lewis Dupois , William Denneson , Walter Fetzgerard . John Fingle , George Watts , Charles Gilles , and Henry Hill good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Samuel Parrot came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Samuel Parrot not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatick and distracted on the twenty fifth day of March in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. James within the Liberty and County aforesaid one end of a certain piece of thick Cord unto an Iron hook fastened a Wooden Beam in a Garret in the Dwelling House of John Becket situate and being in Jermyn Street in the said Parish Liberty and County and the other End thereof about his own neck tie and fasten and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle himself of which said hanging suffocation and strangling he the said Samuel Parrot then and those died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath said do say that the said Samuel Parrot not being of Memory and Understanding but Lunactick in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner Walter Buckmaster Foreman of the said Jur of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coronor Walter Buckman Foreman
