City and Liberty of Westminster , in the Country of Middlesex .}  to wit.

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Ann within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of February in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Francis Aliamet then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Long , Thomas Bishop , Richard Lee , Nathaniel Luthew , William Cook , John Cole , James Show , Abraham Reynolds , Edward Rushell , Thomas Tanton , Water Taylor , Andrew Stafforey , Robert Bowcock , and Richard Lee good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Francis Aliamet came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Francis Aliamet on the twenty seventh day of January in the Year aforesaid, was walking along in Greek Street in the said Parish of Saint Ann within the Liberty and Country aforesaid, and Edward Boguet and Fanner being then on the Roof of a certain House in the said Street, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune a Large stone fell off and from the Top of the said House upon the Head of the said Francis Aliamet, then standing in Great street aforesaid, by means whereof the said Francis Aliamet then and there Accidentally, Casually and by Misfortune received a Mortal wound and contusion in and upon the Back part of his Head about the Length of Three Inches of which said mortal Wound and contusion, he the said Francis Aliamet at the said Parish Liberty, and County from the said twenty seventh day of January in the Year aforesaid until the Fifth day of February in the same year did Languish and Languishing did live, on which said Fifth day of February in the year aforesaid he the said Francis Aliamet at the saidParish Liberty and Country of the mortal wound and contusion aforesaid and the Inflaminaiton upon the Brain caused thereby did die.And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the Accidentally,Casually and by Misfortune Aliamet in manner and by the means aforesaid of the mortal wound contusion and Inflammation aforesaid did die.

In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said John Long Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the Day Year and place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Jns. Long Foreman
