City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Paul Covent Garden , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twenty first day of February in the Fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Eastgate then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Staines , Thomas Stephens , John Bladwell , Thomas Davies , William George , Benine Thacker , Percival Bentley , Thomas Settrie , Joseph Harris , Charles Moran , John Jackson , Richard Steward John Tinkler , Robert Williams , Kemp Bridges , John Cropley , Robert Hughes , David Fountaine , William Hughes , and Thomas Nicholls , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Thomas Eastgate came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Eastgate , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, on the Twentieth day of February in the year aforesaid from and out of a certain three pair of Stairs Window, then and there being in the Apartment of him the said ) Thomas Eastgate , in the Dwelling House of Thomas Eastgate  der Father of the said Thomas Eastgate the deceased) situate in Russel Street in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, did violently cast and throw himself to the Ground, to and against the Stone Pavement in the said Street before the said dwelling House; by Means of which said casting and throwing, he the said Thomas Eastgate did then and there receive one Mortal Fracture on the back part of the Head of him the said Thomas Eastgate , of which said Mortal Fracture he the said Thomas Eastgate then and there instantly died and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Thomas Eastgate , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Richard Haines foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho Prickard Coroner Rd Haines Foreman
