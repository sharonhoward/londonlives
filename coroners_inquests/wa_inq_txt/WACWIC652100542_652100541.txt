City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex }to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of December in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Harrison then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Mason , Thomas George , James Gordon  Anthony Peacock , John Finlayson , John Cock , Thomas Hughes , John Smith , William Bussey , George Wheley , Richard Dewdney . Edward Potter and Andrew Rogers , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King , when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Harrison came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That William East a Drayman on the Twenty Eighth day of November in the Year aforesaid being carefully driving a certain Caravan drawn by two Horses and leaded with Empty Butts in Orchard Street in the Parish aforesaid within the said City Liberty and County, And that the said Joseph Harrison was then and there passing along the said Street, and being Old and Infirm, It so happened that the said Joseph Harrison fell down close to the said Caravan, and that the near Wheel of the said Caravan, so drawn as aforesaid did then and there Accidentally Casually and by misfortune pass over the Right Leg of the said Joseph Harrison , by means where of the said Joseph Harrison did then and there receive one Mortal Wound and Bruise in and upon his Right Leg, of which said Mortal Wound and Bruise he the said Joseph Harrison from the said Twenty Eighth day of November in the Year aforesaid until the Tenth day of December in the same Year, at the Parish and in the City Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Tenth day of December in the Year aforesaid, he the said Joseph Harrison , at the Parish aforesaid, of the Mortal Wound and Bruise aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Joseph Harrison in Manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise, And that the said near Wheel of the said Caravan was moving to the Death of the said Joseph Harrison , and is of the Value of five shillings and the property and in the possession of John Scott of the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in Westminster Esquire or of his Assigns. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said James Mason Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner
