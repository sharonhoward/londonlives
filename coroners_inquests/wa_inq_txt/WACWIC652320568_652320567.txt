City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty ninth day of August 1792 in the thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq.  Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Day then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John Atkins , Thomas Wilkinson , John Powell , Charles Mason , John Tripp , James Shelton , Thomas Blow , Richard Bardley  Thomas Haigh , Thomas Gatter , John Thorton , James Hicks , George Lucas , and Thomas Robinson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Day came to his Death, do upon their Oath say that the said James Day on the twenty third day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid being seized with a violent Fever on the Brain, and by reason of the Violence thereof being delirious and out of his Mind, from and out of a certain two pair of Stairs window, in a certain House, situate in the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist in the Liberty aforesaid did last and throw himself, by means of which said Casting and Throwing be the said James Day did then and there receive divers Wounds and Bruises on the Body of him the said James Day . That the said James Day from the said twenty third day of August untill the twenty sixth day of the same Month in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit in a certain Hospital there called the Westminster Infirmary of the violent Fever on the Brain aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said twenty sixth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid, To Wit at the Hospital aforesaid of the Fever aforesaid did die and not from the Wounds and Bruises received by Reason of the Fall from and out of the two pair of Stairs Window aforesaid. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Atkins  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner

John Atkin Foreman
