City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the nineteenth day of december in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain,France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman,Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Benjamin Butcher then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Chivers , Robert Mavey , Henry Taylor , James Keefe , William Botley , Jahaziel Burdentranbridge , Duncan Watson , Mathew Bolldis , Williams Jordan , William Whitworth John Bangham and Robert Bowen good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enqure for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Benjamin Butcher came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the sixteenth day of December in the year aforesaid the said Benjamin Butcher a Labourer being at work at a Crane upon the Whart of William Stairs upon Milbank in the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist within the Liberty and County aforesaid and then and there landing a certain Price of Portland Stone out of a Barge the Horse working the said Crane being taken out. It so happened that the wooden Bar or Capstain of the said Crane in going round Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune struch against the Head of the said Benjamin Butcher by means whereof he the said Benjamin Butcher then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon the right side of the Head and a violent Concussionin the Brain of him the said Benjamin Butcher of which said mortal Fracture and Concussion he the said Benjamin Butcher at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said sixteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid until the nineteenth day of the same Month and Year did languish and languishing did live on which and nineteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Benjamin Butcher of the mortal Fracture and Concussion aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Benjamin Butcher in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Chivers Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Thos. Prickard  Coroner

John Sworn Foreman
