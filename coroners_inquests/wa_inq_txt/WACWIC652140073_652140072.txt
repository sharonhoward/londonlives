City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church St. Pater, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of February in the Fourteenth Year of the Regin of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Dell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Curtis John Brigham , George Whitroe , William Ewer , Joseph Barwell , William Nesham James Shorrock , Francis Bachelor , John Heath , Richard Tagg , John Taylor , Edward Haselham  Thomas Staphes and Edward Nester good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Dell came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That the said Joseph Dell a Carter on the fifth day of January in the Year aforesaid, being driving a certain Empty Cart drawn by three Horses in Brewer's Yard situate in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid and then and there Riding upon the Copse of the said Cart, and that the said Joseph Dell then and there fell from the Copse of the said Cart upon the Stone Pavement in the said Yard, and thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Fracture in and upon his Right Leg, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Joseph Dell from the said Fifth day of January in the Year aforesaid until the Tenth day of February in the same Year. at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said tenth day of February in the Year aforesaid, at the said Parish of St. Margaret, he the said Joseph Dell of the mortal Fracture aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise.In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Curtis Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner John Curtis Foreman
