City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty eighth day of March in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Rock then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of George Healey , William Harvey , John Wilton , Peter Davidson , Thomas Fisher , Andrew Mc. Cormick  John Gwynne , Charles Lescheim , John Himlock  James Cooper Christian Schrouts George Shepherd , Jams Allen , George Dickinson , Richard Sims and William Brown good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Rock came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Joseph Rock an Infant, to wit, of the Age of three Years, on the Twenty sixth day of March in the Year aforesaid, being in a Room in the dwelling House of Mrs. Bruce situate in Jermyn Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there sitting in the Window, and the Sash being up It so happened that the said Joseph Rock Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell out of and through the said Window being two Stories high, unto and upon the Stone, Pavement in the Court before the said House, and thereby received divers Bruises in and upon his Body of which said mortal Bruises he the said Joseph Rock then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Joseph Rock in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said George Healey Foreman of the Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Geo: Healey Foreman
