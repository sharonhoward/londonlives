City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of March in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Wall an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Herxes , Luke Ideson , Samuel Mountfield , John Gray , Thomas Clement , Henry Drury , Alexander Stewart , Francis Stocken , John Dixon , Charles Fowler , Robert Wright , Thomas Matthews , Robert Calvert , John Arkill and Richard Groome good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Wall came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Mary Wall Infant, to Wit, of the Age of Seven Months, on the Fifth day of March in the Year aforesaid being in Bed alseep with her Mother Elizabeth Wall , and that it so happened that the said Mary Wall the Infant in the Night Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fall out of the Bed from her said Mother into a Washing Tub, containing about three Quarts of Water, standing on the Floor by the side of the Bed wherein the said Mary Wall was Sleeping, and in the Water in the said Tub was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning she the said Mary Wall then there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Mary Wall the Infant in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise. I Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Herxes Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Wm. Herxes Foreman
