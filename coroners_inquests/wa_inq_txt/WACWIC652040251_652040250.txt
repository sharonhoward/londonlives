City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of July in the Fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman, Coroner of Our said Lord the King for the City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Henry an Infant then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Robert Jones , Thomas Barteline , Hugh Jones , Richard Beach , John Drover , Evan Davies , John Lediman , John Robinson , Thomas Kennedy , John Cartwright , Adam Brown , Michael Gano , Edmund Ley , and Richard Burdow good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said Robert Henry came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That on the First day of July in the Year aforesaid the said Robert Henry the Infant being in a little Chaise of Chair drawn along by William Henry (Father of the said Robert Henry ) in the Publick Road or Highway near the Bunhoun at Chelsea, in the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, which said Chaise or Chair then and there Accidentally Overturned, and the said Robert Henry the Infant lay in the said Road or Highway; And that a certain Man to the Jurors aforesaid Unknown was then and there briskly driving a Hackney Coach drawn by two Horses, along the said Publick Highway leading from London towards Chelsea College , It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the two off wheels of the said Hackney Coach did then and there go upon and pass over the Head and Neck of him the said Robert Henry , by Means whereof he the said Robert did then and there receive divers Mortal Bruises in and upon his said Head and Neck, of which said Mortal Bruises he the said Robert Henry then and there instantly Died: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Robert Henry in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. And that the said two off wheels of the said Hackney Coach, so drawn as aforesaid, were moving to the Death of the said Robert Henry , that the same are of the Value of Thirty shillings, and are of the Goods and Chattels, and in the Possession of a Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid unknown In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Robert Jones Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Robt. Jones Fore Man
