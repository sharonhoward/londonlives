City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of August in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Rogers then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Sutter John Lander , Edward Mears , James Simpson , William Lloyd , John Day , George Wilson  Lawrence Welch , James Hartley , James Barell , Joseph Biven , John Hand , James Knight , Richard Holloway Charles Harrison , Richard Jackson , Edward Knight , and Timothy King good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Rogers came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say

That the said John Rogers on the Sixth day of August in the Year aforesaid being employed as a Labourer at a certain New Building now carrying an for a Work house situate in Castle Street in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid and being walking on a Board placed aslant from a Scaffold on to the Wall of the said Building and being much intoxicated with Liquor, it so happened that he the said John Rogers then and there accidentally casually and by Misfortune fell from the top of the said Building off the said Board to the Ground by Means of which said falling he the said John Rogers then and there received a Violent and Mortal Briant and Concussion on his stead and dislocated his Collar Dane of which said Mortal Bruise Concussion and Dislocation he the said John Rogers then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Rogers in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune come to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as they Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner John Suller  Forman
