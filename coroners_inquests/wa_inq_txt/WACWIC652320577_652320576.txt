City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of September 1792 in the thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq.  Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Arnold then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Thomas Hitchin , William Vincent , Alexander Miller , Charles Chant , Robert Mann , Edward Newcomb , James Mason , John Whittmarsh , Thomas Robison , Robert Askey , Thomas Hunt , John Neice , George Ross , and Jonathan Page good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Arnold came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Joseph Arnold on the fourth Day of September in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid was then and there suddenly seized with a violent Fit of Apoplexy. Of which said Fit of Apoplexy he the said Joseph Arnold at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit at a certain Watch house called Saint Margarets Watch house and also at the Workhouse called Saint Margarets Work house there did languish and languishing did live untill the Eighth day of the same Month, in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid. On which said Eighth day of September in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit at the Work house aforesaid he the said Joseph Arnold departed this Life by the visitation of God in a natural Way, To Wit of the Fit aforesaid. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Kitchin Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day, Year and Place above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner

Thos. Hitchin Foreman
