An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Cause of James Hill Sign of the Barley Mews in Mount Sheet  Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of st. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twelfth day of november 1793 in the thity fouth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain,France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman,Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Susannah Waine then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whore Names and hereunder written and Seals Affixed

 Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty. duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enqure for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Susannah wine came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Susannah Waine not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding but lunatic and distracted on the Eleventh day of november in the Yea aforesiad at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid to wit in a Certain Price of water in Hyde Park called the Bason there did cast and throw herself, by Means of which said casting and Throwing, she the said Susannah waine , in the said Water was then and there suffocated and drowned Of which said Suffocation and Drowning she the said Susannah waire in the Wakhouse belonging to the said Parish some short time of the did die and so the Jurors aforesiad upon their Oath aforesaid, so say, that the said Susannah waine , not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding but lunatic and distracted in Manner and by the Mean aforesaid did hill herself. In Witness where of as well the said Eovoner as the said Jurors have to this Inqusition set teir hands and seals the day Year & Place first above written

Anthy Gell Coroner

Thos French Foreman Jaques Dalby Henry Brandon John fifcell Timothy Conner
