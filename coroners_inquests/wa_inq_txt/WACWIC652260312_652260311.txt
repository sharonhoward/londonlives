City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirty first day of May in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Hazel an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Cole , Abraham Skeggs , John Dobbins , George Readman , George Saunders , Robert Forbes  John Weatherhead , John Richardson , Jacob Presler , John Ward , Samuel Collings , Mathew Clark , and John Dalbey good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Hazel came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Sarah Hazel an Infant of the Age of One Year and an half, on the Thirtieth day of May in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, being laid to sleepin a certain turn up Bed, then and there beingin a Room in the dwelling house of Francis Hazel the Father of the said Sarah Hazel the Infant, and Elizabeth Jones the Servant of the said Francis Hazel, or some other Person coming into the said Room, and not knowing that the said Sarah Hazel the infant was then and there of leep or lying in the said Bed, It so happened that the said Elizabeth Jones or some other person Accidentally Cusually and by Misfortune, then and there innocently turned up the said Bed. by Means whereof She the said Sarah Hazel the Infant in the Cloaths of the said Bed was then and there suffocated and Smothered, of which said Suffocation and Smothering. She the said Sarah Hazel, then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, That the said Sarah Hazel the Infant, in manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, came to her Death.

In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said John Cole  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Cole Foreman
