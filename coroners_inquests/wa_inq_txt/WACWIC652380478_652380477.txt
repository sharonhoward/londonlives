City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Percival Ewer the Sign of the three (rowns in Richmond Street  princes Street in the parish of St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of the St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty first day of July 1798 in the thirty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Anne Tett then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good an lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Anne Tett came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Anne Tett on the twelfth day of July in the year aforesaid the Dwelling House of the said Anne Tett situate in Princes Street in the Liberty aforesaid casually and by Misfortune, took Fire and the said Anne Tett then and there being in her own Apartment It so happened that she was then and there burnt by the flame arising from the said fire: of which said Burning she the said Anne Tett did languish and languishing did live until the twentieth day of July in the Year afoersaid: On which said twentieth day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid of the said Burning she the said Anne Tett, did did, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say that the said Anne Tett in manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to her death and not otherwise IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy. Gell Coroner }
