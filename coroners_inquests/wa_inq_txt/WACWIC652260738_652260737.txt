City of Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of November in the Twenty Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Benjamin John Agace Le Grand then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Morrison , James Stuart , William Sheldon , George Hawks William Gend , Andrew Oliver , William Ponney , George Swinley , Samuel Archibald , Thomas Brown , John Hosier , John Green , William Payne , William Compers , James Ingles , David Nicolson and Daniel Blockley good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Benjamin John Agace Le Grand came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Benjamin John Agace Le Grand not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and Distracted, on the Sixth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Dwelling House of Mr. Benjamin Copper the Hungerford Coffee House in the Strand there Situate with a certain Razor made of Iron and Steal which he the said Benjamin John Agace Le grand then and there had and held in his Right Hand the Throat or Gullet of him the said Benjamin John Agace Le Grand did then and there Strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there going unto himself with the Razer aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Benjamin John Agace Le Grand one Mortal wound of the length of New inches and of the depth of one inch of which Mortal Wound he the said Benjamin John Agace Le Grand did languish and languishing did Live untill the Ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid on which said Ninth day of November in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Benjamin John Agace Le grand of the said Mortal Wound did Dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid on their Oath aforesaid do say That the said Benjamin John Agace Le Grand not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did Kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the Foreman of the Said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Willm. Monfield  Foreman
