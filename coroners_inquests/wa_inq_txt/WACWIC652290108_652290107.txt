City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Knightsbridge in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of February in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Brown then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Redden , William Pinner , Robert Jackson Stephen Simmons , Thomas Barber , Richard Richards  George Stone , Robert Anderson , George Puffer , William Keble , William Sharp and William Glee good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Brown came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Brown the Infant on the first day of February in the year aforesaid Aged one Year and an half being left with another Infant in the Lodging Room of Rebecca Edford situate at Knightsbridge in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid. It so happened that the Linen Cloaths which the said John Brown the Infant then and there had on his Body Accidentally Casually and by misfortune took fire by means whereof the said John Brown was burnt in and upon the right side of the Body Neck and Face of which said Burning he the said John Brown from the said First day of February in the Year aforesaid until the second day of the same Month at Knightsbridge aforesaid did languish and live On which said second day of February in the Year aforesaid at Knightsbridge aforesaid he the said John Brown of the Mortal burning aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Brown the Infant in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Rodden Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner James Rodden Foreman
