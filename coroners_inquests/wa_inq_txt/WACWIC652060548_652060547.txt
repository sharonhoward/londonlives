City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster with in Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of October in the Sixth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard  Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of John Chapman then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Small , Thomas Stanley , William Bennett , James Keyes , John Tomkinson , William Mason , William Hepbourn , John Wells , Henry Callis , William Spratley , William Llewellin , and John Parish , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said John Chapman came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That the said John Chapman on the Twentieth day of September in the year aforesaid being in the dwelling House of John Dick a Baker situate at Hungerford in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid and carrying a Sack full of wheat flour on his Shoulder down Stairs into the Bakehouse of the said John Dick , It so happened that the said John Chapman then and the Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fall off and from the said Stairs unto the Floor, and by Reason thereof he the said John Chapman did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one Mortal Bruise in and upon his Back, and also Rupture in his Bladder of which said Mortal Bruise and Rupture he the said John Chapman from the said Twentieth day of September in the Year aforesaid, until the Fifth day of October in the same Year, at the Westminster Hospital situate and being in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live; on which said Fifth day of October in the year aforesaid, he the said John Chapman at the Hospital aforesaid, of the Mortal Bruise and Rupture aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said John Chapman Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Richard Small Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in the presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Rids Small Foreman
