City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty first day of May in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grave of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Alexander Douglas then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Gardner  Robert Anderson , Matthew Hill , John Smith , Thomas Carter , John Leech , William Price , John Parish , John Archer , Vensel Mann  John Lane and John De Jarsey , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Alexander Douglas came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Alexander Douglas on the Eighteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist within the Liberty and County aforesaid going up a Step Ladder in the Workshop of Charles Kither there situate and being Intoxicated with Liquor It so happened that the said Alexander Douglas then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said Ladder unto and upon the Floor and thereby did then and there receive divers Bruises in his Body and a Compound Fracture in and upon the Right Arm, of which said Mortal Bruises and Fracture he the said Alexander Douglas at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesd. from the said Eighteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Twentieth day of the same Month did languish and languishing did live on which said Twentieth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Alexander Douglas of the mortal Bruises and Fracture aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Alexander Douglas in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Gardner Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Thos. Prickard Coroner

John Gardner Foreman}
