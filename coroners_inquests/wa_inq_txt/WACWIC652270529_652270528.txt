City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the tenth day of December in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Williams then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Gray  James Robertson , Philip Barton , Peter Caird , William Hewitt , Thomas Kennett , John Robinson , James Cotter , Thomas Warren , Benjamin Fry , Alexander Bennett , Michael Barrett  Robert Boulter , James Trepass  William Conyers , and Lawrence Fagan good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Thomas Williams came to h is Death , do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Williams on the ninth Day of December in the Year aforesaid was driving a certain Hackney Coach in Bedford Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and the night being dark It so happened that one of the hender Wheels of the said Coach geting on the inside of a certain Post in the said Street the said Thomas Williams by a sudden Jolt was thrown off and from the Coach Rot of the said Coach unto and upon the Stone Pavement in the said Street by means whereof the said Thomas Williams then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did receive a mortal Concussion in and upon the Brain of him the said Thomas Williams of which said mortal Concussion he the said Thomas Williams then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Williams in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Williams  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos Gray Foreman
