City and Liberty of Westminster in the county of Middlesex }to wit   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of August  in the Twenty Second Year of the Reign o four Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before, Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Aldon then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of

John Gilbert , Thomas Guy , Robert Tombling , Thomas Mort , William Cook , John Cook , John Hamon , John Ivison , John Ward , Thomas Smith , William Chambers , and Alexander Grant

 good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said  John Aldon came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say

That the said John Alden on the Twenty ninth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid being Rolling the Gravel with a Rolling Stone in the Garden of the House of Mrs. Robinson in Berkeley Square there situate if so happened that he the said John Alden coming to near the descent or slope of the Garden Ground near to the Area of the said House and over powered by the force of the Roller Accidentally casually and by Misfortune fell from the said Garden Ground into the said Area with the said Rolling Stone, by means whereof he the said John Alden then and there received at violent and Mortal Concussion in the Brain of which said Mortal Concussion he the said John Alden then and there instantly Died And to the Jurors aforesaid upon there Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Alden in Manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisitions set their Hands and Seals the Day and Year abovementioned

Tho Prickard Coroner

John Gilbert Foreman
