City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fifth day of June in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joshua Churchill Esquire then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Clough , Thomas Mort , William Brittle , Thomas King  Joseph Shore , William Shells , John Dwerrihouse , John Harriott , Charles Briere , William Bolton  Samuel Gould , Thomas Wells , Charles Alton , John Hughes and John Lamb good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joshua Churchill came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Joshua Churchill, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Twenty third day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid with a certain Penknife made of Iron and Steel which he the said Joshua Churchill then and there had and held in his Right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said Joshua Churchill did then and there strike stab and Penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Penknife aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Joshua Churchill one mortal Wound, of which said he the said Joshua Churchill then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do do say, that the said Joshua Churchill, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Clough Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Thos. Clough Foreman
