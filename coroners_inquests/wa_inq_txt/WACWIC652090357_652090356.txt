City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth Day of August in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Lee then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard James William Crockett , Thomas Scott , George Twigg , William Davis , John Mulling , William Patterson , Robert Bramham , John Thomas , William Cronder , Thomas Wheeler , Francis Ruswell , and John Parish good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Lee came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say

That the said William Lee on the Thirteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the Liberty aforesaid being Standing on a Wheel of a certain Court London with Bricks at Mr. Machraths Lime Wharf on Milbank, with one Leg, and the other leg on the said Cart, it to Happened that the said Cart being on a Sloping Ground Moved back wards whereby he the said William Lee was thrown from the said Cart to the Ground and the said wheel accidentally Casually and by Misfortune went over the left Thigh of him the said William Lee by Means whereof he the said William Lee did there and there receive One Mortal Bruise or Wound on his said left Thigh of the length of Horse Inches and Depth of One Inch of which said Mortal Bruise or Wound he the said William Lee at the said Parish of St. John the Evangelist aforesaid, and also at the Parish of St., Margaret in the Liberty aforesaid from the said Thirteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid to the Twelfth day of August in the Year Aforesaid did Languish and Languishing did Lure on which said Twelfth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of Saint Margaret aforesaid (to wit) at the Westminster Hospital in James Street, he the said William Lee of the Martal Bruise or wound Aforesaid did Dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said William Lee in manner and by there Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise, and that the Wheel of the said Cart was Moving to the Death of him the said William Lee and is of the Value of Two Shillings and six pence of Lawfull Money of Great Britain and is the Property and in the Possession of Thomas Beckham of the said Parish of St, John the Evangelist or of his Assigns In Witness Whereof aswell the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day and Year frist above written

Tho Prickard Coroner Richd. James Foreman
