City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegian Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the tenth day of March in the Third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of William Border then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Woodin . John Ross , John Mace , James Davis , David Thompson , Thomas Jenkins , Edward Cooper , James Gordon , Lewis Morgan , Anthony Rovell , John Hardy , John Budd , Edward Weller , Robert Chapman , Henry Freeman , John Stewart , Robert Wright and Richard Brown good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said William Border came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That on the Ninth day of March in the Year aforesaid the said William Border being in a certain Hayloft (where he used to lye and sleep) belonging to John Lock , situate in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid; It so happened that the said William Border , being intoxicated and in Liquor, then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell from the said Hayloft into a Rack in a Stable, in the said Parish Liberty and County and then and there by the fall aforesaid received divers Mortal Bruises on the Head of him the said William Border , of which said Mortal Bruises he the said William Border then and there instantly Died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid , do say that the said William Border in manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Woodn Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place fist above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Woodin Foreman
