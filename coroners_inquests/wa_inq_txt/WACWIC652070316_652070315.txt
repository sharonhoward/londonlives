City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Sixth day of May in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Mary Gardiner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Thorley , Samuel Oliver , Francis Proudfoot  Thomas Palmer , Thomas Mitchell , Thomas Bailey  William Chapman  John Golden , John Lawrence , Richard Bethell , Joseph Stanhope , John Shields , John Glascott  John Jupp William Mc. Millen , William Anglin and John Jackson , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when how and by what Means the said Mary Gardiner came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That Thomas Puts Swinoe late of the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid Gentleman, on the Fifth day of May in the Year aforesaid, with Force and Arms, at the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said Mary Gardiner , in the Peace of God and our said Lord the King then and there being, feloniously did make a Assault, And that the said Thomas Swinoe with his Right Hand her the said Mary Gardiner in and upon the Cheek, near the left Temple, of her the said Mary Gardiner , did then and there feloniously strike and beat, and her the said Mary Gardiner , to and upon the Kitchen Floor, which said Kitchen was paved with Stones, in the dwelling House of Elizabeth Ellison situate and being in the said Parish Liberty and County, did cast and throw, And that the said Thomas Swinoe by the Striking beating, casting, and throwing to the Floor aforesaid, did then and there give unto her the said Mary Gardiner one Mortal Wound in and upon the back part of the Head of her the said Mary Gardiner , of which said mortal Wound she the said Mary Gardiner from the said Fifth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Sixth day of the same Month in the same Year at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Sixth day of May in the Year aforesaid she the said Mary Gardiner at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, of the mortal Wound aforesaid, did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Swinoe, her the said Mary Gardiner , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Feloniously did hill and slay, against the peace of our said Lord the King, his Crown and Dignity. And that the said Thomas Swinoe at the time of committing the Felony and Manslaughter aforesaid, or at any time since, had no Goods or Chattels, Lands or Tenements within the said County, or elsewhere, to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Thorley , Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

James Thorley Foreman
