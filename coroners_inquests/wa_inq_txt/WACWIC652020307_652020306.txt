City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the third day of September in the Second Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Thurlin an Instant these and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Sliper , Charles Simson , John Smith , James Johnson , John Taylor John Ramsay , Thomas Bowlin , William Showel  Joseph Dell , John Hyett , Matthew Percival John Pugh , Samuel Bowerman , Thomas Ward , Patrick Anderson and John Pitcher good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, and who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Sovereign Lord the King, when how and by what means the said Mary Thurlin came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the thirty first day of August in the year aforesaid, one Harper and another Man whose name is unknown to the said Jurors, were in the Kings Highway there called Oxford Road , throwing a seven pound Iron Weight for a Wager, which of them could exceed in distance, And that at the same time the said Harper and the said Man unknown were so throwing the said Weight to decide the said Wager, the said Mary Thurlin the Instant aforesaid, was standing in the said Highway before the dwelling House of John Thurlinker Father there situate, near the Sill of the Door, and at a small distance from the Door Post belonging to the said House; And that it so happend said Harper was then and in the said Highway beyond the space and distance thrown to by the said Man unknown, the King belonging to the said Weight did then and there hitch to the Fingers of the said Harper, by reason whereof the said Weight upon the said Harper's quitting and delivering the same, in his throwing did pass obliquely and contrary to the Course intended by the said Harper, and did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune strike against the said Door Post of the said House, and did from thence violently rebound to and against the Head of the said Mary Thurlin, then and there casually accidentally and by misfortune giving to the said Mary Thurlin in and upon her said Head a Mortal Fracture, Of which said Mortal Fracture the said Mary Thurlin from the said thirty first day of August in the Year aforesaid until the third day of September in the same Year at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, On which said third day of September in the same Year the said Mary Thurlin at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid did Dye: And that the said Iron Weight was moving to the Death of the said Mary Thurlin and is of the Value of one Shilling, and the Property of John Upton of Oxford Road in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid Victualler. In Witness whereof a well the said Coroner as the said John Sliper Foreman, on the behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Sliper Foreman
