City of Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of August in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on on View of the Body of Eleanor Smith then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Whitworth , John Eves , John Taylor , Matthew Ginger Richard Bradley , James Loolidge , George Lucas , Humphrey Chadwick Richard Carter , Henry Duval , Christopher Hart , Robert Groves and John Horn good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elean or Smith came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the said Thirteeth day of August in the year aforesaid the said Eleanor Smith being in a Window in the Dwelling House of John Smith situate in Gardener's Lane, in the said Parish of St. Martaget, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and hanging out a Quit to dry upon a certain Pole on the outside of the said House, It so happend That the Line broke which fastened the said Pole, and that he said Eleanor Smith fell down from the said Window at the Height of Three Storys upon the Stone Pavement in the said Lane, and thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received a mortal Fracture and Contusion in and upon the left side of her Head, of which said mortal Fracture and Contusion she the said Eleanor Smith languished about Two Hours at the said Parish, and in the Liberty and County aforesaid and then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Eleanor Smith in Manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise,

In Witness where of as well the said Coroner , as the said William Whitworth  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Whitworth Foreman
