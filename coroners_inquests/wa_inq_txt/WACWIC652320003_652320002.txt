City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of January in the Thirty Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Hyatt then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of William Payne , Thomas Evans , Joseph Sharp , William Moore , John Summers , James Perry , George Webb , William Payne John Hammond , John Hicks , John Roberts , John Dilling , John Dupree William Slater and William Jones good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Hyatt came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That the said William Hyatt not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted on the Fourth day of January in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid, a great Quantity of Landanum being a deadly Poison, did take drink and swallow down by means whereof the said William Hyatt became then and there distempered in his Body and of the Poison aforesaid and of the and Distemper thereby occasioned then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said William Hyatt not being of Sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatick and distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did poison and kill himself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Payne  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner Wm Payne  Foreman
