City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Intended, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Dwelling of William Buckland in little Mount Street  Parish of St. George Hanover Square  Sign of the Bell within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty sixth day of March 1793 in the thirty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Alexander Stuart then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Several Jurors when Names are hereunder signed and Seals affixed good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Alexander Stuart came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Alexander Stuart not being of Sound Mind Memory and understanding, but lunatic and distracted, some time past (but to there Jurors unknown) in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To wit in the Serpentine Riser in the Hyde Park , there did cast an throw himself by means whereof He the said Alexander Sturat in the Water of the said River was then and there suffocated and drowned. Of which saud Suffocation and Drowning he the said Alexander Stuart then and there instantly died, And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Alexander Stuart not being of sound Mind Memory & Understanding but lunatic and distracted in manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day Year & place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }

James Gibbons foreman Willm Waters John Jones John Bennett Charles Clifford

Henry Branden William Harris Gorge Carr James Boulte Tho. Nichols Jas. Mills
