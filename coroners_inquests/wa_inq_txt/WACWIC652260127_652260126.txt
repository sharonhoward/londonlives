City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventeenth day of February in the Twenty Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Geryer then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Thomson , Ebenezer Taylor , Edward Barnett , Thomas Hitchcock , John Doran , Robert Silcock , Prickard Farrell , John Withers , Edward Winstanly , William Wetchman , William Brown , Martin Dessaline , Abraham Arons Robert Chamberlayne , and Alexander Gillis good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Greyor came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Greyer on the Sixteenth day of February in the year aforesaid being in the Cellar of Thomas Sessions  Victualler situate in Russel Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid. And that the said John Greyer then said there suddenly deported this life by the Visitation of God in a natural way and not by any Injury or Violence from the said Thomas Sessions any other Person whatsoever to the Knowledge of the said Jurors.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Thompson  Foreman of the said Jurors on behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above Written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Thomson Foreman
