City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Ann within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the twenty ninth day of November in the twenty seventh year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard  Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord for the said City and Liberty, on View, of the Body of Claude Lapiere then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Elles Peter Petit  Thomas Reed  Edward Southbuck  James Newman  Deadrick Smith  Christian Closes  John Thomas  Samuel Furnace  John Fedell  Henry James  Edmund Cooks  John Brown Alexander Clarke  Thomas Steen  Thomas Vese  Adam Bare  Philip Adams and William Bailey good and lawful men of the said liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord then King, When, how, and by what Means the said Claude Lapiere came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Claude Lapier not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatick and distracted on the twenty eighth day November in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the liberty and County aforesaid a certain Frazer made of Iron and Steel which he the said Claude Lapiece then and there had and held in his Right Hand the Throat or Gullet of him the said Claude Lapiece did then and there abide stab and Penetrate thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Razer aforesaid in and upon the throat or Gullet of him the said Claude Lapecer one Mortal Wound of the Length of two Inches and of the Depth of one Inch of which said Mortel wound he the said Claude Lapiece then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do says that the said Claude Lapiece not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatick and and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did Kill himself:

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Elles Peter Petit  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inguisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Ells Petit Foreman
