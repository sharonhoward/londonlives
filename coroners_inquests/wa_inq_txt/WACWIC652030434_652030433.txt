An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Anne-within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of December in the fourth Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Thomas Thornton then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Charles Drew , John Sone , William Cubbidge , Samuel Holt , William Paul , Thomas Biffin , John Swan , Benjamin Dorset , Frederick Pecklat , John Best , Jeremiah Nichols , Peter Janvier , Francis Lacour , Thomas Tagg , and William Hooper , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King when how and by what Means the said Thomas Thornton -came to his deathy, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty seventh day of December in the Evening, in the Year aforesaid the said Thomas Thornton , and several other Persons being walking together in a certain Street called Newman Street , in the Parish of St. Mary le bone , in the said County of Middlesex , and that the said Thomas Thornton was then and there Intoxicated and in Liquor, and being very dark, It so happened that the said Thomas Thornton then and there Accidentally Casually and by misfortune fell into the Area of a New built uninhabited House, situate in Newman Street aforesaid, by Means whereof he the said Thomas Thornton did then and there receive a violent Concussion upon the Brain, of which said violent Concussion he the said Thomas Thornton from the Twenty seventh day of December in the Year aforesaid, until the Twenty eighth day of the same Month, in the same year, at the Parish of St. Anne within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty eighth day of December in the Year aforesaid, at the said Parish of St. Anne within the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Thomas Thornton of the violent Concussion aforesaid did dye, And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Thomas Thornton , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Charles Drew , Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Charles Drew , Foreman
