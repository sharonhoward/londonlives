City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit} 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of May in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Tryphosa Matthews then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Edward Padget, Walter Tyrrell , Alexander Marr , John Marson , George Winnall , Robert Smith , Thomas Hazard , Richard Priest , John Taylor , Silas Payne , Joseph Gant , Job Heary , John Clarke , John Curtis , and Samuel Morrison good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire of our said Lord the King when, how, and by what Means the said Tryphosa Matthews came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Tryphosa Matthews not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Ninth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto an Apple Tree growing in a Garden adjoining to the dwelling House of Mary Orson (with whom the said Tryphosa Matthews Lodged) situate and being in Rochester Row in the said Parish City Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about her own Neck did fix, tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang, suffocate, and strangle herself, of which said Hanging, Suffocation and Strangling she the said Tryphosa Matthews then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Tryphosa Matthews not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward Padget Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Edwd. Padget Foreman
