City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex , To wit.}  In Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Parish of St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Eleventh Day of April in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & liberty on View of the Body of George Baggot then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of George Musson , Samuel Wood , Thomas Love , Matthew Beetham , William Painter , Robert Johnson , William Collins , Peter Bartholomew , Christopher Reeves , Peter Allcock , Abraham Hepworth , George Gerrard , John Gregg  Frederick Cudgoa , Henry Andrews , and John Lee good and lawful Men of the said liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said George Baggot came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said George Baggot on the said Eleventh day of April in the Year aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did fall into a certain Water Tub containing a great Quantity of Water, standing in the Yard adjoining to the dwelling House of him the said George Baggot situate and being in Carnaby Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and in the Water in the said Tub was Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning, he the said George Baggot then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said George Baggot in manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said George Musson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Geo. Musson Foreman
