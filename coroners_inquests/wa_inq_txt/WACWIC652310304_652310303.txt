City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of July in the Thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain,France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Allen then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John Harriott , John Lambert , Joseph Mills , Philip Peckham , John Adamson , Thomas Williams , William Mumby , William Stewart , John Bristol , Hugh Anderson , George Pearce and James Dunston Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Allen came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the first day of July in the Year aforesaid the said Thomas Allen in a public Street called new Bond Street in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster suddenly departed this Life by the visitation of God in a natural Way to wit by the bursting of a blood Vessel of which he instantly died

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Harriott  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of the Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisiton set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and place abovementioned

Thos Prickard  Coroner

John Harriot Foreman
