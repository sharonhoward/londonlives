City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of July in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain,France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Banks then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Richard Oakes  Richard Tonson , William Jeffreys Thomas Goswell , Randall Boxhall , Richard Harvey , Robert Walker , Samuel Garrard , Joseph Dean , George Eaken, John Hood and William Fenn good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Banks came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the first day of July in the Year aforesaid the said John Banks was riding upon a certain Bay Horse in a Lane Leading to Turnham Green in the Parish of Cheswick in the said County of Middlesex , and that it so happened that the said John Banks then and there accidentally Casually and by misfortune fell off and from the said Horse and thereby received a Mortal Concussion in the Brian of him the said John Banks of said mortal Concussion he the said John Banks at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty & County aforesaid from the said first day of July in the Year aforesaid until the second day of the same month and Year did languish and languishing did live on which said second day of July in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square the the said John Banks of the Mortal Concussion aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Banks accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Richard Oakes  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his fellows in their presence have to this inquisition set their hands and seals the Day Year and place abovementioned

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Rd Oakes  Foreman
