City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Kensington in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the first day of May in the twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so Forth, before thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Dobinson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Benjamin Baily, James Etwell , Samuel Pauncett , William Trent , John Roberts , James Lake  James Mc.Intosh , Roger Murley , Robert Lyne , Thomas Tyne , William Mc.Cleish , John Andrews and Thomas Andrews good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for out said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Ann Dobinson came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Ann Dobinson an Infant, to Wit of the Age of Six Years, on the Twenty ninth day of April in the Year aforesaid being alone in the Lodging Room or Apartment of Joseph Hughes in the Dwelling House of Elizabeth Dobinson at Kensington in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the Linner Cloth which the said Ann Dobinson the Infant then wore. Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took Fire, by Means whereof the Breast Neck Face and Hands of her the said Ann Dobinson were then and there violently Scorched and Burnt, of which she the said Ann Dobinson from the said Twenty ninth day of April in the Year aforesaid until the said first day of May in the same Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish, and of the said Burning then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Ann Dobinson in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Benjamin Baily  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals, the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Benj Baily Foreman
