City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields Within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of November in the Twenty Fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Theodosius Forrest then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edmund Antrobus , William Winter  Samuel Firmin John Davenport , Thomas Hodgson , Richmond Blamire John Bown , Richard Henston , John Richards Hassell Hutchins , John Henderson , and Edward Blackmore good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by What Means the said Theodosius Forrest came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Theodosius Forrest not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted on the Fifth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Dwelling House of the said Theodosius Forrest there situate with a certain Knife made of Iron and Steel which Forrest then and there had and held in his Right Hand the Throat or Gullet of him the said Theodosius Forrest did then and there strike stab and Penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Knife aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Theodosius Forrest one Mortal Wound, of which said Mortal Wound he the said Theodosius Forrest then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Theodosius Forrest in Manner and by the Means aforesaid not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted did kill himself, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Edward Andtrobus  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner Ed. Antrobus Foreman
