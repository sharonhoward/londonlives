City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of James Fozard of Parish Lane in the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of February 1793 in the thirty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great - Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Wood then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Fozard , Alexander Henderson , James Blackwell , John Simpson , Peter Fozard , Jonathan Bray , Joseph Higgs , James Johnson , William Dorsett , John Cann , Robert Griffin , Jonathan Hutchins and John Robinson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Wood came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Wood not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted on the said sixteenth day of February, in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid a certain Pistol charged with Gunpowder, and a leader Bullet, which he the said John Wood then and there had and held in his hand, to and against the head of him the said John Wood , did then and there shoot off and discharge by means whereof he the said John Wood did then and there give unto himself, with the leaden Bullet aforesaid, so discharged and slot out of the Pistol aforesaid, by the force of the Gunpowder aforesaid in and upon the head of him the said John Wood , one mortal Wound of the breath of One Inch, and of the depth of three Inches, of which said Mortal Wound He the said John Wood then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said John Wood not being of sound Mind, Memory, and understanding, but lunatic and distracted in manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself.In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day, Year, & Place above - written.

Anthony Gell Coroner } Sworn Alexr: Tlenderson Js. Blackwell John Simpson Peter Fozard John Pray
