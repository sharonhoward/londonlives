City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty ninth day of March in the thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Mather then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Thomas Barr  William Frowse , Joseph Dane , Henry List , Joseph Worner , James Blackwell , Robert Watson , Thomas Hodgson , Alexander Bald , Joseph Wild , Hugh Davis and William Ferm good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Mather  came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty Eighth day of March in the year aforesaid the said William Mather and one John Lewis Quarreled and fought in Hyde Park in the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and that the said John Lewis then and there with his left Hand the said William Mather did Strike upon the right side of his Head, and that the said William Mather thereby did then and then Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of him the said John Lewis receive a mortal Bruise and Concussion on the said Right side of his Head under the Right Ear of him the said William Mather , of which said mortal Bruise and Concussion he the said William Mather then and these died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Mather in manner and by the means aforesaid Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of him the said John Lewis come to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Barr  Foreman of the said Jurors , on the behalf of himself, and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thomas Barr Foreman
