City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex the Seventeenth day of May in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, Frances and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Perry then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Sutton , Richard Prime , George Jones , Charles Lyon , John Smith , Benjamin Ixer , George Pratt , John Lawrence , William Anglin , John Bright , Thomas Palmer  Charles Dubellamy  Samuel Ealand , John Brown , John Elliott William Coalthurst , and John Dewbery , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Perry came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Perry on the Seventeenth day of May in the Year aforesaid, was found dead in his Lodging Room or Apartment in the Dwelling House of Robert Durnford situate and being in Drury Lane in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, that no Marks of Violence appeared on the said James Perry , and that he came not to his Death by any Hurt or Injury from any Person whatsoever. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Sutton Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Joseph Sutton Foreman
