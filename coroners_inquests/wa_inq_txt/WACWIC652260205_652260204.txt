City & Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex . } to Wit. 

An Inquitition Indented, taken for our Sovererign Lord the King at the parish of saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the Counrty of Middlesex , the Fourth day of April in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Charlotte Frost an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Tindell , Ward Shepperd , Hunter Mitchell , Thomas Latham , Samuel Archibald , Josuha Davis , John Clarke , John Broadfield  James Graves  David Lewis , John Glening Alexander Mc Time Francis Hingham , Peter Conoley , Neal Mc Kenzie  Fredrick Buck , and George Willson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by that what Means the said Charlotte Frost came to h er Death do upon their Oath say, That on the third day of April in the year aforesaid, Richard Shoubron was carefully driving a certain empty Cart Drawn by two hores in St Marins lane in the Parish aforesaid, within the liberby abd County aforesaid , That the said Charlotte Frost and two other Children being in the same lane, it so happened that the said Charlotte Frost in Croping the End of Charlotte street fell down and that the near Wheel of the said Cart or drawn as aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the Head of the said Charlortte Frost by means whereof, she the said Charlotte Frost then and there received one Mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of the Head of his the said charlotte Frost, of which said mortal Farcture she the said Charlotte Frost the Infant then and that died and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Charlotte Frost in manner and by the means aforesaid, accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to the Death.

In Witness where of ad well the said Coroner , as the said John Tindall Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence, have to this Inquisition, set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and place above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

John Findall Foreman
