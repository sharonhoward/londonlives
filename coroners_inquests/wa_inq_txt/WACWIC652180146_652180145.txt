City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of May in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Margaret Griffiths then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Harvey  William Woodward  Humphrey Humphreys  Thomas Harlston John Woodward , Francis Joyce , Thomas Davies , John Bruce , John Jones , Jacob Forster , George Hickster  Anthony Powell and Thomas Harrison good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Margaret Griffiths came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Margaret Griffiths - not being of sound Ming Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted on the Third day of May in the Year aforesaid, one End of a certain Piece of small Cord around the Door Post in the Lodging Room of her the said Margaret Griffiths in the Dwelling House of John Higginbottom  Victualler situate and being in Piccadilly in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the other End about her own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle herself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling she the said Margaret Griffiths then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Margaret Griffiths , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill herself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Harvey Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellow, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Wm: Harvey Foreman
