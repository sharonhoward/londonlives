City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventeenth day of September in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary French then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Francis Preston , Joseph Thacker , William Conway , George Whitefoot , William Thompson , John Bailey  Francis Foster , Thomas Ralph , Thomas Ralph , Thomas Lewis , William Leader , John Knight , and Paul Robell good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary French came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said Mary French , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Sixteenth day of September in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, One End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto an Iron Staple fastened into the Window Frame in the Lodging Room or Apartment of her the said Mary French in the House of Mr. James, situate in little Newport Street in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about her own Neck did fix tye and faster, and there with did then and there hang suffocate and strangle herself, of which said Hanging, Suffocation, and Strangling she the said Mary French then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Mary French , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Francis Preston Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Preston Foreman
