City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventeenth day of November in the thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Adam Wein then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Oakes , Thomas Mose , Joseph Higgs , William Cullen , William Conyers , John Collingwood , Alexander Henderson William Warren , John Street , John Cheriton , William Peartree and Charles Dyson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Adam Wein came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That on the Eleventh day of November in the Year aforesaid the said Adam Wein was driving a certain Hackney, Coach in the Public Road or Kings High way at Knightsbridge , in the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the Horses drawing the said Coach took fright, and that the said Hackney Coach was overturned in the said High way, by means whereof the said Adamwein then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received a Mortal Fracture in his Collar bone on the Right side and Bruises in his Body of which said mortal Fracture and Bruises he the said Adam Wein from the said Eleventh day of November in the Year aforesaid until the Fourteenth day of the same Month and Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said Fourteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Adam Wein of the Mortal Fracture and Bruises aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Adam Wein in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Richard Oakes Foreman of the said on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner Richd Oakes Foreman}
