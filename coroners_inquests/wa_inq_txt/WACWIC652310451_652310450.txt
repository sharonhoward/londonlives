City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of September in the Thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Faith Wastie then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John Clark , Samuel Gray , William Green , Samuel Hobson John Fowler , John Simkin , Peter Blackstock , Bartholomew Dowling Thomas Gleaves , Samuel Morsey , Francis Sims , Thomas Breach , Richard Mims , Bizzall Richardson & John Stephens good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Faith Watie came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Faith Wastie not being of sound mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distructed on the second day of September in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid One End of a certain Price of Cord unto an Iron Curtain Rod at the Feet of a Bed in the Lodging Room or Apartment of her the said Faith Wastie in the Dwelling House of Thomas Fawson situate and being in Long Acre in the said Parish Liberty and County and the other End thereof about her own neck did fix tie and faster and therewith did then and these hang suffocate and strangle herself of which said hanging Suffocation and changling she the said Faith Wastie then and these died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Faith Wastie not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic & distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill herself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Clark  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set then Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jno. Clark Foreman
