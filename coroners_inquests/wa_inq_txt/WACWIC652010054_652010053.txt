Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter of Westmr. the Frist day of September in the Frist Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith, and so forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one . Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid, Upon a view of the Body of Thomas Wise then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of John Madders , John Starr , John Jackson , John Eldrigg , Benjamin Coates , John Keatly , William Townsend , John Whitbey , Thomas Morris , George Nelson , James Moody , Thomas Whitney and Caleb Atkinson Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Thomas Wise came to his Death say upon their Oaths. That the said Thomas Wise not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted on the Twenty ninth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid one end of a peice of Cord round a peice of Timber frist in the Hayloft belonging to the said Thomas Wise situate in Grosvenors Mews in the said Parish and the other and thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then there hang Suffocate and strangle himself of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said Wise then and there of Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Wise not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted in manner and by the, means aforesaid did Kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day and year abovementioned

Jno. Feary Coroner .

John Madders Foreman
