City of Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of April in the Fourteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Daniel Delany then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Nathaniel Hall , Ernest Lesley , Robert Bray  Joseph Greaves , James Payne , Michael Castledine , William Bundon , Richard Birch , John Smith , John Lawrence , James Grierson and Robert Bramham good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Daniel Delaney came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Daniel Delaney a Laborer on the Thirtieth day of March in the Year aforesaid being at Work upon a certain House situate in Charles Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being then and there upon a certain Scaffold on the outsaid, and near the top of the said House, It so happened that the said Scaffold gave way, and that the said Daniel Delaney Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down from thence unto the Ground, and thereby received a mortal Fracture in and upon his right Ankle, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Daniel Delaney from the said Thirtieth day of March in the Year aforesaid until the fourteenth day of April in in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said fourteenth day of April aforesaid he the said Daniel Delaney at the Parish aforesaid, of the mortal Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Daniel Delaney in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Nathaniel Hall Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Nathnl Hall Foreman
