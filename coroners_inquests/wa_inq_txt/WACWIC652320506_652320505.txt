City & Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit,   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of August 1792 in the thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq.  Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Charles Stutely then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Benjamin Bradley , William Stanfield  James Waldie  Timothy Sheldrake Richmond Blamire  Joseph Christian , Samuel Sheldon , David Hilliard Micheal Burke  Philip Sapple , William Powell and Jacob Life good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by that what Means the said Charles Stuteley came to his Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Charles Stuteley on the Seventh death of August, in the Year aforesauid, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square and in Liberty aforesaid, being on the Outside of the third Story from the Ground of the Dwelling House of Lady Essex in Albesmoule Street there at work it so happened that accidentally, casually and by Misfortune, he the said Charles Stutely then and there fell from of the said Story of the said Dwelling House to the Ground, by means whereof he the said Charles Stuteley then and there received divers mortal Wounds on the Body of him the said Charles Stuteley of which said said Mortal wounds he the said Charles Stutely untill the evening of the said Seventh day of August in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit at the Dwelling House of Joseph Stuteley the Father of the said Charles Stutely, did languish and languishing did live on which said Evening of the said Seventh day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields in the liberty aforesaid To Wit at the Dwelling House of the said Stuteley of the said mortal Wounds did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Charles Stuteley in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually, and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Benjamin Bradley foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition, set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year and Place above Written.

Anthy. Gell Coroner

Benj: Bradley Foreman
