City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the  Eleventh day of October in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Morris then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Nicholas Gascoyne , Charles Lebas , William Nodes , William Keeling , James Gyles , William Wilkes , John Taylor , James Wingfield , James Simmons , William Moseley , Peter Cockburne , Thomas Scholar , John FitzPatrick , William Bayley , George Stubbs , Gideon Dare and John Burk good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Morris came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Morris , not being of sound mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the said Eleventh day of October in the year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, a certain Pistol charged with Gun powder and a leaden Bullet, which he the said William Morris then and there had and held in both his Hands, to and against the Head of him the said william Morris did then and there shoot off and discharge, by Means whereof he the said William Morris did then and there give unto himself with the leaden Bullet aforesaid, so dischared and shot out of the Pistol aforesaid by the force of the Gun powder aforesaid, in and upon the right  of the Head of him the said William Morris one mortal Wound, of which said mortal Wound he the said William Morris then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said William Morris , not being of sound Mind memory and understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did Kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Nicholas Gascoyne Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Follows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above Written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Nich. Gascoyne Foreman
