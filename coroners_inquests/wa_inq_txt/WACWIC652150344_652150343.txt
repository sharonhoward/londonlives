City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King. at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirty First day of October in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Wood then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Story , Edward Hill , Thomas Owen , William Cook , Thomas Watts , Newport George Houghton  Thomas King , John Chant , John Richards , Robert Tomlin  Joseph Johnson  John Ward & John Durham good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire of our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Robert Wood came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Robert Wood a Laborer on the Thirtieth day of October in the Year aforesaid being at Work and standing upon a Ladder on the inside of the Jee Well of Joseph Exer Confectioner situate in Mount Street in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, And that William Jones and several other Workmen with a Rope were then pulling up a large piece of Timber out of the said Well, And the said William Jones being in Liquor, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down to and against the said Robert Wood , then and there standing upon the Ladder as aforesaid, and thereby forced the said Robert Wood to the bottom of the said Well, whereby the said Robert Wood in the Water thereof Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Robert Wood then and therefored. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Robert Wood in Manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Story Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner Wm Story Foreman
