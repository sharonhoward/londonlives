City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Ann within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth Day of November in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of John Isham James then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Benjamin Gee , Thomas Tagg , Andrew Max , Nicholas Saunders , Attewell Timmes , Benjamin Jones , Benjamin Goodall , William Williams , Richard Cresser , James Price , George Reynolds , Thomas Dobson , James Smeaton , John Battersby , Edward Shea and Thomas Lewis , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when how and by what Means the said John Isham James came to his Death do upon their Oath say That the said John Isham James an Infant of about the Age of Years being a Scholar in the Academy situate in Soho Square in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Seventeenth day of November in the Year aforesaid, at the Academy aforesaid in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid being in Company with William Breton an Infant of about the Age of Sixteen Years and other Young Scholars there, and then and there wantonly and without Judgment thrusting and parrying with a drawn Sword which he then and there had and held in his right Hand, to and against the Body of him the said William Breton , he the said William Breton , in order to prevent the said John Isham James from doing any personal hurt or mischief therewith, did then and there take the said drawn Sword from him the said John Isham James, and the said William Breton then and there endeavouring to put the same into the Scabbard, It So happened that Causally and by Misfortune and against the Will of him the said William Breton , he the said John Isham James suddenly and without discretion ran upon the Point of the said Sworn, by means whereof he the said John Isham James then and there received one mortal Wound in th Belly of him the said John Isham James below the Ribs on the right side of the Body of him the said John Isham James, penetrating the Stomach and Liver of him the said John Isham James, of which said mortal Wound he the said John Isham James from the said Seventeenth day of November in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, until the Eighteenth day of the same Month in the same Year, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Eighteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid he the said John Isham James of the mortal Wound aforesaid then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, That the said John Isham James in manner and by the means aforesaid Casually, by Misfortune, and against the Will of him the said William Breton came to his Death, and not otherwise, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Benjamin Gee Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

Benjn. Gee Foreman
