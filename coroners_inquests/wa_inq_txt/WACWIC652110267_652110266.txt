City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of June in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Sarah Caut then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of David Watt , Thomas Clean , James Jones , James Harries , Francis Edgar , William Dawson , Robert Boulton , James Knight , James Ivory , James Jones , James Naylor , Christopher West , Alexander Moore , Henry Smith , Francis Foulkes , and George Bowman good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Caut came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Sarah Caut laboring under a grievous Disease of Body, to Wit, a Fever, and by reason of the violence of the said Disease, being delirious and out of her Mind, on the Fourteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, in the Dwelling House of John Harrison there situate, with a certain Knife made of Iron and Steel, which she the said Sarah Caut then and there had and held in her right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of her the said Sarah Caut, did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby, then and there giving unto herself the said Sarah Caut, so being delirious and out of her Mind as aforesaid, with the Knife aforesaid, in and upon the Throat or Gullet of her the said Sarah Caut one mortal Wound of the Length of two Inches and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound she the said Sarah Caut form the said Fourteenth day, of June in the Year aforesaid, until the Seventeenth day of the same Month and Year, at the Parish aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Seventeenth day of June in the Year aforesaid, She the said Sarah Caut, of the mortal Wound aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Sarah Caut, by reason of the Disease aforesaid, being delirious and out of her Mind as aforesaid, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said David Watt Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

David Watt Foreman}
