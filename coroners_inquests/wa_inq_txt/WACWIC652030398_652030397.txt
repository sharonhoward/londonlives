City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord The King at the Parish St. Marin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twenty sixth day of December in the fourth Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of god of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Elizabeth Marlin (the Wife of Edward Marlin ) then and there lying Dead, Upon the Oath of Thomas Lowe , John Dick , James Hyde , William Kent , George Stephens , James Bigin , John Surflin , Thomas Phillips , Christopher Makemead Richard Langham , Robert Coward , John Bevan and Robert Keling good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Marlin came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Marlin, not being of sound Mind, Memory, and Understanding but Lunatic and distracted, on the Twenty fouth day of December in the Year aforesaid, in her Lodging Room or Apartment in the dwellng House of George Smallet situate in Charles Court , at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, with a certain Case Knife, made of Iron and Steel, which she the said Elizabeth Marlin then and there had and held in her right hand, the Throat or Gullet of her the said Elizabeth Marlin did then and there strike, stab and penetrate, thereby, then and there giving unto herself the said Elizabeth Marlin, not being of sound Mind, Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted as aforesaid, with the Knife aforesaid, in and upon the Throat or Gullet of her the said Elizabeth Marlin one Mortal Wound of the length of four inches and of the depth of one inch, of which said Mortal Wound she the said Elizabeth Marlin then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Elizabeth Marlin, not being of sound Mind, Memory, and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Lowe Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Thos Lowe Foreman
