City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of July in the twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Job Pegg then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Stone , Edward Taylor , William Baldwin , Thomas Sanders William Spratley , Aaron Loton , John Burn , Thomas Tiller Robert Mins , George Jackson , John Leach & Hugh Payne good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Job Pegg came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Job Pegg not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Fourth day of July in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, a certain Gun or Fowling Piece charged with Gun Powder and a leaden Bullet, to and against the left Breast of him the said job Pegg did then and there shoot off and discharge; by means whereof he the said Job Pegg by the Bullet aforesaid, so discharged out of the Fowling piece, by force of the Gunpowder aforesaid did then and there receive one Mortal wound in and through his Body on the left side, of which said mortal wound he the said Job Pegg then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Job Pegg not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Stone Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner . John Stone Foreman
