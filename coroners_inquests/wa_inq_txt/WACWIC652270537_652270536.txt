City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the tenth day of December in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Inwood then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Gray , James Robertson , Philip Barton , Peter Caird , William Hewitt , Thomas Kennett , John Robinson , James Cotter , Thomas Warren , Benjamin Fry , Alexander Bennett , Michael Barrett , Robert Boulter , James Trepass , William Conyers and Lawrence Fagan good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Inwood came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Inwood not being of sound, Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatic and distracted on the said tenth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Work house belonging to the said Parish with a certain Knife made of Iron and Steel which he the said Thomas Inwood then and there had and held in his Right Hand the Throat or Gullet of him the said, Thomas Inwood did then and there strick stab and penetrate thereby then and there giving unto himself the said Thomas inwood with the Knife aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Thomas Inwood one mortal Wound of the Length of three Inches and of the Depth of one Inch of which said mortal wound he the said Thomas Inwood then and there instantly died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Inwood not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill himself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Gray  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos Gray Foreman
