City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint george Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty eighth day of October in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland; King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of George Charott then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Price , Peter Pearce , Thomas Reeves , William Chandless , James Payne , David Wernham , Thomas Rowe , Edward Rawlings , Rowland Edwards John Crane , George Man , and Philip Cheeseman good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said George Charott came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said george Charott a Laborer on the said Twenty eighth day of October in the Year aforesaid being at work in repairing a House in St. James's Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there with other Men carrying a large Stone upon a Hand barrow in the Hall of the said House, It so happened that by the Joyfts giving way, the Floor fell in, and that the said George Charott fell down from thence into a Passage leading into the Area of the said House and thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received divers mortal Bruises and Fractures in and upon the right side of his Body, of which said Bruises and Fractures he the said George Charott then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said George Charott in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Price  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellow, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written.

Tho Prickard Coroner James Price Foreman
