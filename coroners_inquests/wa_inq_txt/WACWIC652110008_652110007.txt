City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of January in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Sills an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Birks , James Coleman , George Harden , William Thomson , James Ward , John Pring , John Wynn , William Folks , Thomas Wills , Henry Lawrence , Benjamin Baldwin , Thomas Hilton , William Thornhill , Thomas Clay , Charles Pollard , and Richard Green good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Sills came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Sills an Infant of the Age of Seven Years on the Tenth day of December in the Year aforesaid, being alone in his Mother's Lodging Room or Apartment in the Dwelling House of Thomas Reynolds situate in Holles Street in the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, and there sitting upon a low Stool and having a lighted Candle standing upon the Hearth before him, It so happened that the Shirt and Linnen Waistcoat which the said Thomas Sills had on his Body Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took Fire, by Reason whereof and from the Smoke and Name arising from the said Fire, he the said Thomas Sills , in his Breast Neck and Head, was then and there very much Burnt and Scorched, of which said Burning he the said Thomas Sills from the said Tenth day of December in the year aforesaid, until the Third day of January in the same Year of his said Majesty's Reign, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Third day of January at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Thomas Sills of the Burning aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Birks Forman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above Written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

W Briks Foreman
