City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the first day of June in the Twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Harford then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Phipps , James Shearman , Edward Feild , Edward Gregory William Fellkampf , John Pearson , William Weston , William Vincent . William Jonson , Joseph Ashmore , James Wade , William Mourne Robert Mimms , James Bridgeland , Thomas Venables , Thomas Hinds Richard Carter  Thomas Gosherroon good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ann Harford came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Ann Harford an Infant of the Age of thirteen Years on the Thirty first day of May in the Year aforesaid being at a Window on the First Floor in the Dwelling House o Richard Harford situate in Petty France in the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty & County aforesaid & then and there kneeling upon a Board on the outside of the said Window it so happened that by the Weight of the said Ann Harford the said Board was broken and thrown down and that the said Ann Harford Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down out of and from the said Window unto the Ground and thereby then and there Received two Wounds upon the Head and a violent Concussion in the upon the Brain o her the said Ann Harford of which said Mortal Wounds and Concussion she the said Ann Harford then & there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Ann Harford in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to her Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner the said James Phipps  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year & Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard Coroner

Jas Phipps Foreman
