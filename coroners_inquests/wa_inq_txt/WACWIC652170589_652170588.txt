City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex }to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of December in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Honor Spicer then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Jeeves , John Tibbs , Thomas Kent , Roger Slater  Christopher Wildman James Thorpe , William Baldwin  Mills Scotcher , John Mc. Phail , William Barrett , John Varneck , Thomas Smith and George Hill Good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Honor Spicer came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the Honor Spicer, not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the said Fifth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Pieced of Small Cord unto the Door of the Lodging Room or Apartment of the said Honor Spicer in the Dwelling House of John Tibbs situate in off Alley in the said Parish Liberty and County, and other End about herown Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there Hang Suffocate and Strangle herself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling she the said Honor Spicer then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Honor Spicer not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Jeeves Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Wm Jeeves Foreman
