City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to Wit}  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of November in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ire- and King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on view of the Body of Dennis Clarke then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Richard Warren , John Davies Robert Oliver , John Evans , Thomas Tamerlaine  Francis David  John Hammond , James Hearn , Robert McEvoy  Joseph Pearson , James Robinson , John Millar , Walter Hughes , Thomas Griffiths , George Adcock , Samuel Smith , Samuel Barnes , John Porter , Cornelius Neep , Robert Robinson , William Erwin and Walter Reed , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by what man the said Dennis Clarke Came to his Death do upon their Oath say That the said Dennis Clarke a Labourer on the Eleventh day of November in the Year aforesaid being at Work on a New built House situate in a certain Street called Pall Mall in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there standing upon a loose Board lying upon the Joists in the third Story in the said House, and serving the Slaters who were then and there likewise at Work; It so happened that the said Dennis Clarke did then and then Accidentally Casually by Misfortune fall down between the said Joists on the third Story in the said House (the Floor not being then laid) unto the Ground; by Means whereof he the said Dennis Clarke Accidently Casually and by Misfortune did then and there receive divers mortal Bruises in and upon his Head and Body, of which said mortal Bruises he the said Dennis Clarke then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Dennis Clarke in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Richard Warren , Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day of Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

R Warren Foreman
