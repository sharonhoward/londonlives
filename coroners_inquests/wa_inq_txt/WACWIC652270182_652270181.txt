City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of March in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Garce of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Walter Pencutt then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Holmes  William Jeffrey  Samuel Owen  William Monnington  Robert Harday  Adam Brown  Joseph Rowell Samuel Palmer  James Hair  Simon Webb  John Godfrey Robert Pitter  George Callow  Philip Buckingyoung Davis Webster Jno Nunn  Jno Taylor  Jas. Aldons & Wm. Carter good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Walter Pencutt came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Walter Pencutt not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatick & distracted on the said thirtieth day of March in the Year aforesaid into a certain Water tub in the Kitchen within the Dwelling House of him the said Walter Pencutt situate and being within the said Parish Liberty & County did cast & throw himself by Means of which said Casting & Throwing he the said Walter Pencutt in the Water in the said Tub was then and there Suffocated and Drowned of which said Suffocation and Drownding he the said Walter Pencutt then and there instantly died & so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Walter Pencutt not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick & distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Holmes Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands & Seals the Day Year and Place above mentioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Holmes  Foreman
