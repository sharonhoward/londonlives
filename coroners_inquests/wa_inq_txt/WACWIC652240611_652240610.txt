City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of November in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Scorer then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Maitland , John Scaif , Samuel Crowther , William Wilkinson , John Thompson , William Watson , Henry Crane George Nash , George Landstaff , Robert Bond , Robert Smith James Hill , Thomas Burton , John Drake , Joseph Syday William Mace and Richard Roberts good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Scorer came to his Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said William Scorer not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the said Twenty sixth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Dwelling House of William Wright Victualler there situate, with a certain Razor made of Iron and Steel which he the said William Scorer then and there had and held in his Right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said William Scorer did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Razor aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said William Scorer one mortal Wound, of which said mortal Wound he the said William Scorer then and there instantly died: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Scorer not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Maitland  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho Prickard Coroner John Maitland Foreman
