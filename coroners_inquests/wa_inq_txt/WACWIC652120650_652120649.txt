City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of September in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Hodges then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Johnston , Gilbert Edward , James Barbar , John Walker , William Stevenson , Henry Wilson , Peter Hay , Kennet Meckleman , Thomas Robinson , Robert Moxam , Robert Thatcher , John Austin and John Morris , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duty sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Hodges came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Hodges a Laborer on the Second day of September in the Year aforesaid being walking upon and over certain Lighters loaden with Coals, and Floating in the River Thames , at a certain Wharf called Brewers Yard, in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said James Hodges then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from one of the said Lighters into the said River Thames , and in the Water thereof was then and there Drowned and Suffocated, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said James Hodges then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Hodges , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Johnston Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands, and Seals, the Day Year and at the Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Johnston Foreman
