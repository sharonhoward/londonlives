City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  In Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the sixth day of July in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Margaret Price then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Birchall , Joseph Buckinger , George Gartar John Ranken , James Mc. Lean , John Robinson , George Walne Thomas Williams . Thomas Griffiths , Christopher Braddon , John Warne , George Wynn , John Warnicke , Humphrey Broad head Peter Grimalde and Thomas Skillern good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Margaret Price came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Margaret Price not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted on the first day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid a great Quantity of Corrosive sublimate being a deadly Poison did take and swallow down, by means whereof she the said Margaret Price became then and there Sick and distempered in her Body, and of the Poison aforesaid, and of the Sickness and Distemper thereby occasioned from the said first day of August in the Year aforesaid until the Fifth day of the same Month in the same Year at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty, and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said Fifth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid she the said Margaret Price of the Poison aforesaid and of the Sickness and Distemper thereby occasioned did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Margaret Price not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatick and distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did poison and kill herself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Birchall  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself found the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jas Birchall Foreman
