City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty eighth day of February in the twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Young then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Perryman , Jacob Knight , Elias Glair , George Booth , Daniel Hawkes , Richard Sherlock , Thomas Cock William Mucklow , William Booth , Samuel Ray , James Wilson and William Woods good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Young came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Thirtieth day of January in the Year aforesaid, the said Thomas Young being in a Publick Street called Duke Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Tile then and there fell off and from the top of a House in the said Street upon the Head of the said Thomas Young , whereby the said Thomas Young Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did then and there receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the top of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Thomas Young from the said thirtieth day of January in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty seventh day of February in the same Year did languish at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and of the said mortal Fracture then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesiad upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Young in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Perryman Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner Robt Perryman Foreman
