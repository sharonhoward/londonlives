City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirty first day of March in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and LIberty, on View of the Body of John Barber then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Ashmore , Thomas Brown , William Squibb Robert Moore , William Athinson , George Parsons , John Bradley John Kelly , James Wade , Matthew Baylis , George Lucas Charles Larr , Thomas Watson , James Turner and Adam Schweyether good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Barber came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty first day of January in the Year aforesaid the Coach of the Right Honourable Hugh Lord Fortescue and other Carriages were passing along the Publick Street opposite the Admiralty in the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that the said John Barber was then on Foot crossing the said Street, In so happened that the said John Barber fell down and that the off hinder Wheel of the said Coach then and there Accidentally Casually and by misfortune passed over the Legs of the said John Barber then lying upon the Ground as aforesaid, by means whereof he the said John Barber that and there Accidentally Casually and by misfortune received mortal Fractures in and upon both his Legs of which said Fractures he the said John Barber from the said Twenty first day of January in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty Ninth day of March in the same Year at the said Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields and also at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and languish and languishing did live, On which said Twenty ninth day of March in the year aforesaid he the said John Barber at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid of the mortal Fractures aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Barber in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Ashmore Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jos. Ashmore Foreman
