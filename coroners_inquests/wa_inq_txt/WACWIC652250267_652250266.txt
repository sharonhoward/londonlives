City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of May in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Jurquet then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Francis Barrow , Joseph Sharp , Daniel Cameron , William Collins , Robert Hinchsman , John Wilson , Henry King , Isaac Farlow , William Pann , William Underwood , Thomas Donaldson , Adam Walker , Samuel Broadban , and John Green good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Robert Jurquet came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Robert Jurquet not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted on the said Twentieth Day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Shop of him the said Robert Jurquet there situate with a certain Razor made of Iron and Steel, which he the said Robert Jurquet then and there had and held in his Right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said Robert Jurquet , did then and there Strike stabbard Penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Razor aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Robert Jurquet one Mortal Wound of the Length of three Inches, and of the Depth of One Inch, of which said Mortal Wound, he the said Robert Jurquet then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Robert Jurquet in manner and by the Means aforesaid not being of sound Mind, Memory, and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Francis Barron Foreman of the said Jurors, on the Behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, In their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Frans. Barren  Foreman
