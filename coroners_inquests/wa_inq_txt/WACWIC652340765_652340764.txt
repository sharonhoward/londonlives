City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex .} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Thomas Baynton Sign of the old Chesterfield Arms in Shepperd Street  Mayfair  Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty fourth day of Decr. 1794 in the thirty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Fuller the Younger then and there lying head, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written and Seals affixed good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Fuller came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Richard Fuller not being of sound Mind Memory & Understanding but lunatic and distracted, on the said- twenty fourth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid from and out of a certain two pair of Stairs Window, then and there being in the Chamber or Apartment of the abovesaid Thomas Baynton, did violently cast and throw himself to the Ground, by Means of which said Casting and Throwing be the said Richard Fuller did then and there receive one mortal Wound on his Head and also on the left Collar Bone: Of which said Mortal Wound be the said Richard Fuller then and there instantly died: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Richard Fuller in manner and by the Means aforesaid, not being of sound Mind, Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted did is kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day, Year and place first above written.

Anthy. Gell Coroner }

P Osborn John Shacklin W Sibey Thos Hodgson John Gordon
