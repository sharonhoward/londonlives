Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } in wit An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St. Anne in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the thirteenth day of July in the Frist Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one . Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid. Upon a View of the Body of Thomas Austin then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of John Horne , Peter Brown , Richard Price , Moses Wilson , Henry Ottley , Gilbert Ogleby Robert Woodcock , Francis Naniant , William Dangerfeild , William Cubbidge , William Morall , Joseph Fisher , Ralph Fossey , James Young , John Mitchell , John Bolton ,and Joseph James Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Thomas Austin came to his Death, say upon their Oaths That the said Thomas Austin not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted on the twelfth day of July Instant in the Year aforesaid in the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid at his Lodging Room in a House Lett into Separate Apartments situate in Newport Market in the said Parish with a certain knife made of Iron and Steel which he the said Thomas Austin then and there had and held in his right hand the Throat or Gullet of him the said Thomas Austin did then and there strike, slab, and penetrate thereby then and there giving unto himself the said Thomas Austin so being Delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid with the Knife aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Thomas Austin , one Mortal Wound of the length of three Inches, and of the Depth of One Inch of which said Mortal Wound he the said Thomas Austin then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that that said Thomas Austin so being Delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid in manner and by the means aforesaid did Kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition put their hands and Seals the day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Jno. Feary Coroner

John Horne Foreman
