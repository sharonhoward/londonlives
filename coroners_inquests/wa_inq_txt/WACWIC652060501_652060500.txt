City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Ninth day of September in the Sixth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on view of the Body of James Napper (suspected to have been Murdered) then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Darbey Murphy , William Fraser , Jonathan Porter , William Williamson  Edmund Dunn , Daniel Wood , John Arthur , Thomas Eddie  Charles Evitt , John Morgan , Thomas Bush , Patrick Duggin , Joseph Stanhope , John Wilson , James Robinson  Francis King , John Prior , Timothy Cahill , Samuel Ashby and Patrick Forne , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and those duly sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said James Napper came to his Death do upon their Oath say, that the said James Napper on the said Ninth day of September in the year aforesaid, died suddenly in the Street near Charing Cross , in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, that no marks of Violence appeared on his Body, and that the said James Napper died by the Visitation of God a natural Death, and not by any Hunt or Injury received from any Person whatsoever to the Knowledge of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Darbey Murphy Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Darbey Murphy Foreman
