City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fifth day of May in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Corner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Loramea then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Ivison George Martin , Paul Webster , George Churchman , Richard Exley , Abraham Reynolds Thomas Legg , George Elward , Edward Johns , William Humby , Alexander Grant and Robert Walker . good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Loramea came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Joseph Loramea of the Age of Fifteen Years or thereabouts being In some on the Nineteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid to wit into a certain Water or Bason in Hyde Park there situate did cast and threw himself by means of which said Casting and Throwing he the said Joseph Loramea in the Waters of the said Bason was then and there Suffocated and Drowned of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Joseph Loramea then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Joseph Loramea in manner and by the means aforesaid being In same did Drown and Kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on the behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner John Ivison Foreman
