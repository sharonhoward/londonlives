City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of February in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Jocelyne Bloomfield Esqr. then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Collins , Edward Stephens , Edward Stephens Francis Hazle  John Taylor , Morgan Moore , Richard Heathfield , John Geshen , William Field , John Murray , John Milliner and Henry Humphreys good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Jocelyne Bloomfield came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said Jocelyne Bloomfield not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and distracted on the fifteenth day of February in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Dwelling House of William Sandford there situate with a certain Pen Knife m of Iron and Steel which he Jocelyne Bloomfield then and there had and held in his Hand the Throat or Gullet of him the said Jocelyne Bloomfield did then and there strike stab and penetrate thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Penknife aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Jocelyne Bloomfield one mortal Wound of the Length of four Inches and for the Depth of one Inch of which said mortal Wound he the said Jocelyne Bloomfield than and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Jocelyne Bloomfield not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill himself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Samuel Collins Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner . Samuel Collins Foreman
