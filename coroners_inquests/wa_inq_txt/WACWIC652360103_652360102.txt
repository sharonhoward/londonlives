City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of John Woolbridge the Sign of the three Compasser Upper Mount Street Parish of Saint George Hanover Sq : within the Liberty of Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty fourth day of March 1796 in the thirty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of Body of a Man unknown Joseph Whiteman then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said Man unknown Joseph Whiteman came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Joseph Whiteman on the twenty third day of March in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit in the Serpentine River in Hyde Park not being of sound Mind Memory & Understanding but lunatic & distracted that did cast and throw himself by means of which said Casting and throwing he the said Joseph Whiteman in the Waters of the said River was then & there Suffocated & drowned Of which Said Suffocation and Drowning He the said Joseph Whiteman then and there instantly died & in the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Joseph Whiteman not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic & distracted in manner and by the Means aforesaid did. drown & Kill himself IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }
