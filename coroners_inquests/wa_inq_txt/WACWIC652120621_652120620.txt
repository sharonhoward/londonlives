City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fourth day of August in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Johns then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Strong Edward Lambert , William Wallace , Thomas Rennison , Richard Eveson , Samuel Poucherl Robert Fuller , James Johnson , Michael Fentiman , James Chamberlain , william Gibbs , and James Urquhart good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duty sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Johns came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Johns on the Twenty Second day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid to wit at the Flood Gates Chelsea going into the River Thames there to Bathe himself and not being skilfull in Swimming, it so happened that accidentally casually and by Misfortune he the said James Johns was in the waters of the said River then and there suffocated and Drowned of which said suffocation and Drowning he the said James Johns then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said James Johns in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of Himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands, and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Richard Strong Foreman
