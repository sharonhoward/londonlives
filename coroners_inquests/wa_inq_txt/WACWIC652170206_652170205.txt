City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square Within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of May in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Price then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Charles Smith , Robert Webb , Peter Henderson , Thomas Forest , Philip Bruguiez , John Bruton , Thomas Samuel Nicker , Joseph Eyres , George Darling  Walter Simpson George Allen , and William Harrock good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by What Means the said Mary Price came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Mary Price on the Fifth Day of May in the Evening in the Year aforesaid being in Liquor and crossing St. James Street in the Parish of St. James within the Liberty and County aforesaid then and there Accidentally fell down, And that a Man unknown being than driving certain Coach drawn by two Horses in the said Street And that the Wheels of the said Coach then and there Accidentally Causally and by Misfortune did pass over the Body of the said Mary Price by Means whereof the said Mary Price did then and there receive one Mortal Bruise in her Body, of which the said Mary Price from the said Fifth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Seventh day of the same Month at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Seventh day of May aforesaid at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County by aforesaid She the said Mary Price of the Mortal Bruise aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Mary Price in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Charles Smith Foreman of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above Written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Chas. Smith Foreman
