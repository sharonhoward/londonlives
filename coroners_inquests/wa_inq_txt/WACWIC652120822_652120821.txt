City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of November in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Moore then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of George West , John Palmer , Robert Chisholm , Richard Martin , Edward Sheffield , Edward James , Thomas Fidgeon , John Poole , John Goldsmith , William Briggs , William Almond , Thomas Cooper and William Suckling good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Moore came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Moore on the Ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid, being with several other Persons in a certain place called Manchester Buildings , in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and Viewing the Barges of the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor, and the several Companys of the City of London upon the River Thames , It so happened that the said John Moore then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune Fell down upon the Stone Pavement in the said Buildings, and thereby did then and there receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the leftside of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said John Moore from the said Ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid, until the Tenth day of the same Month and Year, did languish and languishing did live; on which said Tenth day of November aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said John Moore of the mortal Fracture aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say, That the said John Moore in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said George West Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Years and Place First above Written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

George West . Foreman
