City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Michl Bryant Sign of the Swan in Upper Mount Street parish of Saint George Hanh. Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourteenth day of July 1794 in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Stoneham then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written and Seals a Fixed good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Stoneham came to his Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Thomas Stoneham on the thirteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit in the Serpentine River in Hyde Park there going in to Cathe himself. It so happened that accidentally, casually and by Misfortune he the said Thomas Stoneham in the waters of the said River was then and there suffocated and drowned; Of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Thomas Stoneham then and there instanlty died. And so the Sworn aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Stoneham in manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Juror River to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the day Year and place first above writting

Anthy. Gell Coroner} Benj Blake John Coard Thos. Foster Benja Ahmead Wm Mortlock
