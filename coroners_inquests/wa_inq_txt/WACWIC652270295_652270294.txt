City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourth day of June in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Margaret Rush then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Palmer , Joseph Simpson , Samuel Vizard Thomas Woolams , John Denew , Thomas Aslin , william Lusty John Darbyshire , William Addey , James Spear , George Gabb . Thomas Wheathy, William Bamford , William Newcomb , Richard Frith , Robert Buckie , John Myers , Adam Brodie , Samuel Bark Robert Extall , and John Pearce good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Margaret Rush came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Margaret Rush on the third day of June in the Year aforesaid being in a Hayloft in a certain Stable Yard called Paviours Arms Yard situate in Wardour Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and then and there coming down a Ladder out of the said Haylost. It so happened that the said Margaret Rush Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Ladder upon the Stone pavement in the said Stable Yard & thereby did then and there receive a Bruise in and upon the left Temple and mortal Concussion in and upon the Brain of her the said Margaret Rush of which said Mortal Bruise and Concussion she the said Margaret Rush then & there died and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Margaret Rush in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally, Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Samuel Palmer  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Thos. Prickard Coroner

Saml. Palmer Foreman
