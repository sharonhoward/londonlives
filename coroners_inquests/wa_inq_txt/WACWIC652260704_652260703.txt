City of Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex . } to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth day of October in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Sarah Heath then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Burgen , John Barnard , William Dickens , John Ladley Thomas Barnett , Henry Slack , Thomas Whiting , Richard Burguex William Gibson , Alexander Black , Thomas Watts , and Thomas Shackstone good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Sarah Heath came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Sarah Heath not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and Distracted, on the Thirtieth day of October in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid to wit in a certain Room called the Queens Ward in the Workhouse of the said Parish one and of a linnen Handkerchief fixed to an Angle of Wood the top of the Bedstead there situate and the other and Whereof about her own Neck did five tye and faster and there with did then and there Hang Suffocate and Strangle herself by Means of which Hanging Suffocation and Strangling she the Said Sarah Heath then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say That the said Sarah Heath not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid, did kill herself. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the Foreman of the Jurors, aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jno. Burgan Foreman
