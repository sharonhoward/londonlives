City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of April in the twenty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Kelly then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Michael Castledine , William Ashden , Joseph Dashwood , George Grant , George Brown , Thomas Munday , John Brown  William Wade , John Balls , George Radford , William Eccles , and William Woods , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Kelly came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Kelly a Laborer on the Second day of April in the Year aforesaid being Employed to take down and Old House in St. Anns Lane in the Parish of St. John the Evangelist within the Liberty and County aforesaid It so happened that the said John Kelly in forcing down with an Iron Bar part of the Wall of the said House, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down the height of two Stories unto the Ground and thereby then and there received a mortal Wound and Fracture in and upon the Right side of the Head of his the said John Kelly , of which he languished and lived until the said Eighth day of April in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there of the said mortal Wound and Fracture did die: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Kelly in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Michael Castledine  Foreman of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Michl Castledine Foreman
