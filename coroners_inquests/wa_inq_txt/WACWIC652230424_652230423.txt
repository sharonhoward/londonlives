City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of September in the twenty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Coney then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Higgs  John Milliner  William Warren George Knox , James Hudson , Lewis Voyer , Robert Haynes  Thomas Brown  Samuel Exom , William House  Charles and John Cross good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Coney came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Coney a Laborer on the Sixth day of September in the Year aforesaid being at Work in the House of Henry Holland  Esquire situate in Hertford Street at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being then and there upon a short Ladder standing upon a Scaffold on the inside of the said House It so happened that the Ladder slipped and that the said John Coney then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down the height of two Stories unto and upon the Stone Pavement in the Hall of the said House, and thereby then and there received, one mortal Fracture in and upon the Forepart of the Head of him the said John Coney , of which said Mortal Fracture he the said John Coney then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Coney in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Higgs  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho Prickard Coroner

Joseph Higgs Foreman
