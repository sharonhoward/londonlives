City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

In Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of November in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Edward Clare then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Evan Williams , Ralph Jordan , William Martin , Henry Savory , Robert Southby , William Luxton , William Martin , Edward Thompson , William Pyniom , William Price , John Hudson and William Woods good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Clare came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Edward Clare a Painter on the Twenty sixth day of October in the Year aforesaid being at Work upon the dwelling House of Stephen Remnants in Crown Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being then and there upon a Ladder on the outside of the said House, It so happened that the Ladder slipped and that the said Edward Clare Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down unto the Ground, and thereby then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon the Right side of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Edward Clare from the said Twenty sixth day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Third day of November at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Third day of November aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty & County aforesaid he the said Edward Clare of the mortal Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Edward Clare in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Evan Williams Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Evan William Foreman
