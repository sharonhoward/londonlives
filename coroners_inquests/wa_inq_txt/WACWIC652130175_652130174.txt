City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of April in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of  Edward Hoy a Labourer then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Owen Hudson  Peter Dargent , John Keith , Michael Castleden , Robert Bray , George Fairbone , Peirce Dent , Edward Thomas , Anthony Shepperd , John Lumsdale Leonard Clow and George Kitchingman good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for out said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Hoy came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That Thomas Carrol late of the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields in the County aforesaid Labourer on the Eight day of April in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the said Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields in the County aforesaid in and upon the said Edward Hoy in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being, feloniously did make and Assault. And that the said Thomas Carrol with both his Hands him the said Edward Hoy then and there feloniously did throw and cast to the Ground, And that the said Thomas Carrol with his Knees, him the said Edward Hoy, then and there lying upon the Ground as aforesaid, in and upon the Ribs on the Right side of the Body of him the said Edward Hoy, then and there feloniously did strike beat and thrust, thereby then and there giving unto him the said Edward Hoy in and upon his Ribs on the Rightside of his Body one mortal Bruise and Fracture, of which said mortal Bruise and Fracture he the said Edward Hoy at the Parish last mentioned, and also at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, from the said Eight day of April in the Year aforesaid until the Sixteenth day of same Month in the same Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Sixteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Edward Hoy of the mortal Bruise and Fracture aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Carrol him the said Edward Hoy in Manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously did kill and slay, against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. And that the said Thomas Carrol at the time of doing and committing the Felony and Manslaughter aforesaid, or at any time since, had no Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements within the said Liberty or elsewhere to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Owen Hudson Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above witten.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Owen Foreman
