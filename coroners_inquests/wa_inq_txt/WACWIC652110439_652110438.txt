City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of September in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Spratley then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Hales Joseph White , Alexander Linelle , Richard Sunningburg , William Godbold , John Scott , Richard Small , James Miller , William Watson , Thomas Young , Edmund Dunn , Edward Partridge , James Chalk , Benjamin Perrin , Benjamin Watson , and Matthew Walker good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Spratley came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say

That the said Mary Spartley on the Third day of September in the Year aforesaid at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid [to wit] at the Westminster Hospital in James Street by the Visitation of God did then and there Dye a Natural Death. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors and other of the said Jurors have hereunto set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

John Scott

Alexd. Small  Benjamin Perrins

Josh. White  Matthew Walker

Edmond Dunn  James Chalk

Thos. Young  Ed. Partridge
