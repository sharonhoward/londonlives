City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty sixth day of December in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Nicholas Obrien then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Poole , John Neiss , Robert Ward , George Lucass Walter Barrey , Samuel Milliner , James Barrett John Gilliott , Isaac Row , William Young  John Wildman , Charles Hawkes , and John Surman good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Nicholas Obrien came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Nicholas Obrien an Infant of the Age of four Years on the twenty fifth day of November in the Year aforesaid being in the Lodging Room of Lawrence Obrien his Father in the Dwelling House of William Bellamy situate in Angel Court in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the LIberty and County aforesaid It so happened that the Muslin Frock which the said Nicholas Obrien the Infant had on his Body Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took fire by means whereof the said Nicholas Obrien was then and there burnt in and upon his Belly Neck the Left Armand Left side of which said burning he the said Nicholas Obrien at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said twenty fifth day of November in the Year aforesaid until the twenty fifth day of December in the same Year did Languish and Languishing did live on which said twenty fifth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Nicholas Obrien of the Mortal burning aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Nicholas Obrien Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Poole  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Poole Foreman
