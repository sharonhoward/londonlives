City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty eighth day of October in the Twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Whitehead an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Gilbert , Samuel Critchen , William Smith Richard Exley , John Tibby , Perceval Gregory , Thomas Banner , Thomas Fisher , Thomas Henderson , Richard Rice , Nathan Carrington , John Hughes , and John Annesley , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what by Means the said Thomas Whitehead came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Whitehead an Infant of the Age of five Years on the Twenty fifth day of October in the Year aforesaid being in the Lodging Room or Apartment of James Whitehead in a certain Stable Yard in Park Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty. and County aforesaid, And a Tea Kettle full of Boiling Water being then standing upon a Trivet on the Fire in the said Room, It so happened that the said Thomas Whitehead then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune threw down the said Tea Kettle and by the Boiling Water therein was then and there violently Sealed and Burnt in and upon his Neck Sides and Stomach of which said Scalding and Burning he the said Thomas Whitehead from the said Twenty fifth day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty sixth day of the same Month at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish, and thereof then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Thomas White head the Infant in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Gilbert  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition Set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Gilbert Foreman
