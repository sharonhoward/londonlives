City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of June in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Granger then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Fisher , William, Paul , Alexander Tate , George Sewell , James Fitz Patrick Christopher Cook , Robert Desborough , John Creese , John Young , John Spencer , Joseph Johnson , John Cloak  Thomas Hignett , Frederick Westerland , John Coote , John Nalder and Richard Clark good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Granger came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Granger a Lad of the Age of thirteen Years, on the Eighteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid being riding upon a certain black Mare in a Street called the French Exchange in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said Thomas Granger then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Mare, and was dragged along the said Street, the left Foot of the said Thomas Granger being in the Stirrup Leather, by Means whereof the said Thomas Ganger then and there received divers Bruises and Wounds in and upon the Head of him the said Thomas Granger of which said Wounds and Bruises he the said Thomas Granger then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Thomas Granger in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Fisher Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place frist about written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Jos. Fisher Foreman
