City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Eighth day of November in the Twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Joseph Longhurst then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Pettis John Stone  George Bound  William Eaves  Peter Fossard  Robert Washborne , Charles Walpole , William Pitkin , Joseph Sandell , Michael Love , William Storey Robert Seabrook , Godfrey Ragg , John Mew  Thomas Hill and William Pease good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by what Means the said Joseph Longhurst came to his death do upon their Oath say, That on the Fifth day of November in the Year aforesaid, in the Evening one Burst (whose Christian Name is at present unknown) being with other Neighbours rejoycing at a Bonfire at Twickenham in the County aforesaid, And the said Burst having charged a Gun with Gunpowder left it with one Hubbard a Lad of the Age of fourteen Years, who put the Iron Rod into the Barrel of the said Gun, And that the said Burst on his return did let of and discharge the said Gun (not knowing of the Iron Rod being therein) by Means whereof the said Joseph Longhurst with the Iron Rod aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received a mortal Wound in and through his Head of which said Mortal Wound he the said Joseph Longhurst from the said Fifth day of November in the Year aforesaid until the Seventh day of the same Month and Year at the said Parish of Twickenham , and also at the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and live on which said Seventh day of November at the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square he the said Joseph Longhurst of the Mortal Wound aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Joseph Longhurst in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Pettis  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Pettis Foreman
