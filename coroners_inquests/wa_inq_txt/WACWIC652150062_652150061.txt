City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of February in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Jonathan Haleston then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Carter , William Stanbank , James Nelson  Henry Gordon , John Watson James Wallis , Robert Richardson , Benjamin Cordell , William Dorset , Robert Martin , Robert Holland and John Garth Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty. duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Jonathan Haleston came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That Lewis Roberts late of the Paris aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid Laborer on the Thirteenth day of February in the Year aforesaid, with Force and Arms, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, in and upon the said Jonathan Haleston , in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being, feloniously did make an Assault, And that the said Lewis Roberts with both his hands him the said Jonathan Haleston in and upon the Head of him the said Jonathan Haleston did then and there feloniously Strike and beat, and that the said Lewis Roberts by the striking and heating aforesaid did then and there give unto him the said Jonathan Haleston one mortal Brurse in and upon under the left Ear of him the said Jonathan Haleston , of which said Mortal Bruise he the said Jonathan Haleston then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Lewis Roberts him the said Jonathan Haleston in Manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously did kill and slay, against the Peace of our said Lord the King his brown and Dignity and that he said Lewis Roberts at the time of committing the Felony and Manslaughter aforesaid, or at any time since, had no Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements within the said Liberty or elsewhere, to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Carter Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Thos. Carter Foreman
