City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields Within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Second day of April in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Smith then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William House , William Prosser , Francis Gendre , William Corderoy , William Adams John Bishop , James Doyle  James Church , William Jones , John Stephenson Tobias Willliams , Henry Sawyer a Peter Conelly good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Smith came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twentieth day of April in the Year aforesaid the said Thomas Smith was found dead in a certain Alley called Pipe Makers Alley in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, that no Marks of Violence appeared on the Body, and that the said Thomas Smith came not to his Death by any violent Means or Manner whatsoever to the Knowledge of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William House Foreman of the said Jurors, on this behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Days Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm: House Foreman
