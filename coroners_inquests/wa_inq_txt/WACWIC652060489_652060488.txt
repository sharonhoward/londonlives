City and Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex To wit. }  An Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Prison of Bridewell in the Parish of St. Margaret in the City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex in the First Day of September in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & Liberty on View of the Body of Elizabeth Whetston a Prisoner in the said Prison then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Woolley , Samuel Tyler , Alexander Gordon , James White , Alexander Donold , Joseph Mutlow , Thomas Hitchins , John Bank , Nathaniel Hunt , John Sweet , John Griffiths , John Lane and William Gally good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Whetston came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Whetston being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields Bridewell aforesaid, in the Parish City Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Thirtieth day of August in the Year aforesaid, at Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid, departed this Life, by the Visitation of God, in a natural Way, to wit of a Dropsy, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Samuel Woolley , Foreman of the Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Saml Woolley  Foreman
