An Inquilicion Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of st. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of August in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Benjamin Softly then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Beckett , William Dean , John Cliverley , Thomas Orme , John Jones , Thomas Pudner , William West , Richard Edwards , William Edwards , Charles Newman , William Mc. Hardey , and William Stronach Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enqure for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Benjamin Softly came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Benjamin Softly on the Second day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid to wit at a certain Water near to Chelsea Bridge, Going into the said Water there to Bath himself, it so happened that accidentally casually and by Misfortune he the said Benjamin Softly was in the said Waters then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said benjamin Softly then and there instantly Died And so the Sworn aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Benjamin Softly in manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally casually and by misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Thos Prickard Coroner J Beckett Foreman
