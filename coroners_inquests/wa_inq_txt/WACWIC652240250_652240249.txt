City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of May in the twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Neale then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of David Stoddart  Philip Holland , John Birch Robert Tibey , William Rober , William Pritchard , John Bishop , Henry Slack , Owen Scott , Michael Bryan , James Martin and Peter Lysom good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Neale came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say ,That the said John Neale a Laborer on the Third day of May in the Year aforesaid being Driving a certain Empty Cart in the Strand in the Parish of St. Clement Danes within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being then and there Intoxicated with Liquor, and riding in the said Cart, It so happened that by a sudden jolt of the said Cart the said John Neal Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell out of and from the said Cart upon the Stone Pavement in the said Street, and that the Near Wheel of the said Cart instantly passed over the Body of the said John Neale whereby the said John Neale then and there received divers Bruises in and upon his Body, of which said mortal Bruises he the said John Neale languished about two Hours at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square , within in the Liberty and County aforesaid, and there on the same Day of the Bruises aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Neale Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his Death In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said David Stoddart  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to the Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho Prickard Coroner

David Stoddart Foreman
