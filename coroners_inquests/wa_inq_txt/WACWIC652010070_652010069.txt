Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit An Inquisition indented and taken at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster within th Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Twenty eighth day of October in the First Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one . Before John Feary  Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid Upon a View of the Body of Avice Betts then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of John Madders , Edward Brookes , James Moody , Robert Wilson , Richard Heene , Charles Waters John Vere , James GoodChild , Edward Marsh , Richard Moors , James Dellamere , Edward Bishop , and John Surby Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Inquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when and in what manner the said Avice Betts came to her Death say upon their Oaths That on thursday the Twenty Second day October Instand in the Year aforesaid the Said Avice Betts being in a certain hired Coach drawn by Two Horses with other Person in the same, and a Large Quantity of Rubbish lying in Arlington Street in the said Parish in the Highway against an Empty House in the said Street then Repairing and there being no Hourd or France to Secure the Same nor any Light or Watch set to give Notice of the said Rubbish lying there it so happened that the Coachman who was carefully driving the said Coach and Horses between Eight and Nine o'Clock at Night of the same Day through Arlington Street aforesaid (in which said Street the said Avice Betts at that time Lived) and it being then Dark and the said Coachman not seeing the said Rubbish so lying in the said Street or Highway as aforesaid, in going along the said Street with the said Coach and Horses the near force Wheel of the said Coach went upon the said Rubbish, by means whereof the said Coach was then there and thereby Casually Accidentally and by Misfortune overturned, and the said Avice Betts so being in the said Coach as aforesaid was then and there violently thrown against the side of the said Coach next the Ground and by means thereof did then and there receive several Bruises on her Head and Body which together with the Surprize and Fright occasioned thereby brought on Convulsions of which said Bruises and Convulsions as aforesaid the said Avice Betts from the said Twenty second day of October in the Year aforesaid untill the Twenty fifth day of the same Month in the Same Year at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid did Languish and Languishing did Live on which said Twenty fifth day of October in the Year aforesaid in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid she the said Avice Betts of the Bruises aforesaid and of the Convulsions occasioned by the Same and by the Surprize as aforesaid did Dye.
