City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Sixth day of April in the Theirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Howard then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Henry King , William Cox , Thomas Warning , Joseph Smith John Dilong , Thomas Griffiths , Thomas Clout , William Vinson Joseph Holly , Charles Davidson , Samuel Holmes , Robert White William Hall , Roger Woodhouse , William Hewett and William Forrester , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Howard came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said John Howard on the seventeenth day of April in the year aforesaid was on beard as Certain Coal Barge or Lighter in and upon the River Thames and being Intoxicated with Liquor It so happened that the said John Howard Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune Fell out and from the said Barge or Lighter into the said River Thames and on the Twenty Fourth day of the same Month the said John Howard was taken out of the Water of the said River Thames suffocated and drowned at Black Lyon Hairs in the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid. And so the Jurors on their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Howard in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Henry King  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their present have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Henry King Foreman
