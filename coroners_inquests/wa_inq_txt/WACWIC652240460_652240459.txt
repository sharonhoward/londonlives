City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of August in the twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Quiver then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Alexander Holliday  Benjamin Watson , Nathaniel Gulsworthy , Samuel Exell , Thomas Goodburn , Nathaniel Giddiss , John Smith  John King , Charles Dempsey , Abraham Wivell  John Cross and John Milliner good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Quiver came to h is Death, do upon their oath say That the said John Quiver a Plaisterer on the thirteenth day of August in the Year aforesaid being at Work on an Empty House situate in Curzon Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being upon a Ladder on the Outside and near the top of the said House, It so happened that the said Ladder slipped and that the said John Quiver then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Ladder unto the Ground, and thereby then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon the back part of the Head of him the said John Quiver , of which said Mortal Fracture he the said John Quiver then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said John Quiver in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Alexander Holliday  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten.

Tho Prickard Coroner

Alexr: Holliday Foreman
