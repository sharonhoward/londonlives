City and Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex To wit}  An Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Gate house Prison situate in the Parish of St. Margaret within the City & Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex the thirteenth Day of January in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING. Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & Liberty on View of the Body of William Morgan then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard James , Walter Tyrrell John Banks , Silvester Freeman , Richard Vaughan , Joseph Smith , John Davis Colling Hood , Robert Gildon , John Lomas , Benjamin Virtue , William Porter William Morgan and James Surridge good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, who and by what Means the said William Morgan came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Morgan being a Prisoner in the Gate house Prison aforesaid, in the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Twelfth day of January in the Year aforesaid at the Gatehouse Prison aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way to wit, of a Fever, and not other wise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Richard James Foreman of the said Jurors, on behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, n their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Rd. James Foreman
