City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty ninth day of October in the Thirty First Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Charles Dugard then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Tindal , John Waller , David Mc. Callon , Thomas Godfrey , Thomas Morgan , William Idis , Thomas Clout , John Bastar , William Payne , Allexander Miller , Joseph Dashwood , Robert Thompson , John Mc. Intosh Burges Tranter , John More , William Penn , Saml. Sheldon , Robert Frampton Robert Shephard and John Warnick good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Charles Dugard came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That on the Twenty seventh day of October in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid the said Charles Dugard (suspected to have been murthered) departed this Life by excessive Drinking And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Charles Dugard in manner and by the means aforesaid came to his Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Tindal Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Tindell Foreman
