City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fifth day of April in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Rose Davis then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Bowers , John Slye , Joseph Flemings , George Gardner , William Ellvin Peter Russedge , James Sanger , John Latham , John Moore , Matthew Hill , Robert Lawford , John Langford , William Wager , Robert Fuller , William Nurse and Samuel Atkinson , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Rose Davis came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Rose Davis on the Twenty fifth day of April in the Year aforesaid being much in Liquor, and Locked out of her dwelling House situate in Nags Head Court Drury Lane in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And tha the said Rose Davis in endeavouring to get into the said House between the Hours of One and Two in the Morning, then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune Stuck fast between the cover of the Cellar and the Wooden Plate, by Means whereof the said Rose Davis was then and there Suffocated and Strangled, of which said Suffocation and Strangling she the said Rose Davis then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Rose Davis in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Bowers Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner John Bowers Foreman
