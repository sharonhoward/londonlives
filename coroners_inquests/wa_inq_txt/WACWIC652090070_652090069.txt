City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty seventh day of February in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Bankes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Osborn , William Harrison , George Deck , Thomas Humphreys , William Figg , Moses Leny , John Hockley , William Burn , Matthias Sted , Thomas Davies , James Panton  John Dalton , Joseph Nesbit , Thomas Scott , James Morton , Henry Keppen and John Allen good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Bankes came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Mary Bankes not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, on the Twenty sixth day of February in the Year aforesaid, one End of a certain Linnen Napkin unto a Wooden Neg fastened into one side of the Lodging room or Apartment of her the said Mary Bankes in the Dwelling House of Andrew Batty situate and being in Shelton Court in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, and the other End thereof about her own Neck, did fix, tye and fasten, and there with did then and there hang suffocate and strangle herself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling, she the said Mary Bankes then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Mary Bankes not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill herself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Osborn Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Osborn Foreman
