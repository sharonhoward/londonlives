City of Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of April in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Benjamin Freeman then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Tomkins , James Hastings  Ralph Walker , William Esom , Edward Pike , Richard Carrington , Daniel Wardle , Thomas Baker , John Tench , Alexander Hill , William Beckett and Spencer Draper , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Benjamin Freeman came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Benjamin Freeman laboring under a grievous Disease of Body, to Wit, a Fever, and by reason of the Violence of the said Disease, being delirious and out of his Mind, on the Fifteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, in the dwelling House of Francis Milsted there situate, with a certain Clasp Knife made of Iron and Steel, which he the said Benjamin Freeman then and there had and held in his Right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said Benjamin Freeman , did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby, then and there giving unto himself the said Benjamin Freeman , so being delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid, with the Clasp Knife aforesaid, in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Benjamin Freeman , one Mortal Wound of the length of four Inches, and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound he the said Benjamin Freeman then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Benjamin Freeman  being delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid, by Reason of the Fever aforesaid, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Tomkins Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and throat of his Fellows, in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Will: Tomkins Foreman
