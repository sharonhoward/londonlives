City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to Wit}  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign, Lord the KIng at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the Country of Middlesex , the Fifth day of October in the Fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thos. Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on view of the Body of Ann Barthrop then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas- Watson , William Ball , Thomas Little , John Child , Robert Weight , Benjamin Plantin , John Spense , Thomas Jenkins , Robert Saunders , Panton Beteau , Henry Heitland , George Muller , Edward Powell , and Daniel Wood good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duty chosen, who being then and there duty Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what means the said Ann Barthrop cane to her Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Ann Barthrop on the Fifth day of October in the Year aforesaid, being in the Dwelling House of William Askew situate in little Poultney Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there rea Stair Case in the said dwelling House, and learning upon a small Cord It so happened that the said Cord, by the Weight of the said Ann Barthrop, then and there did break, And that tha said Ann Barthrop Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did then and there fall out of and through the said Window, being two Stories high, into a Backyard adjoying to the said House, which said backyard was paved with Stone, by Reason whereof she the said Ann Barthrop did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the Right side of her Head, of which said mortal Fracture she the said Ann Barthrop then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the Ann Barthrop in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidently whereof as well as the said Coroner,as the said Thomas Watson Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho.Richard Coroner .

Thos. Watson Foreman
