City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty ninth day of June in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Charlotte Wood then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Abraham Griffin , William Gillman , William Green , John Weatherhead , John Stephens , Coulson Pearce , John Lewes , Thomas Jackson , Charles King , Mark Buckingham , John Osborne Joseph Willett  Samuel Robinson , & William White , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Charlotte Wood came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Charlotte Wood not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick & distracted on the said twenty ninth day of June in the Year aforesaid one End of a certain Worsted Garter unto a Wooden Teg in a Closet within the Apartment of her the said Charlotte Wood in the dwelling House of William Combe situate and being in South Molton That in the within the Liberty and County aforesaid and the other end thereof about her own Neck did fix tie and fasten & therewith did then & therehang suffocate and strangle herself of which said hanging suffocation and strangling she the said Charlotte Wood then & there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Charlotte Wood not being of sound Mind Memory & Understanding but Lunatick and distracted in manner & by the Means aforesaid did till herself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Abraham Griffin  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands & Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Abraham Griffin  Foreman
