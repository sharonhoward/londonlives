City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the dwelling House, of Franciss Buckingham the Sign of the Gun in Stafford Row Punlies at the Parish of St George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the twenty fourth day of August 1796 in the thirty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Yates then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors Whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Yates came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that John Sellers , Elizabeth Jones and Richard Footner not having the Tean of God before their Eyes but moved and seduced by the Instigation of the devil on the twenty second day of August in the year aforesaid with force and Arms, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said Thomas Yates in the peace of God & of our said Lord the King, then and there being feloniously wilfully and of their Malice fore- thought, did make an Assault. And that the said John Sellers a certain Pistol charged and loaded with gunpowder and aleaden Bullet which he the Said John Sellers then and there Gadand held in his right hand to and against the Breast of him the said Thomas yates did then and there shoot off and discharge; By Means whereof he the said John Sellers feloniously wilfully and of his Malice forethought did then and there give unto him the said Thomas Yates with the leaden bullet aforesaid, so as aforesaid, shot off and discharged out of the Pistol aforesaid, by the force of the Gunpowder aforesaid in and upon the Breast of him the said Thomas Yates one Mortal Wound penetrating through the Body of him the said Thomas Yates ; Of Which said Mortal Wound he the said Thomas Yates did languish and languishing did live until the twenty third day of August in the Year aforesaid of which said twenty third day of August he the said Thomas Yates at the Parish of St . George Hanover Squere aforesaid of the said Mortal Wound did die And the Jurs aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Elizabeth Jones and Richard Footner at the time of the committing of the Felony and Minder aforesaid felomiously wilfully and of their Malice forethought were present chetting, comforting and maintaining the said John Sellers to Kill and murder the said thomas Yates in manner aforesaid And so the Jurors aforesaid about heer Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Sellers Elizabeth Jones and Richard Footnev him the said Thomas Yates in manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously wilfully and of their Malice fore thought did Kill and Murder against the peace of our said Lord the King his Gown and Dignity IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }

John Nichols John Jones Lewes Otto John Wallis Joh Gately
