Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit,  An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St Peter at Westminster the fourteenth day of January in the Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty two Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid, Upon a View of the, Body of Sarah Skyring then lying Dead in the Parish of St. George hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster aforesaid by the Oaths of John Steward , Edward Thompson , Henry Allibone , Robert Cheeseman , Mackland Ben , William Jones , Richard Burlow , William Hammond , Alexander Corneg , James Jobbins , John Dewell , Michael Jones , Charles Spikes , Richard Bedel and Henry Lewes Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Sarah Skyring came to her Death say upon their Oaths. That some Person or Persons to the Jurors unknown not having the fear of God before his her or their Eyes but moved and Seduced by the Instigation of the Devil on the first day of January Instant in the year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square , in the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said Sarah Skyring in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously wilfully and of his her or their Malice forethought did make an Assault, And that the saw Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid unknown a certain Instrument made of Wood and Iron called a Wheelwrights Adze of the value of Six pence which the said Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid unknow then and there had and held in his her or their right hand or hands, her the said Sarah Skyring in and upon the head of her the said Sarah Skyring then and there did strike but and penetrate, and that the said Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid unknown did then and there give unto her the said Sarah Skyring, in and upon the head of her the said Sarah Skyring with Instrument aforesaid several Mortal Wounds of the length of three inches and depth of half an Inch of which said Mortal Wounds she the said Sarah Skyring from the said first day of January to the Eleventh day of the same Month in the Year aforesaid at St. George's Hospital in the said Parish of St. George hanover Square in the Liberty and County aforesaid did Languish and Languishing did Live, on which said Eleventh day of January in the Year aforesaid, at the Hospital aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid she the said Sarah Skyring of the Mortal Wounds aforesaid received as aforesaid, did Dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Person or Persons to the Jurors aforesaid unknown her the said Sarah Skyring
