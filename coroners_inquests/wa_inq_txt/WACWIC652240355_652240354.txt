City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of June in the Twenty fourth  Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Paige then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Jonathan Hutchins , Francis Danyer , William Brodrick  Alexander Henderson , John Ham , John Maxwell James Ross , Thomas Bond , John Millener , Reynold Mc. Donald , William Warren and Richard Oakes good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Paige came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That the said William Paige a Coachman of the Twenty fourth day of June in the year aforesaid being upon the Coach box driving a certain Coach drawn by two Horses at the Little Theatre in the Hay Market in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid It is happened that the Near wheel of the said Coach got upon the Footway, and that the said William Paige was then and there by a sudden jolt Accidentally casually and by misfortune thrown off and from the said Coach box unto and upon the Stone Pavement in the said Street by Means whereof he the said William Paige did then and there receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of the Head of him the said William Paige, of which said Mortal Fracture he the said William Paige at the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields and also at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid languished and lived about the space of one hour, and at the Parish last mentioned he the said William Paige of the Mortal Fracture aforesaid on the same day did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Paige in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jonathan Hutchins  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten.

Tho Prickard Coroner Janar Hutchins Foreman
