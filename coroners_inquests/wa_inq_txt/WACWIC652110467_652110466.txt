City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields Within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of September in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great- Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of  George Gill then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Corner Thomas Dederaft , John Benson , Joseph Poynton , Thomas Spiers , Robert Thatcher , Andrew Wilson , William Watts , Alexander Yull , John Finch , Henry Turing , Martin Stephens , John Hicks , Samuel Fetherstone and Charles Garthwaite good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said George Gill came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That a Man whose Name is unknown on the Eighth day of September in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms, at the Parish aforesaid in the County aforesaid, in and upon the said George Gill in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously did make an Assault And that the said Man unknown him the said George Gill then and there feloniously did throw and cost to the Ground, And that the said Man unknown with a certain Stoke which he the said Man unknown then and there had and held in his Right Hand him the said George Gill ,. then and there lying upon the Ground as aforesaid in and upon the Head of him the said George Gill did then and there divers times feloniously Strike and beat, thereby then and there giving unto him the said George Gill in the Corner of the left Eye of him the said George Gill one Mortal Wound and Fracture, of which did mortal Wound and Fracture he the said George Gill for the space of four Hours Languished and lived and on the said Eight day of September in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said George Gill of the mortal Wound and Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon there Oath aforesaid do say that the said Man unknown him the said George Gill in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Feloniously did kill and slay against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. In Witness whereas well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Coroner Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Tho. Corner Foreman
