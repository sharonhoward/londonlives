City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of November in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Andrew Amos then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Tyrrerman , Richard Lloyd , Samuel Wigg , Thomas Pegg , William Branch , James Matthews , William Wills , Philip Sligell  Jacob Smart , James Morton , Samuel King , Edward Wevill , Edward Pardon and John Smith good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said, Andrew Amos came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said Andrew Amos on the Ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid being with several other Persons upon the Leads on the top of a certain House known by the Sign of the Merry Watermen, situate at Black Lyon Stairs in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, to View the Procession of the Right Honourable the Lord Mayor of London, towards Westminster upon the River Thames , And then and there looking over and leaning upon a certain Wooden rail on one side of the said Leads, It so happened that the said Andrew Amos Accidently Casually and by Misfortune did then and there fall from the said Leads, over the said Wooden rail, unto the Ground, and thereby did then and there receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the left Thigh, and divers mortal Bruises in and upon the Body of him the said Andrew Amos, of which said mortal Fracture and Bruises he the said Andrew Amos from the said Ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid until the Eleventh day of the same Month and Year, did languish at the Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, and thereof then and there did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Andrew Amos Accidently Causally and by Misfortune in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Tyrrerman Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself, and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos. Tyrrerman Foreman
