City and Liberty of Westminster  in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Ninth day of May in the Seventh year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Robinson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Major , John Wood , Thomas Coward , Francis Musgrave , John Wisdom , William Jones , William Hart , William Wilkinson , Edward Langbridge , Philip Cross , James Frith , William Davis , Peter Taylor , William Mason , James Noble , Joseph Gillam and Samuel Bass , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Robinson came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Robinson on the Twenty eighth day of May in the Year aforesaid being Riding on a certain Bay Horse in Hyde Park in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the said Horse being then in the Serpenting River within the said Park, It so happened that the said William Robinson Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did then and there fall down off and from the said Horse, over the Head of the said Horse, into the said Serpentine River, and in the Waters thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said William Robinson then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said William Robinson in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Henry Major Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner Henry Major Foreman
