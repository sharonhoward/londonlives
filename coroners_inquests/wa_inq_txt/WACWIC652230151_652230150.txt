City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirty first day of March in the twenty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Barett then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Nicholas Clark  John Dalby , Robert Hair , James Linden , Thomas Chapman , Edward Ellis , Jeremiah Glanvill , John Varty , Thomas Slack , William Heath , Gabriel Kingham and John Hunham good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Barett came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Barett on the Twenty fourth day of March in the Year aforesaid being alone in his Lodging Room in the Dwelling House of John Cole situate and being in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And the said Thomas Barett being in Liquor, It so happened that the Bed Curtains and other Furniture in the said Room, then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took Fire, by Reason whereof the Face Arms and Body of the said Thomas Barett were very much Burnt, of which he there languished and lived until the Twenty ninth day of the same Month in the same Year, and on the said Twenty ninth day of March he the said Thomas Barett at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, of the Burning aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath Aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Barett in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Nicholas Clark  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Nicholas Clark  Foreman
