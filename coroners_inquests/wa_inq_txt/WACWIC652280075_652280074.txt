City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of February in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the ing for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Hook then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Gosheron , Charles Mills , Davis Owen , Jonathan Griffiths , John Bateman , Richard Carter , William Black , John Grist , Philip Townsend William Weston , John Gossling , Ebenezer Johnson , and John Best good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chose, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Hook came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Hook a Soldier in the First Regiment of Guards Labouring under a grevious Disease of Body to wit a Fever and by Reason of the Violence of the said Disease being Delirious and out of his mind on the said Eleventh day of February in the year aforesaid being in the Hospital belonging to the said Regiment situate in Petty France at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid with a certain Knife made of Iron and Steel which he the said John Hook then and there had and held in his right hand the Throat or Gullet of him the said John Hook did then and there strike stab and penitrate thereby then and there giving unto himself the said John Hook so being Delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid with the Knife aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said John Hook one mortal Wound of the length of four Inches and of the Depth of one Inch of Which said mortal Wound he the said John Hook then and there Instantly died,

And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Hook by reason of the Disease aforesaid being Delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid in manner and by the means aforesaid did till himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Gosheron Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Tellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thomas Gosherson Foreman
