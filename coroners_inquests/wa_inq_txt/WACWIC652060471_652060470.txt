City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty second day of August in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so Forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty. On View of the Body of Ann Raynor Hyett then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Ireland  James Stone  Thomas Alanson  Christopher Reeves  Isaac Hureau , Rowe Brown  John Le Fountain  Jacob Deacon  Richard Benning  John Hart  John Vobe  Thomas Sherzaker  Richard Williams , Thomas Donovan , William Gilks  John Roberts Ralph Harbert Richard Gray and William Griffin Good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said Ann Raynor Hyett came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Ann Rayner Hyett on the Eighteenth day of August in the Year aforesaid being on foot,and crossing a certain Street called St. James's Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Man (to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown) being then and there Riding a certain Horse up the said Street, near unto the said Ann Rayner Hyett, It so happened that the said Ann Rayner Hyett, by endeavouring to shun the said Horse, Accidentally, Casually and by Misfortune did then and there fall down with great force, upon the Edge of the Stone Pavement in the footway in the said Street, whereby the said Ann Rayner Hyett did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive divers mortal Bruises in and upon the Stomach and Belly of her said Ann Rayner Hyett, of which said mortal Bruises she the said Ann Rayner Hyett from the said Eighteenth day of August on the Yeara aforesaid, until the Twenty first day of the same Month in the same Year at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live on which said Twenty first day of August in the year aforesaid, She the said Ann Rayner Hyett at the said Parish Liberty and County, of the Mortal Bruises aforesaid did dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Ann Rayner Hyett in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally, Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Ireland Foreman of the said Juror,on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence,have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Ireland Foreman
