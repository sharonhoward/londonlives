City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex the Fourth day of August in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Hughes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Michael Castleden , John Davis , John Martin , Samuel Wood , Frederick Duperoy  John Brown , John Mckay , James Bague , Edward Jones , William Newton , Phillip Lecoq , Evan Williams  Andrew Henney , Robert Stomrill , Silvester Freeman and John Dunford good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how , and by what Means the said Thomas Hughes came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That George Wells  William Atkins and John Emerson late of the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid Laborers not having the fear of God before their Eyes but being moved and seduced by the Instigation of the Davil on the said Fourth day of August in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said Thomas Hughes in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously wilfully and of their Malice afore thought did make an Assaut And that the said George Wells  William Atkins and John Emerson with their several Hands and otherwise then and there feloniously wilfully and of their Malice afore thought him the said Thomas Hughes , about the Head of him the said Thomas Hughes did hit Strike and beat And that they the said George Wells  William Atkins and John Emerson with their Hands about the Neck of him the said Thomas Hughes then and there fixed, he the said Thomas Hughes did then and there feloniously wilfully and of their Malice afore thought choak strangle and suffocate, of which said Beating Choaking Strangling and Suffocation he the said Thomas Hughes then and there died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said George Wells  William Atkins and John Emerson him the said Thomas Hughes in Manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously wilfully and of their Malice afore thought did hill and Murder against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity And that the said George Wells William Atkins and John Emerson at the time of committing the Felony and Murder aforesaid or at any time since had no Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements with in the said Liberty or elsewhere to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Michael Castled in Foreman of the said Jurory on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho Prickard Coroner

Michl. Castleden Foreman }
