City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex . } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Ann within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty eighth day of October in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Jane Pope an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Reddish  Ralph Street  George Wildman  George Hudson  Edward Taylor , Andrew Hopton  Robert Davidson  James Atkins  Richard Diggory , Henry Sampson , Richard Vaughan  John Crece  David Taylor John Keate and Joseph Davies good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Jane Pope came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Jane Hope the Infant on the Seventeenth day of October in the Year aforesaid being in the Apartment of Davies Pope her Father in the Dwelling House of George Cordery situate in New port Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being near the Fire Place, It so happened that the said Jane Pope the Infant then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune threw down a certain Saucepan, Standing upon the Hearth, fell of Boiling Water, by Means whereof the said Jane Pope was then and there Sealed and Burnt in and upon her Breast Stomach and Right Arm, of which said Sealing and Burning she the said Jane Pope from the said Seventeenth day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty fourth day of the same Month and Year, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and live, on which said Twenty fourth day of October she the said Jane Pope at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid of the mortal Scalding and Burning aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Jane Pope the Infant Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Reddish Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos Reddish Foreman
