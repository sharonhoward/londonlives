City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of October in the Thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of A new born Female Child then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Samuel Palmer , John Glazier , John Oram , Samuel Paynes Edward Burridge , William Adams , John Brown , John Sansom , Moses Freeman , Thomas Butler , William Godfrey , Paul Lemaitre , James Shaw and John Watkins good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said New born Female Child came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twelfth day of October in the year aforesaid a new born Female Child was found dead in a Paper Box upon a certain Tomb Stone in the Church Yard belonging to the said Parish of St. Anne within the Liberty and County aforesaid that no marks of Violence appeared thereupon and that the said Female Child was still born.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Samuel Palmer  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Saml. Palmer Foreman
