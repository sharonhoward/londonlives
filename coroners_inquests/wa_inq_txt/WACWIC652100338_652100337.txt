City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex  } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of July in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth; before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of the Reverend Bernard Diemel then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Evan Davies Anthony Peacock , Joseph Skinner , James Foord , Thomas Spencer , William Locky , John Blatch Hector Neale , Richard Herring , Joseph Charles , Francis Redhedd , John Hale , and Samuel Mitchell good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Bernard Diemel came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say

 That the said Bernard Diemel on the Sixteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid being walking in a certain Street called Little Chaple Street in the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid, it to happened that he the said Bernard Diemel was then and there suddenly Leized with a Fit of Apoplexy ; and by reason of the Violence thereof he the said Bernard Diemal fell to & against the Ground in the said and then and then instantts Died And so the Jurors of their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Bernard Diemel by the Violence of the aforesaid, and in manner and by the means aforesaid came to his Death and not otherwise. In Wintness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Leabs the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Evan Davis  Foreman
