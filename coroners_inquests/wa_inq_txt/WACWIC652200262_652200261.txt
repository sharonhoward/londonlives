City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Third day of July in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Williams Simmons then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Thatcher , James James , James Hoare , Richard Willett Nathaniel Chandler , Aaron Bourne , Charles Lockington James Mc.Farlane , James Deane , William Braine , James Bennet and William Hill good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Simmons came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Simmons on the first day of July in the Year aforesaid being driving a certain Cart loader with Coals and drawn by two Horses in Swallow Street in the Parish of St. James within in the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that it so happened that the said William Simmons fell down in the said Street by the side of the said Cart and that the Near Wheel of the said Cart then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the left side of the Body of him the said William Simmons , by where of the said William Simmons then and there received mortal Bruises in and upon his said left side, of which said mortal Bruises he the said William Simmons dead the same day, at the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields with in the Liberty and County aforesaid. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Simmons in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Thatcher Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Robt. Thatcher Foreman
