City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of December in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Eleanor Mathews then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Christopher Pinchbeck , William Smith , John Hog , Peter Valletten , Richard Golden , John Bull , Thomas Sams , Henry Hadley , David Parret John Showrd , William Bayley , James Hartley , Monger Baikie , John Dunson and Robert Thatcher good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said George Clarke came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Eleanor Mathews on the Eleventh day of December in the Year aforesaid, being Sweeping the Crossing in Cock Spur Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Man unknown was then carefully driving a certain Cart along the said Street towards Charing Cross , And the said Eleanor Mathews being Old and infirm, and in the Middle of the said Street, It so happened that the said Eleanor Mathews , in endeavouring to escape a Coach then passing along the said Street, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down, by Means whereof the Off Wheel of the said Cart then and there passed over the Head of the said Eleanor Mathews , whereby the said Eleanor Mathews received one mortal Fracture in and upon the right side of her Head, of which said mortal Fracture she the said Eleanor Mathews then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Eleanor Mathews in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Christopher Pinchbeck Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Chrispr Pinchbeck Foreman
