City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of October in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of Thomas Peirce then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Daniel Hancock , Morris Rice , William Saunders , William Brattle , Joseph Follett , Richard Collington Samuel Smallman , John Tasker , Thomas Marshall , Joseph Frightler , Thomas Woodbridge  John Pratt , and Christopher Nettleton , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when; how; and by what Means the said Thomas Peirce came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Peirce , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but Lunatick and distracted, on the Nineteenth day of October in the Year aforesaid, out of and through a certain Garret Window in the dwelling House of Thomas Thornhill Esquire situate and being in Berkley Square in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did cast and throw himself, into the back Yard adjoyning to the said House, which said Yard is Paved with Stone thereby then and there giving unto himself one mortal Wound and Fracture, in and upon the right side of the Head of him the said Thomas Peirce , of which said mortal Wound and Fracture, he the said Thomas Peirce then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Thomas Peirce , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Daniel Hancock Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Danl. Hancock Foreman
