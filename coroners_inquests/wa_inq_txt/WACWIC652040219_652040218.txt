City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex the Twenty Second day of June in the Fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Robert Grinley an Infant, then & there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Jennings  Burt Wade , David Columbine , Edward Pyke , Thomas Boland, William Wells , Martin Maney , William Cunningham , Oliver Warner , Timothy Marshall , Thomas Shelsher , Thomas Allison , and William Greenwood , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said Robert Grinley came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That on the Nineteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid, the said Robert Grinley being with other Children playing in a Hayloft situate and being in a certain Stable Yard called George Yard , at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and Country aforesaid, and the Door of the said Hayloft being then open, It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the said Robert Grinley did then and there fall out of and from the said Hayloft into the said Stable Yard, which said Stable Yard was paved with Stone, whereby the said Robert Grinley did then and there receive one Mortal Fracture on the right side of his Head behind his right Ear, of which said Mortal Fracture he the said Robert Grinley at a certain Hospital called St. George Hospital in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesd from the said Nineteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty first day of the same Month and Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty first day of June in the Year aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Robert Grinley in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness Whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Jennings Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Jennings Foreman
