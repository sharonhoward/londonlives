City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of March in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Morgan then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Elvidge , John Fox , David Brough , William Atwood , Richard Carter , John Leach , Robert Ward , Samuel Keep , James Tulleth , Samuel Tucker , John Nussey , George Lane and Thomas Denham good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Morgan came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Morgan on the Twelfth day of March in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of Saint Mary Lambeth in the County of Surrey in a certain Road there called the Bridge Road, being Riding on a laddle Horse, and the said Horse being on a full Gallop or Canter, the Legs of the said Horse flew from under him on the near side and thereby accidentally casually and by Misfortune fell on the flag Pavement in the Footway of the said Road and in his said Fall fell on the said John Morgan who at the same time fell himself by means whereof he the said John Morgan then and there received a Violent and Mortal Concussion on the Head and Brain of him the said John Morgan which Ruptured the Vessels of the Brain of which said violent and Mortal Concussion on the Head and Brain he the said John Morgan Languished in the said Parish of Saint Mary Lambeth in the County of Surrey , and also at the Westminster Hospital in James Street in the said Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster from the said Twelfth day of March in he Year aforesaid until the Thirteenth day of March being the Day following, and languishing did Live, on which said Thirteenth day of March in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster aforesaid he the said John Morgan of the Mortal Concussion on the Head and Brain of him the said John Morgan did Dye. And so the Jurors on their oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Morgan in Manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place first abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Elvidge Foreman
