City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St.Martin in Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of November in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Henry Steadman then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Sutton , Henry Hansaw , John Harkes , Richard Katharine , Robert Gayes , John Field , John Marshall , Charles Card , John Sowardsby , Joseph Bridgford , Jacob Allen , John Sarbitt , Samuel Conno , Thomas Batrick Simon Bayley and Joseph Fleming good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, what means the said  Henry Steadman came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That on the Thirty first day of October in the year aforesaid a man unknown was carefully driving a certain Carrige, Loaded with Timber and drawn by four Horses, in a Street called little St. Martins Lane in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and Country aforesaid, And the said Henry Steadman , being then and there intoxicated with Liquor, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down in the said Street near unto the Wheels of the said loaded Carriage, and the near Fore-Wheel of the said Carriage did then and there Accidentally casually and by Misfortune pass over the Neck and right Shoulder of him the said Henry steadman , by reason whereof the said Henry Steadman did then and there receive one mortal Bruise and Fracture in and upon his Neck and Right Shoulder, of which said mortal Bruise and Fracture he the said Henry Steadman then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Henry Steadman , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Sutton foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and place first above written.

Tho.Prickard Coroner

Thos. Sutton Foreman.
