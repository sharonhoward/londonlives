City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of February in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Christopher Taff then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Peter Vallotton , Thomas Cockburn  Samuel Towell , Robert Low , John Hicks  James Frazer , Edward Kenyon , Richard Sells , Thomas Grey , Anthony Hardy , William Williams and Joseph Poynton good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Christopher Taff came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Christopher Taff on the Ninth day of February in the Year aforesaid being sliding upon the Canal in St. James's Park in the Parish of saint Margaret in the County aforesaid It so happened that the Tee broke, and that the said Christopher Taff then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell into the said Canal, and in the Waters thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Christopher Taff then and there died. And the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Christopher Taff in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Peter Vallotton Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Peter Vallotton Foreman
