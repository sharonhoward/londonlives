City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Thomas Shirland the Sing of the Brown Bear , Knightsbridge in the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the seventh day of March 1796 in the thirty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of Body of Elizabeth Gray an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said Elizabeth Gray came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Elizabeth Gray on the twenty ninth February in the year aforesaid being with other Infant Children in a certain Room in which there was a Fire It so happened that accidentally casually and by Misfortune the Aaron and other Apparel which she then and there had on her Body took Fine by reason whereof and from the Flame arising from the said Fire, she the said Elizabeth Gray did languish and languishing did live until the fifth day of March in the same Year: on which said fifth day of March in the same year in their the Liberty aforesaid she the said Elizabeth Gray of the Burning aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Elizabeth Gray in manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to her Death, and not otherwise

 IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }
