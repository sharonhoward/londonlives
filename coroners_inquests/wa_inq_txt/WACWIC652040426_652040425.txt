City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fourth day of October in the Fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Great of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth , before Thomas Prickard  Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Thomas Height then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of William Gibson , Thomas Thompson , John Mc.Laughlin  Thomas Lee , Adam Digby John Perry , Thomas Heyre , Michael Artis , Thomas Dyer , Martin Waddington , William Skeat , Henry Haymow , and Thomas Gray good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to Inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Height came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, that on the Twenty third day of October in the Year aforesaid, the said Thomas Height being Driving a Certain Cart drawn by three Horses in a certain Street or Publick Highway called Oxford Road , near unto the Turnpike called Tyburn Turnpike , in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And the other Persons were then driving other Carts and Carriages in the said Publick Highway, And the said Thomas Height being Inta and in Liquar, It that the said Thomas Height fell down upon the Ground in the said Publick Highway, and that a Wheel belonging to one of the said Carts or other Carriages then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, did pass over the right side of the Head of him the said Thomas Height by Means whereof he the said Thomas Height did then and there receive one Mortal Fracture on the right side of his Head, of which said Mortal Fracture he the said Thomas Height on the same day did Dye, within the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do day, That the said Thomas Height in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally, Casually, and by Misfortune, came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Gibson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coronoer.

Wm Gibson Foreman
