City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of he Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Thirteenth day of January in the Seventh year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King, for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Honor Bromsden then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Oliver , James Simonds , William Donaldson , Richard Green , William House , John Jeffreys , Thomas Allison , Edward Winwood , John Brand , Alexander Lalivere , John Dixon , Charles Ransford , William Jelks Charles Steward , James Hough , Richard Dixon , and John Wilson , good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said Honor Bromsden came to her Death do upon, their Oath say, That the said Honor Bromsden on the Tenth day of January in the Year aforesaid, died suddenly in Carnaby Market , at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, that no Marks of Violence appeared on the Body of the said Honor Bromsden , and that the said Honor Bromsden, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way, and not by any hurt or Injury received from any Person whatsoever. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Robert Oliver Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

Robert Oliver Foreman
