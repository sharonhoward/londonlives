City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of October in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Gray then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Lockie , William Stevens , John Cartwright Lewis Sulsh , William Jordan , James Miller , Ernest Augustus Thistleton , John Stevenson , Robert Archer , Duncan Watson James Taylor , John Mackintosh , John Beatman , William Atwood and John Robinson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Gray came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said William Gray an infant of the Age of four Years on the twenty sixth day of June in the Year aforesaid was playing with other Children in the Kitchen of Thomas Gray his Father situate in Fulham field in the County aforesaid Gardener and that a large pot full of boiling water was then standing upon the Hearth in the said Kitchen It so happened that the said William Gray the Infant Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell backwareds into the said boiling water by means where of the said William Gray the Infant was scalded and burnt in and upon the back sides and Thighs of him the said William Gray of which said sealding and burning he the said william Gray the Infant from the said twenty sixth day of June in the year aforesaid untill the Third day of october in the same Year at Fulham Field aforesaid And also at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and live on which said third day of October in the year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Margaret he the said William Gray of the scalding and burning aforesaid did die, And so the Turns aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said William Gray in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Lockie Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Wm Lodkie Foreman
