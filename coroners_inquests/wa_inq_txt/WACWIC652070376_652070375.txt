City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth Day of June in the Seventh year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Thomas Bull then and there lying dead upon the Oath of Thomas Tapper , Joseph Sandell , George Mortimer , Thomas Sturgeon , James Whitlock , William Briddon , Ambrose Pennington , William Vaughan , William Chapman , William Crockford , Richard Thomas and William Bean , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Bull came to his Death, do upon their Oath says That the said Thomas Bull not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Sixth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid a great Quantity of Laudanum being a deadly Poison, into a certain Quantity of strong Beer did then and there put and mix, and the said Laudanum, so put and mixt as aforesaid, he the said Thomas Bull , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted as aforesaid, did take, drink, and swallow down; and that he the said Thomas Bull by reason of the taking drinking and swallowing the said Laudanum, in a few hours, to Wit, on the said sixth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish Liberty and County, did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Bull , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did Poison and kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Tapper Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Thos Tapper Foreman
