City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of June in the twenty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Brookhouse then and there lying dead, upon she Oath of James Masson , Thomas Bamford , George Nesbit , Joseph Mabley , William Cottrell , Robert Anderson ; Gilbert Rosa , John Pybus , William Whitworth , John Grist , Richard Warren and William Couridge good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Brookhouse came to is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Brookhouse on the fourteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid being at Work in the Powder Mills upon Hounslow Heath in the County aforesaid, and that the Powder in one of the said Mills wherein the said John Brookhouse then Worked, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took Fire and went off, whereby the said John Brookhouse was burnt in his Face, Arms, Legs, and part of his Body, of which said Burning he the said John Brookhouse at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the LIberty and County aforesaid from the said fourteenth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Eighth day of June in the same Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Eighth day of June in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said John Brookhouse of the mortal Burning aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said James Masson  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

James Masson Foreman
