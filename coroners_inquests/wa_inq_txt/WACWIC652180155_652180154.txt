City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of May in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Phoenix Felton then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Pegrum Thorpe , William Hancock , Thomas Stanley , Stephen Cant , William Barker , Charles Wisdom , John Heath , Peter Dunbabin , Andrew Steans Robert Watson , William James , Peter Jackson , Joseph Gill  Thomas Green George Dike and William Ower good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Phanix Felton came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Phanix Felton on the fifth day of May in the Year aforesaid being in a certain Boat in and upon the River Thames at a Place called Mill Bank in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there in the said Boat Sailing up the said River It so happened that by a sudden Gust of Wind the said Boat in which the said Phanix Felton was Sailing, their and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune was Overset, and that the said Phenix Felton Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in the Water of the said River Thames was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Phanix Felton then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Phenix Felton in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Pegrum Thorpe Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner Pegrum Thorpe Foreman
