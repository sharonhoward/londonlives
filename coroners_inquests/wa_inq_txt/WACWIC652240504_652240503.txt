City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of September in the Twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Henry Haltzman  then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Rogers , John Cardo , John Tate , Christopher Tennant Joseph Stringer , Richard Norman , Andrew Sheppard , Simon Shepperson Charles Geary , William Angus , Jeffry Jolly , and John Holmes good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Henry Haltzman came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Henry Haltzman not being of Sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatic and Distracted on the Sixth day of September in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, a Certain Razor made of Iron and Steel which he the said John Henry Haltzman then and there had and hold in his Right Hand the Throat or Gullett of him the said John Henry Haltzman did then and there strike stab and penetrate thereby then and there giving unto himself the said John Henry Haltzman with the Razor aforesaid one Mortal Wound of the length of three inches and of the depth of one Inch of which said Mortal Wound he the said John Henry Haltzman then and there instantly Died. And to the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Henry Haltzman not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and Distracted in manner and by the Means aforesaid did Kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors Lane to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Edward Rogers  Foreman
