City and Liberty Westminster in the County of Middlexsex.} to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty -fifth day of August in the Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard  Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Alexander Mearnes an Instant then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Lewis Sulsh , Samuel Grange , William Brook  John Lowrie  Thomas Jennings Thomas Watkins , William Smith , William Luckey , Robert Branham , John Blatch , Robert Thornton Gilbert Knifton , Abraham Longstaff , Isaac Asser , and Nathaniel Hutchinson good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, and who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how and by what means the said Alexander Mearnes came to his Death, Do upon their Oath say, That on the twenty fourth day of August in the year aforesaid the said Alexander Mearnes being with other Children in a certain Place called Mallors's Wharf in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, And the said Alexander Mearnes in going over certain Barges then floating on the River Thames , near and adjoining to the said Wharf, in order to get into a certain Boat or Cutter then and there lying, It so happened that the foot of the said Alexander Mearnes slipped, and missing his stop, He thereby Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell into the said River of Thames , and in the Waters thereof was then and there suffocated and Drowned, of which Suffocation and Drowning he the said Alexander Mearnes then and there instanstly Died; And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Alexander Mearnes in Manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally casually and by misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Lewis Sulsh Foreman, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and place first abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Lewis Sulsh foreman
