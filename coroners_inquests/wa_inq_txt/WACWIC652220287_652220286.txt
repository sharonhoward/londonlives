City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of August in the Twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Owen then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Pettis , Thomas Shephard , Thomas Brown , William Dorsett Daniel Fryer , William Coward , Edmund Frontbeck , William Elmer , Richard Oakes , John Mew , Cyrus Isaac , and Peter Fozzard good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Owen came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Owen on the Thirty first day of July in the Year aforesaid in the County aforesaid to wit at or near Hammersmith Turnpike being riding on a Horse full speed or Gallop, it being dark he unfortunately Rode against the Shaft of a Cart then and there coming along and then and there accidentally casually and by Misfortune received a violent and Mortal Fracture on his Thigh, of which said Mortal Fracture he the said Thomas Owen from the Thirty first day of July in the Year aforesaid until the Fifth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the County aforesaid and also at the Parish and Liberty aforesaid to wit at Saint George's Hospital near Hyde Park corner did Languish and Languishing did Live, on which said Fifth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Hospital aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did Dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Owen in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place first above mentioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Pettis Foreman
