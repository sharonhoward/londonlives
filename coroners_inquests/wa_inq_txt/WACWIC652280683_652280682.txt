City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented. taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourth day of November in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Bunn an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Byrchmore , John Geddis , Samuel Griffiths John Rickerby , Peter Cockburn , William Pilling , William Bushby William Lawton , John Barker , James Bumpstead  George Graves John Wallice and Isaac Wright good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Bunn came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Bunn an Infant of the Age of six Years on the first day of November in the Year aforesaid being left with another Infant of the age of Three Years in the Lodging Room or Apartment of their Father John Bunn in the Dwelling House of Barney Smith situate in Haydon Yard in the said Parish of Saint James within the Liberty and County aforesaid and the said Elizabeth Bunn the Infant going too near the Fire It so happened that the Linen Cloaths which the said Elizabeth Bunn had on her Body then and there Accidentally Casually and by misfortune took fire by means whereof the Stomach neck Face and Arms of her the Said Elizabeth Bunn were then and there burnt of which said burning she the said Elizabeth Bunn at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said first day, of November in the Year aforesaid until the third day of the same Month and year did languish and live on which said third day of November in the year aforesaid at the said Parish Liberty and County she the said Elizabeth Bunn of the mortal burning aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say that the said Elizabeth Bunn in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise

In Witness Whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Byrchmore Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Robt Byrchmore Foreman
