City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate, Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of June in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Margaret Carrick then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Hedges , Thomas Harwood , Richard Dovey , John Wallis  Samuel Fidmus Joseph Halsey , William Edwards , William Bolton  William Patterson , John Mew , George Sandham and Thomas Paul good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Margaret Carrick came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Margaret Carrick on the Twenty fourth day of June in the Year aforesaid being in a certain Cart drawn by one Horse in the Kings Highway at a Place called Sheppards Bush in the County aforesaid, and being then and there driving the said Horse, It so Happened that by a sudden jolt the said Margaret Carrick was then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune thrown out of the said Cart unto the Ground, and thereby then and there received a Mortal Concussion in and upon the Brain of her the said Margaret Carrick, of which said Mortal Concussion she the said Margaret Carrick at the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square with in the Liberty and County aforesaid on the said Twenty fourth day of June in the Year aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Margaret Carrick in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Hedges Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner Thos. Hedges Foreman
