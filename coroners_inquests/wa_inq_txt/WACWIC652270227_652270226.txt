City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex the Thirtieth day of April in the twenty seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King of the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Patrick Crooke then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Mangaar  Charles Wiseman , Joseph Henloy Richard Butler , William Watchman , John Hitchton John Broomfield , Thomas Woodhouse John Phillips  William Sorrell  Joseph Whithers  Walter Gage good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being, then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Patrick Crooke came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Patrick Crooke on the twenty ninth day of April in the year aforesaid in the night time died suddenly in a certain street called Exeter Street at the said Parish of St. Pauls Covent Garden within the Liberty and County aforesaid that no marks of Violence appeared upon the Body and that the said Patrick Crooke departed this Life in a natural way and not by any hurt or Injury from any Person whatsoever to the Knowledge of the said Jurors

In Witness whereof as well the said Corner as the said John Mangaar Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself & the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day year & Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Jo Mangaar  Foreman
