An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the First day of December in the Thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Morris Lynard then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Samuel Strong , David Fenlow, Michael Rush , John Keeling John Dennison , John French , John Clarke , John Lightfoot , Samuel Knight Richard Bollow, Samuel Goldsmith , Bingham Noon , John Davis and John Price good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Morris Lynard came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Morris Lynard (suspected to have been murthered) on the Twenty ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid died suddenly in a certain Cook Shop Situate in Chandes Street in the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County found that the said Morris Lynard departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way and not by any Violence or Injury from any Person or Person whatsoever to the Knowledge of the said Jurors

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Samuel Strong  foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence house to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coron .

S. Strong Foreman
