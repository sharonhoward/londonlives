City & Liberty of Westmr . in the County of Middlesex To wit.  

 An Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Parish of St. Margaret within the City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex the fourteenth Day of March in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & Liberty on View of the Body of James Osborn then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of George Hewitt , John Cooknol Joseph Smith , Thomas Ackstall , Dennis Cummins , William Stores , William Lowdy , Samuel Everingham , John Stanfield , William Edger , William Mott , and Thomas Watkins good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said James Osborn came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Osborn , Servant to John Price of Scotland Yard in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields in the said County of Middlesex Coal Merchant, on the Second day of March in the Year aforesaid, being Watering several Horses of the said John Price in Scotland Yard aforesaid, It so happened that one of the said Horses kicked at the said James Osborn , and that the said James Osborn by endeavouring to avoid the Stroke, fell against the Wheel of an empty Cart there standing, by Means whereof the said James Osborn did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive divers mortal Bruises in and upon his Head Back and Breast of which said mortal Bruises he the said James Osborn from the said Second day of March in the year aforesaid until the Thirteenth day of the same Month and Year, at the Westminster Hospital situate and being in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said thirteenth day of March in the year aforesaid, he the said James Osborn at the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, of the mortal Bruises aforesaid, did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said James Osborn Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said George Hewitt Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

George Hewitt  Foreman
