City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square , within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Seventh day of December in the fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the King forth said City and Liberty on View of the Body of William Hunter then and there lying Dead upon the Oath of Edward Gray , Edward Richardson , William Herne , William Sleigh , Richard Mason , Ralph Walker , James Martin , Oliver Ball , John Coats , John Rumsey , John Smith , Edward Truden , John Lyons , Edward Prebell , Joseph Watters , William Bower , John Sansbury , Tall scott , Henry Skeats , William Thomas and Richard Cook good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there Duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how and by what Means the said William Hunter came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That the said William Hunter being a Lodger in an Old decayed House, situate and being in a certain Court called Silver Court, in the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that between Eleven and twelve of the Clock at Night, of the Fifth day of December in the Year aforesaid, the said old decayed House, in which the said William Hunter , together with several other Persons, were then and there lodged, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, sunk and fell in, by Means whereof the said William Hunter was then and there under the ruins and Materials thereof Smothered, Suffocated and Killed, of which said Smothering, Suffocation, and Killing, he the said William Hunter then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said William Hunter in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally, Casually, and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward Gray Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner.

Edward Gray Foreman
