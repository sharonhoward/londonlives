City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented Taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Seventh day of December in the fourth Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Mary Anderson then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Edward Gray , Edward Richardson , William Herne , William Sleigh , Richard Manson , Ralph Walker  James Martin , Oliver Ball  John Coats , John Rumsey , John Smith  Edward Truden , John Lyons , Edward Prebell , Joseph Watters , William Bower , John Sansbury , Tall Scott , Henry Skeats , William Thomas and Richard Cook , good and Lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how and by what Means the said Mary Anderson came to her Death, do upon there Oath say That the said Mary Anderson , being a Lodger in an old decayed House, situate and being in a certain Court called Silver Court, in the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that between Eleven and twelve of the Clock at Night, of the fifth day of December in the Year aforesaid, the said old decayed House, in which the said Mary Anderson together with several other Persons were then and there lodged, accidentally Casually and by Misfortune Sunk and fell in, by Means whereof the said Mary Anderson was then and there under the ruins and Materials thereof Smothered, Suffocated, and Killed; of which said Smothering, Suffocation and Killing, he the said Mary Anderson then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid; upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Mary Anderson in manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, came to her Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward Gray Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard  Coroner .

Edward Gray Foreman
