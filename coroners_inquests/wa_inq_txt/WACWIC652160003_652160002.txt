City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of January in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Rowlet then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Kilsby , Patrick Fulton , John Booge , Robert Thatcher , William Newman , John Berdd, John Wilkinson , George Green , George Collett , William Rentull Jossop , John Wall , John Lawder , William Gordon , Peter Hill , and John Lucas , Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Rowlet came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the Said John Rowlet on the Eighth day of January in the Year aforesaid being upon and walking over certain Lighters and Barges in and upon the River Thames at Black Lyon Stairs in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being then and there Intoxicated with Liquor, It so happened that the said John Rowlet did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fall over and from one of the said Lighters into the River Thames , and in the Waters thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said John Rowlet then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid Upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said John Rowlet in Manner and by the Means aforesaid. Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Richard Kilsby Foreman of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Richd. Kilsbry Foreman
