City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of November in the Seventieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Benjamin Bennet then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Wilson , John Brown  Henry Wilson , William Dixwell , John Jowett , Richard Cust  Lewis Boyden  James Benn  John Lawrence , John Gale , Joseph Grieves  John Rogers  Daniel Hockel  William Newton Nathaniel Mann and John Pool good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Benjamin Bennet came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Benjamin Bennet a Bricklayer on the fifth day of November in the Year aforesaid being at Work on the outside of the dwelling house of Mrs. Martham situate and being in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And being upon the Roof of a certain Dormer Window in the front of the said House It so happened that the said Window gave way, and that the said Benjamin Bennet then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said House unto the Ground, and thereby then and there received divers Bruises and Fractures in and upon the Head Body and left High of him the said Benjamin Bennet , of which said Mortal Bruises and Fractures he the said Benjamin Bennet then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Benjamin Bennet in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally casually and by misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Wilson Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, that Day Year and Place above written

Thos Prickard Coroner

Jas: Wilson Foreman
