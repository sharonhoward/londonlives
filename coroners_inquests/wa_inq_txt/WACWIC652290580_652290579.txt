City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of St Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of October in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Brown then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Whitworth , Thomas Hamilton , Robert Watson , Matthew Gingell , Richard Bradley , Samuel Keep , Robert Ward , Richard Carter , Thomas Barret , William Foster , William Cook , Thomas Reeves , Joseph Perrin , James Laws and Charles Yates good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Brown came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Richard Brown on the ninth day of October in the Year aforesaid being with several others unloading certain Wooden Pipes out of a Barge at the Fire Engine at Pimlice, in the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that the said Richard Brown who drove the Horse in the Crane by undoing the Link let the Horse loose, and Crance going round, It so happened that the Bigg or Wooden Bar of the said Crane forces the said Richard Brown against an upright Wooden Post by means whereof the said Richard Brown then and there Accidentally Casually and by misfortune received a mortal Bruise in and upon the lower part of his Belly, of which said mortal Bruise he the said Richard Brown , at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square , and also at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid, from the said ninth day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Fifteenth day of the same month did languish and langusihing did live On which said Fifteenth day of October in the year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Margaret he the said Richard Brown of the mortal Bruise aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Richard Brown in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Whitworth  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition at their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Whitworth Foreman
