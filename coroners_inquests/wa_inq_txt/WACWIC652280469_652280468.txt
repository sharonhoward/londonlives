City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex . } to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourth day of August in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Stephens then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Francis Cann , John Keeling , Thomas Bayley , Peter Gornall , Richard Wrather , Edward Creasey , Richard Hoy Richard Clark , William Mends , John Rock , Thomas Andrews and Ralph Peacock good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Stephens came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Stephens on the first day of August in the year aforesaid driving a certain Waggon in the Kings Highway at Kensington in the County aforesaid and then and there riding upon the Shaft of the said Waggon and being in liquor It so happened that the said Thomas Stephens Accidentally Casually and by misfortune fell off and from the shaft of the said Waggon into the Ground and that the near Hinder Wheel of the said Waggon passed over the Breast of the said Thomas Stephens by means whereof the said Thomas Stephens then and there received a mortal Fracture on the ribs upon the right side and a mortal Bruise in the Body of him the said Thomas Stephens of which said mortal Bruise and Fracture he the said Thomas Stephens at Saint Geroge's Hospital in the said Parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid languished about one Hour and then and there of the mortal Bruise and Fracture aforesaid did ide. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Stephens in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Francis Cann Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and place above mentioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Francis Cann Foreman
