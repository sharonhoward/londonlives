City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St, Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth Day of January in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of Lewis Harro then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Holbrook , Walter Pencote , John Samuel Cherry , James Mason , Thomas Greenaway , John Tomlinson , Emanuel Lloyd , William Hepburn , William Ridout , William Mott , William Edgar , Robert Horwood , David Williams and John Harriott good and Lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lod the King, when, how and by what Means the said Lewis Harro came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty fifth day of January in the Year aforesaid, the Dwelling House of situate and being in a certain Street called the Strand , at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid, casually took Fire, and the said Lewis Harro being then and there present, and aiding and assisting to extinguish the said Fire, It so happened that a piece of Timber, by the Force and violence of the said Fire then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell from the said House in and upon the Head of him the said Lewis Harro, by Reason whereof he the said Lewis Harro then and there received a mortal Fracture in and upon the Head of him the said Lewis Harro, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Lewis Harro languished about two hours at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there of the mortal Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Lewis Harro Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Mean aforesaid, came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Henry Holbrook Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Henry Holbrook Foreman
