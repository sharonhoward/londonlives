City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Joseph Poynton the Sign of the Phonix in Palan Street peinlic's in the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the eighth day of May 1799 in the thirty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of Body of Elizabeth Payne then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Payne came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Elizabeth Payne on the Sixth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid died Suddenly through Intoxication by an Excess of drinking IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inqusition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }
