City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex . to Wit}  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twenty ninth day of July in the Twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Whittingham then and there lying dead upon the Oath of James Price  John Nock , Michael Barker  Joseph Wray , John Matthews  David Berrry , Richard Smith  Leonard Lad , Thomas Fletcher , William Newman  Richard Sloper , James Duckson , William Pritchard and Robert Moffett good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, who being then an there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King when how and by what Means the said Thomas Whittingham came to his death do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Whittingham and one Thomas Moore on the Twenty seventh day of July in the Year aforesaid being wantonly, and not in Anger, Wrestling together in the Dwelling House Samuel Cretchell in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and that it so happened that the said Thomas Whittingham threw down and with great force fall upon the said Thomas Moore , and thereby then and there Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of him the said Thomas Moore received a violent Bruise in and upon the Private Parts of him the said Thomas Whittingham , of which he languished until the Twenty eighth day of the same Month of July in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid on which said Twenty eighth day of July in the Year aforesaid he the said Thomas Whittingham of the mortal Bruise aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Thomas Whittingham in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Price  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

James Price Foreman
