City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of April in the Eight Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Cunningham then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Lindeman , William Brooke  Richard Weale , George Bussett , Thomas Turner , William Crumbie , Alsop Archer , Peter Bennett , Edward Hicks , Thomas Reader William Aickman , Mark Beaman , John Gossart , Joseph Holbrook , William Slay , Cornelius Long , and Robert Forrester , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Cunningham came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Cunningham on the Eighteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid, at the house of Jenkin Morris Victualler situate and being in Hungerford Market in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, without being compelled or forced ay any Person whatsoever, did take, drink, and swallow down, a great Quantity of distilled Spirituous Liquor, commonly called Geneval; by Reason of which said drinking and swallowing down of the said Spirituous Liquor, he the said John Cunningham then and there become suffocated and choaked, and thereof the same day, to wit, the Eighteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid, at the said Parish Liberty and County, did die. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Lindeman Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner ,

Wm. Lindenman Foreman
