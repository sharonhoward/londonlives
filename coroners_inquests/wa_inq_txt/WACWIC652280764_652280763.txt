City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirteenth day of December in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Day then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Ryan , Thomas Fareham , George Pellit Daniel Brown , William Hawkins , Robert Hardy , Robert Peter . Thomas Asliln , John Denew , Robert Morris , Samuel Aldhouse Joseph Edmonds and Charles Radley good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Day came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the twelfth day of December in the Year aforesaid, the said Thomas Day was found Dead in a certain Hayloft, situate in Portland Mews in the said Parish of St. James , within the Liberty and County aforesaid, that no marks of violence appeared in the and that the said Thomas Day departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural Way, and by the inclemency of the Weather

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Richard Ryan Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Richard Ryan Foreman
