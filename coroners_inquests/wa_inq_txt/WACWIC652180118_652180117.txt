City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirteenth day of April in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grave of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Arthur Leary an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Baker  John Grimes  John Young , Daniel Ellet Mitchell , William Jones , Thomas Rablus , John Sowerby  James Birks  Thomas Keeley , John Leach , William Richardson , John Campion , Peter Robertson , Thomas Stafford , John Cliffe and Moses Wingrove good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Arthur Leary came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That William Agate late of the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid Laborer on the Tenth day of April in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said Arthur Leary an Infant of the Age of two Years in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King, and in a certain Publick Street and Kings Highway there then and there being feloniously did make an Assault, And that the said William Agate then and there unlawfully and violently driving a Coach drawn by two Horses did then and there feloniously and forcibly drive the said Horses so drawing the said Coach towards and unto the said Arthur Leary And that the said William Agate by means thereof did then and there in the Publick Street and Kings Highway aforesaid by and with the Nearmost of the said two Horses feloniously force and thr him the said Arthur Leary a to the Ground, and the two Near Wheels of the said Coach so drawn as aforesaid by the violent and unlawful driving aforesaid did then and there go upon and pass over the Breast and Body of the said Arthur Leary so lying upon the Ground as aforesaid, And that the said William Agate by the unlawful violent and forcibly driving aforesaid the Breast of him the said Arthur Leary by the near Wheels of the Coach aforesaid did then and there Press Crush and Squeese, thereby then and there giving unto him the said Arthur Leary by the Crushing Pressing and Squeesing aforesaid one mortal Bruise and Contusion in and upon the said Breast of him the said Arthur Leary , of which he the said Arthur Leary their and there soon afterwards died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Agate him the said Arthur Leary in manner and by the means aforesaid feloniously did kill and Ray against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. And that the said William Agate at the time of committing the Felony and Manslaughter aforesaid or at any time since had no Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements within the said Liberty or elsewhere to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Baker Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jno. Baker Foreman
