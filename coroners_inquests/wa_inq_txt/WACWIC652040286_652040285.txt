City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth Day of July in the Fourth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith so Forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of Thomas Foy , then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Edward Reynolds , John Almon  William Morgan  James Jackson  Matthew Towns , William York , Saml Agnew , Thomas Partridge , Nicholas Grey , John Lowson , Joseph Frankland , William Couty , Daniel Ingman , James Hobbs and William Bathoe , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charg'd to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Foy came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Foy , on the Twenty eighth day of July in the Year aforesaid, going into the River to bath himself, at a certain Place called Chelsea Bridge situate and being in the Parish of St. George  Hanover Square , within the Liberty aforesaid in the County aforesaid; It so happened that Accidentally, Casually and by Misfortune he the said Thomas Foy was in the Waters of the said River, then and there suffocated and Drowned of which did Suffocation and he the said Thomas Foy then and there instand Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Thomas Foy , in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally, Casually, and by Misfortune Came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof, as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward Reynolds Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of Himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Edward Reynolds Foreman
