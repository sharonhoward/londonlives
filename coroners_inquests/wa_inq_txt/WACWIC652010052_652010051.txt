Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Twenty frist day of August in the Frist year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one . Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid, Upon a View of the Body of George Dewick then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of Walter Tyrrell , William Key , Marshall Fleet , Emanuel Lloyd James Burden , Thomas Purdue , Peter Bunn , James James , Silas Paine , John Euston , John Jacobi , John Osborn , and Samuel Waddington Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said George Dewick came to his Death say upon their Oaths, That the said George Dewick on the Eighteenth day of August Instant in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Liberty and County aforesaid to wit at a certain place called the Point at the Neat Houses on Millbank , there, to Swim and Bath himself in the River of Thames , it so happened that accidentally, casually and by Misfortune he the said George Dewick was in the Waters of the said River then and there suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said George Dewick then and there instantly Died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said George Dewick in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition put their hands and Seals the day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Jno Feary Coroner .

Walter Tyrrell Foreman
