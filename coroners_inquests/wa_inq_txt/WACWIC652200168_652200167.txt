City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquitition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of April in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Dillon an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Leonard Clow , William Ambler , Jacob Knight , William Newton , John Lewis , Robert Bray , William Pasmore , John Hewlett , John Gale , Matthew Turner , Samuel Daniel , and William Sutton good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Dillon came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say ,That the said Elizabeth Dillon the Infant on the Sixth day of April in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, being Card to Sleep in a certain turnup Bed then and there being in the Room of the dwelling House of Thomas Dillon Father of the said Elizabeth Dillon the Infant, And the said Thomas Dillon coming into the said Room, and not knowing that the said Elizabeth Dillon the Infant was then and there sleep or lying in the said Bed, It so happened that the said Thomas Dillon Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, then and there innocently turned up the said Bed, by Means whereof she the said Elizabeth Dillon the Infant, in the Cloaths of the said Bed was then and there suffocated and Smothered, of which said Suffocation and Smothering she the said Elizabeth Dillon then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Elizabeth Dillon the Infant in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Leonard Clow Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner Leod: Clone Foreman
