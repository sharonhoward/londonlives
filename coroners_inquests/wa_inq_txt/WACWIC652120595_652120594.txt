City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty second day of August in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Aaron George then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Youngton , James Young Junior, David Woodley , Thomas Newman , John Wilson , Thomas Midwinter , David Rees , Edward Jones , John Southby , William Potter , Acquilla Kirby , Samuel Dark , William Gardner , and Thomas Rumford good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Aaron George come to h is Death, do upon their Oath say

That the said Aaron George on the first day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of St, Martin in the Fields in the Liberty and County aforesaid being employed as a Labourer at the Queens New Library at the Queens House in St. James's Park in the said Parish Liberty and County and being on a Ladder set up to and against the said Library it so happened that accidentally casually and by Misfortune a Stone of near Two hundred Pounds Weight fell from off the Shoulders of a Man then on the top of the said Ladder and then and thereby hit and struck the said Aaron George in his Head by means whereof he Received a Violent and Mortal Bruise and Concussion on the Head of him the said Aaron George of which said Violent and Mortal Bruise and Concussion he the said Aaron George from the said first day of August in the Year aforesaid until the twenty second day of the same Month in the same Year at the Parish aforesaid and also at the Parish of St. Margaret in the Liberty and County aforesaid (to wit) in the Westminster Hospital in James Street did languish and languishing did Live which said twenty second day of August in the Year aforesaid he the said Aaron George of the violent and Mortal Bruise and Concussion aforesaid at the Hospital in the said Parish of St. Margaret in the Liberty and County aforesaid did Die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Aaron George in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. And the said Jurors on their Oath further say, that the said Stone was Moving to the Death of him the said Aaron George and is of the value of One Shilling and is the property and in the Possession of John Devoll of the Parish of Saint Mary le bone in the said County of Middlesex , or of his Assigns. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands, and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

James Young Foreman
