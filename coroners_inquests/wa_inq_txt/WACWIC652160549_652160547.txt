City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition intended for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Thirtieth Day of December in the Seventeenth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of Lord of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, On View of the Body of George Keith Gentleman then and there lying dead upon the Oath of Henry Wilson , Thomas Field , Peter Cockran , Thomas Scott , Edward Taylor , Andrew Rogers , James Mason , Anthony Parham , John Storr , John Noble , John Burgess  Abraham Probart and William Michel good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said George Keith came to his Death, doupon their Oath say That Bennet Storer Clerk late of the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid on the Twenty eighth day of December in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said George Keith in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously did make an Assault, And that the said Bennet Storer a certain Pistol of the Value of five shillings charged and loaded with Gunpowder and a leaden Bullet which he the said Bennet Storer then and there had and held in his Right Hand, to and against the Body of him the said George Keith did then and there shoot off and discharge, by Means whereof he the said Bennet Storer did then and there feloniously gvie unto him the said George Keith with the leaden Bullet aforesaid so as aforesaid shot off and discharged out of the Pistol aforesaid by the force of the Gunpowder aforesaid, in and through the Body on the Right side of the Belly of him the said George Keith one mortal Wound, of which said mortal Wound he the said George Keith at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Twenty eighth day of December in the Year aforesaid until the Twentyninth day of the same Month in the same Year did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty ninth day of December in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said George Keith of the mortal Wound aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Bennet Storer him the said, George Keith in Manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously did kill and slay, against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. And that the said Bennet Storer at the time of doing and committing the Felony and Manslaughter aforesaid, or at any time since, had no Goods or Chattals Lands or Tenements within the said Liberty or else where to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Henry Wilson Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner Henry Wilson Foreman
