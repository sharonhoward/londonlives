City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Mr. Jones Sign of the Compass Chelsea parish of St. George  Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty sixth day of March 1794 in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq . Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of a female Infant Child, Margaret Hunt then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are hereunder written and Seals affixed good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Female Child came to her Death, do upon their Oath say that the Said Female Child on the twenty fourth day of March in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the liberty aforesaid To Wit in the High Read leading from Chelsea to Buckingham House , there being It so happened (that accidentally casually and by Misfortune) that as William Gape on Hackney Coachman was diving his Master's Coach(-Mariott ) one of the horses struck the said female Child to and against the Ground by means as whereof she the said Female Child received a mortal Wound or its head of which said Mortal wound she the said Female Child then and there instantly died: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said female Infant Child in Manner and the Means aforesaid accidentally casually & by Misfortune came to her Death & not otherwise that the said Horse was the came of her Death and is of the Value of One Shilling. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their hands & Seals the day Year and Place first above written

Anthy Gell Coroner } Saml. Windle For man James Chamberlin Fields Tombling Thos Younger Robert Meredith Wm Fenn Wm Shepherd Henry Sutton Tho Reynoldson Rd Waghorn Fras Lye
