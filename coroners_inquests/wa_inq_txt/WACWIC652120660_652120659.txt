City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of September in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Bransford then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Mason , Robert Holland , John Wilson , John Green , John Young , Joseph Higgs , John Stainer , James Jeffreys , Nicholas Preston , William Bridge , John Howell , and Thomas Webb good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Bransford came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Bransford a Painter on the Eighth day of September in the year aforesaid being at Work with another Man Upon the Skylight in the dwelling House of Governor Elletson situate in Curzon Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there standing upon a Scaffold in the Stair case near the said Skylight, It so happened that the said James Bransford then and there accidentally casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said Scaffold through the well Stair case unto the bottom of the Stairs, and thereby received one mortal Fracture in and upon the back part of his Head, and divers Bruises in and upon his Body, of which said mortal Fracture and Bruises he the said James Bransford then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Bransford in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Mason Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands, and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm. Mason Foreman
