City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fifth day of August, in the Fourth Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Robert Cottingham then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Sugg , William Murray , John Morrice , William Kees , John Austin , Abraham Naylor , Allen Burnham , Richard Herring , Edward Williams , George Hewit  John Rosier and Thomas Waller , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to inquire for Our said Lord the do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty Fourthday of August in the Year aforesaid, the said Robert Cottingham, a Bricklayer, being at Work in a certain New built House belonging to the Right Honourable the Lady Carpenter situate in Great George Street in the Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, And the said Robert Cottingham being then and there standing upon a Ladder by the side of a Window, (which said Window was open and not Sashed,) It so happened that the said Ladder, on which the said Robert Cottingham stood, slipp on one side, and that the said Robert Cottingham then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell out of and through the said Window, being two Stories high, into the backyard belonging to the said House, which said backyard was paved with Stones, by Means where of the said Robert Cottingham by the fall aforesaid, did then and there receive on Mortal Fracture on the left side of his Head, of which said Mortal Fracture he the said Robert Cottingham the and there instantly died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Robert Cottingham , in manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually an by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Jugg Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of the said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals , the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner . Jno Sugg Foreman
