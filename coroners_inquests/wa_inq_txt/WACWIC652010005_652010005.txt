Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of Saint Peter at Westminster the Twenty first day of January in the First Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven Hundred and Sixty one . Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid. Upon a view of the Body of John Chambers then and there Lying Dead, by the Oaths of Richard Eames ,. Jarvis Wells , Joseph Clark , James Freeman , Fean Taylor , Peter Fribourg , David Nelson , Daniel Wood , Charles Fowlen , Thomas Jordan , William Simpson John Westen , Francis White , George Owen , and Hugh Hopes Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Inquire for our Sovereign Lord the King, how when where, and in what manner the said John Chambers , come to his Death, say upon their Oath. That the said John Chambers not being of Sound Mind, Memory and Understanding, but Lunatick and Distracted either on the thirteenth of Fourteenth day of January Instant in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid one end of a certain peice of small Cord unto on Iron Hook fastned into on upright in the Lodging Room or Apartment of him the said John Chambers in the Dwelling House of Thomas Broughton situate in Wind mill Street in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid and the other end thereof fastned said Handkercheif about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten and therewith did then and there Hang Suffocate and Strangle himself of which said Hanging, Suffocation, and Strangling he the said John Chambers then and there Died, and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oaths aforesaid do say That the said John Chambers not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid come to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition put their hands and Seals the day Year and at the Place abovesaid.

Jno. Feary  Coroner

Richard Emes Forman
