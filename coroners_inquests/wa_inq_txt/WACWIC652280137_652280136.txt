City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the eighth day of March in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Edward Mac Ginnes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Lackington , John Strong , John Savigney Jenkins Depeke , Cockham Steward , Jacob Watson Richard Devonport , John Mills , George Smith , Thomas Carney , Thomas Hill & George Knowls , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Edward Mc. Ginnis came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Edward Mac Ginnis on the said Eighth day of March in the Year aforesaid being at Work at Carlton House in the said Parish of St. James within the Liberty and County aforesaid and standing upon a Scaffold near the Roof of one of the Garretts of the said House It so happened that the said Edward Mac Ginnis Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Scaffold, upon a certain Piece of Timber lying upon the first Floor of the said House at the Height of thirty five Feet as thereby then and there received a mortal Wound and Fracture in and upon the bach part of the Head of him the said Edward Mac Ginnis of which said mortal Wound and Fracture he the said Edward Mac Ginnis then and there Instantly died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Edward Mac Ginnis in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Lackington Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Tho. Luckington Foreman
