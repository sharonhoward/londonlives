City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint James Westminster with in the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty sixth of September 1792 in the Thirty Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France,and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq.  Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Matis then and there lying, dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Boot , William Halson , Anthony Ridler , John Williams James Thompson , William Haines , John Stoples , Folliott Clarke , Gayen Rogers , Lewis Gutteridge , Joseph Evans , Anthony Hooper , Thomas Booren , Robert Greinson , Robert Young , and John Hornsby good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ann Mortis came to her Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Ann Mortis on the twenty first day of September, in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid, being Much intoxicated by excessive, drinking It so happened, that, accidentally, casually, and by Misfortune, she the said Ann Martis fell several times to and against the Ground, by Means whereof she the said Ann Martis, being Intoxicated as aforesaid, received divers Mortal Wounds and Bruises on the Body and Head of her the said Ann Martis, fron which said twenty first day of September untill the twenty fourth day of the same Month in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid John at a certain Infirmary there situate called Saint Jamess Infirmary of the Mortal Wounds and Bruises aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live untill the said twenty fourth day September On which said twenty fourth day of September in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Writ at the Infirmary aforesaid of the said Mortal Wounds and Bruises aforesaid did die And to the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Ann Matis in Manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally. casually and by MIsfortune, came to her Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Boll  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and there of his said Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their hands & Seals the Day, Year & Place abovementioned Anthy Gell  Coroner Thomas Boot  Foreman
