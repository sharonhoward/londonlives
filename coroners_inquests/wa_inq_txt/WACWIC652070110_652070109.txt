City & Liberty of Westmr . in the County of Middlesex To wit.  

 An Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twelfth Day of March in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & Liberty on View of the Body of Henry Jones then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Toulmin , John Baylis , Robert Knight , John Blackett , George Snowball , Francis Gowan , Morgan Evans , James Clark , John Jones , James Macklane , William Townshend , Edward Bullock , Robert Berry and William Clement good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said Henry Jones came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Henry Jones , not being of sound mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Twelfth day of March in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain piece of small Cord unto a Wooden Bar fixed in an Iron Grate, over the Cellar of William Duffin , situate and being in Bull Inn Court, in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck, did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang, suffocate, and strangle himself, of which said Hanging, Suffocation, and Strangling, he the said Henry Jones then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Henry Jones , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Toulmin Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner Thos: Toulmin Foreman
