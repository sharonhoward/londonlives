City of Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fifth day of June in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Good then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Wignall , John Price , Daniel Corney John Farmer , James Patterson , Thomas Hall , John White Joseph Pyburs , John Blackburn , John Keeble , Henry Fry James Bell and John Twedell good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Good came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Thomas Good on the fourth day of June in the Year aforesaid (being his Majesty's Brithday) was firing off certain small Guns on Milbank Row in the said Parish of St. John the Evangelist within the Liberty and County aforesaid, being charged with Powder, It so happened that one of the said Guns Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune burst, and that one piece of the said Gun struck against the right Breast of the said Thomas Good , whereby the said Thomas Good then and there received one mortal Wound and Contusion in and upon his right breast, of which said mortal Wound and Contusion he the said Thomas Good then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Good in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Thomas Wignall  Foreman of the Said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos. Wignall Foreman
