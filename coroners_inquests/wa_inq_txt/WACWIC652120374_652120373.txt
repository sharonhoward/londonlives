City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of June in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Barry then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Alisbury , William Kendall , John Hermon , John Cunningham , Robert Archbutt , John Daylie , Thomas Hawarth , Thomas Wilton , Joseph Spencer  Charles Kelly , Richard Lane , Charles Brunsdon , Daniel Mc. Gregor , Elizah Russell and Thomas Winteland good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Barry came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Joseph Barry on the Ninth day of June in the Year aforesaid being upon the top of a certain Building belonging to Charles Shebbs Staples keeper situate in Mason's Yard in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said Joseph Barry Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Building upon the Stone Pavement in Mason's Yard aforesaid, and thereby did then and there receive one mortal Wound in and upon the back part of his Head, and a large Contusion upon his Back and a Fracture of One of his Ribs on the Right side, of which said Wound Contusion and Fracture he the said Joseph Barry then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Joseph Barry Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to his death. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Alisbury Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Alisbury Foreman
