City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex ,} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Tenth day of February in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the Said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Bailey then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Jeremaih Church , George Gunnery , William Masters , William Savill  Robert Mims Ephraim Woods , Samuel Ray  John Snell , Esau Alfred , Thomas George Richard Herring and Joseph James good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Bailey came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Bailey on the Ninth day of February in the Year aforesaid being sliding upon the Canal in St. James's Park in the Parish and County aforesaid, It so happened that the Tee broke, and that the said James Bailey then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell into the said Canal, and in the Waters thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned ,of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said James Bailey then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Bailey , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jeremiah Church Foreman of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Jeremiah Church Foreman
