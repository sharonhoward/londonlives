City and Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex } to Wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty ninth day of December in the Fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so froth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of a New born Male Child then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Boyle  Matthew Towns , William Walkden , John Edwards , Jacob Watson , John Thomas , Thomas Jones , Patrick Collins , Thomas Falconer , Thomas Ford , John Muller , John Burnet , John Miller , John Heathcourt , Robert Robinson and Gilbert Eggleson good and lawfull Men of the said Leberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when how and by what Means the said Male Child came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty seventh day of December in the Year aforesaid, the said Male Child was found in a certain Publick Street, called little Warwick Street in the said Parish of St. James , within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a certain Woman to the Jurors aforesaid unknown, on the said Twenty seventh day of December in the year aforesaid, did bring forth the said Male Child, And that the said Male Child, not having any Nails on the fingers, or Hair on the Head of him the said Male Child and not being come to Maturity, was not born alive, No Marks of Violence appearing on the said Male Child. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Male Child, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid was born Dead In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Boyle Foreman of the said Jurors, on the bahalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

John Boyle Foreman
