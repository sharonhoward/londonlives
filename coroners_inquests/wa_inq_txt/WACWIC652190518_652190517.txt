City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex . } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of November in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Edridge then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Benjamin Coates , William McDowel ., Charles Grooby , Merry Wilson , Christopher Warton , William Greenhaugh  Charles Saxon , James Baker  James Hooper  John Greenhaugh  Ralph Walker Benjamin Laver and John White good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and changed to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what by Means the said John Edridge came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Edridge not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted on the First day of November in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, One End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto a Piece of Wood fastened into the Brickwall in the Stable of him the said John Edridge situate and being in Bird Street in the said Parish Liberty and County and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle himself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said John Edridge then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Edridge not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Benjamin Coates Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Ben, Coates Foreman
