City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of December in the Thirty Second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Stock then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John Noble , James Green , Robert Southby , William Hawkins , Joseph Hollingworth , James Sewell , Timothy Butler , Edward book , John Way , Thomas applebee , Thomas Huggins and Henry albon good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Stock came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Eleventh day of December in the Year aforesaid the said James Stock was skating upon the Canal in St. James's Park in the said Parish of St. Margaret Westminster in the County aforesaid, It so happened that the Ice broke and that the said James Stock Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell into the said Canal and in the Waters thereof was suffocated and drowned of which said Suffocation and drowning he the said James Stock on the same day did die at the Parish of St. Margaret Westminster aforesaid And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said James Stock in Manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Noble  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard Coroner John Noble Foreman
