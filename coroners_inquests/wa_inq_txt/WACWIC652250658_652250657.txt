An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at Tothill Fields Bridewell in the Parish of St. Margaret in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of December in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Sharp a Prisoner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Pearse , Samuel Prestreidge , James Hendre , William Fisher , Samuel Cannon , Samuel Carter , William Seruce , Charles Copp , Thomas Dewick , Thomas Harrison , William Allen , and Charles Carey good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Sharp came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Sharp being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields Bridewell aforesaid, in the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Tenth day of December in the year aforesaid, at Tothill Fields  Bridewell aforesaid, departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural way. To wit, of a Fever; and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Thomas Pearse Foreman of the said Jurors, on the Behalf of himself and the Rest of his Fellows, in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands, and Seals the Day Year, and Place above mentioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos Pearse Foreman
