City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

In Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the First day of March in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Woodridge then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Peter Osborne , Thomas Kaygill, Samuel Evers , John Duffield , Thomas Sheffield , Joseph Wyld , John Gridon William Carr , John Large , John Bass , John Arnold and Peter Copland good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ann Woodridge came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That on the twenty seventh day of February in the year aforesaid the said Ann Woodridge being in the House of John Martin situate in Doser Street in the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and being upon the Ritchen Stairs. It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the said Ann Woodridge fell down the said Kitchen Stairs unto the Bottom thereof by means whereof the said Ann Woodridge did then and receive one mortal Wound and Fracture in and upon the left side of her Head, of which said mortal Wound and Fracture she the said Ann Woodridge then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Ann Woodridge in manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise,

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Peter Osborn Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Peter Oborns Foreman
