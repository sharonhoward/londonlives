City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of March in the Thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Catherine Standfield then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of  Richard, Holloway , Joseph Fox , Francis Baron , Thomas Pitter , Thomas Sherborn , Benjamin Aked , Thomas Gray , Thomas Goodhall , Henry Mongford , Robert Withy , Thomas Lea , Michael Burk William Payne and John Waller good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Catherine Standfield came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Ninth day of March in the year aforesaid the said Catherine Standfield being alone in her apartment in the Dwelling House of William Standfield situate in the Strand in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and sitting at a Table with a lighted Candle thereon, It so happened that accidentally Casually and by Misfortune the Cloaths which the said Catherine Standfield had on her Body took fire by means whereof the said Catherine Standfield was then and there violently burnt in and upon her Right arm Breast and Neck of which said burning she the said Catherine Standfield at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said Ninth day of March in the Year aforesaid until the Tenth day of the same Month did languish and live on which said Tenth day of March in the Year aforesaid she the said Catherine Standfield at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say that the said Catherine Standfield in manner and by the means aforesaid accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Richard Holloway  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands & Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Richard Holloway Foreman
