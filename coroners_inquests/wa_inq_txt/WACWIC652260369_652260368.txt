City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fourth day of June in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Ferguson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Daniel Luckhurst  Peter Fozard , John Collier , James Forbes , Francis Daneyer , James Davis  John Baker , William Fryer , Charles Gade , John Stephens , Thomas Moses , and Thomas Shankster , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Ferguson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Ferguson a Carpenter on the first day of June in the Year aforesaid,being at Work, in repairing an Empty House in Arlington Street in the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid It so happened that a certain piece of Timber then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down upon the Head of him the said John Ferguson by reason whereof the said John Ferguson did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive a Mortal Wound in and upon the Right side of the Head, and divers Bruises in the Body of him the said John Ferguson of which said Mortal Wound and Bruises he the said John Ferguson from the said first day of June in the Year aforesaid, until the twenty third day of the same Month and Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and Languishing did live on which said twenty third day of June in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said John Ferguson of the Mortal Wound and Bruises aforesaid, did die; And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said John Ferguson in manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Daniel Luckhurst  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Danl: Luckhurst Foreman
