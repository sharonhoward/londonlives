City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of January in the twenty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of a new born Female Child then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Archer , John Moor , John Greenwood , John Dixon , Thomas Honeyman , John Anthony , Joseph Hill , George King , Thomas Cummins , Giles White , Robert Hinksman , Thomas Jones , William Sands , Samuel Galispy , John Ruffin and Charles Gibson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Female Child came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said new born Female Child on the Third day of January in the Year aforesaid was found dead by the side of the River Thames in a certain Place at the bottom of Charles Court in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, That no Marks of violence appeared on the Body, and that the said Female Child was dead born.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Robert Archer  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Robt Arch Foreman
