City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twelfth day of December in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Benjamin Markham then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Burgan , Peter Osborn , William Sibery John Watson , John Wansell , John Keepling , James Hudson , Robert Spratt  Thomas Bailey , Richard Clark  William Sheats , and Michael Bryan good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Benjamin Markham came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Benjamin Markham a Bricklayer on the tenth day of December in the Year aforesaid being at Work at the House of Thomas Wyatt situate in Upper Mount Street in the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid and standing upon a Ladder repairing the Tiling on the Roof of the said House It so happened that the said Benjamin Markham Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said Ladder upon the stone step at the back dove of the said house and thereby then and there did receive one mortal Wound and Fracture in and upon the back part of the Head of him the said Benjamin Markham of which said mortal Wound and Fracture he the said Benjamin Markham then and there instantly died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Benjamin Markham in manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Benjamin Markham Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Jno Burgan  Foreman
