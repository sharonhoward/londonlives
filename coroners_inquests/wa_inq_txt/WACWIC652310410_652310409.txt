City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex ,} to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Sixth day of August in the Thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner , of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Simpson then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of John Salt , James Pagan , John Cosby , John Jones William Geldard , Charles Walpole , Samuel Hinders, Robert Meredith , Henry List , William Fenn , Francis Newstead and Thomas Kirst good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there lying duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Simpson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Richard Simpson not being in, sound mind memory and Understanding but Lunatic and distracted on the Twenty second day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish of Putney in the County of Surry with a certain Razor made of Iron and Steel which he the said Richard Simpson then and there had and held in his right Hand The Throat or Gullet of him the said Richard Simpson did then and there strike stab and penetrate thereby then and there giving into himself with the Rason aforesaid one mortal wound of the length of Three Inches and of the Depth of one Inch of which said Mortal wound he the said Richard Simpson at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the Twenty day of August in the Year aforesaid untill the Twenty fifth day of the same month and Year did lauguish and languishing did live on which said Twenty fifth Day of August in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St.George Hanover Square he the said Richard Simpson of the Mortal Wound aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Richard Simpson not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and distracted in Manner and by the means aforesaid did Kill himself

 In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said John Salf Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to to their Inquisition oct their Hands and Seals the day Year and place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard  Coroner John Salt  Foreman
