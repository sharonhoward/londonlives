City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex .} to wit, An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Dwelling House of William Hall in Ranelagh Row Chelsea in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square called the union coffee house within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty third day of November 1792 in the thirty third Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq.  Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Hussey then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Henry Sumpter , John Newins , Richard Popple , James Daly , Jacob Woodward , John Pagler , Benjamin Hillingbeck , John Hutton , Joseph Rogers , Joseph Hood , Richard Waghorn , Henry Howard and John Adams good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Hussey came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, that the said Mary Hussey on the twenty first day of November in the year aforesaid, at the Parish and in the liberty aforesaid was then and there suddenly seized with an Apoplectie Fit And that on the said Twenty first day of November, in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid, she the said Mary Houssey departed this Life, by the visitation of God in a natural way To Wit of the said apopletic Fit.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day year and Place first above written

Anthy Gell  Coroner Henry sumpter  foreman John Nowene Richd Popple James Daly
