City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex }to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Second day of August in the Fourteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Patrick Cullen then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Beckett William Dean , John Appleby , Cuthbert Smith , Reece Williams , Thomas Porrin , William Leastrange , Thomas Harwood , Thomas Hedges , John Russell , John Wallis , and Malcolm Mackfarson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Patrick Cullen came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Patrick Cullen not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and Distracted on the Twenty first day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Dwelling House of Richard English Victualler known by the Sign of the Wheatsheaf the Cornn of Exton Street Publics there situate, with a certain Knife made of Iron and Steal which he the said Patrick Cullen then and there had and held in his Right Hand the Throat or Gullet of him the said Patrick Cullen did then and there Strike slab and penetrab thereby then and there giving into himself the said Patrick Cullen with the Knife aforesaid in and upon the Threat or Gullet of him said Patrick Cullen one Mortal Wound of the length of three inches and of the depth of one inch, of which said Mortal Wound he the said Patrick Cullen then and there instantly Deed. And so the Jurors aforesaid their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Patrick Cullen not being of Lound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and Distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did Kill himself. In Witness where of as will the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Tho Prichard Coroner Beckett Foreman
