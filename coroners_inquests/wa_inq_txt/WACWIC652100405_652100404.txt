City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of August in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Nicholas Foord then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Fynmore Thomas Robinson , Thomas Holland , John Forster , James Sewell , George Ballard , Laughlin Sinclair , Thomas Bail David Owen , Hugh Matthews , James Cuddan , Matthew Dewley , Giles Burton , Thomas Adams , William Farmer , Thomas Turner , John Colliban , James Bellis  Nathan Hirst , Thomas Jessling , Robert and Charles Finlow , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquired for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Nicholas Foord came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Nicholas Foord not being of Sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatic and Distracted on the Thirteenth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty aforesaid, a certain Pistol charged with Gunpowder and a Leaden Bullet which he the said Nicholas Foord then and there had and held in his Right Hand to and against the Head of him the said Nicholas Foord did then and there Shoot off and Discharge by means whereof he the said Nicholas Foord did then and there give unto himself with the Leaden Bullet aforesaid so Discharged and shot out of the Pistol aforesaid, by the force of the Gunpowder aforesaid in and upon the Head of him the said Nicholas Foord One Mortal Wound and Fracture of the Breadth of one Inch and of the Depth of three Inches of which said Mortal Wound and Fracture he the said Nicholas Foord then and there instantly Died.

And to the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Nicholas Foord not being of Sound Mind Money and Understanding, but Lunatic and Distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did Kill himself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place above mentioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

James Fynmore Foreman
