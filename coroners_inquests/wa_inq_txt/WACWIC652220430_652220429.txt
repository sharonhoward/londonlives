City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the First day of November in the twenty third Year of Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great, Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Peter Holten then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Ivison , John Brock , Jasper Smith  Thomas Masters , James Linton  James Giblett  John Ward , Henry Mc.Laughlin Thomas Gilbert  George Churchman  James Piper  Thomas Weston and George Saunderson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, When, how, and by what Means the said Peter Holten came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Peter Holten not being of sound Mind, Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the said First day of November in the Year aforesaid a certain Piece of small Cord unto a Wooden Joyst in a certain Neccessary House or Privy situate in South molton Lane in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and Strangle himself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said Peter Holten then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Peter Holten, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Ivison  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Ivison Foreman
