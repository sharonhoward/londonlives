City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of November in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ann Tompson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Stubbings , Charles Scott , William Clarke , William Curteis , Joseph Manlove , George Clarke , Spencer Draper , Reuben Richardson , John Ducket , Thomas Simmons , Miles Roldonson & John Pickering good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ann Tompson came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Ann Tompson on the Fourteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid, in going down the Kitchen Stairs in the dwelling House of Peter Fransom situate in Chapel Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down part of the said Stairs upto the bottom thereof, and thereby then and there received one mortal Wound and Confusion in and upon the left side of her Head, of which said mortal Wound and Confusion she the said Ann Tompson then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Ann Tompson in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death, and not otherwise. In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said John Stubbings Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prichard Coroner

John. Stubbing's Foreman
