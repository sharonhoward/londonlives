City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex , to Wit}  An Inquisition Indented for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of March in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on view of the Body of John Gold then and there lying Dead upon the Oath of John Doughty , Henry Friday , James Dunn , John Darippey , Samuel Webb , Robert Bray , John Banks , Silvester Freeman , William Whitehead George Hewett , Robert Gildon , and John Lomus good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what means the said John Gold Came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That, the said John Gold on the first day of March (at Night) in the Year aforesaid, being walking in a certain Place called three boney Walk, in the Parish of St. Mary Lambeth , in the County of Surry, It so happened that the said John Gold Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did then and there fall upon and over a quantity of Bricks and Rubbish, being part of an old Brick Wall, lying in the footway, by reason whereof he the said John Gold did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the Right Arm of him the said John Gold near his Body, of which said mortal Fracture he the said John Gold from the said first day of March in the Year aforesaid, until the Seventh day of the same Month and Year, at the Westminster Hospital , in the Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Seventh day of March in the Year aforesaid, he the said John Gold at the Hospital aforesaid , of the said Mortal Fracture, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said John Gold in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Doughty Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho Prickard Coroner

Jno Doughty Foreman
