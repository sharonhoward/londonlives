City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegite Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of March in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Samuel Newth then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Evans , Edward Hewitt , Robert Hare , John Cornish , Francis Jones , John Harrison , Spencer Draper , William Chandless , John Hinton , John Farrendon , Francis Muxworthy and Richard Aslat good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Samuel Newth came to h is Death, do upon their Oath Say, That Hannah Farmer late of the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid Spinster, on the Fourth day of February in the Year aforesaid, with Force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, in and upon the Said Samuel Newth in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously did make an Assault; And that the said Hannah Farmer with a certain Case Knife made of Iron and Steel of the Value of Six pence which she the said Hannah Farmer then and there had and held in her Right Hand him the said Samuel Newth in an upon the left side of the Body near the hipbone of him the said Samuel Newth did then and there feloniously strike stab and penetrate, And that the said Hannah Farmer by the striking and stabbing aforesaid did then and there give unto him the said Samuel Newth in and upon the leftside of the Body near the hipbone, with the Knife aforesaid one mortal Wound of the breath of one Inch and of the Depth of three Inches, of which said mortal Wound he the said, Samuel Newth from the Said Fourth day of February in the Year aforesaid until the Fifth day of March in the same Year, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Fifth day of March in the Year aforesaid he the said Samuel Newth at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid of the mortal Wound aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said Hannah Farmer him the Said Samuel Newth in Manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously did kill and slay against of the Place of our said the King his Crown and Dignity And that the said Hannah Farmer at the time of the doing and committing of the Felony and Manslaughter aforesaid, or at any time since, had no goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements within the said Liberty or elsewhere, to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Richard Evans Foreman of the the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Richd. Evans Foreman
