City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Seventh day of May in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Iles then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Squibb , Thomas Lightfoot , Morris Crawford , Robert Webb , Edward Clemson Richard Barnett , James Erwin , John Bonnell , Richard Shaw , Richard Collington , John Stephens and William Allen , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Iles came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Iles a Bricklayer on the Twenty sixth day of May in the Year aforesaid being at work on the Outside of the dwelling House of John Yorke Esquire Situate in Berkley Square in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there Pointing the Gable End at the top of the said House, It so happened that the said John Iles Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the Top of the said House unto the Ground, by Means where of the said John Iles then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did receive divers Fractures and Bruises in and upon his Head and Body, of which said mortal Fractures and Bruises he the said John Iles then and there instantly died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Iles in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Squibb Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellow, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

James Squibb Foreman
