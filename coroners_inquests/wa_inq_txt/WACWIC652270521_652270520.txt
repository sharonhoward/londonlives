City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.}  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty ninth day of November in the twenty eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Wellings then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Waldring , Thomas Parting , George Tiperton William Williams , Richard Weetherall , Thomas Thompson John Grist , Thomas Collin , James Smith , George Young , William Armsteed & Isaac Roe good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said Mary Wellings came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Mary Wellingsan Infant Viz of the Age of four Years on the twenty seventh day of November in the year aforesaid being sitting near the Fire in the Lodging Room of John Wellings her Father in Old Tye Street in the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist within the Liberty and County aforesaid It so happened that the Linen Cloaths which the said Mary Wellings the Infant had on her Body Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune took fire by means whereof the said Mary Wellings was then and there burnt in and upon her Belly Breast Neck Arms and Thighs of which said burning she the said Mary Wellings at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said twenty seventh day of November in the Year aforesaid till the twenty eighth day of the same month did languish and live on which said twenty eighth day of November in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of Saint Margaret she the said Mary Wellings of the Mortal burning aforesaid did die and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid to say that the said Mary Wellings the Infant in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Waldring Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Richard Coroner

John Waldring Foreman
