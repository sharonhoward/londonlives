City and Libety of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of June in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Harrison then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Harris  John Blatch , William Green  Simon Syfrett , John Stowers  Gabriel Percy  Samuel Kemp  John Nicholson  David Jones  John Bunt , John Mollineux , Thomas Hyett  Thomas White  Joseph Allen  Abraham Terry , Stephen Evans and Timothy Chill good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said John Harrison came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John Harrison a Carpenter on the thirteenth day of June in the year aforesaid being at work in the workshop of John Edwards Coach Painter (two Stories high) situate in Vine Street in the Parish of St James within the Liberty and County aforesaid and then and there standing upon a Ladder near the Felding doors of the said Shop which said Doors were open, It so happend that the said John Harrison did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fall off and from the said Ladder into the said Street, and thereby did receive a mortal Fracture in and upon the Fore part of the Head of him the said John Harrison of which said Mortal Fracture he the said John Harrison did languish until the Fourteenth day of the same Month and Year at St George's Hospital situate in the Parish of St. George Hanover square with the Liberty and County aforesaid and then and there of the Mortal Fracture aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Harrison in Manner and by the Means aforesaid accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Edward Harris Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

Edwd Harris Foreman
