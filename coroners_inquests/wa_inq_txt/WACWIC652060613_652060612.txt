City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fifth Day of November in the Seventh year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of a New born Male Child then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Austin John Shaw , James Keys , Samuel Highley , Thomas Stanley , John Waldron , James Demeid , Robert Wright , Francis Norris , Silas Payne , Thomas Mason , and John Taylor good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our Sovereign Lord the King, when, how and by what Means the said New born Male Child came to his Death, do upon their Oath says That the said Newborn Male child on the Twenty fifth day of November in the Year aforesaid, was found dead in an old Wig Box upon the Stone steps leading to the Parish Church of St. John the Evangelist , in the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, That no Marks of Violence appeared on the said New born Male Child and that the said Male Child was still Born. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John Austin Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovewritten.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner . John Austin Foreman
