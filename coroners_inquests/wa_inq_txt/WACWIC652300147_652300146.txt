City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of March in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Whetstone then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Jefferies , Thomas More , Thomas Jackson , Henry Norton , Joseph Worner , William Grant , John Floyd , Thomas Hogeson , John King , Philip Thomas , Allen Reed and Abraham Miller good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Whetstone came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Whetstone an Infant of the Age of Nine Years, on the Seventh day of March in the Year aforesaid being at Play with John Randyck another Infant of about the Age of Nine Years in the Publick Street called St. Albans Street , in the Parish of Saint James, within the Liberty and County aforesaid and the said John Randyck the Infant being then and these in play, wantonly and without Judgment parrying with a certain piece of Iron, commonly used for engraving Pewter Pots, which he the said John Whetstone had in his Hand to and against the said Thomas Whetstone, It so happened that the said Thomas Whetstone Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of him the said John Randyek did then and these receive one mortal Wound in and upon the Back of him the said Thomas Whetstone of which said mortal Wound he the said Thomas Whetstone from the said Seventh day of March in the Year aforesaid until the Eighth day of the same Month in the same Year at the said Parish of Saint James, and also at the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, On which said Eighth day of March in the Year aforesaid at the Parish last mentioned he the said Thomas Whetstone the Infant of the mortal Wound aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Thomas Whetstone in Manner and by the means aforesaid, Casually and by Misfortune and against the Will of him the said John Randyek the Infant came to his Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Jeffries  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Jefferies Foreman
