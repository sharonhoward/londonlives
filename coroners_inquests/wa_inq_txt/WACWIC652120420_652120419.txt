City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Pater, Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty second day of June in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Harris and Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Smith , Robert Walker , John Tubb , Richard Pratt , John Bower , James Lawrence , James Topscott , Andrew Richier , George Briant , George Hewitson , David Watson , Francis Cooper Thomas Douglas , John Srisier , Samuel Bradford , Roger Flewett and John Burroughs good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Harris came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That the said Harris the Infant, to wit, of the Age of three Years, on the Nineteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid being in the Room or Apartment of Richard Harris his Father on the First Floor in the dwelling House of Walter Head Bannister , situate in Ryder Court in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and on hearing a Noise in the said Court, the said John Harris the Infant then and there got upon a Chair near one of the Windows in the said Room, and looking out (the lower sash of such Window being up) It so happened that the said John Harris then and there Accidetally Casually and by Misfortune fell out off and through the said Window in the said Court, upon the Stone Pavement, and thereby did then and there receive one mortal Concussion in and upon the Brain of the said John Harris of which said mortal Concussion the said John Harris at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, from the said Nineteenth day of June in the Year, aforesaid until the Twentieth day of the same Month and Year, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twentieth day of June aforesaid, at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said John Harris of the mortal Concussion aforesaid, did dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Harris the Infant, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward Smith Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals, the day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Edwd Smith Foreman
