City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King. at the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty first day of December in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Manwaring then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Howard , Samuel Potts , Stephen Goddard , Joseph Palmer , William Yarnold John Tapps , Vincent Hinton , Thomas Dobson , Joseph Edmond , Walter Williams , William Adams & Stephen Joyce good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire of our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Manwaring came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Manwaring , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted on the Twentieth day of December in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto the Wooden side Rail of the Bed in the Lodging Room or Apartment of him the said James Manwaring in the Dwelling House of Mary Robinson situate and being in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Nock did fix tye and therewith did then and there hang suffocated and stangle himself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said James Manwaring then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said James Manwaring , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Howard Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Thos Howard Foreman
