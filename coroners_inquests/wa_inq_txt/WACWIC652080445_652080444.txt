City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Clement Danes within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of October in the Eighth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Raine then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Birks , John Catmore , John Cribb , Joshua Trimble , James Taff , Thomas Magrath , John Whitfield , John Hadley , Charles Taylor , Edward Brankinton , John Miller , Christopher Gumm  John Morris  Charles Wyatt , John Smith , Hugh Atkinson and Richard Phillips good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Raine came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Richard Raine not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Eleventh day of October in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, in the Dwelling House of Hannah Poole there situate, with a certain Case Knife made of Iron and Steel, which he the said Richard Raine then and there had and held in his Right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said Richard Raine did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself the said Richard Raine, with the Knife aforesaid, in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said Richard Raine one mortal Wound of the Length of three Inch and of the depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound he the said Richard Raine then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Richard Raine, not being of of sound Mind Memory and Under lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means of himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner William Birks Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm. Birks Foreman
