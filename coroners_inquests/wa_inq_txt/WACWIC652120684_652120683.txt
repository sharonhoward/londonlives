City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

 An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of September in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Pearson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Teess Campbell , George Goodall , William Piper , Benjamin Moore , William Parmar , Nathaniel Jackson , John Vernon , John Collins , William Vallett , James Halsey , Abraham Webber , Nathaniel Silverthorn and John Lewis , Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Pearson came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Pearson , laboring under agrievous Disease of Body, to Wit, the Venereal Disease, and by reason of the violence of the said Disease, being delirious and out of his Mind, on the Thirteenth day of September in the Year aforesaid one End of a certain piece of thick Cord unto a Wooden Beam in the Hayloft over the Stable of Roger Hope Elletson Esquire situate in Pitts Head Mews, in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there Hang Suffocate and Strangle himself of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said William Pearson then and there died: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Pearson , by the violence of the Disease aforesaid being delirious and out of his Mind, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Teess Campbell Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Wm Teess Campbell Foreman
