City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Demand Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty seventh day of March in the Ninth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so. forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of Joseph Curtis then and there lying dead upon the Oath of George Jacques , John Jermain , John Russel , Michael Spear , William Chandless , James Chandler , Joseph Haines , John Perkins , David Davis , George Heald , Mark Ireland , Robert LittleJohn , Thomas Taylor , John Taylor and Edward Widows , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how and by what Means the said Joseph Curtis came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That William Baker late of the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid Laborer, not having the Fear of God before his Eyes, but moved and seduced by the instigation of the Devil, on the Twenty third day of March in the Year aforesaid, with Force and Arms, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said Joseph Curtis , in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being feloniously wilfully and of his Malice forethought did make an Assault, And that the said William Baker with a certain Knife or other sharp Instrument, which he the said William Baker then and there had and held in his right Hand, him the said Joseph Curtis in and upon the left side of him the said Joseph Curtis then and there feloniously wilfully and of his Malice forethought did strike and stab, and that the said William Baker with the Knife, or other sharp Instrument aforesaid did then and there give unto him the said Joseph Curtis , in and upon the said left side, between the Second and third Ribs of him the said Joseph Curtis one mortal Wound, of the Breadth of one Inch, and of the Depth of three Inches, of which said mortal Wound he the said Joseph Curtis from the said Twenty third day of March in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty sixth day of the same Month in the same Year, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty sixth day of March in the Year aforesaid, he the said Joseph Curtis of the mortal Wound aforesaid, did then and there die, and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Baker him the said Joseph Curtis in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, feloniously wilfully and of his Malice fore thought did kill and murder, against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. And that the said William Baker at the time of the doing and committing of the Felony and Murder aforesaid, or at any time since, had no Goods or Chattels Lands or Tenements, within the said Liberty or elsewhere, to the Knowledge or Notice of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said George Jacques Foreman of th said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

G Jacques Foreman
