Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit 

An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Twenty sixth day of August in the thirty fourth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Second by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand Seven hundred and sixty . Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster  aforesaid. Upon a View of the Body of Richard Mills then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of John Niblow , William Watson , William Hepworth , Marshall Fleet  John Sugg , Robert Murray , Robert Clarke , Thomas Castle , Edward Prebbell , John Hallow , John Burdon , Thomas Rigg , John Thompson and Francis Fleet Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Inquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Richard Mills came to his Death say upon their Oaths, That on Saturday the twenty third day of August Instant the said Richard Mills being found in a certain River called the River of Thames  near the Horseferry in the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City and County aforesaid and no marks of Violence appearing on the Body of the said Richard Mills to occasion his Death, not any Evidence to be found to prove how he came into the said River. We the said Jurors upon our Oaths do say and find that by the appearence of the Body of the said Richard Mills he in the said River aforesaid was Drowned, but whether by his own wit or by any Accident or Misfortune the Jurors know not. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition put their hands and Seals the day Year, and at the place abovesaid

Jno. Feary  Coroner

John Nibbett Foreman
