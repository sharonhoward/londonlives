City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of st. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of August in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of George Bradshaw then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Jon Randall Robert Stroud , William Cook , Robert Jackson , Edward Bailey , Thomas Exton , James Burn John Lee , John Stephens , John Simmons , William Oram , and Lawrey Gilpin good and lawful Men of the said Liberty. duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said George Bradshaw came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say,

That the said George Bradshaw on the first day of August in the Yar aforesaid at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square in the Liberty aforesaid to wit in the Serpentime River in Hyde Park , going into the said river there to Bath Himself it so happened that Accidentally casually and by a Misfortune to the said George Bradshaw was in the Waters of the said River and there suffocated and Drowned of his said suffocation and Drowning he the said George Bradshaw then and there instantly Died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say That the Said George Bradshaw in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition set their Hande and Seals the Day Years and Place abovementioned

Thos Prichard Coroner John Radall Foreman
