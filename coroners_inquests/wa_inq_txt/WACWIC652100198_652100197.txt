City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of April in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Richards then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Charles Rogers , John Hammond , samuel Towell , John Smith , James Morris , William Anderson , Adam Brown , John Baynham , Thomas Tireman , Benjamin Tolley , George Gillin , Bernard Masters , George Tollfree , John Drawith and Martin Bollee good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Richards came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Richards a Lighterman on the Seventeenth day of April in the Year aforesaid being alone in a Barge loaden with Coals upon the River Thamas, and with a certain Staff or Hitcher Shoveing the said Barge into a Dock belonging to Leonard Phillips situate in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said John Richards then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune felt out of the said Barge into the said River Thames , and in the Waters thereof was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said John Richards then and there died: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Richards in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Charles Rogers Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner Charles Rogers Foreman
