City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King. at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of December in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner, of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of  Charles Collyer and Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Adam Wright John Fowler , John Chapman , John Sheppard , John Looker , Thomas Green , John Murgatroyd , Samuel Littleton  John Sinceton , Humphrey Fifield , William Eyre William Robinson and Robert Steward good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire of our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Charles Collyer came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Charles Collyer the Infant, to Wit, of the Age of six Months, on the Eighth day of December (in the Evening) in the Year aforesaid, being in the Arms of Sarah, Collyer Mother of the said Charles Collyer, in Newport Street in the Parish of St. Ann within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And that a Man unknown was then walking briskly along the said Street, And being dark It so happened that the Man unknown in passing by the said Charles Collyer the Infant, being in the Mother's Arms as aforesaid, did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune Press to and against the left side of the said Charles Collyer, by reason whereof the said Charles Collyer w then and there Choaked and Stangled of which said Choaking and Strangling he the said Charles Collyer the Infant then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Charles Collyer in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Adam Wright Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place First above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Adam Wright Foreman
