City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex . } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of John the Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church St. Peter , Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty seventh day of July in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Francis Hirons then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Henry Harrison , James Lewis , Grant Harris , Henry Dippain , Peter Hunter , Charles Rayner , William Watts John Sanson , Lionel Galley , George Dyke , John Wilson and Thomas Lambert good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Francis Hirons came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Francis Hirons the twenty sixth day of July in the year aforesaid, being a Barge floating upon the River Thames at Mill Bank in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and putting a Hitcher against another Barge, then likewise floating upon the said River, in order to force the Barge into the Wharf there. It so happened that the Hitcher slipped and that the said Francis Hirons, Accidentally. Casually and by Misfortune fell into the said River Thames between the said Barges and in the Water of the said River was then and there Suffocated and Drowned of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Francis Hirons their and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say. That the said Francis Hirons in manner and be the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise

It Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Henry Harryson Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the Rest of Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner Henry Harrison Foreman
