City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of October in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of  Elizabeth Lawes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Jonathan Fentum , Alexander Sheriff , Thomas Fish , Robert Knipe , John Tuach Robert Watson , Francis Cluley , John Strachan , William Butler , Henry Thompson , Thomas Hamilton , William Barrett good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Lawes came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Elizabeth Lawes, laboring under a grievous Disease of Body, to wit a Fever, and by reason of the violence of the said Disease being Delirious and out of her Mind on the seventh day of October in the Year aforesaid from and out of a certain two pair of Stairs Window, then and there being in the Dwelling House of George Dance situate in Salisbury Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, to the Ground did violently cast and throw herself to and against the Stone Pavement in the said Street, by Means whereof she the said Elizabeth Lawes so delirious and out of her Mind as aforesaid did then and there received a Wound on the back part of her Head, and a Fracture on the left Leg, of which said mortal Wound and Fracture she the said Elizabeth Lawes from the said Seventh day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Eighth day of the same Month and Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and live, on which said Eighth day of October she the said Elizabeth Lawes of the mortal Wound and Fracture aforesaid did there die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Elizabeth Lawes in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, being Delirious and out of her Mind as aforesaid by reason of the violence of the Fever aforesaid did kill herself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jonathan Fentum Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Follows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jonn. Fentum Foreman
