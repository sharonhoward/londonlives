City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourteenth day of June in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Howel then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Coomes , John Walker , John King , John Bamport , John Perry , John James , Simon Groves , Francis Henderson , Francis Everet , James Eyton , Thomas Dixon , Benjamin Clark & William Nestbitt good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Howel came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Howell a Laborer on the Thirteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid being Riding upon a certain Dray drawn by two Horses in the Kings High Way in the Parish of Paddington in the County aforesaid, and that the said Horses took fright and run away, and that the said William Howell in endeavouring to stop the said Horses full off and from the said Dray unto the Ground, and thereby did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive on mortal Bruise in and upon the left side of the Body of him the said William Howel , of which said mortal Bruise he the said William Howell on the said Thirteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Howel in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and Misfortune came to his Death In Witness where of as well the said Coroner as the said William Coomes Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Willm Coomes Foreman
