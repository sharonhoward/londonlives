City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex , to Wit.}  An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty ninth day of January in the Sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on view of the Body of John Murray then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Heard , Thomas Leff , Robert Forsyth Alexander Brody , Walter Hughes , Alexander Stuart , Robert Sparlin , William Rineyson , Caesar Child , James Pulsford , Samuel Firser , and Richard Worrall good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there Duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when, how, and by what means the said John Murray Came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Twenty eighth day of January in the Year aforesaid the said John Murray a Slater being at Work on certain New built Stables situate in Swallow Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there being upon a Scaffold on the inside of the said New built Stables, It so happened that the Board on which the said John Murray was standing slipped and gave way, and that the said John Murray , thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did fall off and from the said Scaffold unto the Ground, by Means whereof he the said John Murray did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive divers mortal bruises in and upon the Head and Body of him the said John Murray , of which said mortal Bruises he the said John Murray then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said John Murray , in manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Heard foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner . W Heard Foreman
