Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit An Inquisition Indented and taken at then Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Seventh day of July in the first Year of the Reign of Our Sovereign Lord George the third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one . Before John Feary  Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid. Upon a View of the Body of Joseph Pargiter , then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of James Frith , Benjamin Carter , Thomas Carter , Russell William Miles , John Jennings , Richard Noodles , Thomas Reeves , Thomas Owen Joseph Hammond , and Thomas Parker , Jeremiah Coronella , John Taylor , and John Buffham Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty, who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Joseph Pargiter came to his Death Say upon their Oaths, That on Sunday the fifth day of this Instant July the said Joseph Pargiter being Bathing in a certain River called the River of Thamas and near a certain Place called Neptunes Farm in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, and not being able nor skill to Swim, it so happened that the said Joseph Pargiter by the violence of the Tide was forcibly carried forward into the said River, and the said Joseph Pargiter not being capable of swimming as aforesaid was in the Waters of the said River then and there unfortunately and accidentally Drowned. And so the said Jurors upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Joseph Pargiter the day and Year aforesaid in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid in manner and by the means aforesaid unfortunately and accidentally came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Jno. Feary Coroner .

James Smith Foreman
