City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquitition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the Counrty of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of November in the twenty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Roots then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Collins  Edward Taylor , William Sheppard , William Simpson , John Morgan , Joseph Goteson , John Mitchell  Johnson Mc.Cartney Alexander Brooke , Richard Asgrey , Myles Ryley , Robert Tomlinson and Charles Egerton good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by that what Means the said William Roots came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Roots an Infant on the Twenty first day of October in the Year aforesaid being with other Boys in a certain Boat upon the River Thames in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid and that it so happened, that the said William Roots Accidentlly Casually and by Misfortune fell out of and over the side of the said Boat into the said River Thames , and in the Water thereof was Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said William Roots then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Roots in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Collins Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquitition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten.

The Prickard Coroner

Joseph Collins Foreman
