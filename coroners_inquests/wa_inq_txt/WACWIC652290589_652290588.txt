City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Paul Covent Garden within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of October in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Basley then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Peter Vincent , Jesse Gray , James Chamberlain Thomas Priestman , Colin Burney , James Hulme , William Croydon , William Hisson , John Withers , William Reed , Nathaniel Morgan and Henry Wood good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Basely came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Basely labouring under a grevious Disease of Body (to wit) a Fever and by reason of the Violence of the said Disease, being Delicious and out of her Mind on the Nineteenth day of October in the Year aforesaid she the said Elizabeth Basely so being delicious and out of her Mind as aforesaid, out of and through a certain Garret Window in the Dwelling House of William Combe situate in Chandos Street in the said Parish Liberty and County did cast and throw herself, by means whereof she the said Elizabeth Basely did then and there Accidently Casually and by Misfortune receive mortal Bruises and upon the Head and a Fracture on the left Arm of her the said Elizabeth Basely of which said Mortal Bruises and Fracture she the said Elizabeth Basley then and there Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Elizabeth Basely, being delirious and out of her Mind as aforesaid in manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill herself.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Peter Vincent Foreman of the said Jurors on behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Peter Vincent Foreman
