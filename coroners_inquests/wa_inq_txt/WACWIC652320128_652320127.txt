City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of February in the thirty second Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Corner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Mendham then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Thomas Lyne , Alexander Reed , William Hebb  William Miller , John Christopher , Edward Roberts , Charles Elkins , Christopher Ross , John Hayward , Thomas Wilson , Thomas English and William Rutledge good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Mendham came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Mendham on the Thirteenth day of February in the Year aforesaid was driving a certain Carriage in Old Bond Street in the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid and meeting a Coach in the said Street It so happened that the said John Mendham in pulling his Horses out of the Way of the said Coach Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the Box upon the Stone Pavement in the said Street and then and these received a mortal Contusion in and of his Head of which said mortal Contusion he the said John Mendham from the said Thirteenth day of February in the year aforesaid until the Nineteenth day of the same Month and year at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live on which said Nineteenth day of February he the said John Mendham at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid of the mortal Contusion aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath say that the said John Mendham in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Lyne  Foreman of the Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos. Lyne Foreman
