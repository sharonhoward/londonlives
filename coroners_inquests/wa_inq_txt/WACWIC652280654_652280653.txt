City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex . } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indedented. taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty Seventh day of October in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Miles then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Lake , Richard Huntley , William Sheppard Joseph Hughes , James Silvester , Edward King , William Kent Richard Richards , John Peysley , Thomas Tyne , William Cliverley and Joseph Prince good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas MIles came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That th said Thomas Miles an Infant of the Age of Twelve years on the twenty fourth day of October in the Year aforesaid being with other Boys at Play at Kensington Palace Green in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid and there endeavouring to pull down the Branch of a certain Tree growing upon the said Green, It so happenes that the Branch broke off and that the thick end there of fell against and upon the left Temple of the said Thomas Miles whereby the said Thomas Miles then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did receive a mortal Fracture in and upon the left side of the Head near the left Temple of turn the said Thomas Miles of which said mortal Fracture he the said Thomas Miles at the said Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid from the said twenty fourth day of October in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty fifth day of the same month and Year did languish and languisting did live On which said twenty fifth day of October in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Thomas Miles of the mortal Fracture aforesaid did die, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say That the said Thomas Miles in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Lake Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

James Lake Foreman
