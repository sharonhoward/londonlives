City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King. at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the First day of November in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Rootes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Brandes , William Newton , John Slater , Thomas Bull , William Ackman , Samuel Wigg , Richard Barry , Samuel Webb , Samuel Middleton  John Hammond , William Godbeer , Kenith Mc. Clannan , Peter Connelly  John Collins Charles Baker , James James & Richard Jones good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire of our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Rootes came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Rootes, laboring under a grievous Disease of Body, to Wit a Fever, and by reason of the Violence of the said Disease, being delirious and out of his Mind, on the Thirty first day of October in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Worsted Garter unto a foot of the Bed ( which said Bed was turned up) in the Lodging Room of Apartment of him the said William Rootes in the Dwelling House of John Crooks situate and being in Church Court within the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck, did fix tye and fasten and therewith did then and there suffocate and strangle himself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said William Rootes then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said William Rootes, by reason of the Disease aforesaid, being delirious and out of his Mind as aforesaid, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Richard Brandes Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Richard Brandes Foreman
