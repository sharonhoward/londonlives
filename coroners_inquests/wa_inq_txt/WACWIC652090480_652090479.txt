City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Margaret in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Thirteenth day of December in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth Sharp then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Hudson , John Williams , James Bowers , William Lockie , John Ellis , Thomas Strawn , Jacob Knight , Thomas Marthey , Hugh Beach , Charles Burnet , George Young , William Arnold and Joseph Cowper  good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Sharp came to her Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Elizabeth Sharp , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the said Thirteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid, one End of a certain piece of small Cord unto an Iron Hook fastened into a Rafter in a Garret in the Dwelling House of Lawrence Callahan situate and being in Channel Row in the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, and the other End thereof about her own Neck, did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and Strangle herself, of which said Hanging suffocation and Strangling she the said Elizabeth shall then and there did so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Elizabeth Sharp , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but Lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid came to her Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Hudson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the Behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Thos. Hudson Foreman
