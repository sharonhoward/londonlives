City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Ann within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twelfth day of June in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, Before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Turner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Welsh , Richard Marriner , Michael Cransaz , George Rays , Joseph Palmer George Bowes , Edward Denby , Joseph Johnson , John Newcome James Barber , Stephen Baynton & John Taylor good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Turner came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Thomas Turner a Laborer on the said Twelfth day of June in the Year aforesaid being carrying a Sack with Coals down the Kitchen Stairs in the Dwelling House of Samuel Falconer situate in Macclesfield Street in the Parish aforesaid within the LIberty and County aforesaid, And that the said Thomas Turner then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down the said Stairs, unto the bottom thereof, and thereby did then and there receive one mortal Fracture in and upon the Right side of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Thomas Turner then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Turner in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally, Casually and by Misfortune came to hid Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Welsh Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jas. Welsh Foreman
