City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented. taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty first day of May in the thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Rachel Cuttle then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Joseph Smeeton , William Woodcock , Warneford Taylor , Thomas Green , Stephen Thomas , William mumford Matthew Berberrow , Edward Pelth , James Mc.Cawley , Edward Baythom Thomas Powell , Richard Munday and Thomas Hallett good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Rachel Cuttle came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That on the Twentieth day of May in the Year aforesaid the said Rachel buttle being Into cated with Liquor Accidentally Casually and by misfortune fell down upon her Face in a corner of her Lodging Room in Moors Yard situate in the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid and was then and there suffocated and strangled of which said Suffocation and strangling she the said Rachel buttle then and then died, And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Rachel Cuttle in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to her Death and not otherwise.

In Witness Whereof as well the said Coroner us the said Joseph Smeeton  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Joseph Smeeton Foreman
