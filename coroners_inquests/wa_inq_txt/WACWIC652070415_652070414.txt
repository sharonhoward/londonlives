City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit  An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist in the City of Westmr, within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of June-in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of Wiseman Holt Clerk, then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Firth John Tomkinson , Henry Ridcout , John Hardman , John Bath , John Middleton , Andrew Lunes , John Walden , Thomas Ede, William Salmon , William Hayward , William Franklin , John Macking , John Ryall , Abraham Wilkinson and Samuel Highley , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly Chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and Charged to inquire for Our said Lord the King when how and by what Means the said Wiseman Holt came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Wiseman Holt, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatic and distracted, on the Sixteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, one End of a certain Linnen Handkerchief unto a large Nail fastened into the Wall, in the Lodging Room or Apartment of him the said Wiseman Holt, in his Dwelling House, situate and being in Rochester Row in the said Parish City Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck, did fix, tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hang, suffocate and strangle himself, of which said Hanging Suffocation and Strangling he the said Wiseman Holt then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Wiseman Holt, not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but Lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did hill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Samuel Firth Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Samuel Firth Foreman
