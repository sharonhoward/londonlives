City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fifth day of January in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Perkins then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Bray , John Thoms , John Musgrave , James Eves  Henry Wood , James Toplis , Joseph Smith John Minnett , William Freestone , Thomas Hitchins , Robert Ashey and Joseph Dixon good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Perkins came to h is Death, do upon their Oath Say That the said Thomas Perkins in the Third day of January in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid departed this Life though the of the Weather.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Bray Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos: Bray Foreman
