City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixth day of October in the Eighteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on view of the Body of William Manby then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Dod , Charles Millar , Thomas Knight , William Medway , William Frisbey , John Burdon , Henry Reynell , John Bland , John Sheppard , Isaac Boyd , John Shirley  William Lendon , William Sharpe and Christopher James good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire fore our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Manby came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Manby not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted on the said Sixth day of October in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid in the Dwelling House of Christopher James there situate, with a certain Razor made of Iron and Steel which he the said William Manby then and there had and held in his right Hand, the Throat or Gullet of him the said William Manby did then and there strike stab and penetrate, thereby then and there giving unto himself the said William Manby with the Razor aforesaid in and upon the Throat or Gullet of him the said William Manby one Mortal Wound of the Length of three Inches and of the Depth of one Inch, of which said mortal Wound he the said William Manby then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Manby , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Dod Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jon Dod Foreman
