City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. John the Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of May in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Cook then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Edward Padget , Joseph Garlick , Thomas Horrod,  George Topham , Edward Hays , William Edgar , Edward Dyer , Thomas Brench , Richard Lane , John Mc. Kaye , William Green , Joseph Carr and John Cane , good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Cook came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John cook a Laborer on the Twenty fifth day of May in the Year aforesaid being at Work with other Laborers in Piling up Taggots in the Yard of John Williams Coal Merchant, situate and being in Mill Bank Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said John Cook then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down off and from to top o the said Pile at the height of Eighteen feet unto and upon the Stone Pavement in the said Yard, and thereby then and there received one mortal Wound and Fracture in and upon the top of his Head, of which said mortal Wound and Fracture he the said John Cook then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Cook , in Manner and By the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward Padget Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Edwd Padget Foreman
