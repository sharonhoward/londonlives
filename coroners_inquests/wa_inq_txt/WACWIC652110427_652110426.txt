City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of September in the Eleventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of  William Firmary then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Curtis Samuel Fuller , John Gale , John Dover , Joseph Kimberley , Thomas Bywater , Robert Bray , John Hudson , William Ketley , Francis Redhead , John Hargrave , James Bean , Williams Faulkner and Richard Curtis good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Firmary came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Firmary on the Eleventh day of August in the Year aforesaid on hearing Ann Knoll the Wife of Knoll, whose Christian nameis unknown, crying and Murder, in their Lodging Room in Duck Lane in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, went into the said Room, and there found the said Ann Knoll lying down upon the Floor and the said Knoll with his Feet stamping upon her Body, That the said William Firmary parted the said Knoll and Ann Knoll his Wife, and Endeavoured to pacify the said Knoll, That the said Ann Knoll a certain Quart Glass Bottle which she the said Ann Knoll took up and held in her right Hand, at and towards the said Knoll her Husband did cast and throw, and that the said Glass Bottle so thrown as aforesaid did then and there Casually by Misfortune and against that will of the said Ann Knoll alight upon the of the said by means whereof he the said William Firmary then and there received one mortal wound in and upon the back part of the Head of him the said William Firmary of which said mortal wound he the said William Firmary from the said Eleventh day of August in the Year aforesaid until the Thirty first day of the same Month and Year at the Westminster Hospital situate and lying in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Thirty first day of August aforesaid at the Hospital aforesaid he the said William Firmary of the mortal wound aforesaid did did. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Firmary in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Casually by Misfortune and against the Will of the said Ann Knoll came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Curtis Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

John Curtis Foreman
