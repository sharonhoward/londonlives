City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the First day of April in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Emery Puttock then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John James  John Surby , John Lewis , George Curling , Thomas Lyne , Thomas Brooks , Thomas Wells , Thomas Inglehort  Abraham Skegg , Thomas Wilson , Thomas Bayns , William Brookes , Peter Goulding , William Gravel , Jonathan Ordway and John Sutton good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Emery Puttock came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Emery Puttock not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted on the Twenty fourth day of March in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, a certain Pistol loaded with Gunpowder and a leaden Bullet, which he the said Emery Puttock then and there had & held in his Eight Hand to and against the Head of him the said Emery Puttock did then and there shoot off and discharge, by Means whereof he the said Emery Puttock did then and there give unto himself with the leaden Bullet aforesaid, so discharged and shot out of the Pistol aforesaid by the force of the Gunpowder aforesaid, in and through the Head of him the said Emery Puttock one mortal Wound, of which said mortal Wound he the said Emery Puttock from the said Twenty fourth day of March in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty ninth day of the same Month and Year, did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty ninth day of March at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Emery Puttock of the mortal Wound aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Emery Puttock not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself, In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said John James Foreman of the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John James Foreman
