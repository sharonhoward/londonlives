City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit 

Ane Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord we King at the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and pter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westerminster in the County of Middlesex the Seventeenth Day of July in the Fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Saith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty On View of the Body of William Marshall then and there bring dead upon the Oath of Edward James  John Watkins , Thomas Trubshaw , Thomas Hackstall  Charles Thomas  George Friswell  Joseph Cooper  Richard Martin , William Shackleford  Robert Chisholm , Samuel Kirkham  James Don , Henry Friday & Charles Taylor , good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King when how and on what Means the said William Marshall came to his Death do upon their Oath say, That the said William Marshall on the Sixteenth day of July in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, to wit, at Westminster Bridge , going into the River Thames , there to bath himself; It so happence, that accidentally casually, and by Misfortune, he the said William Marshall was in the Waters of the said River then and there suffocated and Drowned; of which said Suffocation and Drewning he the said William Marshall then and there instantly died. And so the Jurers aforsaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Marshall in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Edward James Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inguisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Edwd.. James Foreman
