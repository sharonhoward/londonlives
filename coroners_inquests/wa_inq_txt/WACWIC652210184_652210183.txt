City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the Country of Middlesex , the Seventeenth day of April in the Twenty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Nathan Collins then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Jonathan Hutchins , William Pibus , Thomas Paul , Michael Love , Joseph Bower  James Tovey , Robert Hall , John Hardesty William Storer , Lystan Vaughan , Charles Williams and William Shute good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said NathanCollins came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Nathan Collins on the sixteenth day of April in the year aforesaid Riding a certain Bay Horse in Piccadilly in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and Country aforesaid, and turning into Albemarle Street It so happened that the said Horse then and there fell down, and that the Head of the said Nathan Collins Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did fall upon and against the foot pavement in the said Street, whereby the said Nathan Collins did then and there receive a violent Bruise and Tumour in and upon the right side of his Head, of which said Bruise and Tumour he the said Nathan Collins on the said sixteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid at St. George's Hospital within the Parish Liberty and Country aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do says, that the said Nathan Collins in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Causally and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Jonathan Hutchins Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jonan Hutchins Foreman
