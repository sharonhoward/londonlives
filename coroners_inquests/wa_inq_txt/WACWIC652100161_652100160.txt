City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at Tothill Fields Bridewell in the Parish of St. Margaret in the City of Westmr. within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of April in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Mary Perry a Prisoner then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of George Allen , John Hales , Robert Hawkins , George Tarrance , William Pitt , George Tullock John Martin , Thomas White , John Lane , Richard Saunders , John Snook , George Whitrow and William Ketley good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Mary Perry came to her-Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Mary Perry being a Prisoner in Tothill Fields Bridewell aforesaid in the Parish and in the City Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Thirty first day of March in the Year aforesaid, at Tothill Fields Bridewell , departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a natural way, to wit, of a Fever, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said George Allen Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner . Geo: Allen Foreman
