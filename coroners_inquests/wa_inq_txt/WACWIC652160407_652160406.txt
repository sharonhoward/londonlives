City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex . to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty fourth day of September in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Timbrell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Birch , Henry Slack , Thomas Hacorn, Richard Smith , Richard Sweet  Richard Waklin , Jonathan Butcher , Thomas Guy , Alexander Grand , John Lewis , Barnard Gregory and Samuel Monk good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Timbrell came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Timbrell not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the Twenty third day of September in the Year aforesaid at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, into the Bason in Hyde Park did cast and throw himself, of which said casting and throwing he the said John Timbrell in the Water in the said Bason was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said John Timbrell then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John Timbrell , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill himself In Witness where of as well the said Coroner, as the said John Birch Foreman of the said Jurors to this Inquisition, have set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

John Birch Foreman
