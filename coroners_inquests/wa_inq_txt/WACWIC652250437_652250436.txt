City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter , Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of August in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Christopher Green wood then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Ashmore , Richard Hatch , Thomas Scott , Samuel Mitchell , Samuel Ray , Thomas George , James Masson , William Price , William, Mourn, James Brotherton , Aaron Loton , and Samuel Hewett good and Lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Christopher Greenwood  came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Christopher Greenwood not being of sound, Mind, Memory, and Understanding but lunatick and distracted, on the Ninth day of May, in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid, within the Liberty and County aforesaid, with a certain Rozor, made of Iron and Steel, which he the said Christopher Greenwood then and there had and held in his Right Hand the throat or Gullet of him the said Christopher Greenwood did then and there strike stab and penetrate thereby then and there giving unto himself with the Rozor aforesaid in and upon the throat or Gullet of him the said Christopher Greenwood one Mortal Wound of the length of three Inches and of the Depth of one. Inch of which said Mortal Wound he the said Christopher Greenwood , from the said Ninth day of May in the Year aforesaid until the first day of August in the same Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said first day of August in the Year aforesaid he the said Christopher Greenwood of the Mortal Wound aforesaid at the Parish and in th Liberty and County aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said Christopher Greenwood not being of Sound Mind memory & understanding but lunatick and distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did kill himself. In Witness whereof as well ye Jurors on the behalf of himself and the Rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition. set their Hands, and Seals, the Day, Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jas: Ashmore Foreman
