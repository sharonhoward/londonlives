City & Liberty of Westmr in the County of Middlesex , To wit.}  In Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex the Thirteenth Day of April in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & liberty on View of the Body of Thomas Buck then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Jobbins , Edward Baily , Thomas Meadows , Henry Hyam , John Serjeant , Thomas Reed , Alexander Carnegy , James Parrott , Richard Beedle , Henry Allibone , Jonathan Hutchins , James Whalley , Richard Shute , Lewis Martin , Robert Potter , William Jones , Samuel Bass , Thomas Withers and Edward Chandler , good and lawful Men of the said liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Buck came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Buck , on the Eleventh day of April in the Year aforesaid, was found Dead and floating in a certain Pond of Water in Hyde Park , in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid; That no Marks of Violence appeared on his Body, but how or by what means the said Thomas Buck became Drowned and Suffocated, no Evidence thereof doth appear to the Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said James Jobbins , Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner .

Js Jobbins Foreman
