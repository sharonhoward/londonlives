City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of October in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Samuel Street then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Barr , William Latham , James Wise , James Winks Joseph Dean , John Thomas , William Batterson , William Fenn Thomas Jackson , Francis Lye , Charles Walpole , Henry List and John Clowes good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Samuel Street came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say , That on the Eighth day of October in the Year aforesaid the said Samuel Street a Labourer being at Work upon the New built House of his Royal Highness the Duke of Clarence situate at Rochampton in the County of Surry, and being on the Inside of the said House at the Heigth of Three Storeys It so happened that the said Samuel Street Accidentally Causally and by Misfortune fell down from thence unto and upon the Wooden Joysts (The Floor not being laid in the First Storey, and thereby then and there received one mortal Fracture in and upon the right side of his Head of which said mortal Fracture he the said Samuel Street at Rochampton aforesaid, and also at the said Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the said Eighth day of October did languish and live, and on the said Eighth day of October at the said Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty and County aforesaid he the said Samuel Street of the mortal Fracture aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Samuel Street in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Thomas Barr Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Tho Barr Foreman
