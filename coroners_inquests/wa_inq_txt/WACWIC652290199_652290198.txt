City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourth day of April in the twenty ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Hoddey then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Whitaker , Robt. Plaxton , Edward Waller Anthony Chambers , James Wightman , Alexander Duncan , Thomas Marrot , John Davis , Adam Walker , William Still , Joseph Bate Thos. Morrison and John Kemp good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Hoddey came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Hodday a Nightman on the second day of April in the year aforesaid being at Work with other Men in Emptying the necessary House or Privy of William Burn situate in Bedford Bury in the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid and being down in the said Privy It so happened that the said William Hodday Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune was then and there suffocated of which said Suffocation he the said William Hoddey then and there instantly died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said William Hoddey in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Whitaker Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner Joseph Whitaker Foreman
