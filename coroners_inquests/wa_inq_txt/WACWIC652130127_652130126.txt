City and Liberty of Westminster  in the County of Middlesex } to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the Kings, at the Parish of Saint John Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Ninth day of March in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ailce Martin then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Highley , George Finley , William Lockie , William Ketley , John Mackinder , Thomas Stanley , Thomas Brook , John Sharp , Thomas Wilkinson Robert Reeve , Edward Dyer , Rowland Davies , John Founton , William Hayward and Matthew Dyer good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ailce Martin came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say, That a certain Man to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown, not having the fear of God before his Eyes but being moved and seduced by the Instigation of the Devil, on the Eight day of March in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, in and upon the said Ailce Martin in the Peace of God and of our said Lord the King then and there being, feloniously wilfully and of his Malice afore thought did make an Assault, And that the said Man to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown with a certain Knife made of Iron and Steel of the Value of six pence, her the said Ailce Martin in and upon the Throat of her the said Ailce Martin then and there feloniously wilfully and of his Malice afore thought did strike stab and Cut, thereby then and there giving unto her the said Ailce Martin in and upon her said Throat one mortal Wound of the Length of three Inches and of the Depth of two Inches, of which said mortal Wound she the said Ailce Martin then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Man to the Jurors aforesaid as yet unknown her the said Ailce Martin in Manner and by the Means aforesaid feloniously wilfully and of his Malice afore thought did Kill and Murder against the Peace of our said Lord the King In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Samuel Highley Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Saml Highley  Foreman
