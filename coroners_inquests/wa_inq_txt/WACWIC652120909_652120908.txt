City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of Decembe r in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Martin Cascater then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Cranage , Thomas Abbott , Josiah Day , Evan Tucker , John Murless , Alexander Dudgeon , Jenkin Morris , William Johnson , Charles Healy , John Wright , Samuel Middleton  John Gossart , John Bruce , James Watson , Samuel Wigg and John Steers good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Martin Cascater came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said Martin Cascater on the Thirteenth day of December in the Year aforesaid, in the Evening, being walking upon and over certain Boats and Barges Floating in and upon the River Thames , that is to say, at Hungerford Stairs in the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said Martin Cascater then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell into the said River Thames , and in the Water, and Mud or Slime in the said River, was then and there Suffocated and Drowned, of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said Martin Cascater then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Martin Cascater in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Cranage Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

Thos. Cranage Foreman
