City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of November in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner, of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John James then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Goodhall , John Vaughan , Joseph Waterman , Richard West , Robert Simpson , Richard Eaton , Isaac Peat , Joseph Clark , John Wells , James Simms , Thomas Powell , Joseph Thompson , James Adams , Edward Warren , James Wildon , Henry Absolom , John Gibbs and John Hall good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John James came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That the said John James on the Nineteenth day of November in the Year aforesaid being in the Stable of James Mc. George in a certain Stable Yard situate in York Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there with a Pitchfork beating a certain Chesnut Horse the Property of the said James Mc. George, It so happened that the said Chesnut Horse with his hinder Feet struck at the said John James , and that the said John James did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fall down backwards upon the Stone Pavement on the outside of the said Stable Door, by Means whereof the said John James did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive one mortal Concussion in and upon the Brain of him the said John James , of which said mortal Concussion he the said John James then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said John James in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Goodhall Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Wm. Goodhall Foreman
