City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of John Webb the Sign of the Griffin in half Moor Street Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the first day of August 1796 in the thirty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Elizabeth the wife of John palmer then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Elizabeth Palmer came to h er Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Elizabeth Palmer not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted on the thirtieth day of July in the year aforesaid at the parish and in the Liberty aforesaid To Wit in the Dwelling or Apartment of her the sd. Elizabeth Palmer in half Moon Street aforesaid, a certain Juror which she the said Elizabeth palmer then & there had & held in her right hand to and against the Thrs at or Gullet of her the said Elizabeth Palmer did then and there strike stab and penetrate thereby then and there giving unto herself the said Elizabeth Palmer one Mortal Wound of the depth of one Inch & of the Breadth of three Inches: Ofuluch said Mortal wound she the said Elizabeth palmer then & there instantly did: And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say that the said Elizabeth palmer , not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but lunatic and distracted in manner and by the Means aforesaid, did kill herself

 IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition fet their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }
