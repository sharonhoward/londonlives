City and Liberty of Westminster in the Country of Middlesex , to wit} 

An Inquisition Indented taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James with in the Liberty of the Dean and chapter of the collegiate Church of St Peter Westminster in the Country of Middlesex , the Eleventh day of September in the Fifth year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Irelands King Defender of the Faith and so fourth, before Thomas Prickard  Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on view of the Body of Andrew Gowen then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of John Towers , John Sigrist , Richard Walker , Bowden Brooks , Elisha Whitelock , Thomas Meredith , Joseph Gibbons , John Lloyd , Gabriel Rallat , Henry Houst , Robert Gibson , Henry Jay , John Price , Stephen Birch , Thomas Swardell , Thomas Reid , Saml. Norgrave & Meredith Dodd good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duty chosen, who being then and there duty Sworn and Charged to inquire for our said Lord the king when, how, and by by what Means the said Andrew Gowen Came to his Death, do upon their Oath say That tha said Andrew Gowen a Painter on the Tenth day of September in the year aforesaid being at work on the outside of a Window of a house belonging to Simpson Situated and being in a certain Court called little Crown court , in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid; It so happend that the up right Piece of Wood in the Middle of the said Window, which the said Andrew Gowen had and held in his left hand, broke loose and came out of the said Window,and that the said Andrew Gowen then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did fall off and from tha said house, being two Stories high, into the said Court, which was paved with stone, by Means where of he the said Andrew Gowen did then and there, by the fall aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune receive a mortal Fracture in and upon the right side of his Head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said Andrew Gowen then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Andrew Gowen in Manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness where of as well as the said Coroner, as the said John Towers Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the next of his said fellows, in their Presence, have to this inquisition set their hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above written.

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Towers Foreman.
