City of Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifth day of July in the Thirteent Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the king for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Thirkill then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Bradburne , James Hammond  James Witman  David Landow , Farr Blissett  James Wilkins John Carvell , Adam Wright , Samuel Littleton , Michael Fagan , Daniel Thompson and William Pickar good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Thirkill came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That on the Third day of July in the Year aforesaid two Brown Horses being Gallsping along a certain Street called Long Acre in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and drawing a certain Phacton, without any Drives, And the said Thomas Thirkill then and there having hold of one of the Reins and running by the side of, and endeavouring to stop the said Horses, It so happened that the said Thomas Thirkill fell down over a heap of Stones used for paving in the said Street, and that the Near Wheel of the said Phacton then and there Accidentlally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the Body of the Said Thomas Thirkill , by Means whereof the said Thomas Thirkill then and there received mortal Bruises in and upon his Body, of which said mortal Bruises he the said Thomas Thirkill then and there died. And so the Juory aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Thirkill in Manner and by the means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Corner as the said John Bradburne Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard Coroner .

John Bradburne Foreman
