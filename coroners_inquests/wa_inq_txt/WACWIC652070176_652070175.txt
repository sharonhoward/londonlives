City and Liberty of Westmr. in the County of Middlesex To wit. }  In Inquisition indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the KING at the Parish of St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Twenthfifth Day of March in the Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland, KING, Defender of the Faith, Etc. before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of Our said Lord the KING, for the said City & liberty on View of the Body of John Gillett then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Nelson , Samuel Lawrence , Joseph Robinson , David Richie , Richard Birmingham , James Coden Christopher Smith , William Franklin , Thomas Knight , William Read , James Campbell , Robert May . Samuel Barnes , and Richard Hoy good and lawful Men of the said Liberty duly chosen as by law is required, and who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for Our said Lord the KING, when, how, and by what Means the said John Gillett came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Gillett died suddenly in a certain Hole or Place of Confinement, within the Watchhouse, in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, on the Twenty third day of March in the year aforesaid; That no Marks of Violence appeared on the Body of the said John Gillett ; and that the said John Gillett deported this Life by the Nisitation of God, in a natural Way, and not by any Hurt or Injury received from any Person or Persons whatsoever, to the Knowledge of the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said William Nelson Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seal the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Willm Nelson Foreman
