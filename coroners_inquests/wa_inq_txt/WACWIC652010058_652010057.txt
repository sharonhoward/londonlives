Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } to wit An Inquisition Indented and taken at the Parish of St. James in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter at Westminster the Seventeenth day of September in the frist Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the faith and so forth and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one . Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid Upon a View of the Body of Walter Watkins then and the lying Dead by the Oaths of William Clements , Benjamin Plantin , James Smith , John Sheppard , Thomas Wild , Daniel Joyner , Thomas Harris , Charles Summers , John Forerman Joseph Hardy , William James , Robert Wright , Lewis Morgan , Martin Salkeld , And John Millard Good and Lawfull Men of the said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Walter Watkins came to his Death say upon their Oaths. That on Monday the fourteenth day of September Instant the said Walter Watkins being in the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid did by the Visitation of God of a Fever and Delirious Dye a Natural Death. And so the said Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Walter Watkins the day and Year aforesaid in the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid in manner aforesaid came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day Year and at the Place abovementioned.

Jno. Feary Coroner .

William Clerments Foreman.
