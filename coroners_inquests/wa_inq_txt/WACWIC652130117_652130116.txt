City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the collegiate Church of St.Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of March in the Thirteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Christerfield Burbridge then andri there Lying dead, upon the Oath of Jeremiah Hilhouse , Charles Rogers , James Ward , Andrew Oliver , Francis Thompson , John Bradford , William Stranock , John Hicks , John Rowny , James Cowden , Henry Aistrop , Roger Slater and Samuel Price good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the Kings, when, how, and by what Means the said Christerfield Burbridge came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Christerfield Burbridge a Coachman on the Sixth day of March in the Year aforesaid being driving a certain Chariot drawn by two Horses in Saint Martins Lane in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And the said Chirsterfield Burbridge being then and there Sick and Distempered in his Body, It so happened that he fell down, in affit, off and from the Coach Box unto the Ground, and that the Near Fore wheel of the said Chariot then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune passed over the Head of the said Christerfield Burbirdge, by Means whereof the said Christerfield Burbridge then and there received a Contused Wound in and upon the leftside of his Head, and a mortal Cocussion in and upon the Brain of him said Christerfield Burbridge, of which said mortal Wound and Concussion he the said Christerfield Burbridge then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say, that the said Christerfield Burbridge, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Jeremiah Hilhouse Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of hie Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Jeremiah Hilhouse Foreman
