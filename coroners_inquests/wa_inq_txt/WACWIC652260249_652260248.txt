City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of April in the twenty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Sykes then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Piercy  Isaac Rowe  William Woodcock , John Watts , John Twadle , John Leach , Michael Jordan , Richard Bailey , John Nichols , James Bell , Robert Hall , Joseph Perring , Edward Mills , and John White good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Sykes came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Sykes on the said Nineteenth day of April in the year aforesaid, being Intoxicated and in Liquor, in the Yard of Mary Michael Chimney Sweper situate in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and laying himself down to Sleep near unto a large Quantity of Sifted Ashes in the said Yard. It so happened that Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune Some of the Ashes being part of the large Quantity aforesaid, sliped and came down upon the Body of the said William Sykes so lying asleep as aforesaid by means whereof the said William Sykes was then and there Suffocated and Strangled, of which said Suffocation and Strangling he the said William Sykes then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid, do say that the said William Sykes  in manner and by the means of aforesaid, Accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death

In Witness whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said John Piercy  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jno. Piercy Foreman
