City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty ninth day of June in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Joseph Bumfress then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Cando , John Bedford , Oliver Cowdy Peter Wilson , John Thwaite  John Ritckarby  John Dowing James Bumsteed  James Gough , William Humby  John Moore  Thomas Oxford  John Garden  John Pingle and Joseph Tarcelain good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Joseph Bumfress came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Joseph Bumfress not being of sound Mind, Memory and Understanding, but lunatick and distracted, on the twenty eighth Day of June in the Year aforesaid, at the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, One End of a certain price of small Cord unto an Iron Staple fastened into a peice of Timber. in the lodging Room and apartment of him the said Joseph Bumfress in the dwelling House of Charles Ross situate in Orange Court in the said Parish Liberty and County, and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tye and fasten, and therewith did then and there hand suffocate and Strangle himself of which said hanging, Suffocation and Strangling he the said Joseph Bumfress then and their died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say That the said Joseph Bumfress not being of sound Mind, Memory, and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted in manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said John Cards  Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the Rest of his Fellows, in their Resence have to this Inquisition sit their Hands and Seals, the Day Year, and Place above written.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner John Cards Foreman
