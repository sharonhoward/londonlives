City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Sixteenth day of January in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Leech then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Greaves , George Davey , Edward Price , James Savage , James Coroner , John Lambert , James Soleirel , Samuel Ragley  James Cooper , Thomas Glover  Adam Brown , Ulysses Madden & James Kinlock Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Leech came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Leech a Laborer on the Thirteenth day of January in the Year aforesaid being at Work upon the dwelling House of Mr. Mann situate in Sackville Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then and there going up a Ladder on the outside of the said House, It so happened that the said John Leech then and there Accidentally Causally and by Misfortune fell down off and from the said Ladder upon certain Iron Rails before the said House in Sackville Street aforesaid, and thereby then and there received one mortal wound n his Back, of which said mortal Wound he the said John Leech then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Greaves Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself, and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and place abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Thos. Greaves Foreman
