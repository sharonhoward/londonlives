City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of January in the Twenty Seventh Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Walter Chetwynd  Esquire then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Alexader Palmer , Timothy Bligh , Robert Fawcett , Richard Ash , George Wood , John Herman , Alexander Oswald , William Vawrine , Thomas Wiliams , Jeremiah Moor , Michael Downes , John Blakey , and John Shephard good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duty sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Walter Chetwynd  Esquire came to h is Death, do upon their OAth say That the said Walter Chetwynd not being of sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatic and Distracted on the Twentieth day of January in the year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, a certain Pistol charged with Gunpowder and a leaden Bullet, which he the said Walter Chetwynd then and there had and held in his Right Hand, to and against the Head of him the said Walter Chetwynd ator wear the right temple die then and there shoot off and Discharge by means whereof the said Walter Chetwynd did then and there give unto himself with the Leader Bullet aforesiad to Discharged and Shot out of the Pistol aforesaid by the force of the Gunpower aforesaid in and upon the Head of him the said walter Chetwynd at or near the right temple, one Mortal Wound of the Breadth of one Inch and of the Depth of there Inches of which said Mortal Wound he the said Walter Chetwynd then and there instantly Died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say That the said Walter Chetwynd not being of Sound Mind Memory and understanding but Lunatic and Distracted in manner and by the means aforesaid did Kill himself In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and at the Place first above mentioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner L Palmer Foreman
