An Inquilicion Indented, taken for our Sovereign, taken for our sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of st. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Eighth day of August in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of a Man, name unknown then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of John Smith Thomas Barr , James Barr , James Juffey , William Sharpe , William Geo , Robert Anderson , Reuben Jackson , Charles Williams , Edward Haley , John Blunt , and David Adamson Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty. duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enqure for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Man, name unknown came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Man, name unknown on the Fourth day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid being in a Certain Free in Hyde Park while His Majesty was Reveiwing his Troops, it so happened that the Branch of the said Free on which the said Man name unknown was, brooke, by means whereof the said Man name unknown accidentally casually and by misfortune fell from the said Free to the Ground and thereby received a Violent and mortal Shock or Concussion on the Head of his the said Man name unknown, of which said violent and mortal Shock or Concussion he the said Man name unknown from the said Fourth day of August in the Year aforesaid until the Seventh day of the same Month in the same Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid, to wit, in Saint Georges Hospital near Hyde Park corner did languish and languishing did Live, on which said Seventh day of August in the Year aforesaid at the Parish and in the Liberty and county aforesaid to wit in saint George Hospital aforesaid he the said Man name unknown of the violent and mertal shock or Concussion aforesaid did Dye, and so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid Do say that the said Man name unknown in Manner and by the Sworn aforesaid Accidentally causally and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. InWitness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Thos Prickard Coroner

Jno: Smith Foreman
