City and Liberty of Westminster , In the County of Middlesex ,} to wit,  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the House of Tranfield the Sign of the Triumphant Chariot parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the fourteenth day of October 1796 in the thirty sixth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France, and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Anthony Gell , Esq. Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Ambrose Hean then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of the several Jurors whose Names are here under written, and Seals affixed, good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Ambrose Hean came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say that the said Ambrose Hean not being of sound Mind Memory & Understanding but lunatic & distracted on the ninth day of October in the Year aforesaid, at Brentford in the County aforesaid a certain Sharp Instrument which he the said Ambrose Hean then & there had and held in his right hand to and against the this at or Gullot of him the said Ambrose Hean did the and there strike stab and penetrate thereby then and there with the said sharp Instrument giving unto himself one mortal wound of the depth of one inch and of the breadth of one inch of which said Mortal Wound he the said Ambrose Hean did languish & languishing did hire until the tenth day of October in the year aforesaid on which said tenth day of October in the Year aforesaid at the westminster infirmary in the she parish & Liberty aforesaid of the said Mortal wound, he the said Ambrose Hean , did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their oath aforesaid do say that the said Ambrose Hean not being of sound Mind Memory & understanding but lunatic & distracted in manner and by the Means aforesaid did kill himself

 IN WITNESS whereof, as well the said Coroner as the said Jurors, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day, Year, and Place first above written.

Anthy Gell Coroner }

Abraham Tebbuth Wm Musgrave James Paxon Henry Kent Jno. Chilcott Richard Palmer Thos. Harris
