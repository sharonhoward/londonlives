City and Liberty of Westminster in the Country of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint John the Evengelists within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the Country of Middlesex , the Thirty first day of March in the Twentieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Hodgson then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Ashmore , Henry Staines , William Copcut , Thomas Edge  John Barnes  James Warme  James Davies , William Miles , James Lloyd , Edward Haslam , Robert Watson , James Demaid Matthew Walker and Lionel Galley good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who, being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said James Hodgson came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Hodgson a Lad of the Age of Eleven Years on the Twenty seventh day of March in the Year aforesaid being riding a certain Horse in Tothill Fields in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and Country aforesaid, And that the said James Hodson then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Horse unto the ground, by Means whereof and by being then and there dragged with one of his feet in the Stirrup Strap, he the said James Hodgson then and there did receive divers Bruises in and upon his Body of which said mortal Bruises he the said James Hodgson at the Parish of St. Margaret within the Liberty and Country aforesaid from the said Twenty seventh day of March in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty ninth day of the same Month and Year did languish and love and thereof then and there die die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said James Hodgson Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in Manner and by the Means. aforesaid came to his death and not otherwise.

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Ashmore Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovewritten.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Jos: Ashmore Foreman
