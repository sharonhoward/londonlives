City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit.   An Inquisitions Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of December in the Fifteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Spencer then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Roger Hays , Benjamin Boulton , William Liddell , Richard Parrott , John Lewis , Thomas Exton , William Thompson , Daniel Green , Richard Burton , Edward Jones , John Hooke and John Jackson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Thomas Spencer came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Spencer on the Seventeenth day of December in the Year aforesaid being Riding a certain Bay Horse in the Stable Yards of Joseph Hetherington in the Parish of St. Mary le bone in the County aforesaid Stable Keeper, and then and there unskilfully forcing the said Horse over a Wooden Bar in the said Stable Yard, It so happened that the said Bay Horse fell down with the said Thomas Spencer , and that the said Thomas Spencer thereby then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune received mortal Bruises in his Body of which said Bruises he the said Thomas Spencer at the said Parish of St Mary le bone in the County aforesaid and also at the said Parish of St George Hanover Square within the Liberty and then on the day and Year aforesaid at the Parish of St George Hanover Square aforesaid He the said Thomas Spencer of the mortal Bruises aforesaid did die And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Spencer Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune in Manner and form aforesaid came to his death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Roger Hays Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho Prickard Coroner

Roger Hays Foreman}
