City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit.   An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Seventh day of August in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God. of Great- Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty,on View of the Body of Barnaby Mitchell then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Whalley Richard Burton , Nathaniel Silverthorne , John Worrell  Thomas Wallington , Thomas Cockett , William Scotney , William Hart , John Croucher , Samuel Agar , Thomas Colborn and Russel Stamford good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and Charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Barnaby Mitchell came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say

That the said Barnaby Mitchell on the fourth day of August in the Year aforesaid was found Drowned and Suffocated in the River Thomas (to wit) at Milbank in the Parish of St. George Hanover Square in the Parish and Liberty aforesaid, that the said Barnaby Mitchell had no Marks of Violence Appearing on his Body, but how or by what means he became Suffocated and Drowned no Evidence thereof Oath appear to the said Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid on behalf of himself and the rest of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the day and Year Frist abovewritten

Tho Prickard  Coroner James Whalley  Foreman
