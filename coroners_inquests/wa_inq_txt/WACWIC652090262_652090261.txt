City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fifteenth day of June in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of James Dunn then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Joseph Frankland , William Foot , Alexander Kilgour , Robert Webb , Noel Racine , Thomas Thompson , William Mensies , Joseph shore , mark Last , Isaac Harries , John Busquet , and Edward Taylor good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said James Dunn came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said James Dunn , a Helper in the Stables of the most Honourable the Marquiss of Rockingham, On the Thirteenth day of June in the year aforesaid, being in the Hay left over the said Stables, situate in the parish aforesaid said within the Liberty and County oforesaid, and being in Liquor, It so happened that the said James Dunn Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune did then and there fall out of and from the said Hayloft to the Ground, which was paved with Clinkers; by means of which said Fall he the said James Dunn then and there received one Mortal Fracture in and upon the back part of his head, of which said mortal Fracture he the said James Dunn there languished until the Fourteenth day of the same Month and Year, and then and there of the Mortal Fracture aforesaid he the said Jame Dunn did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said James Dunn in and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Joseph Frankland Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Pickard Coroner .

Jos Frankland Foreman
