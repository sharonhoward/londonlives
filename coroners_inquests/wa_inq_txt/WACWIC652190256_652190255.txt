City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint John the Evangelist within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Nineteenth day of June in the Nineteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Lockstone then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Robert Reeve , William Jewett , William James , William Webb , John White , John Thompson , Thomas Bennet , Edward Phillips , Evan Williams , Robert Maycock , Henry Sumpter & David Spiers good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Lockstone came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Lockstone a Laborer on the fifteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid being at Work in a certain empty Lighter in and upon the River Thames , and with a Hitcher pushing against a certain Barge loaded with Coals, It so happened that the said Hitcher then and there Slipped from the said Barge, and that the said William Lockstone Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Lighter into the said River Thames and in the Waters of the said River was then and there Suffocated and Drowned of which said Suffocation and Drowning he the said William Lockstone then and there died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said William Lockstone in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Robert Reeve Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Robt. Reeve Foreman
