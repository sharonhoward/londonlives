City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty sixth day of May in the Twelfth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Lane an Infant then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Samuel Norgrave , John Horn , John Valant , Thomas Clark , John Crouch , Adam Bowns , William Longley , Samuel Davies , David Medcalf , James Marchant , Samuel Wheeler , Joseph Smith , George Plinge , William Tomlinson , John Lee and William Stubbs good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Lane came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Lane the Infant, of the Age of two Years, on the Twenty third day of May in the Year aforesaid being alone in the Lodging Room or Apartment of William Lane his Father, in the dwelling House of Joseph Rigless situate in Warwick Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, did then and there with both his hands pull down upon himself a certain Tub containing a considerable Quantity of hot Water, standing upon a Table in the said Room; by Means whereof the Face Neck Breast and Shoulders of him the said William Lane were very much Scaled and Burnt, of which the said William Lane from the said Twenty- -third day of May in the Year aforesaid until the Twenty fourth day of the same Month at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty fourth day of May in the Year aforesaid at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said William Lane of the Scalding and Burning aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, That the said William Lane , in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Samuel Norgrave Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Samll Norgrave

Foreman
