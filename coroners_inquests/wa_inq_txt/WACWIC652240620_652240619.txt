City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the twenty ninth day of November in the twenty fifth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Jones then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Jacob Watson , William Lucking , Robert Hutchins Richard Pearce , Thomas Price , William Stuart , Michael Churchman , John Beard , John Marsh , John Walker John Taunton , and Aubrey Broach good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Jones came to his Death, do upon their Oath say , That the said William Jones a Carpenter on the said Twenty ninth day of November in the Year aforesaid being at Work at Carlton House in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, and then standing upon a Scaffold on the Outside of the said House It so happened that the Scaffold gave Way and that the said William Jones Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell down with the scaffold into the Area before the said House, being the Height of Thirty four feet, and thereby then and there received a Wound upon the top of his Head and Bruises in his Body, of which said Mortal Wound and Bruises he the said William Jones then and there instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said William Jones in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Jacob Watson  Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard Coroner Jacob Watson Foreman
