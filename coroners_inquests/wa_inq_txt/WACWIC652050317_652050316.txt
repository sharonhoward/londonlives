City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to Wit 

An Inquisition Indented taken for Our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Margaret, in the City of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter Westminster in the County of Middlesex the Thirtieth day of May in the Fifth Year of the Regin of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth before Thomas Prickard Gentleman Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty on View of the Body of Anthony Wilcox then and there lying Dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Smith , Christopher Latimore , George Young , George Tate , Thomas Griffiths , William Vaughan William Gillitt , Philip Walker , David Simpson , Thomas Hughes , William Smith and John Lawrence good and lawfull Men of the said Liberty duly chosen, who being their and there duly Sworn and charged to inquire for our said Lord the King, when how, and by what Means the said Anthony Wilcox. came to his Death do upon their Oath say That on the Twenty eighth day of May in the Year aforesaid, the said Anthony Wilcox a Carter being driving a Cart loaded with Lime and drawn by three Horses in a certain Place called Scotland Yard, situate in the Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Precincts of his Majesty's Verge, in the sd. County of Middlesex , It so happened that the said Anthony Wilcox was then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune forced down to the Ground by the Near Wheel of the said Cart, And that the said Near Wheel of the said Cart did then and there pass over the Breast and right Arm of the said Anthony Wilcox, by Means whereof he the said Anthony Wilcox did then and there Accidentally Casually, and by Misfortune receive one mortal Bruise in and upon his Breast, and one mortal Fracture in and upon his right Arm, of which said mortal Bruise and Fracture he the said Anthony Wilcox from the said Twenty eighth day of May in the Year aforesaid, untill the Twenty ninth day of the same Month and Year, at a certain Hospital called the Westminster Hospital , situate in the said Parish of St. Margaret within the City Liberty and County aforesaid, did lanquish and languishing did live, on which said Twenty ninth day of May in the Year aforesaid, he the said Anthony Wilcox, at the said Hospital, of the mortal Bruise and Fracture aforesaid, did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid, upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Anthony Wilcox, Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune, in Manner and by the Means aforesaid, came to his Death, and not otherwise And that the said Near Wheel of the said Cart, so drawn as aforesaid, was moving to the Death, and not otherwise Wilcox, and is of the Value of Ten Shillings, and the Property and in the Possession of Tomlinson Busby of Westbourn Green in the said County of Middlesex Esquire or of his Assigns. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Thomas Smith Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his said Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals, the Day Year and Place first abovewritten

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Tho Smith Foreman
