City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at the Parish of Saint Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Second day of July in the Thirtieth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of John Banfield then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Richard Kilsby , Thomas Gosheron , William Higgins John Terry , Christopher Lewes , Thomas Wooddin , William Storey Samuel Harrison , Thomas Middleditch , William Bayley , John Winship William Dodds , John Crighton , Robert Moore , Thomas Con , John Thing , George Downing , Charles Lockington & William Jones good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said John Banfield came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said John Banfield not being of sound Mind Morning Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted on the said Second day of July in the Year aforesaid one End of a certain Piece of small Cord unto a Piece of Timber in the Workshop of the said John Banfield situate and being in Rose Street in the said Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty and County aforesaid and the other End thereof about his own Neck did fix tie and fasten and therewith did then and there hang suffocate and strangle himself, of which said hanging suffocation and strangling he the said John Banfield then and there died And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said John Banfield in manner and by the means aforesaid not being of sound Mind Memory and Understanding but Lunatick and distracted did kill himself

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner , as the said Richard Kilsby Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellow in their Presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place in abovementioned.

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Ricd. Kilsby Foreman
