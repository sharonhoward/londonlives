City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to Wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twentieth day of February in the Seventeenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Deleany then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Thomas Hall , John Hords , Charles Weelding  Edward Marr , Thomas Nevett , John Gilbert  Joseph Seger , George Pickering , Edward Waller  Owen Scott William Stables and Thomas Waters good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly Chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Deleany came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Richard Deleany a Labourer on the Nineteenth day of February in the Year aforesaid being at work at the top of the House of Mr. Selwyn situate in Chesterfield Street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, And then and there standing upon a Ladder on the outside of the said House, It so happened that the said Ladder slipped of the Coping, and that the said Richard Deleany then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell from the top of the said House upon the Stone Pavement in the said Street, and thereby then and there did receive one Mortal Fracture in and upon his Head, of which mortal Fracture he the said Richard Deleany then and their instantly died. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Richard Deleany in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Thomas Hall Foreman of the said Jurors have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner

Thos Hall Foreman
