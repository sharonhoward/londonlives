City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign, Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint Anne within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Fourth day of July in the Thirty first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman , Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Henry Hamilton then and there lying dead , upon the Oath of Edward Peter Petil , Joseph Barber , John Twedell , Charles Blake , William Lowndes , James Meather , James Smart , Stephen Callaham , Thomas Lucas , David niver , Samuel Faulkner Peter Dean , Alexander Gordon , John Webb , Benjamin Smart and David Gwynne Good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enqiure for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Henry Hamilton came to his Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Henry Hamilton (suspected to have been murthered) on the second day of July in the Year aforesaid at the said Parish of St. Anne within the Liberty and County aforesaid Suddently departed this Life by the Visitation of God in a Natural Way and not every any Violence or Injury received from any Person whatsoever to the knowledge of the said Jurors

In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Edward Peter Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of this Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the day Year and Place abovementioned

Tho Prickard  Coroner

Ed: Petit  Foreman
