City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex }  to wit.

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of Saint George Hanover Square within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the Twenty ninth day of May in the Ninth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Thomas Fletcher then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of James Styles , Richard Morris , Jasper Smith , Richard Baker , John Turner , Emanuel Bennett Joseph Frankland , Nicholas Clark , George Heald , John Cunningham , Thomas Williams , Edward Billam and Thomas Hudson good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what means the said Thomas Fletcher came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Thomas Fletcher on the Twenty Sixth day of May in the year aforesaid having a large Ironing Stool in both his Hands, and carrying the same up the Cellar Stairs, near the top thereof, in his dwelling House situate and being in David street in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, It so happened that the said Thomas Fletcher did then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fall down backwards upon the stone Pavement at the bottom of the said Stairs, by means of which said Fall he the said Thomas Fletcher then and there receive a violent Concussion upon the Brain, of which said violent Concussion he the said Thomas Fletcher at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did Languish about two Hours, and then and there of the mortal Concussion aforesaid did dye. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Thomas Fletcher in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally casually and by Misfortune came to his Death, and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said James Styles Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellow on their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place first above Written

Tho. Richard Coroner .

Jos Styles Foreman
