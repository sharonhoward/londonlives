City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex .} to wit  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at the Parish of St. James within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church St. Peter, Westminster , in the Country of Middlesex the Eighteenth day of April in the Sixteenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Paul Nash then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of William Dove , John Sutton , Thomas Well , Michael Saunders , Samuel Warren James Corker , William Rudge , James Their , William Morris , William Lucas , John Gasley , John Jones , William Burch , Joseph Tiercelin and John Jones good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly Sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Paul Nash came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said Paul Nash a Labourer on the Fifteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid being at Work in the House of Francis Calls in Munday's Court in the Parish aforesaid within the said Liberty and County, And then and there standing upon a Ladder on the inside of the said House, and being Intoxicated with Liquor, It so happened that the said Paul Nash then and there Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune fell off and from the said Ladder into the Kitchin, and thereby received divers Bruises in and upon his Body, of which said Bruises he the said Paul Nash from the said Fifteenth day of April in the Year aforesaid until the Seventeenth day of the same Month and Year at the Parish and in the Liberty and County aforesaid did languish and languishing did live, on which said Seventeenth day of April aforesaid at the Parish Liberty and County aforesaid, he the said Paul Nash of the mortal Bruises aforesaid did die. And so the Jurors aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say, that the said Paul Nash in Manner and by the Means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said William Dove Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day Year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard  Coroner

Wm Dove Foreman
