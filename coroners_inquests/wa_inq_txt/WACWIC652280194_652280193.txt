City and Liberty of Westminster , in the County of Middlesex .} to wit.  An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King at The Parish of Saint Margaret within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the thirty first day of March in the twenty eighth Year of the Regin of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman,Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of Richard Martin then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Charles Chance , John Nash , William Vincent Thomas Denham , John Watts , Thomas Sander , Thomas Gardner , John Mitcham , Edward Taylor , Thomas Hill , John Wilson , Thomas Packer , Henry Twitchings , Richard Carter , John Atkins and William Jordan good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said Richard Martin came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say That on the twenty ninth day of March in the Year aforesaid William Bowens a Drayman was carefully driving a certain Dray loaded with one Butt full of Beer and two Empty Butts and drawn by two Horses in a certain Street called king Street in the said of St. Margaret within the Liberty and County aforesaid and that the said Richard Martin was then sitting upon the fore part of the said Dray It so happened that the said Richard Martin fell off and from the said Dray upon the Stone pavement in the said Street by means whereof he the said Richard Martin then and there received Bruises in and upon his Body and a Fracture of two of his Ribbs on the Right side of which said mortal Bruises and Fracture he the said Richard Martin then and there died And so the Juror aforesaid upon their Oath aforesaid do say that the said Richard Martin in manner and by the means aforesaid Accidentally Casually and by Misfortune came to his Death and not otherwise In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner as the said Charles Chance Foreman of the said Jurors on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows in their presence have to this Inquisition set their Hands and seals the Day Year and Place abovementioned.

Tho Prickard Coroner Chas Chance Foreman
