Westminster City and Liberty in the County of Middlesex } of St. Martin in the fields in the Liberty of Westminster within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of Saint Peter at Westminster the Twentieth day of June in the first Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord George the Third by the Grace of God of Great Britain France and Ireland King Defender of the Faith and so forth, and in the Year of our Lord One Thousand seven hundred and sixty one . Before John Feary Coroner for our Sovereign Lord the King for the City and Liberty of Westminster aforesaid, Upon View of the Body of a Female Infant Child of the Age of three Months then and there lying Dead by the Oaths of John Anderson , Edmond Bailey Philip Sligell , Benjamin Jackson , Thomas Scott , Isaac Barnes , Joseph Young , Arthur Robertson , Edmond Daw , Thomas Baxter , William Marshall , George Warren , And Thomas Bowston Good and Lawfull Men of the, said City and Liberty who being Sworn and Charged to Enquire for our Sovereign Lord the King how when where and in what manner the said Female Instant Child came to her Death say upon their Oaths. That on Friday the, nineteenth day of June Instant the said Infant Child being found in a certain Boghouse belonging to a House, situate in Lumley Court in the Strand in the Parish of St. Martin in the fields in the Liberty and County aforesaid the said Jurors do find that some Person or Persons to them at present unknown, not having the fear of God before his her or their Eyes, but being Moved and Seduced by the Instigation of the Devil some time before the, said Nineteenth day of June in the Year aforesaid with Force and Arms in the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid in and upon the said Infant Child in the Peace of God and our said Lord the King then and there being did make an Assault, and that the said Person or Persons to the said Jurors at present unknown the said Infant Child into the Boghouse aforesaid did cast or put, by means of which casting or putting as aforesaid the said Infant Child was there suffocated and smothered. And so the said Jurors aforesaid upon their Oaths aforesaid do say that the said Person or Persons to them unknown the said Infant Child in the Parish aforesaid in the Liberty and County aforesaid in manner aforesaid did wilfully Kill and Murder against the Peace of our said Lord the King his Crown and Dignity. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the Foreman of the Jurors aforesaid have to this Inquisition set their hands and Seals the day Year and at the Place abovementioned

Jno. Feary Coroner John Anderson Forman
