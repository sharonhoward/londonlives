City and Liberty of Westminster in the County of Middlesex } to wit. 

An Inquisition Indented, taken for our Sovereign Lord the King, at The Parish of St. Martin in the Fields within the Liberty of the Dean and Chapter of the Collegiate Church of St. Peter, Westminster , in the County of Middlesex , the  First day of September in the Tenth Year of the Reign of our Sovereign Lord GEORGE the Third, by the Grace of God, of Great-Britain, France and Ireland, King, Defender of the Faith, and so forth, before Thomas Prickard , Gentleman, Coroner of our said Lord the King for the said City and Liberty, on View of the Body of William Graham then and there lying dead, upon the Oath of Alexander Parke , John Morris , William Johnson , William Griffin , Thomas Turner , James Steel , James Fox , Samuel Grammer , Robert Bamforth , James Beggins , Francis Burch , Richard Freebourn , John Lander , John Hand , William Haydon and John Greases good and lawful Men of the said Liberty, duly chosen, who being then and there duly sworn and charged to enquire for our said Lord the King, when, how, and by what Means the said William Graham came to h is Death, do upon their Oath say, That the said William Graham on the said First day of September in the Year aforesaid was found drowned and suffocated in the River Thames , to wit, at Hungerford Stairs , in the Parish aforesaid within the Liberty and County aforesaid, That no Marks of violence appeared on the Body of the said William Graham , but how or by what Means he became Drowned and Suffocated no Evidence thereof doth appear to the Jurors. In Witness whereof as well the said Coroner, as the said Alexander Parke Foreman of the said Jurors, on the behalf of himself and the rest of his Fellows, in their presence, have to this Inquisition set their Hands and Seals the Day year and Place first above written

Tho. Prickard Coroner Parke Foreman
